**Warp Creature** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Unit Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Magic** realm. For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **18** it may be cast on an enemy unit during combat. If the targeted unit fails a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") roll at [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-1**, that unit will be afflicted with one of three different penalties: [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **-50% Melee Attack strength**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **-50% Defense**, or [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-100% Resistance**. The spell may be applied three times to the same unit, in order to apply all three of the above penalties.

**Warp Creature**'s effects last until the end of the battle, and do not linger on to the campaign map. Thus, the spell has no [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

## Effects

**Warp Creature** attempts to inflict one of three different penalties on the target unit's combat statistics. It can make the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") weaker, reduce its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense"), or make it totally vulnerable to further spells.

### Random Status Penalties

Upon casting **Warp Creature** on an enemy unit, that unit gets to make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") roll at a penalty of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-1**.

Failure in this roll means that the unit will be afflicted with one of three different penalties, chosen entirely at random.

#### Option #1: Melee Attack Penalty

:   If this option is chosen, the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack Strength** is reduced by 50%, and then rounded down.

:   This effectively halves both the maximum and average damage this unit will deal with its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), barring any [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") it may have which are completely unaffected.

:   Note that [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Attack Strength** is not affected by **Warp Creature**, if the unit possesses it at all.

#### Option #2: Defense Penalty

:   If this option is chosen, the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rating is reduced by 50%, and then rounded down.

:   This effectively halves both the maximum and average amount of
    damage this unit will be able to block whenever struck by an enemy
    attack. As a result it will be easier to inflict damage on this unit.

#### Option #3: Zero Resistance

:   If this option is chosen, the unit's natural [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") score is immediately set to exactly [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **0**. However, additional modifiers may still apply. In particular, the [Resist Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Magic "Resist Magic") spell will continue to protect the unit despite a successful Warp Creature.

:   This effectively makes the unit completely vulnerable to the majority of [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") and other combat maledictions (such as [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze"), etc.) which require it to make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") roll.

:   In fact, this reduction in Resistance also makes the unit vulnerable to further attempts at casting **Warp Creature** on it, as described below.

### Warp Creature on top of Other Bonuses

When **Warp Creature** is affecting a creature that is enjoying bonuses to its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack Strength**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** or [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**, the penalties from **Warp Creature** are calculated *last*.

For example, assume we have a unit with a basic Melee Attack Strength of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **8** as well as a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+2 bonus** affecting it. When **Warp Creature** is cast on this unit, its Melee Attack Strength is taken as a whole: [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). **Warp Creature** then halves it to [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **5**.

The same occurs when the creature is enjoying [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** bonuses.

Creatures with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance Bonuses** will have their Resistance score lowered to 0 despite those bonuses. For example, a creature with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **8** and a bonus of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **2** will have its total resistance score reduced to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **0**, not to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **2**.

### Multiple Warp Creature

It is possible to cast **Warp Creature** repeatedly on the same unit - in an attempt to inflict all three possible penalties on that unit. In other words, if **Warp Creature** is cast successfully at a unit three times in the same battle, that unit will have [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **-50%**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **-50%**, *AND* [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **0**.

On the first casting, one of the three effects is chosen at
random, with a 33% chance of choosing any of them. On the second
casting, one of the *other two* effects is chosen with a 50% chance between them. The third casting will apply the remaining effect.

It is not possible to inflict the same effect twice on the same unit in the same battle. In other words, if a unit already has [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **-50%** due to **Warp Creature**, it will not get this effect a second time until the next battle (assuming it survives at all).

***Note:**  When the unit already has all 3 different **Warp Creature** effects on it, casting the spell successfully at this unit again will crash the game. See more on this in the **Known Bugs** section, below.*

## Usage

 [A unit affected by Warp Creature appears pink/purple with a brighter outline. This, however, does not indicate which effect is applied to this unit.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/5/54/Screenshot_WarpCreature.png/revision/latest?cb=20120131175831)

A unit affected by **Warp Creature** appears pink/purple with a brighter outline. This, however, does not indicate which effect is applied to this unit.



**Warp Creature** may only be cast during combat, for the [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **18**. Although the spell's in-game tooltip indicates that it may be cast on [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") only, in truth it can be successfully targeted at any enemy unit.

The only exception is that **Warp Creature** may not be targeted at units possessing the [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") ability. The game will refuse to target such units, asking you to pick another target instead.

The target gets to make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") roll at [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-1**, meaning that the effect can potentially fail. In fact, any unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **11** or more is absolutely immune to the spell. Casting **Warp Creature** on such a unit is simply a waste of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**.

Once the spell is successfully cast on a unit, it will
immediately affect that unit's statistics as explained above. A
different icon is added to the unit's "Abilities" list (in its details
panel) depending on which effect was inflicted by the spell. As
explained above, you can place all three effects on a unit, in which
case it will have three different icons in its ability list to indicate
this.

A unit affected by **Warp Creature** will appear on the battlefield map in a purplish/pinkish tint, as shown in the image to the right. To see *which* of **Warp Creature**'s effects are active on this unit, you have to check its details panel as explained above.

As a [Unit Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"), **Warp Creature**'s
effect(s) lingers until the end of the battle. Once combat ends the
spell will dissipate immediately, and will not carry over to the
campaign map. Therefore, the spell has no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") to be paid.

## Acquisition

As a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** spell, **Warp Creature** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least one [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is not guaranteed unless the Wizard acquires at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**7** Spellbooks.

Customized Wizards possessing up to [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**10**
Spellbooks at creation time may choose this spell as one of their
default spells before starting the game, in which case the spell will
already be researched and available for casting immediately on the first
turn. Wizards with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**11** Spellbooks are guaranteed to have this spell available for casting as soon as the game begins.

Wizards with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**6** or fewer Spellbooks who have not chosen **Warp Creature** as a guaranteed spell have a random chance of being able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") it. The chance for this spell to appear increases with the number of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or obtains during gameplay. With [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**−3** Spellbooks, the spell is guaranteed to appear for Research if it is not already available for casting.

**Warp Creature** has a base [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **220**.

With at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**1** Spellbook, the **Warp Creature** spell *may be* acquired as a reward for winning encounters in creature [Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lair "Lair"), [Towers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower"), et cetera, or when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival wizard who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

**Warp Creature** is a very powerful [Unit Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"),
as it can make a unit significantly less dangerous, significantly
easier to kill, or completely susceptible to further spellcasting (or
all three of these!).

The only drawback is that the spell allows the target to make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance")
roll. This means that higher-end units will be completely resistant to
it. As a result, the spell will likely fall out of favor as the campaign
develops, though it can still be used in conjunction with other [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance")-lowering spells.

One powerful use of **Warp Creature** is in combination with [Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain").
Once the unit has no resistance, a hero may drain it for hits or the
wizard may drain it for spell skill, and get the unit as undead after
winning the battle. Heroes with artifacts giving penalties to resistance
can even do this to otherwise impervious units.

## Known Bugs

When a unit is affected by all three **Warp Creature** effects, a successful attempt to cast another **Warp Creature** spell on the same unit during this battle will cause the game to freeze/crash.

This is probably a result of the game trying to make a random
roll to determine which effect to place on the unit, failing to do so
because no further effects are available (the unit already has all 3),
and making another random roll and another and another.

If you've cast lots of **Warp Creature**
spells during a battle, always check your next target's details panel
to see whether it already has all available effects. If so, do not cast
the spell on this target again.
