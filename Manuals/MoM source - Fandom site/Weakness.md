**Weakness** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Unit Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") from to the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5** it may be cast on an enemy unit during combat. The target gets to make one [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") roll as a unit to avert this curse, at a penalty of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-2**. If it fails, it immediately receives [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **-2** and [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **-2**. These penalties will continue to affect the unit until the end of the battle, or until **Weakness** is dispelled. Although the spell is also supposed to apply a penalty of [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **-2**, due to a bug, it does not do so in the official game.

## Effects

**Weakness** can
significantly reduce an enemy unit's attack strength - whether it's a
Melee Attack, Ranged Attack, or both. This renders the unit less
dangerous, and may make it completely incapable of attacking.

### Melee Attack Penalty

While **Weakness** is affecting a unit, that unit's [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength") is reduced by [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **-2**. This decreases the unit's maximum "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output by [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** points (per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**) and, consequently, also reduces the *average* amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that the unit will do with its [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

Any unit reaching [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **0** or less due to this effect will skip the [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") phase of the [Melee Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Attack_Sequence "Melee Attack") altogether, including any [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")
that would be delivered along with this damage. Although it may still
initiate attacks, the AI will no longer do so, even if the unit
possesses [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that it may still be capable of using.

### Ranged Missile Attack Penalty

While **Weakness** is affecting a unit, the unit's [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") is also reduced by [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **-2**, provided that it has such an attack at all. Other types of [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") are not affected. Again, this decreases the "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** potential of this [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") by [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** points (per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**), thereby reducing the target's average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output.

Although a unit reaching [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **0** or less as a result of this effect may still technically be capable of making [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (assuming it has any ammo left), it will inflict no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and may deliver no ranged [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") either.

### Immunities

Units with [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") or [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), and units under the [Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness") [Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness") effect are completely immune to this spell; and so are [Charmed](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charmed "Charmed") [Charmed](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charmed "Charmed") [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). Furthermore, any unit with a total [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **12** or more (after other modifiers) will never fail the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll and thus will not be affected either. The conditional Resistance bonus of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** spell also applies against **Weakness**.

## Usage

 [A unit of afflicted with Weakness.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/5/59/Screenshot_Weakness.png/revision/latest?cb=20120302024346)

A unit of [War Bears](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/War_Bears "War Bears") afflicted with **Weakness**.



**Weakness** may only be cast during combat. It has a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5**,
and must always be targeted at an enemy unit that is not already under
the effect of this curse. The target gets exactly one chance (as a unit)
to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** the effect with a default modifier of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-2**, although if **Weakness** is cast by a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), this may be improved by [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") bearing a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Spell Save**
enchantment. The curse will last until the end of the battle, until it
is dispelled, or until the target unit is destroyed; whichever occurs
first.

## Acquisition

As a [Common Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Weakness** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**1** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With none, the spell can naturally not be learned during the campaign.
This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Weakness** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 30% (with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**1** book), which gradually increases with the amount of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**7** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **40**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**8** or more.

**Weakness** may also be selected as a starting spell by any Wizard with at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**2** books. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

In addition, **Weakness** is available as a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") spell. It is known by [Ravashack the Necromancer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ravashack_the_Necromancer "Ravashack the Necromancer"), although it's worth noting that this feature is bugged in the latest official version of the game, and **Weakness**
is one of the few affected spells. This means that Ravashack will
likely not be able to actually access this spell unless the player
installs one of the unofficial patches.

## Strategy

Weakness can be used in either of two ways: it can neutralize a weak
unit, or it can hinder a strong unit. Depending on the situation on the
battlefield, choosing which unit to inflict with **Weakness**
can be a tough call. However, reducing a very powerful unit's strength
by such a small amount is often pointless, although it may still be a
deciding factor in one-on-one combat between two such unit. Still, in
most cases, there are usually better spells to cast in that scenario.

On the other hand, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** normally suffer more from this spell, as the reduction will affect the attacks of each of their [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. Also, since most [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** do not have a very high [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") to begin with, **Weakness** can actually cut such a unit's effectiveness by half or even more. Finally, weaker units tend to have somewhat lower [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** scores as well, making it more likely that they will fail their check against the spell.

## Known Bugs

The in-game help text of the **Weakness** spell and the tooltip of the **Weakness** effect itself both indicate that the spell also reduces [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"). However, due to a mismatched parameter check in the official game code, this does not actually happen, and [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** are completely unaffected by **Weakness**. This is corrected in the latest release candidates of the [Unofficial 1.51 Patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

In addition, this spell can bring the [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength")
of certain units to zero, which will cause the AI to stop moving or
attacking with the unit, regardless of other properties. This can negate
the use of certain Special Attacks that are otherwise unaffected by the
spell, but would only be executed on voluntary attacks, such as [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath-** and [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**. Units with [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") are also affected, although in this case the situation is marginally better, as they can at least utilize these abilities on [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").
Unless, of course, the bug is exploited to simply gain more time to
kill them off at range. The Unofficial Patch 1.50 corrects this
behaviour. In fact, it also changes the rules slightly, so that "added
effects", such as [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), will trigger even from attacks that have a conventional Attack Strength of zero.

Along with all other [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"), **Weakness**
is also a victim of another bug, namely that dispel-type effects skip
the usual checks when attempting to remove it, and will thus always
automatically succeed in doing so. This error is also fixed in the
Unofficial Patch 1.50.

Finally, the tooltip of the **Weakness**
effect actually indicates that the spell does not allow a Resistance
roll - a feature that was removed in the official patch 1.1.
Unfortunately, not all entries in the in-game help have been updated to
reflect this change, with the effect tooltip being one that has been
left out.
