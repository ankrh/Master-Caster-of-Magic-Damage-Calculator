**Illusions Immunity** (sometimes called "**Illusion Immunity**") is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

Units possessing this ability have several benefits. Primarily, they may not be targeted by the vast majority of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** combat spells. Furthermore, units with **Illusions Immunity** can see [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") creatures during combat, and are not thwarted by other protective [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** spells. Finally, when [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") is inflicted on a unit with **Illusions Immunity**, it does not bypass armor as it would against any other unit.

**Illusions Immunity** is one of the more common immunities in the game. It is possessed primarily by [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), particularly those from the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** realms. Furthermore, any [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit has **Illusions Immunity** as well.

It is possible to add **Illusions Immunity** to any unit by casting the [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") on it. [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can also acquire this ability through certain [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item").

## Description

The [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery Realm** and several of its [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature")
rely on toying with the enemy's mind – using illusions and other
psychological attacks to undermine or even physically hurt the enemy.
There are many ways in which this is done, and many possible adverse
effects for the target of such an attack.

Fortunately, it is possible to protect a mind from such attacks.
Training or even magical barriers can prevent illusions from adversely
affecting a unit. Furthermore, some units in the game have no minds to
attack, and thus are immune to illusions by that virtue.

## Effect

**Illusions Immunity** is a passive ability that protects a unit from a myriad of various effects related primarily to the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery Realm**. **Illusions Immunity** ensures that the unit will not be harmed or adversely affected by them.

### Immunity to Sorcery Combat Spells

During combat, a unit with **Illusions Immunity** cannot be targeted by the following spells from the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** realm:

* [Creature Binding](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Creature_Binding "Creature Binding")
* [Confusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Confusion "Confusion")
* [Vertigo](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vertigo "Vertigo")
* [Mind Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mind_Storm "Mind Storm")

Any attempt to target these spells at a unit with **Illusions Immunity** will result in an error message stating that the target is invalid. You must select a different target or cancel the spell.

Note that some offensive [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** spells *can* target a unit with **Illusions Immunity**:

* [Psionic Blast](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Psionic_Blast "Psionic Blast")
* [Banish](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Banish "Banish")

***Note:**  The Strategy Guide states that [Psionic Blast](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Psionic_Blast "Psionic Blast") cannot target such units, but in this case the guide is in error (as of patch 1.31). Though, units with **Illusions Immunity** will use their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** to negate the first points of damage.*

Furthermore, units with **Illusions Immunity** will not feel any adverse effects from the [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") [Mass Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Invisibility "Mass Invisibility").

Note that overland spells (such as [Stasis](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stasis "Stasis") or [Great Unsummoning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Unsummoning "Great Unsummoning")) can affect units with **Illusions Immunity** without a problem. The immunity applies only during combat.

### Seeing Invisible Units

Normally during combat, it is impossible to see any enemy units on the battlefield that possess [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility"), unless they are immediately adjacent to one of your units. It is also impossible to use any [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") against [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") targets.

However, if your army contains at least one living unit with **Illusions Immunity**, all enemy [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility")
units appear on the battlefield map, partly translucent. You can thus
track such units' movements, and are therefore less open to sudden
flanking attacks by them.

Furthermore, a unit with both **Illusions Immunity** and a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") can use this ranged attack to fire at [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") units anywhere on the battlefield. Note that other friendly [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") units still may not target [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") enemies – attempting this will result in an error message.

Finally, when a unit with **Illusions Immunity** makes any attack against an [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") target, it *does not* suffer the mandatory [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** penalty. This makes **Illusions-Immune** units great for taking out [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") creatures.

**Note:**  **Illusions Immunity** only helps spot [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") enemy creatures during combat. Such creatures are never spotted on the overland map, even if a unit with **Illusions Immunity** is standing in the adjacent tile.

### Immunity to Illusion Damage

Several units in the game have [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") as part of their attack. When such a unit makes an attack against an enemy unit, the enemy does not get to make any [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls to try and block damage. It is as though the enemy's Defense score was suddenly reduced to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**!

However, when [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") is inflicted on an **Illusions-Immune** unit, the unit gets to make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls as normal. Its armor is not ignored.

## Acquiring Illusions Immunity

There are several ways to add **Illusions Immunity** to a unit that does not possess it by default:

* Turning the unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit (there are several ways),
* Casting [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") on the unit,
* Equipping a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") unit with an item imbued with the [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power").

Each of these methods adds the same kind of **Illusions Immunity** described above in this article.

### Undead Illusions Immunity

It is not a coincidence that most of the units with default **Illusions Immunity** area [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death**
creatures: these creatures are not alive, and have no mind to attack
with illusions. Similarly, units that die and are brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units are also **Immune to Illusions**.

Units can be turned into [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") in several different ways:

* The [Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels") spell will turn a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by [Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain") damage is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by an enemy unit with [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle and then targeted by [Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the caster of this spell.

Regardless of which method is used, the unit comes back with most of its original abilities intact – but now possesses the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property which, among other effects, also gives the unit **Illusions Immunity**.

Note that the icon for **Illusions Immunity** is not added to the unit's details panel. However, the presence of the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property icon is always sufficient to indicate that **Illusions Immunity** is indeed in effect.

### True Sight Unit Enchantment

[True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") belonging to the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** realm. When cast on a unit, it will add the **Illusions Immunity** ability to that unit.

When cast on the overland map, [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **100**. It has a lingering effect, so that the targeted unit will keep its [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") ability so long as the spell continues to exist. While it exists, the spell requires an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **2** to be paid at the start of each turn.

Alternatively, it is possible to cast [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") during combat as an impromptu [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). In this case the spell's basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") is only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **20**,
but its effects will only last until the end of the battle (or until
dispelled by the enemy). Since neither the spell nor the effect will
carry over to the overland map, there is no associated [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") with this version of the spell.

Note that [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") also enables a unit to make [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (if it has them at all) through a [Wall of Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Darkness "Wall of Darkness"). This property is not available to units possessing **Illusions Immunity** by default. This can present somewhat of a problem, since [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") cannot be cast on units already possessing **Illusions Immunity**.

### True Sight Item Power

A permanent version of the [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") spell can be imbued into a [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") piece. When a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wears this item, he/she receives the **Illusions Immunity** ability (as well as the ability to make [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") through [Wall of Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Darkness "Wall of Darkness")).

During [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting"), it is only possible to select the "True Sight" bonus if you possess at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**6 Life** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
Otherwise, "True Sight" is not available. Of course, it is always
possible to find an item already enchanted with "True Sight" as reward
for defeating an [Encounter](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter").

The **Illusions Immunity** ability bestowed by an item is transient: it will disappear if the Hero ever removes or loses the enchanted item.

Note that the Hero's details panel will list this as "True Sight" (along with the icon associated with the [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") spell). As mentioned previously, this is simply **Illusions Immunity** coupled with the ability to make [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") through a [Wall of Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Darkness "Wall of Darkness"). It is otherwise identical to **Illusions Immunity** in its basic form.
