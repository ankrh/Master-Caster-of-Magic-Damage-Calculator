[Ability CauseFear](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/1/13/Ability_CauseFear.png/revision/latest?cb=20120205050640)

**Cause Fear** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*. With this ability, a unit makes a powerful [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that delivers Fear Damage - temporarily prohibiting individual enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** from dealing [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") to it. The target may make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll to try and avoid this effect, partially or completely.

Two [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") possess the **Cause Fear** ability by default. In addition, it is possible to add **Cause Fear** to a unit by casting the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear "Cloak of Fear")** [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") upon it. Furthermore, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may be equipped with [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") imbued with a [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear_(Item_Power) "Cloak of Fear (Item Power)")** [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). The resulting effect of all three methods is exactly the same.

## Description

One of the best ways to defeat an enemy is to dissuade it from
fighting at all. For example, by scaring it beyond its wits - freezing
the enemy in place with a debilitating fear. There are creatures in the
world of *Master of Magic* who are so terrifying to behold, that
enemy combatants facing them must overcome their own natural urge to
just flee in terror. The [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**
in particular even possesses ways to make a creature seem as though it
is much scarier than it really is. If the enemy freezes from fear, it
cannot attack - thus providing indirect protection to the fear-inspiring
creature.

## Effect

**Cause Fear** refers simultaneously to a [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") (confusingly called "Cause Fear Spell" in the game), and to its effect - making a single, unique kind of [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") which delivers **Fear Damage**. Both "**Fear Attack**" and "**Fear Damage**" are completely unique, resembling no other [attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") or [damage type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") in the game - and both are used only by the **Cause Fear** effect.

### Fear Attack

**Cause Fear** is triggered whenever the unit is making a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against an opponent, or is making a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to an opponent. The exact timing for this is immediately before the opponent would deliver its [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (and any [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") associated with it).

At this point, **Cause Fear** executes a **Fear Attack** against that opponent, with the intent of preventing some or all of the target's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** from being able to act for the remainder of the melee combat sequence (the exchange of Melee Damage).

In other words, if the **Fear Attack** is successful, the
target unit simply stops fighting for the rest of this combat round. On
the other hand, by this stage, the enemy already had a chance to execute
its [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), or [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") - if it has any. The **Fear Attack**
can only prevent the enemy from proceeding to deliver actual Melee
Damage and any Touch Attacks it might have. Still, the resulting
prevention of damage may still be significant - even though it is not as
effective against, say, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire-Breathing** units.

### Fear Damage

The attack described above delivers only **Fear Damage** at the target, and is the only source of this damage type in the game. **Fear Damage** causes no actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **harm** to the target. Instead, it may cause some (or all) [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in the enemy unit to immediately stop fighting - preventing them from delivering [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack").

**Fear Damage** does this by forcing each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** within the target unit to make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check. Any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** that fails this check is struck with unspeakable terror. Until the end of the current combat process, the terrorized [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** will deliver no more damage, nor make any attacks against the **Fear-Causing** unit.

In technical terms, each and every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** of the target unit rolls a random number between 1 and 10, and then compares it to the unit's current [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** attribute:

* If the rolled number is *higher than* the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** is struck by fear and becomes unable to attack. This prevents that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** from delivering any [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (and added [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") if it has one) at the **Fear-Causing** unit.
* If the rolled number is *equal to or lower than* the unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score, the specific [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** that rolled this number is completely unaffected.

The effect of **Fear Damage** dissipates immediately once the current combat process ends. Thus, any affected [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** that survives can fight as normal the next time it engages in Melee combat.

#### Example

:   Lets imagine a unit of [Gnoll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Spearmen "Gnoll Spearmen") going into Melee Combat against a [Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord"); it doesn't matter which attacked the other, since the process is the same in both cases.

:   The Gnoll Spearmen unit contains [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8 figures**, and has a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **4**. The Demon Lord possesses **Cause Fear**.

:   **Cause Fear** will be triggered whenever the Demon Lord makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against the Spearmen, and also whenever the Spearmen make a Melee Attack against the Demon Lord. **Cause Fear** behaves the same in each case.

:   When it is time for both units to exchange [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), **Cause Fear** first forces each and every **Spearman** to make a Resistance roll: each **Spearman** rolls a random number between 1 and 10, and compares it to the unit's Resistance score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **4**.

:   Any **Spearman** who rolls 5 or higher is struck by **Cause Fear**, and cannot deliver any Melee Damage at the Demon Lord. If all 8 [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** failed their rolls, the unit would deliver no Melee Damage at all! Meanwhile, the [Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord") is free to deliver its own damage as normal.

### Immunities

Units with [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") or [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity"), units under the effect of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**, and [Charmed](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charmed "Charmed") [Charmed](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charmed "Charmed") Heroes will never be affected by a **Fear Attack**.

Since **Fear Damage** has no [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty, units with a modified Resistance score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** or higher are also immune to its effect, since they cannot possibly fail their Resistance rolls. The conditional bonuses of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Resist Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Magic "Resist Magic")** both apply to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks against **Cause Fear**, regardless of its source.

## Units with Default Cause Fear

Two units in the game possess **Cause Fear** by default. They are both powerful [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"):

[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**
[Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord")**

## Acquiring Cause Fear

The **Cause Fear** ability can be also added artificially to any unit via the **Cloak of Fear** effect, which is available both as a [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") and [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") Power. Both of these yield an effect that is identical to the innate **Cause Fear** possessed by the above Fantastic Creatures.

### Cloak of Fear Unit Enchantment

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear "Cloak of Fear")** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") Unit Enchantment. When cast on any unit, it will add the **Cause Fear** ability to that unit (although this is not displayed separately). The ability remains while the spell is in effect.

The spell has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **60** when cast on the overland map. In this case it is a lingering effect, so the unit gets to keep its **Cause Fear** ability as long as the spell remains active. However, the caster must pay an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** per turn to keep the spell in play.

Alternatively, it is possible to cast [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear "Cloak of Fear")** during combat for a cost of only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **12**.
This version of the spell will only last until the end of the battle,
at which point it automatically dissipates. As such, tt has no
additional Upkeep Costs.

### Cloak of Fear Item Power

A permanent version of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear_(Item_Power) "Cloak of Fear (Item Power)")** can be imbued into any [Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shield "Shield"), [Chainmail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chainmail "Chainmail"), [Platemail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Platemail "Platemail") or [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") piece. While a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") is wearing this item, he/she receives the **Cause Fear** ability (not displayed separately).

During [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting"), [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear_(Item_Power) "Cloak of Fear (Item Power)")** can only be selected by [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") possessing at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**2 Death** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). Of course, it is always possible to find an item already enchanted with this [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") as reward for defeating an [Encounter](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter").

The **Cause Fear** ability bestowed by any item is transient: it will disappear if the Hero ever removes or loses the enchanted item.

## Known Bugs

In version 1.31 of the game, there are two distinctive bugs with **Cause Fear** (regardless of its source), both of which are fixed in the unofficial patches:

* If the **Cause Fear** unit is defending against a Melee Attack, the ability has no effect at all.
* If the unit with the ability is the attacker, it is instead *also* affected by the ability, in a rather peculiar way. The *defender* rolls [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** for each of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** (a second time), and for each failed roll, one of the attacking [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** also gets feared and deals no damage. This is in addition to the defender being subjected to **Cause Fear**, and the attacker's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** or immunities play absolutely no part. If there is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
  disparity between the units in favor of the attacker, this can still
  result in the ability being at least somewhat beneficial. However, that
  will never happen for the units with the innate or Magical Item versions
  of **Cause Fear**, as they are all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **single figures**.
