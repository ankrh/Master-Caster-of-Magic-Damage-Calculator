A **Melee Attack** is the term used to refer to direct, physical, face-to-face combat between any two units. A **Melee Attack**
is performed when a single unit moves adjacently to an enemy target on
the battlefield, and then assaults it directly with weapons, claws,
teeth, or whatever other physical weapons are available.

Unlike [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), **Melee Attacks**
cannot be performed at a distance - they require close proximity since
physical contact is always made by the two units. This close proximity
allows the attacker to also simultaneously unleash "subsidiary" attacks,
including [Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), [Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), which can significantly increase the damage caused to the target.

On the downside, the close proximity also gives the target an opportunity to make a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") against the assailant - a process that occurs simultaneously with the **Melee Attack**. In other words, the target gets to inflict its own damage upon the assailant, putting both units at risk.

All **Melee Attacks** deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") by default, which is intended to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage points** to the target - potentially killing one or more of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. Again, if the attacker and/or the defender possess any [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), these are often executed as part of the **Melee Attack**
and may cause all sorts of subsidiary harm to either unit. Most units
do not possess such attacks, and thus will only deliver Melee Damage.

Initiating a **Melee Attack** on an enemy unit takes a number of Movement Points equal to 50% of the attacker's [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"). A **Melee Attack** may not be performed by a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit against a [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit, unless special conditions apply.

## Concept

During a battle between two armies, units may have different ways of
inflicting damage upon their enemies. The most common (and usually most
effective) way of doing so is for a unit to march up to an enemy unit
and perform a **Melee Attack** against it.

In a **Melee Attack**, the attacking unit (known as the **Assailant**)
is assumed to be making direct "hand-to-hand" contact with the enemy -
striking at it with hand-held weapons or (in the case of monsters)
claws, teeth, et cetera.

The defending unit (known as the **Target**) usually gets the chance to retaliate. This is called a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). When this occurs, both the assailant and the target deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") to each other simultaneously! Therefore, a **Melee Attack** is often riskier than [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

**Melee Attacks** are
distinguished from Ranged Attacks and direct-damage spells in several
different ways, due to the physical contact made between the assailant
and the target. Most importantly, for a **Melee Attack** to occur the assailant and the target must be adjacent to each other. A **Melee Attack** may never be performed if there is one or more tiles of distance between them.

Furthermore, once a **Melee Attack** has been initiated, both assailant and target get the chance to employ additional [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") as dictated by their abilities. This may include [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and so forth, most of which take place just prior to the distribution of normal Melee Damage.

### Ordering a Melee Attack

 [Melee Attacks may only be executed against a target that is immediately adjacent to the attacker. Diagonal attacks are fine.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/9/99/Screenshot_Melee.png/revision/latest?cb=20120120161755)

**Melee Attacks** may only be executed against a target that is immediately adjacent to the attacker. Diagonal attacks are fine.



Before initiating any **Melee Attack**
against any target, the attacking unit must first move adjacently to
that target. In other words, the attacker and the target must be in
adjacent tiles on the battlefield for any **Melee** to occur between them. Diagonal **Melee Attacks** are allowed.

While the attacker is selected, put the mouse cursor over the desired target. If a **Melee Attack** is possible, the cursor will change into an image of two crossed swords. Clicking the mouse button in this case will trigger a **Melee Attack**
by the selected unit against the selected target. If the target is
invalid for an attack (for any reason), the cursor will turn into a big
red X, and no attack can be ordered.

Of course, computer-controlled units perform the same process without all the visible clicking.

### Dealing Melee Damage

:   *Main article: [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")*

The main goal in most **Melee Attacks** is to deliver normal **Melee Damage** - damage from physical weapons such as swords, spears, claws or teeth, as available.

Under the simplest circumstances, a **Melee Attack** consists simply of the assailant attempting to deliver as much of this **Melee Damage** as it can to its target. It will try to kill off as many of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the enemy target as it can, or otherwise injure the target unit as much as possible.

[Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is explained in its own article (see also [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") for a thorough explanation of the process). In general, the assailant is required to make a number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls in order to determine how many times it has struck the target successfully. The target then gets to make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense**
rolls to try and block or dodge some (or all) of these hits, minimizing
damage to itself. After all rolls have been completed, "unblocked" hits
are translated into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** and inflicted directly at the target's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health** score.

### Special Attacks

:   *Main article: [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")*

Delivery of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (see above) is only one part of the **Melee Attack** process. For many units, the **Melee Attack**
actually consists of several different attacks being performed in rapid
succession against the target, with each attack potentially delivering
extra harm to the target just before or just after Melee Damage is
processed. The sequence of these attacks is very specific, and is of
extreme importance.

These extra attacks are called "[Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")", and many deliver "[Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage")" which often behaves nothing like the [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") described above. In most cases, the purpose of **Special Attacks** is to kill off extra enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** or neutralize them in one way or another.

**Special Attacks** may include [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"),
and so forth. Each of these attacks delivers a specific type of damage
as defined by the unit's abilities and/or equipment. For example, the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") ability allows a unit to perform a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") delivering [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage").

As explained [below](#Melee_Attack_Sequence), the sequence in which **Special Attacks** are delivered is quite rigid, and is very important. The sooner a **Special Attack**
occurs, the more potential it has for preventing the enemy from
retaliating (explained in the next section), thus giving extra
protection to the attacker.

### Counter Attacks

:   *Main article: [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")*

In most circumstances, making a **Melee Attack** against a unit allows that unit to retaliate. This is known as a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). In essence, the target gets to deliver its *own* [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") on the attacker, in much the same way as described above.

Barring special abilities and so forth, [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")
is dealt simultaneously by both the target and the attacker. Therefore,
the attacker may be in as much risk as the target! This is the complete
opposite of [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), where no retaliation is allowed - making the attacker completely safe while the target is the only one who takes damage.

The process of handling **Counter Attacks** is very complex, and is explained in more details further down this article and on [its own article](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

### Movement Costs

Any **Melee Attack** initiated by a unit (i.e. not a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")) costs that unit *half* of its total [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"). For example, a unit with a Movement Allowance of [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**3** will spend 1.5 Movement Points when making a **Melee Attack** against any enemy.

As a result, a unit may make two separate **Melee Attacks**
each turn - spending half of its movement allowance on one attack, then
the remaining half on another attack. Each attack may be directed at a
different target, if so desired. This of course would leave the unit
with no Movement Points to spend on actual movement this turn.

Alternatively, units may move before and/or after their **Melee Attack** - in other words, spending half their movement points on attacking, and the rest on movement towards or away from the enemy.

If the unit has at least 0.5 Movement Points remaining, but less
than half its Movement Allowance remaining, it will simply spend all
remaining points on the attack, and its turn ends immediately
afterwards.

Units with 0 Movement Points remaining may not attack at all.

## Melee Attack Sequence

As explained earlier, many units' **Melee Attacks** give the unit a chance to use its various [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), if it has any. Also, the target of the **Melee Attack** performs its own [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), which can also be comprised of several Special Attacks executed in rapid succession. [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is only exchanged between the attacker and the target at the very end of the **Melee Attack** process!

In most cases, the sequence is as follows:

| Step | Assailant | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") OR [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") | -- |
|  | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 2 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 3 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") |

The purpose of this stage-based process is to give the combatants a
chance to kill-off or neutralize some or more of the opponent's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, reducing the opponent's overall strength before the next stage begins - especially before the opponent can deliver any of its [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (at stage 3). Thus, a unit with plenty of powerful [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") - particularly [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") and/or [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") - has a chance of completely preventing the enemy from retaliating, and thus minimize or eliminate damage to itself.

The reason is that any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** killed off during any of the above stages *does not* get to participate in the subsequent stages of the attack. For example, if a target loses half of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
during stage 1, its overall strength by step 3 is half what it had at
the start of the process, thus significantly reducing the risk to its
opponent.

If either the attacker or defender has lost all of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** by the end of any of the above steps, then the **Melee Attack** ends prematurely with victory for the other unit (assuming it survived).

A unit executes only those [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that are available to it, and only at the appropriate stages in the process. For example, if the attacker lacks both [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") and [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), then step 1 is skipped. There are many instances where only step 3 is executed, with both units exchanging only [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") since neither possesses any Special Attacks whatsoever.

### Altering the Sequence

For the most part, the sequence of a **Melee Attack** and the enemy's [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") is set in stone, as described by the table above.

However, there is one [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") in the game which alters this sequence: the [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") ability.

When the attacker possesses [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike"), the process is rearranged as follows:

| Step | Assailant | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") OR [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") | -- |
|  | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 2 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 3 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") | -- |
| 4 | -- | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") |

By moving the attacker's [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") and [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") stages up, the attacker has a better chance of killing or neutralizing enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** before they get any chance to retaliate (apart from [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), which are always executed first). Again, the more enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
can be neutralized this way, the less overall strength the enemy has
for stage 4, and thus the less damage is caused to the assailant,
statistically.

[First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") only works for the attacker in any given **Melee Attack** - it is never used when [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). Furthermore, if the defender has the [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike") [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike") ability, then the attacker's [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") does not trigger, and the sequence remains as described [earlier](#Melee_Attack_Sequence).

The complete picture is as follows. It happens when the attacker possesses [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike"), [Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste"), and a couple of others, and the defender has [Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste"), [Wall of Fire](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Fire "Wall of Fire") and a couple of others.

| Step | Assailant | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1st [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") or [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack")\*,   1st [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* | -- |
| 2nd [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") or [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack")\*,   2nd [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* | -- |
| Only 1 [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")\*,   1st [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* | -- |
| 2 | -- | Only 1 [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")\*,   1st [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* |
| 3 | [Wall of Fire](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Fire "Wall of Fire") | -- |
| 4 | -- | [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") |
| 5 | 1st [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")\*\*\*,   3rd/2nd [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* | -- |
| 6 | [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") | -- |
| 7 | 2nd [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")\*\*\*,   4th/3rd [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* | 1st [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")\*\*\*,   2nd [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* |
| -- | 2nd [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")\*\*\*,   3rd [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") & [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")\*\* |

*\* [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), [Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") or [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") is only dealt in this subphase if the ranged attack strength is at least 1.*
*\*\* [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") and [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") is only dealt in this subphase if the previously listed attack is dealt. If the [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") comes from a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), then the previously listed attack must match the weapon's type.*
*\*\*\* [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is only dealt in this subphase if the melee attack strength is at least 1.*

## Ground vs. Air

Units that move by [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** and/or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** cannot initiate a **Melee Attack** against units that [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Fly**. Even if the two units are immediately adjacent to each other, the ground unit may not attack the air unit voluntarily.

The reverse, however, is different. Airborne units may freely
attack ground units at will, barring any special combat effects that may
specifically restrict this for any reason.

There is one main exception to this rule: Ground units possessing [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") or [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") abilities may attack airborne units as normal.

[Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")
are almost always allowed regardless of the units' movement types.
Therefore, when an airborne unit attacks a ground unit, the ground unit
may retaliate as normal. However again, it may not *initiate* an attack against the airborne unit - only retaliation is allowed.
