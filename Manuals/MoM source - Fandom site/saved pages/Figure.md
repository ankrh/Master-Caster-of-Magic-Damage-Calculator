All units in *Master of Magic* are comprised of individual entities called **Figures**, represented by the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") icon throughout this wiki. How many of these are still capable of fighting is known as the unit's **Figure Count**. The maximum amount of **Figures** that a unit can have is hardcoded for each type of unit, and is one its base attributes.

Some units, most notably [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), ships, war machines, and high-end [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), contain only a single **Figure**. They are often called *Single-Figure Units* for this reason, and signified across the wiki by the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") symbol. Combat with these units is somewhat risky, as losing their only **Figure**
means the destruction of the unit itself. However, they are typically
quite powerful in comparison, and suffer no ill effects from taking [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that does not destroy them.

Conversely, units that can have more than one **Figure** are called **Multi-Figure Units**, a fact often denoted using the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure")
icon on the wiki. The entities making up these units are generally
weaker individually, sometimes by a large margin. They become
competitive on the battlefield thanks to their numbers. However, as
these units suffer [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, they gradually start losing their [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, becoming weaker and weaker offensively. The unit is not destroyed though, as long as it has at least one **Figure** remaining.

The amount of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in a unit plays a major role in combat resolution. Most [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks, and even many [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), are executed individually by each standing **Figure** in a unit. Thus, the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, the more potential [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** the unit can deliver. The maximum **Figure** count also determines the unit's maximum health, or [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, which in turn sets out the amount that they can naturally heal between overland turns.

## Concept

Many strategy games represent battle units as groups of individuals. *Master of Magic* is no different. Each **Figure** in a unit is a separate soldier or creature. Unlike most similar games though, attacks made by the unit are typically *not* an aggregate generated from the sum of the **Figures**. Instead, although the act of attacking is still unit-wide, every **Figure**'s
attack is actually resolved on its own. Similarly, if a unit suffers an
attack capable of hurting multiple creatures or soldiers, they can also
each defend on their own. While there are some exceptions in both
cases, this makes combat more realistic overall, and amplifies the
importance of unit attributes, such as [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") and [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense").

## Current and Maximum Figures

In most scenarios where a unit's **Figure** count is examined, what matters will be the amount of its *current* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. This is what determines how many attacks the unit can make in a single action, or how many [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance Rolls** it has to make against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") spells and [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"). The *maximum* **Figure** count instead comes into play whenever the unit is healed, whether naturally, or by magical means. While remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** are dynamic, the maximum count is pre-set, and is one of the defining attributes for each type of unit.

## Single-Figure Units

Units with a maximum **Figure** count of **1** are collectively termed [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure** units. They are typically separated from other units either by size, or by capabilities. The former group includes all ships; [Settlers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Settlers "Settlers"); [Catapults](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult") and [Steam Cannons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Steam_Cannon "Steam Cannon"); [Stag Beetles](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stag_Beetle "Stag Beetle"); [Dragon Turtles](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon_Turtle "Dragon Turtle"); and large [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), with the sole exception of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**, whose heads are actually considered individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. The latter group contains all [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"); [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Magic-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit")** and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirits](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")**; [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Angels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Angel "Angel")** and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Arch Angels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arch_Angel "Arch Angel")**; [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Night Stalkers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Night_Stalker "Night Stalker")**, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Demons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon "Demon")**, and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Demon Lords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord")**; [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Djinni](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Djinn "Djinn")** and [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Efreeti](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Efreet "Efreet")**, and the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**. Units with the [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") ability are always [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figures**, although some [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units also have spell-like [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") that can be used once per battle per unit.

**Single-Figure Units** generally have much higher [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure** values than those that contain multiple [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, although this does not necessarily mean a higher [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** total, especially when compared to mounted units, that have two to four riders. This is important because [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure** units retain their full combat effectiveness until slain or destroyed, meaning that they suffer no ill effect from taking [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

Similarly, the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Armor** and [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength")
of these units also tends to be higher, although the margin here is
often not that large. Because opponents have to attack individually, the
higher [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** means that it requires more potential [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to consistently hurt [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure** units. In other words, units with more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
but lower strength typically have a harder time injuring them. On the
other hand, the higher Attack Strength of these units therefore makes
them better suited to combat other [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Singe-Figure** units.

Because of the singular **Figure**, many combat mechanics are easier to understand in relation to **Single-Figure Units**. The total health of the unit always equals the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of that one **Figure**, while its recorded [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") will always match the "top figure damage" (see below). Since the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** falling in battle automatically destroys the entire unit, there is no system of losing- and regaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** to explain either. However, it may be worth remembering that internally, the game does not distinguish between [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units. The mechanics described below apply equally to **Single-Figure Units** too, even if they don't normally have any practical consequence.

## Multi-Figure Units

Units with a maximum **Figure** count of 2 or more are called [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**. These can range anywhere between [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **2** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **9 figures**, although this last amount is only avaliable on the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**.
The maximum count is typically related to the size or space
requirements of the individual entities that comprise the unit. For
example, most [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") containing foot soldiers have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6 figures**. [Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spearmen "Spearmen") and [Pikemen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Pikemen "Pikemen"), that attack in formation, have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8**; while ground units mounted on horses or similar beasts consist of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4** riders.

 [The](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/9/92/Hydra_Head_Count.png/revision/latest?cb=20181120035625)

The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** is the only unit whose "**Figure**" count is numerically indicated when inspecting it.



The current **Figure** count of **Multi-Figure Units** is not numerically indicated anywhere in the game, with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** again being a sole exception. Instead, the player must visually inspect the unit in combat, or in the *unit statistics window* overland, and count the **Figures**
manually. Although the unit's total health is shown as a bar both
overland and during battles, this is often not a sufficient indicator.
Because a **Figure** with even a single [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** left can perform attacks, whether there is one less or more can make all the difference in certain situations.

### The Top Figure

[Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** tracking in *Master of Magic* is designed such that there can only ever be one injured **Figure** in a unit. For [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units, this naturally means that all other remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in the unit are always at full health: the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure** value. If there is a hurt, but not destroyed **Figure**, the *unit statistics window* indicates the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") done to this **Figure** both numerically, and by darkening out some of the bright [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** icons in its display. This **Figure** is often termed the "foremost-", or "lead-"; but most commonly, the "top figure".

#### Top Figure Damage

:   If there *is* a damaged **Figure**, the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**
    it has taken is usually called the "top figure damage". If the unit is
    unhurt however, or its total recorded Damage Points are divisible by its
    [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure**
    value (without remainder), then the "top figure" actually has no
    significance whatsoever. In fact, during the resolution of most [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), it doesn't matter whether there is an injured **Figure**
    or not. Regardless, this information may still be important to the
    player, for example, when trying to decide which unit to target with a [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing "Healing")** spell.

### Conventional Attacks

When performing [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks ([Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown**, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, or [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged**), a **Multi-Figure Unit** will execute that attack as many times in a row as it has remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. The [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of *each*
of these will match the related attribute of the unit. The game
documentation mistakenly claims that only one attack is performed, with
the product of the Attack Strength and the **Figure** count, but this is not actually the case. Instead, each **Figure** conducts an individual, separate attack with the listed strength. This can make a huge difference against oponents with a high [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**,
which the manual's version of the attack mechanic would have no problem
punching through, but in practice, is often very effective against **Multi-Figure Units**.

It's worth noting however, that while the attacks are resolved individually, any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
they deal is applied collectively, and only once they are all
processed. This also remains true if there are added effects, such as [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), affixed to the conventional ones. Their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is calculated separately, and then added to the total.

#### Cause Fear

:   The [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") **[Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear")** ability, along with its [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") ([Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear "Cloak of Fear")**) and [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") ([Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloak of Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloak_of_Fear_(Item_Power) "Cloak of Fear (Item Power)")**) versions, are designed to prevent some, or all of an enemy unit's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** from participating in a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** engagement. This effect is inserted into the [Melee Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Sequence "Melee Attack") after short-range attacks, but before mutual [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**. The opponent must make as many [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks as it has remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, and for each failed roll, the amount of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** that can perform their [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") is reduced by one. If they have [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), those won't be executed either. The effect is temporary though, and lasts only until this specific sequence is finished.

:   Unfortunately, [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") **Cause Fear**
    is severely bugged in the official game, no matter what source it is
    gained from. It does not work at all when the unit possessing it is
    being attacked, while it will also affect itself if it is the attacker.
    However, both mistakes are corrected in the unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch, starting with which this ability can be particularly useful against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units. Its main weakness is the fact that it does not apply any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalties, which makes it ineffective against higher tier units.

### Conventional Defense

**Multi-Figure Units** struck by Conventional Damage behave in a peculiar way if the "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** exceeds their [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure** value. Their first [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") is made as usual, using the unit's effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. If this can't block enough [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to bring the "raw" value below the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the **Figure**, the attack is deemed to have destroyed a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. When this happens, the remaining "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is directed against another **Figure**. However, it can't go directly to hurting it, as that individual likely has its own [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Armor** or other protective measures. Thus, a new Defense Roll can be made by the unit, potentially reducing the "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** again. This cycle then keeps repeating until all of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is fully processed.

There are two quirks in this system that are not necessarily
intuitive. First, "top figure damage" is ignored entirely if the attack
came from an enemy unit, as opposed to a spell. That is, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** that "take" the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
in this case are always assumed to be uninjured ones, even when there
are none of these left in the unit. Second, as a consequence of this and
the simultaneous application of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
mentioned above, the Defense Roll mechanic remains in place even if the
unit is theoretically destroyed by the point that the attack is being
resolved. This is often called "Overkill Damage", and is explained in
more detail in the article on [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Overkill_Damage "Damage Points").

#### Area Damage

:   [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") is a subtype of Conventional Damage. It is essentially a modifier that changes the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** resolution process in two ways. First, instead of performing a single attack, **Area Damage** executes as many as the target unit has standing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. This can be devastating to [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units, as the strength of the attack will be the same against each **Figure**, and is *not*
    divided among them. However, the other change is that none of these
    attacks can actually inflict more Damage Points than that particular **Figure** has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, meaning that this type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** always has to consider "top figure damage".

:   Naturally, for all but the foremost **Figure**, the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** they can take equals the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure** value. Should the "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** exceed this after the [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll"), the remainder is simply discarded, rather than being directed at another **Figure**, as would be the case with non-Area Damage. The same is true for the "top figure" - the only difference is that it's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** are only [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** minus the "top figure damage", so this is what the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is limited to.

:   On the other hand, once the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is calculated, it will still be applied to the unit as a whole. This is to stay consistent with the system of one injured **Figure** per unit. Thus, quite often, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** that takes the **Area Damage** is not the same as the one that defended against it. This also means that the distribution of the Damage Points among the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** is irrelevant, with regards to the final outcome.

### Figure-based Special Damage

Many sources of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") are **Figure**-based. This usually means that whenever they deal [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, it will be equal to a healthy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** - that is, the units [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure** value. This invariably results in the destruction of exactly one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. The one exception is the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")** spell, which is meant to do only single points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. However, this spell is bugged in the official game, and actually deals much more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** than intended, as noted below.

**Figure**-based Special Damage is always resistable. Whether it deals [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** or not depends entirely on the outcome of a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
check. This is a randomly generated number between 1 and 10 -
essentially a 10-sided die -, and is successful if the result is less
than, or equal to, the unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score. However, the amount of attacks, and therefore the amount of rolls required, varies by [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type").

[Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), which are "added effects" to [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks, are executed once per *attacking* **Figure**. They are technically part of the conventional attack that triggers them. [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and spells, on the other hand, are akin to [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"): they perform one attack per *defending* **Figure**. That is, it is the target unit's **Figure** count that determines how many [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks they have to make against the effect. This is true even when attacking, or being attacked by, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Gorgons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons")**, the game's only [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** Gaze Attack unit. The amount of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Gorgon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons")** **Figures** does not matter. The Gaze effect only activates once, but when it does, it targets each opposing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** at the same time.

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")** deals more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** than it was designed to (according to its documentation) because of a coding mistake. After the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls are made, while summing up the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points"), the game erroneously substitutes the amount of failed checks for the intended [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** before multiplying the two. This yields [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** equal to the square of the failures, rather than their actual amount. The bug is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

### Losing and Regaining Figures

Whenever a **Multi-Figure Unit** reaches a recorded [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** total that is a multiple of its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure**, it loses a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. Conversely, reducing the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
to one point below such a value causes the unit to regain one. This can
also be easily expressed using the "top figure damage": whenever it
reaches the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure** value, a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** is lost, and the counter resets. On the other hand, if it would go negative, and the unit can still regain [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, it will do so, and the new "top figure damage" becomes the negative value plus the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per **Figure**.
Naturally, this can repeat more than once in both directions during the
resolution of a single attack or effect, resulting in the loss or
revival of multiple [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.
