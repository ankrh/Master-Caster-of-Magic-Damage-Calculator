A **Breath Attack** is one of the many [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") Types featured in *Master of Magic*. A **Breath Attack** is only executed as an integral part of a unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). Different **Breath Attacks**
may deliver different types of damage, but their primary purpose (aside
from hurting the enemy) is to try and kill off as many enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as possible before the rest of the Melee combat process (including the enemy's [retaliation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")) can take place.

**Breath Attacks** are only available to units possessing either the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") or [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") abilities. This includes a wide variety of units, among which are many [Draconian](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Draconian "Draconian") [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), several [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), and one [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). It is possible for [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") to acquire [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") thanks to a specific [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** spell, and this is the only way to grant a **Breath Attack** to a unit that does not possess it by default.

**Breath Attacks** are executed at the very beginning of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") sequence. Thus, the more enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** a **Breath Attack** can kill, the less danger there will be to the **Breath Attacker** once the target gets the chance to retaliate.

**Breath Attacks** are only executed when the attacker is voluntarily initiating Melee combat. They do not work when [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to an enemy assault.

The damage delivered by a **Breath Attack** can be either [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"), or a combination of [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") together with [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"). Both types attempt to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target - but have several important differences between them due to the specific types of damage involved. In general, [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") attacks will have a much greater chance of getting through the target's [Defenses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense").

The strength of a **Breath Attack** is indicated in a unit's details panel as the value of its [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") or [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") ability, whichever is present. On this wiki, the strength of a [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") attack is indicated by the unofficial icon of a lightning-bolt: [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath"). This icon does not appear in the game, but is based upon the icon representing the [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") ability. The strength of a [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") attack is indicated by the blazing-cone icon, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath"), which is actually found in the game files but isn't used in the game either. This icon is also used to indicate **Breath Attacks** in general. In either case, the strength of a [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack** indicates how much [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") it delivers.

The strength of a **Breath Attack** will normally grow with [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience** at the same rate as the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") strength. Most effects that improve a unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") will also improve its **Breath Attack**. [Magical Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") do not improve a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s **Breath Attacks**, but [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") can do so if imbued with attack-related bonuses.

Possession of a **Breath Attack** allows a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit to attack any enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit.

## Concept

A **Breath Attack**
involves a creature actually breathing out a blast of energy of one type
or another. Ancient myths contain many creatures that have the ability
to do this, but the most common and most familiar is the dragon, which
breaths fire by some supernatural mechanism. Creatures breathing
lightning are far rarer, and their origins are likely in modern
role-playing games.

Whichever creature is involved, the tactical advantage of a
breath-based attack always comes into play: the creature uses its breath
to attack enemies at some distance, making it more difficult to
approach such a creature without injury or death. In *Master of Magic*,
this is essentially a preemptive strike, whereupon the creature charges
at its target in order to exploit the enemy's weakened state, the
enemy's confusion, and/or the hole in the enemy lines created by the **Breath Attack**.

Thus, a **Breath Attack** is always a prelude to an immediate physical charge by the creature into its target (a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")), and will always occur at the start of such an attack. The desired outcome of a **Breath Attack** is the death of as many enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
as possible, so that once the two units are engaged in physical combat,
the enemy has less strength and thus will pose less of a risk to the
attacker. The **Breath Attack**
is simply meant to weaken the enemy prior to actual engagement. If it
manages to destroy the enemy entirely, so much the better - since that
removes the need to make that dangerous physical contact with the
target.

## Effect

The two different types of **Breath Attacks** - namely [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath** attacks and [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Lightning Breath** attacks - deliver somewhat different types of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage").
While the overall process of damage delivery and the resulting effect
is often similar, the details are radically different. Therefore the
differences are explained separately below.

Nonetheless, the purpose of all **Breath Attacks** is to cause as many [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target, with a specific goal of killing off as many enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as possible. The more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** are killed in this attack, the fewer remain to retaliate against the **Breath Attacker** once physical combat ensues - making that physical combat safer for the attacker.

The amount of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") delivered by a **Breath Attack** is equal to the strength of the attacker's [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") or [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") ability, as indicated in its details panel. This is the maximum potential number of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** that might be caused to the target - though the actual amount is usually much lower. On this wiki, the strength of a **Breath Attack** is indicated by either the blazing-cone or lightning-bolt icons on a blue background: [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") and [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath"), respectively. Therefore, an attack with a strength of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **4** delivers 4 points of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"), and can potentially cause up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4 points of Damage** to the target.

To calculate the actual amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to the target, the game runs through the [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") process - a long and complicated process that's explained in detail in the [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") article. In essence, the attacker makes a series of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine the accuracy of the attack, and the defender makes a series of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
rolls to determine how effectively it blocked and/or dodged the attack.
When the results of these rolls are compared, we get the total amount
of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to the target. Target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** are killed as appropriate, before moving on to the next step of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") process (usually the exchange of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") between the attacker and the target).

Note that both types of **Breath Attacks** deliver [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") of some sort, which means that they will both trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") if present. This effectively negates *all* damage from the **Breath Attack** - leaving the target completely unscathed - regardless of how strong the **Breath Attack** is!

It is currently unknown which [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses apply when processing a **Breath Attack**, and testing is quite difficult. However, it is certain that some bonuses, such as those received from [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry"), certainly do apply to the **Breath Attack**. These will improve the accuracy of the attack, and thus help it deliver more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** on average.

On the other hand, it is almost certain that no extra properties or [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") can be added to a **Breath Attack**, so it will always deliver the specific [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") explained in the next two sections below.

### Fire Breath

A [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath** attack delivers a special kind of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") called "[Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage")". In general, it works exactly the same as explained above. The only difference is felt when the target possesses the [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") ability.

When a [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") attack is used against a [Fire-Immune](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") target, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is temporarily boosted to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. This makes the target extremely durable to the attack, since it can make 50 separate [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls - and can thus block a massive amount of damage. It is not a total immunity, and some few [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") attacks may have a realistic chance of getting through that much armor, but it will certainly be unlikely.

Note that [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") is a type of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"). Thus, as explained in the previous section, [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") will completely block it.

### Lightning Breath

A [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Lightning Breath** attack is a lot harder to explain, because it delivers two [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") in tandem: [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage"), followed immediately by [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"). Only one of these two damage types actually causes any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to the target - making things a little confusing.

The [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") component is delivered first. It does not cause any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** whatsoever to the target on its own. Instead, it will temporarily halve the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. At that point, the [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") component strikes the target, rolling for damage as explained above in this article (and in more detail on the [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage")
article), while exploiting the fact that the target's armor has been
partially compromised. This means that the total damage output of a [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath")
attack is often much higher than the output of some other attack of
similar strength - all because the target is less capable of defending
itself.

No immunity can stop [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") from taking effect. However, the target can still avoid damage completely if it has [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). This immunity, when present, will be triggered by the [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") component of the attack, and will automatically block *all of the damage to the target* as explained previously. Thus, halving the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score has no real effect - the target has no need to any Defense rolls!

### Multi-Figure Breath Attackers

When a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit** uses a **Breath Attack**, each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** executes a separate **Breath Attack**
against the target, in rapid succession. Each of these is treated and
processed as a separate attack, involving the entire routine of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** distribution as described above, from start to finish.

Each of these attacks has the same strength, equal to the value of the unit's [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") or [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") ability. Thus, a unit with "[Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") 3" and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **4 Figures** will execute 4 separate **Breath Attacks** against the target, each with a strength of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **3** (i.e. delivering 3 points of [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") at the target).

Therefore, the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** a unit contains, the stronger its **Breath Attack** is overall. Nonetheless, a "weaker" **Breath Attack** made against a heavily-armored target may still fail to cause any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to that target even with many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
participating. This is because each of the individual sub-attacks is
weak, and thus each has an equal chance of failing to penetrate the
armor.

#### Example

:   A unit of [Draconian Swordsmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Draconian_Swordsmen "Draconian Swordsmen") has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **6 Figures** when fully-healed. By default, this unit possesses the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") ability of strength [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **1**.

:   When the [Draconian Swordsmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Draconian_Swordsmen "Draconian Swordsmen") unit is voluntarily attacking, each of the 6 **Swordsmen** makes a separate **Breath Attack**, and each of these attacks has a strength of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **1** (i.e. delivering 1 [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") point at the target). Therefore, as per the rules of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), each **Swordsman** makes a single [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** roll against the target, and can potentially inflict [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Point of Damage**. If all attacks are successful, the target will suffer a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** \* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **6** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **6 points of Damage**.

:   However, almost all targets are sufficiently-well-armored to withstand this damage, *because* it is delivered in [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **1-point** "packets", instead of a single [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **6-point** attack.

:   For example, an enemy unit with only [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **1 Defense** - extremely light armor - has only a 25% chance of suffering damage from *each* of these [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **1-point**
    attacks. In this case, having 6 separate attacks does help, since a 25%
    chance with each of six separate attacks means roughly a 75% chance
    that the target will suffer at least [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 point of Damage**.

:   On the other hand, if the target has a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5**, the chance of each [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **1-point** attack inflicting *any* damage on it is remarkably slim - only about 5% per attack! This gives only a ~25% chance of hurting the target *at all*. For comparison, a single [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **6-point** attack would've been significantly more useful against the same target, having roughly 45% chance of hurting it to some degree.

:   Thus, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** with a low [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack**
    strength can indeed be dangerous to lightly-armored enemies, simply
    because of the multitude of attacks being executed one after the other.
    However they are still far less useful against heavily-armored targets
    than would a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **single** powerful **Breath Attacker** delivering the same total amount of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") in one go.

## Execution and Sequence

The **Breath Attack** is one of the [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") types. This means that it cannot be executed on its own, but rather is always a part of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") executed by one unit upon the other.

**Breath Attacks** are executed only by the initiator of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). For example, if unit A attacks unit B, and both units have the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") or [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") ability, only unit A gets to make a **Breath Attack** since it initiated combat. Unit B may not use its **Breath Attack**. If both units survive of course, then once unit B's turn begins it can initiate a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against unit A, in which case unit B gets to use a **Breath Attack** and unit A does not.

**Breath Attacks** are always executed at the very beginning of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
process, before any other attack or damage is delivered. This is one of
its primary advantages: the attacking unit strikes with its full force,
before the target gets any chance to hurt the attacking unit and reduce
its strength in any way.

| Stage | Attacker | Defender |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") / **Breath Attack** | -- |
|  | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 2 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 3 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") |

As the table above shows, the **Breath Attack** occurs in stage 1, and shares this stage with the [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"). Note that the rules of the game state that a unit may not have both a [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") *and* a **Breath Attack**, so only one of these will be executed as appropriate. Also note that no unit may ever possess both a [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") and [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") ability - so no unit will ever deliver more than one **Breath Attack** per Melee combat.

Because of its position in stage 1, the **Breath Attack** also has an important tactical advantage: any enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
that are successfully killed off in this attack cannot participate in
any of the following stages. Thus, enemy figures killed by a **Breath Attack** will not deliver their own [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), nor any [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")
back at the attacker. This provides the attacker some security: by
weakening the enemy, the attacker stands to suffer less retaliatory
damage.

## Ground vs. Air

The normal rules of combat prohibit any [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit from initiating a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against any [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** opponent. Such units may only [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to this opponent, allowing the [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** enemy to maneuver freely around them without fear of being attacked.

However, possession of a **Breath Attack** by a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit allows that unit to break the rules: it may freely and voluntarily execute [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units.

Therefore, while such a unit is not actually [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying**, it can still engage enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units on relatively even terms during combat.
