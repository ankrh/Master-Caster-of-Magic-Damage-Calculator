**Special Damage** is one of the two main mechanics for causing harm to units in *Master of Magic*. Unlike [Conventional](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"), or "[Physical](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage")" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, **Special Damage** is defended against using the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** statistic (or not at all), rather than using [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. Its resolution is also significantly different, typically relying on a single die roll to determine whether [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is done or not, with the amount being preset by the attack.

Most attacks that deliver **Special Damage** are performed on a per-[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** basis. Any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** that fails its [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check is instantly slain by these attacks. That is, the unit as a whole suffers [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") equal to a healthy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s worth of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**. In addition, there are also some **Special Damage** attacks that target units in general, and have a slightly different process for determining their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. They either deal it in single point increments, or are designed to kill the unit outright.

## Targeting Types

Arguably the most important characteristic of a **Special Damage** attack is its "targeting type". This sets out whether the attack will target a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in a unit, or the unit as a whole.

### Single Figure Targeting

The simplest **Special Damage** attacks target only one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a defending unit. One [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
roll is made which, if successful, means that the target has evaded the
effect entirely. If it fails however, the unit as a whole suffers
exactly as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** as a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** value, effectively slaying one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. By itself, this targeting type is exclusive to [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), which are always "added effects" to a [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attack. However, effects that target multiple [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** also use this process, the only difference being the actual number of attacks executed at the same time.

### Multi Figure Targeting

Most spells and [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") that deliver **Special Damage** target each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a unit separately. In essence, they perform as many single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** attacks, as there are remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the defending unit. This is similar to [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks that deal [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"), although the consequences are often more dire. The target unit has to make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check for *each* of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, and for each failed check will suffer either a full [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** worth of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** or, in the unique case of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")**, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**.

### Unit Targeting

For some types of **Special Damage**, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** counts are completely irrelevant. The unit makes its [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check, if any, as a whole. [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") and [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") operate this way, as do spells that are designed to destroy entire units, whether in combat ([Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call")**, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Disintegrate](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegrate "Disintegrate")**), or overland ([Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death Wish](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Wish "Death Wish")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Great Unsummoning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Unsummoning "Great Unsummoning")**).

## Resistance Checks

:   *Main article: [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance")*

A [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check is a single roll of a 10-sided die. It is essential for the resolution of nearly all types of **Special Damage**. This check is successful if the rolled number is *equal to or lower* than the unit's effective [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score. In other words, the chance of succeeding it is equal to **10%** times the unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**. A unit with a final score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** or more (after modifiers) can thus never fail this roll.

The [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** statistic is visible in the *unit statistics window* as a row of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Cross**
icons, with the amount of icons corresponding to the score. "Universal"
modifiers that apply against all attacks targeting the unit are shown
as gold (bonus), or greyed out (malus) icons. However, certain effects
also grant conditional modifiers, which are *not* included in this display. Many **Special Damage** attacks also impose a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Save**, or [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance *penalty***, making them more difficult to avoid by lowering this attribute for the duration of resolving their effect.

[Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can also equip [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") imbued with the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **- Spell Save** property to make their [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based **Special Damage**
spells more effective. This attribute is cumulative with any penalties
already applied by the spell. In the original game, this only works with
spells that target a single unit, although [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Disintegrate](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegrate "Disintegrate")**, which is not [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based, is also affected. The unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")
patch changes these rules such that multi-unit spells are also
included, so long as they can be cast by Heroes (i.e. are combat
spells). In addition, it extends the attribute to apply to another
unit-based spell: [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")**.

## Figure Based Special Damage

In terms of their resolution, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based **Special Damage** attacks always follow the same procedure. When a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") or spell acts on all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in a unit, the process is simply repeated as many times as there are remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**-based **Special Damage** always allows a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll. If it is successful, the attack is diverted and nothing happens. The [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** "resists" the effect. Naturally, for [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **multi-figure** attacks, each of them has to be [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resisted** separately. A failed check results in the target unit taking one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s worth of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** in either case. The sole exception to this rule is the [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")** which, on a botched roll, should inflict only one point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

Any effect that is not listed in the table above does not share at least one of these properties.

"Stoning Damage" is another collective term, grouping together all of
the game's petrification attacks. While the most obvious common
property of these **Special Damage** attacks is being nullified by [Stoning Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Immunity "Stoning Immunity") [Stoning Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Immunity "Stoning Immunity"), they also share some other characteristics. All of the attacks that use this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") deal *Irreversible* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, a special type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") that may not be healed during combat. They are also all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based attacks associated with the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm").

### Unique Effects

There are several more attacks and spells in the game that deal [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based **Special Damage**.
Unfortunately, they are not as easy to generalize as the above two
types. Although they all follow the same generic rules of forcing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** to resist or be slain, they vary greatly in terms of the type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") they apply and the protective effects they trigger.

As mentioned above, the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")** spell uses a hybrid mechanic to determine its [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Like other [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **multi-figure** **Special Damage** attacks, it forces its targets to make one [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check for each of their standing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. However, instead of dealing a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s worth of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")** does only a single [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Point** to the unit for each failed roll. Granted, that's still enough to slay a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a low-tier [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), but the effect does not actually scale with the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")**
exerts its effect at the end of each of its caster's turn, after all
units have taken their action, but before the opponent can move. Once
cast, it remains in play until dispelled, or until the battle is over.
When it triggers, it performs its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **multi-figure** attack against every opposing unit at once. It may also be worth noting that this effect precedes that of [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration").

In the official game, and also in [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide"), [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")** has a major bug that makes it even more deadly than it otherwise would be. Instead of dealing its intended [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** to a unit for each botched [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll, it deals the amount of failed [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls times [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** *per failed roll*. That is, the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done is ultimately the *square* of the failed rolls. This can easily annihilate low-tier units in a single attack if they have many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, even when they have mid-range [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**. The bug is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

## Unit Based Special Damage

The most obvious difference between [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**- and unit-based **Special Damage** is that the latter's [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** does not depend on the defender's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**. Although technically neither does [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")**'s, that spell's mechanics are still closer to [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based attacks. Instead, unit-based [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") either use a system that causes [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistable** [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** in increments of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**, or are meant to outright destroy their target.

### Single Point Damage

There are two **Special Damage** types that work in a way that can be broken down into single [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **points**. They differ in both the amount of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls they require, and the method of setting out the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** based on those rolls. However, they can both be avoided entirely by successful [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks.

:   *Main article: [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage")*

:   The [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** of a **Life Stealing** attack is determined by a single [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check, made by the target unit as a whole. If the roll is successful, the attack is avoided entirely. Otherwise, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** is suffered for each point that the check was *failed by*. In other words, the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is equal to the *difference* between the unit's effective [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**, and the number rolled on the 10-sided die. For example, a unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **4** that rolled a **6** for the check would suffer [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

:   Most **Life Stealing** attacks carry a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty, and the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** spell can be infused with additional [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to grant one up to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-8**. This can easily bring some units below [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **0** even, in which case the check will always fail. In fact, a temporary negative [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** value is entirely valid during the resolution of this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** will be calculated accordingly. In the above example, if the unit was struck by a fully empowered [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** instead, that same roll of **6** would yield a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **10** = **6** - ([Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **4** - [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **8**).

:   **Life Stealing Damage** also has two more noteworthy
    properties. First, it is always considered to be "Create Undead Damage",
    and as such is capable of raising [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units under the right circumstances. Second, it actually *heals* the attacker or, in the case of a [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") casting [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")**, provides permanent points towards increasing their [Spell Casting Skill](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Casting_Skill "Spell Casting Skill"). If the **Life Stealing** unit is already at full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, this can even grant it a temporary increase in maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** equal to the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** done, divided by the amount of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the unit, rounded down.

:   Unfortunately, this last feature is also the source of a bug
    that affects all types of combat healing effects. It causes them to
    grant extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** in some [situations](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing#Known_Bugs "Healing") even if the healing does not come from **Life Stealing**. This bug is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50"). Another oversight in the original game causes the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** spell to ignore [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") entirely, even though it works for all the other **Life Stealing** attacks. This is corrected in [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") by preventing any units possessing this immunity from being targeted by the spell in the first place.

:   *Main article: [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage")*

:   This **Special Damage** type is similar to [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") in that its magnitude defines its [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** *potential*, much like [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") does. For each point of Poison strength, the target has to make a single, separate [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check. If successful, the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is avoided. If not, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** is dealt to the unit. In other words, the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** of the Poison attack is the amount of failed [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls.

:   **Poison Damage** is only available in the game through a single [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") ability. As such, it is always affixed to a Conventional Damage attack. It deals regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, except in the case of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Ghouls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ghouls "Ghouls")**, whose [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") ability also affects their **Poison Damage**. There is no method for adding a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty to this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), although there are a few spells that can reduce overall [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**.

:   Since **Poison Damage** is not associated with any magical [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), most conditional protective effects do not work against it. In addition, despite the documantation and in-game tooltip, [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units are *not* immune to this [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** in the latest official game version, unless they happen to be under the effect of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")** spell. This bug is corrected in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

### Overland Unit Destruction

There are two overland spells that are [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistable**, but do not deliver [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. In fact, technically, they don't deal [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** *at all*. They simply remove the affected units from the game. However, since this effect is analogous to suffering [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and because these spells can be defended against with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**, they can be considered as a form of **Special Damage** nonetheless.

### Combat Unit Destruction

Unlike the above two spells, the unit destruction effects that work in battle also do actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. If successful, they deal a fixed [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **200** points of *Irreversible* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. This is more than what any unit can survive.

#### Cracks Call

:   *Main article: [Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call")*

:   Technically, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call")** might be considered to not be a **Special Damage** attack at all, since it can not be [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resisted**. However, it's not exactly [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") either, as neither can its [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** be reduced using [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. It has a flat, unmodifiable **25%** chance of succeeding, and when it does, it exerts the same effect as a successful [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Disintegrate](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegrate "Disintegrate")**.
    Because of this mechanic, conditional protections are meaningless
    against it. However, there are certain ways to make this spell
    ineffective nonetheless.

:   Firstly, this spell does not work against [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units. In addition to many units naturally possessing this [Movement Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Type "Movement Type"), there are also a few ways to imbue it. The [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Flight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flight "Flight")** spell can be cast on any unit, while [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can equip [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") with the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Flight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flight_(Item_Power) "Flight (Item Power)")** [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") also have a chance to gain [Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels") **Demon Wings** as one of the effects of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")** spells. However, [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flight** can in turn be cancelled out by a [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Web](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Web "Web")** spell, which coincidentally belongs to the same [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") as [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call")**.

:   Better protection is offered by the [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal") [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal") ability. Although this is possessed by only a handful of units by default, it can be gained through the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form")** spell, which can target any unit, and the corresponding Item Power: [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraithform](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraithform_(Item_Power) "Wraithform (Item Power)")**. In addition, from the unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch onward, the [Merging](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merging "Merging") [Merging](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merging "Merging") ability also grants complete immunity against [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call")**.

## Simultaneous Damage

In a few cases, multiple different types of attacks can strike a
target at the same time. Typically, if this happens, then all of the
attacks are fully resolved before *any* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is applied. A prime example of this is the "Multi-Gaze" attack of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**. Should this attack be triggered against a unit with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6 figures**, each separate Gaze Attack will treat the target as having [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6 figures**, rather than one attack following the other and only affecting as many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** as remain after the previous one.

The same is true for "added effects": the [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") is not applied until the **Special Damage** is also resolved. For example, a unit of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Gorgons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons")** guarding a [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** may very well be able to kill a few [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** of a weak [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit")
with just the hidden Conventional component of their Gaze Attack.
However, the actual Gaze effect will still be performed with the
opponent's original [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count. In fact, if all four [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Gorgon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons")** [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** are still alive, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from four separate Conventional Damage attacks is being delayed here until the resolution of the **Special Damage**.

Things can get even more complicated with [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") delivered in [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**. Since both units strike at each other simultaneously during the "mutual melee" stage of the [Melee Attack Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Attack_Sequence "Melee Attack"), no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can be applied until every attack has been accounted for. That is, all Conventional Damage *and* all **Special Damage**. If both units have Touch Attacks, they are all resolved before any of the [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is applied. If the units are [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure**, then all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** on both sides will attack before either takes any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

### Number of Attacks

[Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based **Special Damage** spells essentially perform one attack for each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
in the target unit, although some of the spells also affect multiple
units at the same time. Unit-based spells naturally attack each target
only once. A little more complex are [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), of which there are a few from both groups. Since these are "affixed" to [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks, and because every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s attack is considered to be separate within a unit, **Special Damage** Touch Attacks are actually executed once *for each attacking [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure***.

For example, a full unit of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")** will perform **4** individual [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") Touch Attacks when engaged in [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**. Similarly, if both of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** are still alive, a unit of [Manticores](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Manticores "Manticores") will try to poison their opponent twice, requiring a total of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **2** × [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") **6** = **12** [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks to negate the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** completely. Finally, an unhurt unit of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** will use their [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") four times when striking an enemy. The target's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count makes no difference in either case. In fact, should the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** be fighting a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** unit, they will attempt to petrify that same [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** *4 times* in a single [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** engagement!

### Weapon Based Touch Attacks

[Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") have access to several different **Special Damage** attacks via [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") imbued onto [Magical Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). A common theme among these is that just like offensive attributes ([Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** modifiers), they are only granted when the wielder uses an [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") associated with the weapon. When performing other attacks or casting spells, the **Special Damage** attack will not activate. Most weapon types only have a single type of attack that their powers work with. These are [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** for [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace") and [Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** for [Bows](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"), and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical** for [Staves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") and [Wands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand"). The only exception are [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"), which apply their benefits on both [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-** and [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, providing a distinct advantage to Heroes with the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability. Unfortunately though, there are only three of these in the game: [Bahgtru the Orc Warrior](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bahgtru_the_Orc_Warrior "Bahgtru the Orc Warrior"), [Gunther the Barbarian](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gunther_the_Barbarian "Gunther the Barbarian"), and [Shalla the Amazon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shalla_the_Amazon "Shalla the Amazon").

## Damage Points

:   *Main article: [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points")*

*Master of Magic* tracks three types of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** done to a unit. While [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks nearly always deal "regular damage", **Special Damage**
often uses one of the other two types. Their purpose is to determine
whether a unit can be brought back from the dead. They only have any
meaning during combat, and are automatically converted to regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** once a battle is over. However, not before they exert their effect, if any.

### Create Undead Damage

As its name implies, this type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** has the power to raise fallen enemies as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
units. Only the victorious army can bolster its ranks this way though,
meaning that the slain unit must belong to the losing side. In addition,
the "Create Undead" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** suffered by it must be at least as much as the regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and *more* than any Irreversible [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

### Irreversible Damage

This is a type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that may not be removed or healed during combat. If a unit is destroyed with at least as much Irreversible [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
as both of the other two types, it is irrevocably removed from the
game, and can neither regenerate, nor be raised or resurrected. [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") worn by a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")
who is slain this way are lost forever, and will not be recovered after
the battle. However, because of his unique characteristics, [Torin the Chosen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Torin_the_Chosen "Torin the Chosen") can still be summoned again by casting the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Incarnation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Incarnation "Incarnation")** spell.
