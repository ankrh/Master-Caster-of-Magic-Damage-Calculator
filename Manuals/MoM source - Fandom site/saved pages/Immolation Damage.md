**Immolation Damage** is one of the many [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") in *Master of Magic*.

**Immolation Damage** is essentially [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"). This type of damage is unique in that it makes separate [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against *each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure*** in the enemy unit, instead of attacking only the "lead" figure and applying "excess" damage to further [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** (as would happen with conventional [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage")). This generally means that the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** there are in the target unit, the more damage it will suffer on average.

**Immolation Damage** is distinct from the other [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") type, called [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage"), in that it will trigger a target's [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") (if the target possesses this immunity), and will not trigger a target's [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"). Note that either of the two Area Damage types will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity").

**Immolation Damage** is named after the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability (and the [Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)") of the same name). Several creatures and spells deliver this type of damage in various ways or circumstances. The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** spell is by far the strongest of these.

## Effect

:   *Main article: [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage")*

**Immolation Damage** is a type of Area Damage, and as such its purpose is to cause [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target. The results of this damage greatly depend on how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** the target contains.

### Against Single-Figure Units

:   *Main article: [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage")*

When **Immolation Damage** strikes a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure Unit**, it behaves in a manner identical to [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"). This starts with a single set of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against the target unit, equal to the strength of the **Immolation Damage** attack. This is followed by a set of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, exactly as many as the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score.

The number of successful hits and blocks are tallied up, then subtracted, to get the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** caused to the target. Unlike [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), if this amount is higher than the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s remaining [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**,
it is reduced to this amount instead. This will still destroy the unit,
but without any "overkill" damage, which may matter for some effects
such as [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") or [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead").

### Against Multi-Figure Units

**Immolation Damage** behaves very differently when it strikes a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** target. In this case, it acts as a separate [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") attack initiated individually against each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the unit. All [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** get to apply their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** to reduce their own damage.

Unlike in Physical Damage, however, there is no "excess" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. That is, no [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** can take more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** than its current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** total. Still, the result is usually massive damage to the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**.

For a full analysis of this process, please refer to the article on [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"), which explains this in much greater detail, including examples.

## Immunities

**Immolation Damage** is completely blocked by the [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") and [Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness") [Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness") effects. Units possessing either of these will not suffer any **Immolation Damage** under any circumstances.

Units possessing the [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") ability will instead get their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score temporarily raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for the purposes of blocking this damage type.

**Immolation Damage** is always considered to be [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** damage, and thus also falls within the scope of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")**, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements "Resist Elements")**, and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")** protective [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"),
which means that units possessing these will have enhanced Defense
against this type of damage. Furthermore, the Defense bonus granted by
the [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield") [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield") ability also applies against all forms of **Immolation Damage** regardless of their source.

## Sources of Immolation Damage

There are many different sources of **Immolation Damage** in the game, all of which are associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. Each of them delivers a different amount of **Immolation Damage** to its target.

### Fireball

:   *Main article: [Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")*

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") spell. For a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **15**, this spell will deliver **Immolation Damage** with an attack strength of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **5** at a selected enemy target on the battlefield.

The Fireball spell can be infused with additional [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** in order to make it stronger. With every extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **3** invested into the spell, its attack strength increases by [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **1**. The spell can take up to [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **60 extra Mana**, meaning that its strength can be increased up to a maximum of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **25**. This is the strongest available [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") attack in the game, and is highly likely to wipe out an entire enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**.

### Fireball Spell

:   *Main article: [Fireball Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball_Spell "Fireball Spell")*

[Fireball Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball_Spell "Fireball Spell") [Fireball Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball_Spell "Fireball Spell") is a [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") possessed by all [Magicians](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magicians "Magicians") in the game. This ability allows a unit of Magicians to cast the [Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball") spell (see above) once per battle, at no [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** cost whatsoever. However, this spell can not be infused, and the resulting Fireball always has an attack strength of exactly [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **5**.

### Fire Storm

:   *Main article: [Fire Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Storm "Fire Storm")*

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fire Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Storm "Fire Storm")** is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell"). For a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **250**, this spell will strike each and every enemy unit within a given overland map tile with a strength [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **8** **Immolation Damage** attack.

This spell is particularly useful against large enemy armies, since the more units present the more **Immolation Damage** is delivered overall. It is even more powerful if the enemy army contains several [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**. However, the attack strength of only [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **8** is relatively weak against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure units**, and is unlikely to kill any of them. Fire Storm can be useful for weakening an enemy army prior to actual battle.

### Flame Strike

:   *Main article: [Flame Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flame_Strike "Flame Strike")*

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Flame Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flame_Strike "Flame Strike")** is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") spell. For a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **60**, it will simultaneously deliver a strength [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **15** **Immolation Damage** attack against *each enemy unit* on the battlefield.

Due to striking each enemy *unit*, this spell is more worthwhile the more enemy units are present. Each unit is struck with **Immolation Damage** that has an attack strength of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **15**, which is roughly on par with a mid-strength [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")**, and can be expected to cause serious damage to each of those units - particularly [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**. This both cuts down the time required to cast multiple Fireballs at the enemy, and saves a lot of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** if sufficient enemy units can be hit.

### Immolation Ability

:   *Main article: [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation")*

[Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") is a [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") unique to the [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Bat](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Bat "Doom Bat")**.

Immolation affixes a quasi-[Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") to a unit's attacks. The strength of this **Immolation Damage** attack is always [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **4**. In the case of the Doom Bat, it will trigger whenever the creature makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

While a fairly weak attack, it can still dissuade weaker enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** from attacking the [Doom Bat](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Bat "Doom Bat"),
and can be used offensively to kill off such units. Remember, however,
that as a Touch Attack, it does not offer any real protection to the
Doom Bat itself, it only increases the damage that the Bat can do in
melee combat.

### Immolation Spell

:   *Main article: [Immolation (Spell)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)")*

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)")** is also the name of an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). When cast on a friendly unit, this enchantment adds the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability to that unit. Unlike the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Bat](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Bat "Doom Bat")** however, other units may not be limited to [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") in regards to what the Immolation attack can be affixed to (see the [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") article for more information).

When cast on the overland map, Immolation has a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **150**, and will stay in effect on the target unit until dispelled or removed. It will, however, require an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **2** per turn to maintain.

Alternatively, Immolation may also be cast during combat as an impromptu [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), for a base cost of only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **30**.
Though this behaves the same as the overland version, the combat
version will fizzle immediately as soon as the battle is over (or the
unit is destroyed).

This spell allows *any* unit to possess the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability, and thus make that unit much more dangerous to enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**.

### Meteor Storm

:   *Main article: [Meteor Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meteor_Storm "Meteor Storm")*

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Meteor Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meteor_Storm "Meteor Storm")** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment"). It can be cast on the overland map for a base cost of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **900**. As long as it is maintained, it rains down fiery destruction on the entirety of both [Arcanus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcanus "Arcanus") and [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror"). The effect is divided into two parts, based on whether a map tile contains a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") or not.

Every unit on the overland map that is not in a Town at the end of the turn will be subjected to a strength [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **4** **Immolation Damage** attack. Town garrisons are unaffected, as are the guardians of [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone")
(they don't actually exist on the overland map). However, every other
unit in every army will be hit, including the caster's own armies.

Towns instead have a chance of getting buildings torn down, and [Outposts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Outpost "Outpost") are outright destroyed by this spell. Only the caster's Towns are exempt.

### Wall of Fire

:   *Main article: [Wall of Fire](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Fire "Wall of Fire")*

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Wall of Fire](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Fire "Wall of Fire")** is the name of a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Town Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Enchantment "Town Enchantment") spell. When cast on a friendly [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town"),
it will surround that town with flames. Whenever siege combat erupts
for this town, the "town proper" area will be surrounded by a flaming
barrier which - although passable to all units - will inflict a strength
[Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **5** **Immolation Damage** attack on any [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Non-Flying** unit that passes through it.

Furthermore, such a unit performing a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") through the Wall (against a unit inside) will suffer the same effect during the resolution of the attack.

Wall of Fire may be cast on the overland map for a [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **150**. It will remain active on the target town until removed or dispelled, but will also have an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **2** per turn to maintain. The flaming barrier will appear in every battle fought at that town while the spell is in effect.

Wall of Fire can also be cast as an impromptu [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant"),
which will create the same flaming barrier around the town but will
only remain until the end of the current battle. This version of the
spell behaves the same as the overland version, but costs only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **30**. Also, since it lasts only until the end of the battle, it has no [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") whatsoever.
