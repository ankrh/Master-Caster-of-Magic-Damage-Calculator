**Warp Reality** is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Magic** realm. For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50** it may be cast during combat to give a lasting effect, whereby all units on the battlefield except [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm** are inflicted with a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20% To Hit**
penalty, making them significantly less likely to cause damage during
combat. This applies to units on both sides (the caster's units are not
exempt).

## Effects

**Warp Reality** distorts space itself, making it incredibly difficult for units to cause damage during combat. Only [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm** can cope with this warping and are not affected by it.

### To Hit Penalty

**Warp Reality**'s effect is very simple: a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20% To Hit** penalty inflicted on all units on the battlefield except [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** units.

A [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20% To Hit** bonus applies to all [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") and any [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that require a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") roll. Although reduced To Hit values do not change an attack's maximum potential damage, they lower the *average* damage output of that attack.

As a result, all units on the battlefield are going to cause less damage to their opponents. Units with high [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") ratings may subsequently become almost impervious to damage.

Note that most units have [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30% To Hit** by default, so **Warp Reality** will cause those units to deliver only 10% of their maximum damage potential on average.

**Warp Reality** can easily reduce a unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit Value**
to 0%, if the unit is already under the effect of some other To Hit
penalty. If it reaches 0%, the unit will inflict no damage at all with
any of its affected attacks. This is particularly true for [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") units using [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Bows** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulders** - they get [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") penalties when attacking at long range, and may end up with [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **0% To Hit**.

### Affected Targets

**Warp Reality** will affect all [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), all [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), and all [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") that do not belong to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. In other words, the only unaffected units are [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**, including [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") that have been associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm** via the [Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels") spell.

The spell affects both friendly and enemy units without discrimination!

**Note:**  This is one of the few spells that *does* normally affect units possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity")!!

## Usage

**Warp Reality** may be cast during combat for the high [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**. It will immediately affect all valid targets. Units that end up with less than [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%** will have an icon added to their abilities list to indicate this. Units that previously had a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") value above [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%**
will show a change in the value of their inherent bonus (or it may
disappear or turn into the blue-colored "negative to hit" icon, as
appropriate).

After casting **Warp Reality**,
its icon will appear in the "Enchantments" window underneath the
casting wizard's name. The spell will remain in effect until the end of
battle, or until dispelled by the opponent. It is not possible to cancel
**Warp Reality** voluntarily.

Since it does not carry on after the end of the battle, **Warp Reality** has no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

## Acquisition

As a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** spell, **Warp Reality** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**2** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is not guaranteed unless the Wizard acquires at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**10** Spellbooks.

Customized Wizards possessing [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**11**
Spellbooks at creation time may choose this spell as one of their
default spells before starting the game, in which case the spell will
already be researched and available for casting immediately on the first
turn.

Wizards with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**2** to [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**9** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") **Warp Reality** during the game. The chance for this spell to appear for research increases with the number of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or obtains during gameplay. With [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**10** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**11** Spellbooks, the spell is guaranteed to appear for Research at some point, if it is not already available for casting.

**Warp Reality** has a base [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **1,500**.

With at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**2** Spellbooks, the **Warp Reality** spell *may be* acquired as a reward for winning encounters in creature [Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lair "Lair"), [Towers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower"), et cetera, or when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival wizard who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

**Warp Reality** can have many different effects, depending on what kinds of units are on the battlefield.

Obviously, it is best to cast this spell when your army is made up mostly or entirely of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** units, and the enemy's army contains few or none of them. If the situation is reversed, it is usually a better idea *not* to cast this spell at all, to avoid strengthening the enemy and weakening yourself.

Even if no [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** units are present, the spell may be beneficial to you if your units already possess very high [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** scores, and the enemy's units do not. The penalty of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20%** would then render the enemy's units almost useless, while your units still retain some combat effectiveness.

Finally, consider casting this spell when the enemy's army is comprised mostly of [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") units using [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Bows** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulders**. If you keep your units well away from theirs, the enemy may end up wasting their [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), due to having final To Hit scores of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **0%** after range penalties are taken into account. When the enemy runs out of ammunition, your [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") units can march up and eliminate his units.
