**Fire Immunity** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

When a unit with **Fire Immunity** is struck by [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") or [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") from any source, its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is temporarily raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for purposes of blocking this damage. This means that it is exceptionally difficult (though still possible) to inflict [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") or [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") on this unit.

**Fire Immunity** is a rather rare immunity, possessed only by 3 different [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") - all from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** realm. It is not possible to add **Fire Immunity** to a unit that does not already have it.

## Description

Fire is essentially a chemical reaction, causing an object to turn
from solid matter into gas while releasing plenty of heat and light. As
such, it is a destructive force that few creatures can overcome.
Nonetheless, the magical nature of some creatures, closely associated
with fire itself, allows them to be engulfed in flames without feeling
any adverse effects whatsoever.

These creatures, originating from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**, are essentially *made of fire*, which would explain why they do not react to it like all other living creatures would.

## Effect

**Fire Immunity** is a passive ability that protects a unit specifically from [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") and [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage").

Whenever a [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") or [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") attack is performed against a unit with **Fire Immunity**, the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") rating is immediately raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. Therefore, the unit can make 50 [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls, blocking on average of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **15 points of Damage**
- an impressively-high amount. It is unlikely for any attack to
actually get through this much Defense, and normally results in the unit
taking no damage whatsoever.

There are many sources of [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") and [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") in the game, including spells, special abilities and straightfoward attacks.

Sources of [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") include:

* The [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant")
* [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath** attacks

Sources of [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") include:

* The [Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant")
* The [Flame Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flame_Strike "Flame Strike") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant")
* The [Fire Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Storm "Fire Storm") [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell")
* The [Meteor Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meteor_Storm "Meteor Storm") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment")
* The [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability (whether innate or through the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"))
* The [Wall of Fire](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Fire "Wall of Fire") [Town Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Enchantment "Town Enchantment")

Whenever any of the above strikes a unit with **Fire Immunity**, it triggers the immunity as explained above. The [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") spell has the highest chance of causing damage to a **Fire-Immune** unit - but it is still a pitiful ~3% chance *even when the spell is fully-powered*, and is highly unlikely to cause more than [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** if any.

Note that the **Fire-Immune** unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is raised *only* for the purpose of blocking damage from a [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") or [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") attack. Once the unit has made its defense rolls against this specific attack, its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is restored to normal.

### Exceptions

The Game Manual, as well as some in-game data, suggests that the following attacks should deliver [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"), but *they do not*. Thus, *none* of the following attacks will trigger a target's **Fire Immunity**:

* Attacks made by an [Efreet](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Efreet "Efreet"), a [Fire Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Elemental "Fire Elemental") or a [Fire Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Giant "Fire Giant").
* Attacks made by a unit enchanted with [Flame Blade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flame_Blade "Flame Blade") or affected by [Metal Fires](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Metal_Fires "Metal Fires").
* Attacks made by a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") holding a weapon imbued with the "Flaming" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power").
* [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attacks** that are associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**.

## Units with Default Fire Immunity

**Fire Immunity** is a rather rare type of immunity, possessed by no more than 3 different kinds of units by default. All of these are [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** realm:

[Efreet](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Efreet "Efreet") [Efreet](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Efreet "Efreet")
[Fire Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Elemental "Fire Elemental") [Fire Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Elemental "Fire Elemental")
[Fire Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Giant "Fire Giant") [Fire Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Giant "Fire Giant")

## Acquiring Fire Immunity

There are no known methods of adding **Fire Immunity** to a unit that does not already possess it. Thus, the three units listed above will be the only units to ever have **Fire Immunity** in any game.
