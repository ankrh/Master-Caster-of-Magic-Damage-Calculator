**Area Damage** is a collective term used on this wiki to describe types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") that affect all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in a unit at once, rather than trying to deal damage to them one by one. There are only two such [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") in the game: [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") (the Area version of [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage")), and [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage") (the Area version of [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage")).

Like [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), **Area Damage** is primarily designed to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target. It also involves [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls by the attacker and target respectively, similar to how all Physical Damage is delivered. However, **Area Damage** is unique in that it will simultaneously strike each and every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the target unit - resulting in a damage distribution process that is fundamentally different from that of Physical Damage.

The end result is that **Area Damage** becomes stronger the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** there are in the target unit. Against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure units**, **Area Damage** behaves exactly like [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage").

## Concept

All [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") is dealt in a very strict process, intended to cause [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target unit. This process involves the attacker making [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine the accuracy of the attack, and the defender making [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to determine how much of this damage is blocked. When **Area Damage** is directed against a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**, it works exactly like [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") with a damage cap.

However, things change radically whenever **Area Damage** strikes a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**. Where [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") will strike only the "lead" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in that unit and apply excess damage one by one to further [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**, **Area Damage** will strike each and every figure simultaneously. In other words, it will make separate [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit, and each figure gets to make its own [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to try and avert the hits registered specifically against itself.

The end result is that the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** there are in the target unit, the more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** the unit will suffer on average.

## The Mechanism

When used against a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**, **Area Damage** is completely identical to [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), with one exception: its [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is limited to the current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the unit, and can never be greater than that.

When **Area Damage** strikes a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**, it acts as a separate Physical Damage attack initiated individually against each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the unit. All [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** get to apply their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** to reduce their own damage. Unlike in Physical Damage, however, there is no "excess" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. That is, no [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** can take more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** than its current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** total.

### Process Overview

When **Area Damage** hits a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**, the following process takes place:

1. The game calculates the strength of the **Area Damage** inflicted on the unit.
2. The attack makes a set number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against the first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the target unit equal to the Attack Strength. Each successful roll registers a hit against the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.
3. The first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** makes a set number of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls equal to the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. Each successful roll reduces the number of registered hits by 1.
4. If the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is greater than the current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, it gets reduced to this amount. This also takes into account [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** previously suffered by the "lead" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, if any.
5. Steps 2-4 are repeated for all subsequent [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.
6. The game tallies together the number of registered hits that were not successfully blocked, which are then translated into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, and applied to the unit as a whole.
7. The game recalculates the amount of remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** and "lead" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, as appropriate.

Each of these steps is explained separately below.

### Attack Strength

To start the process of inflicting **Area Damage**,
the game must first determine the strength of the attack. This will set
out the magnitude of the attack performed against each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. The Attack Strength is always determined by the source of the **Area Damage**, and is generally indicated both in the in-game tooltips and on the relevant articles on this wiki.

For example, the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability always delivers **Area Damage** with an Attack Strength of 4, while [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Wall of Fire](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Fire "Wall of Fire")**'s **Area Damage** has an Attack Strength of 5. With the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** spell, the Attack Strength is based on how much [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** is invested into the spell: casting it with no extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** will yield an Attack Strength of 5, while spending the maximum amount of extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** results in an Attack Strength of 25 instead.

### To Hit Rolls

Once the Attack Strength has been set, the game makes this many [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit to determine how many hits are registered against each of these [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. Area Damage always uses the base 3-in-10 chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** regardless of its source. Thus, each roll has a 30% chance to succeed.

### Defense Rolls

Each of the target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** gets to make number of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls equal to their unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. For example, if the unit unit has [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5**, each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in that unit may make 5 [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls. The results will be used to separately reduce the amount of successful hits registered against every individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

The base chance to succeed these rolls is the same as for the attacks: 3-in-10, or 30%. However, any [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** modifiers (e.g. [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** or [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky")) that the unit has *do* apply to these rolls. Spells and abilities that increase [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** against certain types of attacks or magic may also apply (e.g. [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield") [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield"), [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")**).

Each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** can only block successful hits that were directed at itself. A [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** cannot block hits registered against another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. In fact, if no hits were registered against a particular figure, that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls are meaningless - even if other figures in the unit *were* successfully hit.

### Calculate Unblocked Hits

In the two previous steps, the game calculated how many hits were successfully registered against each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**,
and how many successful blocks were made by each figure. These are now
subtracted from one another, to get the number of "unblocked" hits
against each figure:

```
Unblocked Hits = Successful Hits - Successful Blocks.
```

This number is limited to a certain range:

* The number of unblocked hits cannot be less than 0.
* The number of unblocked hits directed at any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** cannot be higher than that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**.

This gives a number between 0 and the figure's current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health**.
Note that this takes into account the injuries already sustained by a
"lead" figure from previous combat, so the maximum number of unblocked
hits allowed against a severely-injured figure is lower than that
allowed against a healthy figure. Also remember that only the "lead"
figure in a unit can be injured - all other figures (if any) are assumed
to be at full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **health** at all times.

### Damage Distribution

Finally, the program pools together the number of unblocked hits inflicted on each figure in the unit. This is the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done by the **Area Damage** attack. For example, if we have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **4 Figures**, and each figure suffered 2 unblocked hits, we have a total of 8 unblocked hits - translated into exactly [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **8** points of damage.

These [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** are then applied to the unit as a whole. The current total [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the unit as a whole (which is the sum of the individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**s' [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** totals) is reduced by the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done. If this yields [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0**, the unit is destroyed. Otherwise, the game recalculates the amount of remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** based on the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point total** of the unit and the maximum health of an individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, consaidering that there can only ever be one damaged [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a unit.

Thus, each full [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s worth of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** (as listed on the Unit Panel) from the above total will result in one completely unhurt [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, and any remaining fraction thereof will mean that there is an additional damaged "lead" figure.

## Examples

The process of **Area Damage** delivery can be somewhat confusing. The examples below should illustrate what goes on during an **Area Damage** attack, using various parameters.

### Example #1

:   A [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4-figure** unit of [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") is targeted by a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** spell. Each cavalryman has [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2** and [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3**. The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** is cast with some extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**, giving it an Attack Strength of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **12**.

:   Because the [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**, the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** spell will strike each individual Cavalryman with a strength 12 attack. Thus, the game makes 12 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against each of them. Therefore, each Cavalryman will end up with between 0 and 12 registered hits.

:   Next, each individual Cavalryman may make 2 [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense Rolls** rolls (as per the unit's defense score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2**) to try and avert any hits directed at it. Each successful roll will block one hit.

Correspondingly, both of these attacks are reduced to doing only [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 damage**. Thus, after tallying up, we have a grand total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**, which is then applied to the unit as a whole:

:   1. Since the unit is at full health, it has a total of (6 x [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** = ) [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **6**.
    2. [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **6** - [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3**, thus, the unit as a whole will have [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3 Hit Points** left.
    3. Because each figure has a maximum of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1**, the unit will have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **3 figures** remaining (3 div 1 = 3).

### Example #3

:   A [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Stone Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Giant "Stone Giant")** unit, containing only a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **single** Giant, attacks an enemy unit possessing the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability. The Stone Giant has [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **8 Defense** and [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **20 Hit Points**. The [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability delivers a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **4** strength **Area Damage** attack when triggered, as set by the rules of that ability.

:   Because the [Stone Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Giant "Stone Giant") is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**, only one attack will be made against it. Thus, the game makes 4 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls, registering between 0 and 4 hits on the Giant. The Giant may then make 8 [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls (as per the unit's defense score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **8**) to try and block any hits. The total number of *unblocked* hits is then applied as [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to the Giant.

:   This is obviously a much simpler case than the above examples. Since the [Stone Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Giant "Stone Giant")'s [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** is so high, the attack will likely cause no damage to it at all.

:   This illustrates how **Area Damage** is significantly less powerful against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure units**. Sure, one could cast a fully-powered [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** spell on this Stone Giant, and may even inflict serious damage to it, but this would require investing a very large amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** into the spell. Conversely, a rather cheap [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt")** or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt")** could cause significantly more damage to the Giant - but would be less useful against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** like the Cavalrymen in example #1.

## Area Damage Sub-Types

**Area Damage** is just a term used on this wiki to refer to two [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") that behave almost exactly the same. They are [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") and [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage").

The only difference between the two is the type of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") they are based on, and thus the immunity triggered by them, should the target possess any.

### Immolation Damage

:   *Main article: [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage")*

Immolation Damage is a fairly common [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"). Not only can it be delivered through a [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") (thanks to the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability and the [spell of the same name](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)")), but many [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm** spells will also deliver this type of damage. Immolation Damage behaves as explained above in this article, with two additions.

If the target possesses [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"), Immolation Damage will trigger that immunity. This results in the target's Defense score being raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for the purposes of blocking this damage.

Immolation Damage will also trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), if present. This causes the target to be completely immune to the Immolation Damage attack, and will suffer no damage from it.

### Blizzard Damage

:   *Main article: [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage")*

Blizzard Damage is an extremely rare [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), delivered only by the single [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature Realm** spell called [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm")**. Blizzard Damage behaves as explained above in this article, with two additions.

If the target possesses [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), Blizzard Damage will trigger that immunity. This results in the target's Defense score being raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for purposes of blocking this damage.

Blizzard Damage will also trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), if present. This causes the target to be immune to the Blizzard Damage attack, and will suffer no damage from it.
