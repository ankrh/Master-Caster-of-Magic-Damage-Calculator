**Charm of Life** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") belonging to the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Magic** realm. For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,250** it may be cast on the overland map to grant a bonus of either [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+25% Hit Points** or [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1 Hit Points** (whichever is *greater*)
to each and every friendly unit on the map. This includes existing
units as well as units created while the spell is in effect.

[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** benefit even more from this, since *each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit* receives the same bonus - thus potentially increasing the total [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** by a very large amount.

**Charm of Life** requires an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** per turn to maintain.

## Effects

**Charm of Life** makes all friendly units healthier. This enables them to take more damage (and sometimes *much* more damage) during combat, increasing their chances of survival.

### Health Bonus

While **Charm of Life** is in effect, every unit owned by its caster receives a bonus to its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**.

For units with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **7** or fewer (per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**), the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") of each figure is increased by exactly [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1**. Units with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** or higher (per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**) receive a bonus equal to [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+25%** based on the unit's original number of Hit Points.

For example, a unit of [Manticores](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Manticores "Manticores") has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **7** per figure. With **Charm of Life**, this unit will have [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **7** + [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** per figure. Conversely, an [Arch Angel](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arch_Angel "Arch Angel") has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **18**, so with **Charm of Life** it has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **18** + [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **25%** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **22**.

With extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, the unit can take more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** before being destroyed. In fact, each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** is harder to kill, as explained below.

### Multi-Figure Unit Health Bonus

With [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** score reflects the number of hit points possessed by each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. Therefore, the effects of **Charm of Life** apply to *each individual figure*, rather than to the unit as a whole.

For example, a unit comprised of 2 [Doom Drakes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Drakes "Doom Drakes") has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **20**, equally divided between the two individual drakes - so each drake has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10**. When **Charm of Life** is in effect, each drake's Hit Points are increased by [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+25%**, resulting in each drake having [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **12**.

This is even more clearly seen in lower-tier [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), where this effect can end up increasing the unit's total Hit Points by quite a large amount. For example, a unit of 8 [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers") has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**, meaning [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** per figure. When **Charm of Life** is in effect, each Slinger gets a [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1** bonus, for a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** per Slinger. This is an overall increase of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** for the whole unit - effectively *doubling* its survivability!

As a result, each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** can take more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**
before it is killed. Not only does this delay the unit's death, but it
also slows down the rate at which this unit weakens; it loses figures
more slowly, and thus can maintain full or at least high overall
strength for a longer time, during combat.

### Healing

The extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** bestowed by **Charm of Life** are restored immediately, but only to live [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

For example, with the [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers") in the example above, lets assume that 2 of the slingers are dead, leaving the unit with 6 live Slingers. **Charm of Life** is then cast, increasing each living slinger's health to [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2**, but it does not bring back the lost [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers"). Only healing, whether naturally or via a spell, will restore the dead figures to life - at which point they'll have [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** like the others.

## Usage

**Charm of Life** may be cast only on the overland map, for the very high [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,250**. It is the most expensive spell in the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm**.

As long as the spell remains in effect, *each and every* friendly unit will feel its benefits, including units created before or after the spell was cast.

To keep **Charm of Life** in effect, the casting wizard must pay an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of exactly [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** per turn - regardless of how many units it is affecting. Failure to pay this cost due to lack of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
will cause the spell to dissipate, reducing all units' health scores
back to their normal values. This can have a very negative side-effect
(read more on this below).

After casting the spell, its name will appear on the "Overland
Enchantments" list in the Magic menu. The color in which the name is
printed indicates which wizard controls the spell. If the player
controls the spell, he or she may cancel it manually by clicking the
name of the spell. The primary reason to do this would be in order to
remove the spell's [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"), thus conserving [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** for other spells.

## Acquisition

As a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** spell, **Charm of Life** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**3** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is not guaranteed unless the Wizard acquires at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**10** Spellbooks.

**Charm of Life** may not be acquired at the start of the campaign regardless of how many Spellbooks the wizard possesses. It must either be [Researched](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") during the game, or acquired through other means.

Wizards with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**3** to [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**9** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") **Charm of Life** during the game. The chance for this spell to appear for research increases with the number of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or obtains during gameplay. With [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**10** or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**11** Spellbooks, the spell is guaranteed to appear for Research at some point, if it is not already available for casting.

**Charm of Life** has a base [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **6,000**. It is the most expensive [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** spell in terms of research.

With at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**3** Spellbooks, the **Charm of Life** spell *may be* acquired as a reward for winning encounters in creature [Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lair "Lair"), [Towers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower"), et cetera, or when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival wizard who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

**Charm of Life** is another [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** spell that greatly benefits wizards relying on [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), though it can also have a very good effect on any kind of unit.

The increased survivability resulting from **Charm of Life** means that units are more likely to win battles or at least get out of them alive. This can lend a hand to training up [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), who are otherwise extremely fragile when first recruited.

The **Charm of Life** spell works excellently with several other spells in the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** repertoire, including [Heroism](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heroism "Heroism"), [Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade"), and [Lionheart](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lionheart "Lionheart"). Use these in conjunction to acquire powerful units and armies that are very hard to destroy.

**Charm of Life** can
easily double the effectiveness of your entire army, so try to cast it
as soon as you can and keep it in place. More specifically, casting **Charm of Life** just before launching an offensive against a rival empire can turn the tide of an entire war within a few turns.

## Known Bugs

When **Charm of Life** is removed for any reason, units that have sustained a lot of damage while the spell was in effect may end up with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **0 figures** despite remaining alive conceptually.

This results in "phantom" units. Such units are still visible on
the overland map, and can even be commanded during combat despite being
completely invisible (there are no figures in the unit, so nothing is
drawn on-screen). If such a unit ever receives *any* damage, it will be destroyed immediately. Phantom units do not heal naturally.

This happens because of a fairly obvious bug in the way the game recalculates a unit's health when **Charm of Life** is removed. This is the algorithm the program uses:

1. Reduce the unit's total [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** by the same amount that it gained from **Charm of Life**.
2. Recalculate the number of figures the unit has left, given how many [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** it has left, and given the original maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of each figure.

This is a very messy way of doing things. The examples below illustrate why.

#### Example #1

:   Lets take a unit of [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers") again. This unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **8 figures**, with each Slinger having [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1**, for a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**.

:   Under the effect of **Charm of Life**, each Slinger has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2 Hit Points** - double the amount they originally had. The unit's total number of hit points is [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **16**, so we can say that **Charm of Life** increased the unit's hit points by [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** - this will be important later.

:   Now lets assume this unit is damaged: [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **3 figures** are lost. The unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **5 figures** remaining, each with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2**.

:   The unit survives the battle, but after it's done we remove **Charm of Life**. Here's what happens:

:   1. The unit's total Hit Points count is [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** \* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **5** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10**.
    2. We reduce this by the same amount that the unit received from **Charm of Life**, which was [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**. Therefore, the unit now has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10** - [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2 Hit Points**.
    3. Finally, we recalculate how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** the unit would now have, given that it only has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** left: Without **Charm of Life**, each slinger has only [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1**, so the unit now has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **2 figures** with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** each.

:   Due to this recalculation, we started with a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **5-figure** unit with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** each, but have ended up with a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **2-figure** unit having [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** each.

#### Example #2

:   Now lets assume that the same unit suffered 4 casualties during combat while **Charm of Life** was still in effect. So, the unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **4 figures** left, with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** each, for a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** in the entire unit.

:   Now lets figure out what happens when **Charm of Life** is removed:

    1. The unit's total Hit Points count is [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** \* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **4** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**.
    2. We reduce this by the same amount that the unit received from **Charm of Life**, which was [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**. Therefore, the unit now has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** - [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **0 Hit Points**! You can already see the problem right there.
    3. Finally we recalculate how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** the unit would now have. Obviously, with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **0 Hit Points**, the unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **0 figures** left.

:   This confuses the heck out of the game, because as far as it
    concerns the unit should still be alive. Logically it should be - it had
    4 living figures just a second ago. The game simply doesn't bother to
    check whether the unit should still be allowed to exist.

:   This results in a phantom unit, which, as explained above,
    continues to exist until anything so much as sneezes at it - at which
    point it'll be destroyed.

### So what does this mean?

A phantom unit is inherently unstable; the game was not designed to handle the existence of units with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **0 figures**,
and may end up crashing for all sorts of subsidiary reasons (especially
divide by 0). This is more likely to happen if the unit goes into
combat and ends up being targeted by enemy spells.

Since the unit does not get natural healing, it will likely be
stuck in this condition permanently - or until destroyed. Unfortunately,
it also continues to require an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") - possibly in [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** or [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **Gold**
- despite not being able to contribute anything to your efforts.
Therefore, if you do not possess a way of healing the unit in an
artificial manner, it may be better to *disband it* immediately - especially if the unit would be easy to replace.

Fortunately, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life**-wielding wizards powerful enough to cast **Charm of Life** will usually have one or more types of healing spells. These can be used to restore the unit's dead [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** to life, at which point the unit returns to normal. [Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing "Healing") and [Mass Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Healing "Mass Healing") can do this during combat (though targeting a phantom unit can be a little tricky, since it's invisible). [Stream of Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stream_of_Life "Stream of Life") is a great way to restore many such units to normal, as it will fully heal even a phantom unit to maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

Of course, the best way to handle this is to *never cancel **Charm of Life*** while you have valuable units who are injured, but sometimes this is not in your hands. If at all possible, try to recast **Charm of Life**
as soon as you can, as it will immediately fix any of the problems
caused by its previous removal. In any case, try to keep your phantom
units as far away from combat as possible.
