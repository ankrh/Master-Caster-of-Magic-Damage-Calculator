A **Ranged Magical Attack** (or "**Magical Ranged Attack**") is one of three kinds of [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") available in *Master of Magic*. When a unit is capable of making a **Ranged Magical Attack**, its details panel will show the strength of the attack as a series of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **energy bolts**. The same icon is used on this wiki to represent **Ranged Magical Attacks** and/or their strength.

**Ranged Magical Attacks** have two important advantages over other [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). Firstly, they are accurate at any distance, suffering no [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties based on the range to the target. Thus, a **Ranged Magical Attack** is as effective across the entire battlefield as it is when up-close. Furthermore, **Ranged Magical Attacks** are often stronger and much more common than [Ranged Missile Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack"), and will completely bypass a target's [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity"), if any.

On the other hand, **Ranged Magical Attacks** can be stopped very easily by targets possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). Also, there are spells meant specifically to block a unit's resistance to **Ranged Magical Attacks** of certain types.

Like all other [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), **Ranged Magical Attacks**
may only be used a limited number of times per battle. For most units,
this is a set number displayed in the unit's details panel, as it would
be for a [Ranged Missile Attacker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") or [Ranged Boulder Attacker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack"). Other **Ranged Magical Attack** units must pay [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **3 Mana**
out of their own pool for each attack, and thus are the number of
attacks they can make is limited by the size of their (possibly dynamic)
[Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool.

**Ranged Magical Attacks** are the most common of all [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") in the game, possessed by no fewer than 20 different types of [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), 6 [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), and 14 [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). Most of the [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") possessing a **Ranged Magical Attack** may use a [Staff](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") or [Wand](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand") to increase the strength of this attack.

Finally, for the purposes of this wiki, all direct-damage spells are said to deliver a **Ranged Magical Attack**
against their target, since they usually follow the same rules outlined
below. Therefore, the strength of such spells is also denoted by the [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **energy bolt icon**.

## Ranged Magical Attack Properties

A [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** is one of three different types of [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") in *Master of Magic*, with the other two being [Ranged Missile Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") and [Ranged Boulder Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack"). There are several important differences between each of these attack types. The advantages and disadvantages of **Ranged Magical Attacks** are explained below - for an overall comparison, see [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

### No Distance Penalties

The primary advantage of **Ranged Magical Attacks** is that they suffer no penalties based on the distance to the target. Other [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") will suffer a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalty corresponding to that distance, making them weaker the further away the target.

As a result, **Ranged Magical Attacks**
are exactly as useful when the target is all the way on the other side
of the battlefield as they are when the target is immediately adjacent
to the attacker.

Tactically speaking, this means that **Ranged Magical Attack**
units can unleash this attack immediately at the start of the battle
without having to close the distance first. It also means that enemy
targets cannot minimize their damage by moving away from the attacker.

Overall, this results in an increased damage output for the attacker, compared to similar-strength [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attackers** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attackers**. When going into battle against **Ranged Magical Attackers**, expect weak units to die or be seriously injured before they can even make contact with the enemy.

### No Immunity to Missiles

**Ranged Magical Attacks** do not trigger a target's [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") (if it possesses one at all). Thus, units with a **Ranged Magical Attack** are often brought in to fight Missile-Immune opponents, where [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attackers** would simply have no effect whatsoever.

### Magical Damage

All **Ranged Magical Attacks** deliver [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") to the target. Unfortunately this is a disadvantage, since [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") can easily be stopped by several effects, such as [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity").

When a unit with [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") is struck by a **Ranged Magical Attack**, its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is temporarily boosted to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**
for purposes of blocking this attack. This normally results in the
attack delivering no damage at all to the target. The only exceptions
occur when the **Ranged Magical Attack** is *so* powerful that it can actually defeat [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. Only powerful and highly-[Experienced](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), augmented by powerful [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), will be able to do this.

Furthermore, several spells and abilities will raise the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** against **Ranged Magical Attacks** by a small amount, reducing damage from such attacks. The most common example is [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield"), though [Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements "Resist Elements") and [Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless") may also have some effect against particular types of **Ranged Magical Attacks** (see below).

### Ranged Magical Attack Subtypes

When different units perform a **Ranged Magical Attack**,
the animation of the energy bolt they fire may differ from that of
other units. This is not simply a cosmetic difference, but rather
indicates the [Magical Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") with which the attack is associated. This is handy information for predicting the effects of various protective [Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). For instance, [Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless") or [Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness") will trigger against enemy fire associated with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos**, but grants no protection against that of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature** or [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery**. There are no **Ranged Magical Attacks** associated with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death**.

## Units with Ranged Magical Attacks

The game contains exactly 41 units that possess a **Ranged Magical Attack**. These are the only units that possess such an attack: no unit may acquire a **Ranged Magical Attack** under any circumstance if it does not have one by default.

### Normal Units

21 of the **Ranged Magical Attack** units in the game are [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") from various [Races](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Races "Races"). [Shamans](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shamans "Shamans"), [Priests](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Priests "Priests") and [Magicians](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magicians "Magicians") all possess a **Ranged Magical Attack**. So do the majority of [Dark Elf](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_Elf "Dark Elf") units - including the [Warlocks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlocks "Warlocks") and [Nightmares](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nightmares "Nightmares") - which get a **Ranged Magical Attack** as one of their racial advantages.

All of these units may make up to 4 **Ranged Magical Attacks** per battle. Since these units are all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, their overall attack strength can be pretty high, and thus quite dangerous to opponents.

[Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") acquire [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") over time, which slowly increases the strength of their **Ranged Magical Attack**.

### Heroes

There are 14 [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") in the game who possess a **Ranged Magical Attack**. Each of these [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") is also a [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster"), with his/her own personal [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool and the ability to cast spells during combat. Each **Ranged Magical Attack** performed by such a Hero requires [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **3 Mana** from that pool, and cannot be performed if there is insufficient Mana left for it.

Fortunately, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")' [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pools will gradually grow with [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience"), thus allowing more and more **Ranged Magical Attacks** to be performed (that is, assuming the extra Mana is not used to cast more spells instead).

12 of these 14 Heroes are also capable of holding a [Staff](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") or [Wand](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand"). Any attack-strength bonuses and/or [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses on the item will improve the user's [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attack** strength. Bonuses on [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") items also apply to the wearer's **Ranged Magical Attack**, if he/she possesses one.

[Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") gain [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") over time, and their [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** strength increases at a rate of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **+1** per level. Furthermore, if a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") possesses a **Ranged Magical Attack** as well as the [Arcane Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane_Power "Arcane Power") ability, his/her [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** strength increases by an additional [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **+1** or [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **+1.5** per [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") (depending on the strength of the Hero's [Arcane Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane_Power "Arcane Power") ability). Such [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can eventually have extremely powerful attacks, which may annihilate entire enemy units shortly after the beginning of combat.

The [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") and [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") abilities also influences the Hero's [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack**, but do so indirectly by raising the Hero's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score. This helps the attack inflict more of its potential damage output. Note that the same bonus applies to *all* of the Hero's attacks.

## Spells Delivering a Ranged Magical Attack

Each and every spell that delivers direct-damage is considered on this wiki to be delivering a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attack**.
This is because, for the most part, the process of dealing damage from
such a spell is identical to the process of delivering damage from a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attack** made by a unit. There are a few exceptions, but they still behave much more like a **Ranged Magical Attack** than any other type of attack.

Since all of these spells deliver [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"), they will not harm a target that possesses [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). Unless otherwise stated in the spell's description, the spell's attack must make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls as though it was a regular [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attack**, and the target may make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls as normal. A few spells alter this process, particularly spells delivering [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") (in which case no rolls are made by either party) or [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") (which behaves very differently from any other attack).

Furthermore, many direct-damage spells have an adjustable attack strength; the more [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** is put into the spell at casting time, the stronger the attack. There is always a maximum limit on the amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** that can be added (and thus, on the strength of the attack), and different spells may have different [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Attack Strength** ratios.
