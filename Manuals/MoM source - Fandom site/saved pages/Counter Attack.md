Whenever a unit makes a deliberate [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against an enemy unit, the enemy may be able to retaliate by performing a **Counter Attack**. In other words, while the attacker is delivering [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") to the target, the target also delivers Melee Damage to the attacker.

This occurs in almost all circumstances. The only cases where the target of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") is not allowed to **Counter Attack** occur when the target is incapacitated in some way, for example due to the [Unit Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") "[Black Sleep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sleep "Black Sleep")".

Barring special abilities of the attacker or certain negative spell effects on the defender, [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")
from the defender is calculated the same as Melee Damage from the
attacker, and they are also delivered simultaneously. This means that
the attacker is in as much risk as the defender in most cases.

Some [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), like [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), may be performed by the defending unit as part of its **Counter Attack**.
These stand some chance of killing several of the enemy figures, thus
weakening the enemy unit and subsequently reducing damage to the
defender. Conversely, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** and [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") (if at all available) may not be performed by a defending unit - only by the attacker.

Unlike [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), **Counter Attacks** cost the defender no Movement Points. A unit may **Counter Attack** even if it had spent all of its Movement Points during the previous turn. There is no limit to the number of times a unit may **Counter Attack**
- it may do so each time an enemy unit attacks it in Melee. However,
being attacked multiple times during the same combat turn does impose a
penalty on units, that will affect their subsequent **Counter Attack**(s) (see below).

**Counter Attacks** may not be performed when a unit is defending against a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). On the other hand, [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** and [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** units may freely **Counter Attack** a [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack").

## Concept

When a unit makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
against an enemy unit, it is said to make physical, face-to-face
contact with its target, using hand-held weapons like swords or
halberds, claws, teeth, or whatever other close-combat weapons are
available to it.

The required close proximity to the defender allows that defender to retaliate, in an action called a **Counter Attack**. The defender in effect uses its own close-combat weapons to deliver damage back at the attacker.

As a result, [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") put the attacker himself at risk. This is unlike [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), which are always safe to the attacker.

Due to **Counter Attacks** being a reaction rather than voluntary, the defender may not use all of its [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"). [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") and [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") all require the unit to deliberately engage an enemy, and so cannot be used when **Counter Attacking**. Nonetheless, defenders may use [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") against their assailant, as these are considered to be a natural part of the way they deal damage.

### Triggering a Counter Attack

**Counter Attacks** are automatically triggered whenever one unit makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against another unit. While the attacker performs a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), the defender simultaneously executes a **Counter Attack**.

This will *always* occur unless specific conditions are applied - such as the defender being under the effect of the [Black Sleep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sleep "Black Sleep") spell, which specifically prohibits it from **Counter Attacking**.

Other than this, there are no prerequisites that the defender needs to meet in order to be able to deliver a **Counter Attack**. It may **Counter Attack** as many times as it is attacked during combat!

### Dealing Melee Damage

:   *Main article: [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")*

As with [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), the main goal of **Counter Attacks** is to deliver normal [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") - damage from physical weapons such as swords, spears, claws or teeth, as available.

In fact, unless the defender has any special abilities, its **Counter Attack** will consist only of attempting to deliver as much of this [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") as it can to its assailant. It will try to kill off as many of the enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** as it can, or otherwise injure the enemy unit as much as possible.

Thus, both the [Melee Attacker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and the **Counter Attacker** deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") at each other - and usually they will do so simultaneously at the very end of the Melee Attack process, as explained below.

[Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is explained in its own article (see also [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") for a thorough explanation of the process). In general, the **Counter Attacker** is required to make a number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls in order to determine how many times it has struck the enemy successfully. The enemy then gets to make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense**
rolls to try and block or dodge some (or all) of these hits, minimizing
damage to itself. After all rolls have been completed, "unblocked" hits
are translated into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** and inflicted directly at the enemy's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health** score.

### Special Attacks

:   *Main article: [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")*

During a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), combatants get the opportunity to utilize several additional attacks, called "[Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")",
in order to cause extra harm to the enemy and/or neutralize it in some
way. However, many of these require the unit to be the initiating party
to the Melee, and are thus not available to a defending unit.

The defender is limited to making only [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"),
since these are the only two attacks that could be considered
"integral" to the way it fights - not requiring any deliberation or a
stand-off stance, which a defender does not enjoy. The defender may not
use [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks** and [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, and may not utilize the [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") ability.

The sequence of attacks is also somewhat different between the
attacker and defender, as explained below, to the detriment of the
defender.

## Counter Attack Sequence

As explained earlier, both the [Melee Attacker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and the **Counter Attacker** may use various [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"),
if they have any, to cause extra damage to the opponent. The sequence
in which these attacks are performed is of paramount importance. The [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") itself is only exchanged between the attacker and the target at the *very end* of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") process!

A **Counter Attacker**'s options for [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") are, however, somewhat more limited (as explained in the previous section): it may only make [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") against its opponent, if it has any at all - whereas the opponent may use many additional attack types if available.

Furthermore, the sequence in which [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") are executed by either party is biased against the **Counter Attacker**. The defender's [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") comes *after* the attacker's (assuming both units have a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")), making it somewhat less useful.

Here is the sequence of attacks as it is normally executed:

| Step | Assailant | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") OR [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") | -- |
| 2 | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 3 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 4 | [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") | [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") |
| 5 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") |

As explained in the article on [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"),
the purpose of this stage-based process is to give the combatants a
chance to kill-off or neutralize some or more of the opponent's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, reducing the opponent's overall strength before the next stage begins - especially before the opponent can deliver any of its [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (at stage 5). Thus, a unit with plenty of powerful [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") - particularly [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks**, and/or [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") - has a chance of completely preventing the enemy from dealing Melee Damage, and thus minimize or eliminate any risk to itself.

The reason for this is that any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** killed off during any of the above stages *does not* get to participate in the subsequent stages of the attack. For example, if a target loses half of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
during stage 3, its overall strength by step 5 is half what it had at
the start of the process, thus significantly reducing the risk to its
opponent.

As the table shows, the **Counter Attack** only begins at stage 3. Prior to this, the assailant unleashes any [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, and then [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"). Only then can the **Counter Attacker** unleash a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), if available at all.

When no [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks**, [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") or [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**
are present however, the fight is not biased towards neither the
attacker nor the defender (steps 4 and 5). In such a case, the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and **Counter Attack** overlap entirely, and are executed simultaneously. Thus, both units must exchange [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and/or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") at the same time, offering the defender just as good a fighting chance as the attacker.

Either unit executes only [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that are available to it, and only at the appropriate stages in the process. For example, if the defender lacks a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), step 3 is completely skipped. There are many instances where only step 5 is executed, with both units exchanging only [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") since neither possesses any Special Attacks whatsoever.

### Altering the Sequence

For the most part, the sequence of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and the enemy's **Counter Attack** is set in stone, as described by the table above.

However, there is one [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") in the game which alters this sequence: the [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") ability. When the attacker possesses this [ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability"), the process is rearranged as follows:

| Step | Assailant | Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") OR [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") | -- |
| 2 | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 3 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 4 | -- | [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") |
| 5 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") | -- |
| 6 | [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") | -- |
| 7 | -- | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") |

In this sequence, the **Counter Attacker**'s [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and Melee [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") occur *at the end of the process*, which is a serious problem for the defender. The more defending [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
are killed off or neutralized by the assailant in step 5, the fewer
remain to deliver attacks and damage in step 7. In fact, it is possible
for a First Strike attack to eliminate all opposition by the end of step
5 - preventing the defender from causing any further harm to the
assailant.

Note that [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") is never used by a **Counter Attacking**
unit. This is a major reason why it is imperative to maneuver on the
battlefield in order to prevent enemy First Strike units from making
voluntary [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") - forcing them to **Counter Attack**
instead. A First Strike unit that is allowed to exercise its ability
can often cause significant damage without taking any damage of its own
(aside from the defender's [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")).
To engage them on more even terms, you must make your own voluntary
attacks against them, in which case the First Strike ability is not used
and the sequence remains unchanged (as described [above](#Counter_Attack_Sequence)).

Furthermore, if the defender has the [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike") [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike") ability, then the attacker's [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") does not trigger, and the sequence remains as described [earlier](#Melee_Attack_Sequence).

## Ground vs. Air

A unit may **Counter Attack** against any target, regardless of their respective [Movement Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Type "Movement Type"). Thus, while a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** and/or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** cannot initiate a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against units that [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Fly**, it can **Counter Attack** when such units initiate combat against it without issue.

Therefore, while [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying**
units have a maneuvering advantage and can pick their targets easily,
they are not invulnerable once they actually engage the enemy. The
entire exchange of [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") and [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") operates the same even if the attacker is [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying**.

Thus, powerful ground units have less of a problem fighting
against weak air units, since all they need to do is wait for the air
unit to attack them - in which case the **Counter Attack** may be able to destroy the air unit easily.

## Temporary To Hit Penalty

Usually, a unit's **Counter Attack** is as powerful as when the unit initiates the attack (unless [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") and [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") are involved). However, if a unit is targeted by too many enemy units in the same enemy turn, then it will receive a temporary [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalty to its **Counter Attacks**. For every 2 incoming attacks, the unit will receive -10% (more precisely -10 percentage points) to its [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls. Thereby, every [Ranged](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") and [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
count as an incoming attack. The temporary penalty is removed after
said enemy turn and a roll will always have at least a 10% chance to
succeed.

**Example**

:   :   Consider a [Magic Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit") which is attacked by 2 [Bowmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bowmen "Bowmen") and 2 [Barbarian Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Barbarian_Spearmen "Barbarian Spearmen") (in this order). The [Magic Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit") has no innate To Hit bonus, so each of its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons have usually a **30% chance** of inflicting damage (which can be parried by the opponent's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shields**).
        Now, the 2 Bowmen units use their Ranged Attack. After the second
        attack, the Magic Spirit will receive a temporary -10% chance to its
        future [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
        rolls. It plays no role, if the Bowmen inflict damage to the Magic
        Spirit or not. Now the first Spearmen unit initiates a Melee Attack
        twice. For both **Counter Attacks**, each of the Magic Spirit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons have only a **20% chance** of working. After the second attack, the Magic Spirit will receive a temporary -20% chance to its future [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls. For the calculation of the **Counter Attack**'s [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
        penalty, it plays no role that the Spearmen unit also had a Thrown
        Attack. Now comes the second Spearmen unit and initiates a Melee Attack
        twice again. For both **Counter Attacks**, each of the Magic Spirit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons have only a **10% chance** of working. Now, the enemy turn has ended. All temporary [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties are removed and each of the Magic Spirit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword**s icon would have a **30% chance** of working again.
