**Elemental Armor** is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") from the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **175**, it may be cast on a friendly unit on the overland map to give it two transient bonuses: [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+10** against [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") and ill effects associated with the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** and [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Realms; and [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+10** against all direct-damage attacks from the same Realms. This benefit can be maintained with a [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5** per turn [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

Alternatively, a cheaper version of the same spell may be cast for [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **35**
during combat, in which case it will temporarily bestow the same
bonuses on a unit for the duration of the battle (with no permanent
effects or Upkeep Cost).

## Effects

**Elemental Armor**
protects a unit against specific types of magic. It bestows two separate
bonuses of the same magnitude, both of which apply conditionally
against attacks associated with either the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). However, neither bonus is cumulative with those granted by the similar, but significantly weaker, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements "Resist Elements")** spell.

### Defense Bonus

The second benefit is a powerful [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+10** bonus, that applies whenever the unit is targeted by any [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attack associated with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**. Naturally, this only works if the attack allows a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") (i.e. is not a [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") attack). It's also worth noting that this bonus can be halved by [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing"), or negated by [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion"),
if applicable. Otherwise, it grants 10 extra dice on the Defense Roll,
making it much more likely that the enchanted unit will suffer less, or
even no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from these attacks.

## Usage

**Elemental Armor** may be cast both overland and in combat, for the basic [Casting Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **175** and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **35**
respectively. If the casting is successful, the game will prompt for a
friendly unit to be selected as the spell's target. On the overland map,
this typically requires clicking on the army stack that contains the
unit which, in turn, brings up a list view of all units in that stack,
allowing for the target to be picked individually. Only units not
already under the effect of **Elemental Armor** may be chosen. Once the spell is cast, a glowing [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[green](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** outline appears around the unit (unless it is overridden by a stronger enchantment); and **Elemental Armor**, along with its icon, will be displayed in the *unit statistics window* when inspecting this unit.

The overland version of **Elemental Armor** has an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5**
that must be paid at the beginning of every turn to keep the spell in
effect. This is automatically deducted by the game from the owner's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool as long as the enchantment remains in existence. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
in the pool to pay for the spell, then it will dissipate, ceasing its
effect immediately. It may also be cancelled manually by left-clicking
its name in the *unit statistics window* of its target unit. However, this action can only be performed outside of combat, during the caster's own turn.

The combat version of **Elemental Armor**
does not carry over to the overland map: it will disappear
automatically as soon as the battle is over. As such, it has no Upkeep
Cost; but at the same time may not be manually removed by its caster
either (as enchantments can not be cancelled during a battle).

## Acquisition

As a [Rare Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") of the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Elemental Armor** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**2** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With any less, the spell can not be learned during the campaign. This
is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Elemental Armor** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 10% (with [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**2** books), which gradually increases with the amount of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**10** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **880**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master"), and/or [Nature Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Mastery "Nature Mastery") [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"); or a bookshelf containing [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**8** or more.

**Elemental Armor** may also be selected as a starting spell by players who allocate all of of their picks into [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** books when creating their Wizard. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

## Item Power

**Elemental Armor** can also be duplicated as an [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") and imbued onto a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). This requires the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact")** spell, and has the advantages of being non-dispellable, and requiring no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"). While enchanting [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor_(Item_Power) "Elemental Armor (Item Power)")** onto an item can't be done unless the [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") possesses at least [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[4](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), knowledge of the actual spell is *not necessary*. Selecting this Power increases the item's total value by [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **500**.

[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor_(Item_Power) "Elemental Armor (Item Power)")** can be enchanted onto any type of Magical Item. Naturally, this means that it is available to any [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), as all of them possess at least one [ItemSlot Misc](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/d/d8/ItemSlot_Misc.png/revision/latest?cb=20120702054432) miscellaneous [Equipment Slot](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item#Equipment_Slots "Magical Item").
If an item with this Power is created and given to a Hero, then they
will benefit from the effect described above for as long as they keep
the item equipped. Since [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor_(Item_Power) "Elemental Armor (Item Power)")** is present on [18](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor_(Item_Power)#Overland "Elemental Armor (Item Power)") pre-fabricated Magical Items, it is also possible to occasionally find an item already imbued with this Power in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be offered one for purchase by a wandering [Merchant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant "Merchant").

## Strategy

**Elemental Armor** is an excellent protective spell against enemies that use either [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, or some form of petrification. Its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** bonus is high enough to afford significant reduction even on [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage"). As both its [Casting-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") and [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") are fairly high however, **Elemental Armor** is typically not worth maintaining unless going up against opponents that either use [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** magic, or have a large amount of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magic-** or [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breathing** units.

Unfortunately, its combat Casting Cost is also quite high, making
it unlikely to be usable on a situational basis; which means that when
faced with enemies against whom it does work, it's often better to cast
it overland despite the costs. This is especially true if the player is
usually the attacker in the battles, as the defenders will always get to
shoot first, possibly destroying a would-be target before the spell
could be cast.

The [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
bonus is enough to make a target practically immune to every effect
against which it triggers. It takes multiple different penalties to
lower the resulting score to a level where these have any chance of
working again. That is, the only way of accomplishing this would be
through the use of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Mind Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mind_Storm "Mind Storm")**, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Prayer "Black Prayer")**, or [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Spell Save** [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item").
