**Bless** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25**
it may be cast on a friendly unit on the overland map to give it a
little divine providence. As a result, whenever the unit is targeted by
spells or attacks from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Realms, it receives temporary [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") bonuses of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+3** for the duration of resolving the effect of that spell or attack. **Bless** can then be maintained for an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** per turn.

Alternatively, a cheaper version of the same spell may be cast for [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5**
during combat, in which case it will bestow the same benefits on a unit
only for the duration of the battle (with no permanent effects or
Upkeep Cost).

## Effects

**Bless** gives a unit a modicum of divine protection from various sources of harm associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), including [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") made by [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") that belong to those Realms.

### Resistance Bonus

Whenever the **Blessed** unit is targeted by *any* [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance")-based effect associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), it temporarily receives a bonus of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+3**
to its score while resolving that effect. This will make it harder for
opponents to inflict ill effects on the unit, and is cumulative with all
other modifiers to the unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
score, be those persistent or similarly temporary; universal or
conditional; positive or negative. If the total, "effective" score is [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**
or more, the unit becomes completely immune to the effect, as it no
longer has any chance of failing its Resistance Check against it.

### Defense Bonus

Whenever the **Blessed** unit is targeted by *any* [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attack associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score receives a temporary bonus of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** for the duration of resolving that [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. On average, this allows the **Blessed** unit to block an extra [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0.9** from these attacks. This bonus is cumulative with most, but not all effects that modify [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. Most notably, it is applied *before* any of the following, and will thus be overriden by them, since they all *set* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** instead of modifying it: [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"), [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**, [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage"), [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage"). It is also applied before the [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") effect, which means that attacks marked with this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") will halve the bonus granted by **Bless**.

Determining what Realm an attack is associated with may not
always be straightforward. For spells, it is naturally the Realm that
the spell comes from. The same is true for [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), although in this case it may be worth remembering that all [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units always belong to the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Realm, even if they were created from a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") of some other Realm; and that [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** units are always marked as [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** units.

Things get more complicated with [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") and [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")
however. In this case, the color of the attacking unit is completely
irrelevant. Instead, the attacks themselves have preset Realm
associations. For Special Attacks, this is mostly intuitive, but for [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**
it certainly is not. From the player's perspective, short of memorizing
the table below, the only clue here is that attacks that use the same
projectile graphic and sound effect are considered to be the same type,
and are thus associated with the same Realm, no matter what type of unit
is making the attack. For example, both [Warrax the Chaos Warrior](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warrax_the_Chaos_Warrior "Warrax the Chaos Warrior") and [Storm Giants](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Storm_Giant "Storm Giant")
shoot identical looking lightning bolts when they make Ranged Attacks,
which means that both of these are associated with the same Realm. In
this particular case, it is [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**, despite the fact that Storm Giants are [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")** creatures, and Warrax is a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") with no real Realm affiliation besides his title.

### Combined Bonus

In the unique case of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Call Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Call_Chaos "Call Chaos")** spell, which bonus is granted by **Bless**, if any, is determined on the spot. Two of the random effects of this [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") are [Resistable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance")
(Warp Creature and Disintegrate), and will thus trigger the Resistance
bonus, while two others (Fire Bolt and Warp Lightning) deal (non-[Doom](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage")) [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"). These both activate the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") bonus instead, although in the case of the Warp Lightning effect, the bonus is halved, as it deals [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage"). Altogether, **Bless** offers a degree of protection against 4 of the 5 possible harmful effects of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Call Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Call_Chaos "Call Chaos")**.

## Usage

**Bless** may be cast both overland and in combat, for the basic [Casting Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25** and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5**
respectively. If the casting is successful, the game will prompt for a
friendly unit to be selected as the spell's target. On the overland map,
this typically requires clicking on the army stack that contains the
unit which, in turn, brings up a list view of all units in that stack,
allowing for the target to be picked individually. Only units not
already under the effect of **Bless** may be chosen. Once the spell is cast, a glowing [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[white](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** outline appears around the unit (unless it is overridden by a stronger enchantment); and **Bless**, along with its icon, will be displayed in the *unit statistics window* when inspecting this unit.

The overland version of **Bless** has an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1**
that must be paid at the beginning of every turn to keep the spell in
effect. This is automatically deducted by the game from the owner's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool as long as the enchantment remains in existence. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
in the pool to pay for the spell, then it will dissipate, ceasing its
effect immediately. It may also be cancelled manually by left-clicking
its name in the *unit statistics window* of its target unit. However, this action can only be performed outside of combat, during the caster's own turn.

The combat version of **Bless**
does not carry over to the overland map: it will disappear
automatically as soon as the battle is over. As such, it has no Upkeep
Cost; but at the same time may not be manually removed by its caster
either (as enchantments can not be cancelled during a battle).

## Acquisition

As a [Common Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Bless** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**1** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With none, the spell can naturally not be learned during the campaign.
This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Bless** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 30% (with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**1** book), which gradually increases with the amount of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**7** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **20**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**8** or more.

**Bless** may also be selected as a starting spell by any Wizard with at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**2** books. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

## Item Power

**Bless** can also be duplicated as an [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") and imbued onto a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), either as a stand-alone enchantment, or as part of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Avenger](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Avenger_(Item_Power) "Holy Avenger (Item Power)")** power, which combines it with a [Dispel Evil (Ability)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil_(Ability) "Dispel Evil (Ability)") [Dispel Evil](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil_(Ability) "Dispel Evil (Ability)") [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"). Since the former only increases the item's value by [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **200**, it can be imbued even with the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Enchant Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchant_Item "Enchant Item")** spell. For the latter however, with its value increase of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **500**, casting [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact")** is always necessary.

[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless_(Item_Power) "Bless (Item Power)")** and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Avenger](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Avenger_(Item_Power) "Holy Avenger (Item Power)")** have [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") requirements of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[2](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[3](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** to enchant onto an item respectively, but knowledge of the actual **Bless**
spell is not needed for either. In both cases, the resulting effect has
the advantages of being non-dispellable, and requiring no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"). On the other hand, they are also transient, meaning that a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") will only enjoy their benefit while they keep the item equipped.

[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless_(Item_Power) "Bless (Item Power)")** can be enchanted onto any type of magical [Platemail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Platemail "Platemail"), [Chainmail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chainmail "Chainmail"), [Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shield "Shield"), or [Accessory](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry"). Naturally, this means that it is available to any [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), as all of them possess at least one [ItemSlot Misc](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/d/d8/ItemSlot_Misc.png/revision/latest?cb=20120702054432) miscellaneous [Equipment Slot](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item#Equipment_Slots "Magical Item"). [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Avenger](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Avenger_(Item_Power) "Holy Avenger (Item Power)")**, on the other hand may only be placed on [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** weapons - [Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace"), and [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"); which excludes pure caster Heroes from utilizing it. As these powers are present on [7](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless_(Item_Power)#Overland "Bless (Item Power)") and [10](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Avenger_(Item_Power)#Overland "Holy Avenger (Item Power)") pre-fabricated Magical Items respectively, it is possible to occasionally find an item already imbued with one of them in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be offered such an item for purchase by a wandering [Merchant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant "Merchant").

## Strategy

**Bless** is a deceptively useful spell. The premise that it only works against [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** attacks suggests that it is not worth casting unless going up against [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") or [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") using or featuring these [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). However, that is not actually the case, as indicated by the tables above, that list a large number of [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") who use attacks against which **Bless** offers a measure of protection. In particular, it can be quite helpful in the early game on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror") against [Dark Elves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_Elves "Dark Elves") and [Draconians](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Draconians "Draconians"), even if they are not controlled by a Wizard.

Regardless though, the main appeal of **Bless** is that unlike other similar spells, it also works against [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). Combined with the also [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Armor "Holy Armor")**, they grant [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** equivalent to the [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Iron Skin](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Iron_Skin "Iron Skin")** spell against enemies that trigger the **Bless** effect with their attacks. This results in an average [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") reduction of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1.5** from each attack, which is rather powerful, especially on [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**.

In defensive battles, or when attacking enemies that don't use [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") that would trigger **Bless**,
it is typically better to cast it during the battle. This is especially
true if there are one or more Heroes available with the [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") ability. Given its minimal combat [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost"), even the lowest level, lowest [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
Heroes are able to throw this spell at least once per battle. Of
course, for units that see a lot of combat, it's nearly always better to
have **Bless** maintained overland instead. The exceptions would be the very early game, where even those [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1**'s add up quite quickly; and possibly during ongoing struggles against rival Wizards, since **Bless** is fairly easy to dispel.

In the original game, an average of roughly 31% of all Encounter Zones feature [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** creatures, against whom **Bless** will exert its full power. The unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch raises this value even further, to over 48%, making **Bless** an excellent [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") to maintain on an army whose primary goal is to clear these sites. If Heroes are used for this task, imbuing this spell onto a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") is likely even better, since it's one of only a handful of non-attribute [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") that can be placed on armor, especially when [crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting") with [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Enchant Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchant_Item "Enchant Item")**.
