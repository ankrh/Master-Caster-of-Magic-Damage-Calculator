The term "**Special Attack**" describes a group of [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type"), all of which have one thing in common: **Special Attacks** can only be performed alongside conventional attacks.

There are four main types of **Special Attacks** in the game. Three of these ([Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), and [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")) are short range attacks that can only be executed as part of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") sequence. The fourth type of **Special Attack**, called [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), are essentially added effects that can be attached to other Attack Types that a unit makes, including [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), and even short range **Special Attacks**! Furthermore, [Fear Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") are considered a fifth, "unofficial" type of **Special Attack**, although they are not actually offensive in nature.

During the Melee Attack process, a unit will deliver as many **Special Attacks**
as it is allowed to make. These are executed in a strict sequence: the
attacker's short range ability occurs first, followed by the defender's
Gaze Attack(s), if any (other short range abilities may not be used when
[Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")), and finally the exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")
between the attacker and defender. Fear Attacks occur just before the
opposing unit would deliver its Melee Damage, while Touch Attacks are
simultaneous with both short range attacks and Melee Damage (and may
thus be performed more than once during the sequence).

The timing of a **Special Attack**
within this sequence has a lot of bearing on its potential tactical
usefulness: the sooner it occurs, the more it reduces the risk of damage
to the unit performing it.

A unit can only make a **Special Attack** if it possesses an appropriate [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability"), [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), or [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). The source of a **Special Attack** also defines which [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type")(s) it delivers - and thus what kind of effect the attack will have on the target. For example, the [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") ability allows the unit to make a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") delivering [Stoning Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Damage "Stoning Damage"), which forces the target to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** or be turned to stone (i.e. die). The source of the **Special Attack** also defines either its [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), or its [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **Resistance penalty**.

## Concept

When a unit is making a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
against an opponent, it first approaches that opponent with the intent
of making physical contact. However, before this contact can be
established, some units are capable of executing various short-ranged
attacks that do not involve hand-to-hand weaponry. These "additional"
attacks, called **Special Attacks**,
may precede hand-to-hand combat, and can involve such things as
throwing short-range weapons the target, or breathing a blast of fire at
it.

Furthermore, while charging at its target the attacker also opens itself to **Special Attacks** made by that target. In other words, the close proximity of Melee combat also allows the defender to use some of its own **Special Attacks**
against its assailant. While the defender can not use thrown weapons or
breath-based attacks, it may still use any other kind of **Special Attack** to try and harm the assailant before or during physical contact.

**Special Attacks** and [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") are inextricably tied together: the only opportunity to use most **Special Attacks**
is during Melee combat - they cannot be used in any other circumstance.
Furthermore, it is not possible for a unit to execute a Melee Attack
without also executing all of its available **Special Attacks**.

Despite this close relationship, each **Special Attack** is essentially considered to be a separate entity (both to other **Special Attacks** and the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") itself); and they all occur either just before the delivery of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), or concurrently with it. Because both the attacker and defender are allowed to strike each other with **Special Attacks** and/or Melee Damage, the timing of each **Special Attack** is of great importance: an "earlier" attack that hurts the opponent may weaken that opponent's "later" attacks.

For the most part, **Special Attacks** can be quite deadly - and potentially much more deadly than the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
itself. They can often hurt the opponent to a much greater degree than
the Melee Attack alone could, and often completely ignore the target's
combat skill or armor values. Units possessing certain types of **Special Attacks**
are therefore extremely dangerous to engage in Melee combat, and should
be avoided on the battlefield if at all possible. The only way to
prevent such units from executing their deadly **Special Attacks** is to prevent them from engaging in Melee combat.

## Special Attack Types

The game contains either four or five different **Special Attack**
types, depending on whether the manual is to be taken at face value.
Each of these is decidedly different from the others, based primarily on
its timing within the Melee Attack sequence (see below). However they
all share one common property: they are always executed as part of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), or [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). It is not possible to execute a **Special Attack** on its own.

The three short range **Special Attacks**
(Thrown, Breath, and Gaze) are mutually exclusive with both each other
and all types of Ranged Attacks, meaning that a unit can only ever have
one of these attack types. This makes the melee sequence somewhat easier
to understand, as there is no real need to remember the actual order in
which these would take place. Any short range attacks available to a
defending unit also always come after those of an attacker.

### Thrown Attacks

One of the three short range types of **Special Attacks** is the [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"). This is an attack performed with short-ranged weapons, such as balanced axes, throwing-spears, and so forth.

A Thrown Attack is executed at the very beginning of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), which gives it a strong advantage: if it can kill one or more of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
in the target unit, it will reduce the enemy's strength for later
stages of the Melee combat process, including the actual exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage").

A unit can only use a Thrown Attack when it is voluntarily
initiating a Melee Attack against a target. It cannot use a Thrown
Attack when [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")
to an enemy's assault. Thus, units with Thrown Attacks need to try to
keep the initiative, and should constantly maneuver to prevent enemies
from voluntarily attacking *them*.

For a unit to deliver a Thrown Attack, it must possess the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability. The value of this ability indicates its [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"). Note that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** deliver one such attack per each live [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** they contain.

All [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** deliver simple [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), which involves [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls, and unblocked hits will result in actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** applied to the target.

### Breath Attacks

[Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack")
are somewhat rare, and are very similar to Thrown Attacks. In this
attack type, the unit breathes a blast of energy (whether fire or
lightning) at its target from a safe distance, before proceeding to
charge that target for physical contact.

Breath Attacks are executed at the very beginning of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), giving them the same advantage that Thrown Attacks have: any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** killed by a Breath Attack cannot retaliate - giving the attacker more safety as it proceeds to charge the target.

A unit can only use a Breath Attack when it is voluntarily initiating a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against a target. It cannot use a Breath Attack when [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")
to an enemy's assault. Thus, units with Breath Attacks need to try to
keep the initiative, and should constantly maneuver to prevent enemies
from voluntarily attacking *them*.

There are two types of Breath Attacks in the game, and they
differ from one another only by the type(s) of damage they deliver. In
both cases however, it is still [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), which involves [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls, and unblocked hits will result in [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** applied to the target.

For a unit to deliver a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), it must possess either the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") or [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") ability. The value of this ability indicates its [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"). As with Thrown Attacks, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** deliver one Breath Attack for each live [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** they contain.

### Gaze Attacks

[Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")
involve a metaphysical property of a unit: it only has to make
eye-contact with its target, and this magically causes damage to that
target. The process still requires both units to be within close
proximity to each other ("staring distance").

Gaze Attacks differ from other short range **Special Attacks** in that they can also be used during a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").
For an attacking unit, they are executed at the beginning of the Melee
sequence, while a defending unit has to wait until after the attacker's
short range attacks, if any. In both cases however, the attack takes
place before the actual exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage").

There are three types of Gaze Attacks in the game, differing from
each other only by the type(s) of damage they deliver. In all cases,
this involves very powerful [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"),
and is quite likely to severely hurt or even completely destroy the
target. Thus, Gaze Attackers make very dangerous opponents in Melee
combat, and are often countered by striking at them from a long distance
away via [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") or combat spells.

As mentioned earlier, a unit can use its Gaze Attacks both when
attacking voluntarily and when defending. Thus, it is dangerous even
when the enemy retains the initiative. However, when both attacker and
defender possess a Gaze Attack, the attacker still has a significant
advantage (see above).

For a unit to deliver a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), it must possess either the [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze"), [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") or [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") abilities. The value of the ability indicates either its [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") (for Doom Gaze), or the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty it inflicts on the target (for Stoning and Death Gazes).

Unlike Thrown and Breath attacks, only one Gaze Attack is delivered (for each ability possessed), regardless of how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** are present in the attacking unit.

### Touch Attacks

[Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"),
as their name would indicate, should take place only once physical
contact has been established between both combatants. As they strike
each other with their physical weapons, the Touch Attacker would deliver
additional damage, in one form or another, against the opponent.
However, this is not actually the case. Instead, Touch Attacks are *affixed to* all (or most, in the case of [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")) [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") that the combatants make, including both short range **Special Attacks** and the exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). This makes it possible for them to be used during [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") as well.

Touch Attacks are executed simultaneously with the attacks that they are *"attached to"*.
Furthermore, if both the attacker and defender possess Touch Attacks,
they will exchange them simultaneously during the exchange of Melee
Damage - hurting each other at the same exact timing. This is very
important, and means that neither combatant has a tactical advantage.
This can be changed, however: if the attacker possesses the [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike")
ability, it wins an advantage here (delivering Melee Damage and Touch
Attacks first), and may end up weakening the opponent before retaliation
occurs.

Touch Attacks are the most varied of all **Special Attack** types - delivering a wide variety of [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type").
Some Touch Attacks are more deadly than others, but in all cases they
pose a significant threat to the opponent. Units with a more deadly
Touch Attack are best engaged at a long distance, i.e. with Ranged
Attacks and combat spells. Units with a weaker Touch Attack can be
engaged in Melee combat, particularly by units with [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), or [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), since these are executed first in the sequence (see below).

For a unit to deliver a [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), it must possess one of five different [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities"): [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch"), [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch"), [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation"), [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal"), or [Dispel Evil](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil "Dispel Evil") [Dispel Evil](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil "Dispel Evil").
Where applicable, the strength of the ability indicates either how much
damage the attack delivers, or what sort of penalty is inflicted on the
target while it is defending itself from the attack. [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wielding powerful [Magical Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") may also be able to spread [Death (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_(Item_Power) "Death (Item Power)") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_(Item_Power) "Death (Item Power)") and [Destruction (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Destruction_(Item_Power) "Destruction (Item Power)") [Destruction](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Destruction_(Item_Power) "Destruction (Item Power)") with but a Touch.

[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-figure** units perform their Touch Attacks once for every remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** they have at the time of execution. The only exception is Immolation, which instead attacks each target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** individually, but occurs only once regardless of the amount of initiating [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

### Fear Attacks

Although not mentioned in the original game manual, it seems that the [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") ability gives the unit a **Special Attack** that does not match any of the other categories. This wiki refers to this as a [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack").

Fear Attacks are executed immediately before the exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") between the two combatants. It delivers [Fear Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Damage "Fear Damage"), the sole purpose of which is to prevent a number of enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** (or all of them) from delivering any of their Melee Damage or related [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"). It effectively reduces the strength of the enemy's retaliation, though it doesn't cause any actual harm to the target.

Both the attacker and defender in Melee combat may use their Fear
Attacks, if they have any. If both units possess a Fear Attack, they
will exchange these attacks simultaneously. Therefore, it is quite
possible for both units to completely shut each other down - resulting
in no Melee Damage or Touch Attacks being exchanged.

## Melee Attack Sequence

As repeatedly mentioned above, no **Special Attack** can be executed on its own. To perform nearly all **Special Attacks**, a unit must either *make* a Melee Attack or *come under* a Melee Attack. The exact situation will determine which **Special Attacks** (if any) the unit is allowed to make - but they will always happen as part of the overall [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") process.

As a result, the Melee Attack process can be quite complex - especially when both units possess a wide variety of **Special Attacks** each (for example, two [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")** fighting each other...). However there is a strict sequence that can be followed to tell the order in which all of these **Special Attacks** are executed - and it matters *a lot*.

Normally (see below for exceptions), the Melee sequence has 3 distinct stages:

| Stage | Attacker | Defender |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") / [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") / [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 2 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 3 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") |

At each stage, the game will process each and every relevant attack,
whether made by the attacker, the defender, or both. At the end of each
stage, the game applies any injuries, casualties, and effects resulting
from these attacks, and only then does it move to the next stage. If at
any stage one of the combatants is completely destroyed, it will not be
able to deal any more damage or use any abilities. However, it can still
*take* damage, which may be important for the concepts of Irreversible- and [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") damage.

Furthermore, it's important to note that the strength of a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**'s attack often relies greatly on the number of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** it has remaining. Therefore, when either combatant is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**, it hopes to retain as many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as possible from each stage to the next. The more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** survive each stage, the more damage potential the unit has. In particular, [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (at the very bottom of the list!) relies greatly on the number of surviving [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. It is possible to weaken a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** opponent earlier in the process to limit the amount of Melee Damage it will deliver at the very end of it.

This gives a clear advantage to an attacking unit that has a
short range attack. Since these are executed at the very start of the
process (at stage 1), they have a good chance of weakening the defender,
and thus minimizing or eliminating any damage to the attacker, at any
of the stages that follow.

For the defender, a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") is a little less advantageous tactically - it can only prevent the attacking unit (or its figures) from delivering their [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")
- or at least weaken the enemy somewhat before those are delivered (at
stage 3). However, this is still very important against an attacking [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**, since the potential strength of Melee Damage (and many Melee Touch Attacks) relies heavily on the number of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** the unit has left.

At stage 3 of the process, each of the two combatants deliver their [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") at the opponent, combined with any [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")
they may have. The effects of all of these (potentially many) attacks
are processed and recorded in memory, so injuries and casualties to
either combatant apply only after the game is done processing. This
prevents Touch Attacks and Melee Damage from weakening either combatant
any further before, at the very end of the combat process, they both
suffer the effects and the entire attack is concluded.

The process is strictly organized (with one exception, detailed below). In order to determine whether a unit with a **Special Attack**
will win in combat against another unit, this sequence must be followed
precisely. Any casualties inflicted on either unit between each of the
stages in the list above can have serious consequences on the results of
the combat round.

### First Strike

There is exactly one situation where **Special Attacks** are performed out of the sequence explained above: when a unit with [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") makes a voluntary [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against a unit without [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike") [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike").

When this condition is met, the attacker has a significant advantage: it will deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and affixed [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") (if any) *before* the defender gets to do the same.

So with [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") in effect, the table looks like this:

| Stage | Attacker | Defender |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") / [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") / [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 2 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 3 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") | -- |
| 4 | -- | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") |

Note how the defender's Melee Damage was moved down to a new stage (stage 4). This means that the attacker may deliver this *first*, and most importantly, that the implications of his Melee Damage is applied on the target *before* it can react.

As a result, the attacker gets a very big advantage: when stage 3 concludes, the defender may have lost some or all of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. Therefore, its own [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") may have reduced in strength, or the defender could even be completely destroyed.

Again, this only occurs when the attacking unit is the one possessing First Strike. A defender may not use its own [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") ability, even if it has one. Furthermore, if the defender possesses the [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike") [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike") ability, then the above condition is not met and [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") will not take effect this combat round.

Tactically speaking, this is a very good reason why [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") units often possess good maneuvering properties - they need to be able to make as many *voluntary* [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
against their targets as they can, ideally without ever being
voluntarily attacked by the enemy. If they allow the enemy to attack
voluntarily, they don't get to use [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike"), and thus lose a great advantage.

This is also why [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") can be extremely useful against enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**. By pushing the enemy's [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and related [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")
to stage 4, there's a good chance of weakening the enemy (and hence
those retaliatory attacks) beforehand. Remember again that both Melee
Damage, and most Touch Attacks delivered by a unit, may rely greatly on
the number of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** it has remaining!

### Fear Attacks

Note that neither of the tables above mentions [Fear Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack"). This is an "un-official" attack type which is granted by only one single ability, called [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear"). Essentially, Fear Attacks are executed immediately preceding the opponent's [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), and may be utilized by both an attacking or defending unit if they possess it.

The only purpose of a Fear Attack (and the "Fear Damage" associated with it) is to prevent one or more enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** from participating in stages 3 or 4, as applicable - preventing those [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** from delivering any Melee Damage or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") of their own. Other than this, Fear Damage causes no harm to its target whatsoever.

If the Fear Attack is particularly successful, it can prevent *all* enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
from participating in stages 3 or 4, which reduces the risk to the Fear
Attacker considerably. In the case of both units having a Fear Attack,
it is quite possible for both attacks to be successful - thus
neutralizing both the attacker and the defender simultaneously.

Bear in mind, however, that Cause Fear has major bugs in game version 1.31, and it requires at least the unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")
patch to get any practical use out of Fear Attacks. Without this, it
will generally work against the unit possessing it (or have no effect at
all).

### Multiple Special Attacks

Several units possess a variety of **Special Attacks** by default. [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") and/or [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can also add certain **Special Attacks** to a unit. A good example is the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**, which possesses 3 different [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), a [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), *and* a [Fear Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack") by default.

When a unit has a variety of **Special Attacks**,
it will deliver as many of them as are allowed by the attack it is
making. Returning to the Chaos Spawn example, this unit will deliver all
three of its Gaze Attacks, as well as its Touch Attack and Fear Attack,
whenever it enters Melee combat. It will even execute its Touch Attack
twice if it is not destroyed before the [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") stage.

As noted already, while a unit may possess multiple types of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and/or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"),
short range attacks (Thrown, Breath, and Gaze) are otherwise mutually
exclusive. They are also mutually exclusive with any kind of [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). This is especially important when talking about the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** spell, which can add a [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack** to a unit that does not already have it: if the unit had a [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack** before the spell was cast on it, it will be *replaced*
by the Breath Attack (which is somewhat inferior for several reasons,
as explained in the Chaos Channels article, and has prompted this
behaviour to be changed in the unofficial patches).

When multiple Gaze Attacks or multiple Touch Attacks are
delivered by the same unit, they are processed simultaneously. This
means that the game first calculates the effects that each attack will
have on the target (that is, it makes all the necessary rolls to
determine what harm will be done to the target), and only once all of
these attacks have been processed will it apply any damage or kill any
enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. For example, with multiple Gaze Attacks, the game first calculates each Gaze Attack to see what harm it *will* do to the target, and only once done processing all of these attacks will it actually inflict that harm.

This means that the sequence in which these attacks are processed
is largely irrelevant. For example, if a unit delivers both an [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") Touch Attack and a [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage")
Touch Attack, the order in which they are processed does not affect the
outcome - both are processed as though they were stand-alone attacks,
and only when the game is done with all that processing does it apply
the accumulated total damage to the target unit.

## Immunities

Almost all **Special Attacks** will trigger the [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") of a target, if it possesses that effect from any source. The only ones that do not are [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") Thrown, [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") Doom Gaze, and [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") Poison Touch. When this happens, the target's defensive attributes are improved temporarily: [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") is raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**, and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") is increased by [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **30**;
for the purposes of resolving the effect of the attack. In most cases,
this is equivalent with total immunity, but very strong attacks (e.g.
the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack** of a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Great Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Drake "Great Drake")**) may still inflict some damage.

The [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")** has an identical effect on [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath")[Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Breath Attacks**, [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze")[Death (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_(Item_Power) "Death (Item Power)") Death Gaze/Touch, [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") Life Steal, [Destructiin (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Destructiin_(Item_Power) "Destructiin (Item Power)") Destruction, [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") Immolation, and [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") Cause Fear.

[Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") will trigger *no immunity whatsoever*. This makes them more reliable against a very wide variety of targets. Of course, since all [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** deliver simple [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), they can in turn be stopped by a target with heavy [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Armor**, but that isn't (strictly speaking) an "immunity".

[Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") also triggers no immunities, and its damage can not be reduced by any means either (apart from reducing the actual [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")** making it). This makes it similarly useful, however, this ability is only possessed by a single unit in the game.

Other than these, most [Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") have a specific immunity that they trigger, such as [Poison Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Immunity "Poison Immunity") [Poison Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Immunity "Poison Immunity"), [Stoning Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Immunity "Stoning Immunity") [Stoning Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Immunity "Stoning Immunity"), or [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"). This always depends on the [Type of Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") delivered by the attack: [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") Stoning Gaze (delivering [Stoning Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Damage "Stoning Damage")) will trigger a target's [Stoning Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Immunity "Stoning Immunity") Stoning Immunity, but not its [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity"). Conversely, [Poison Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Immunity "Poison Immunity") Poison Immunity will be triggered by [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") Poison Touch, but not by [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") Immolation.

Therefore, when trying to determine whether a unit's **Special Attacks** will be effective against a target, it is important to know what [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type")
it delivers, and what immunities the opponent possesses. If the target
has any of the immunities corresponding to that damage type, it will
likely not suffer any harm from the **Special Attack**.

There are also some conditional effects, such as those conferred by the spells [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")**, that, while not technically immunities, can push a target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** to levels where they will no longer be able to fail their rolls with respect to **Special Attacks** that belong to the Realm of magic these effects protect against.
