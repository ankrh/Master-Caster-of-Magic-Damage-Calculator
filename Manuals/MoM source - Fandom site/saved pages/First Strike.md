**First Strike** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

**First Strike** is sometimes considered a type of [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"),
but instead of allowing a unit to deliver extra damage to its target,
it actually changes the order in which damage is exchanged between the
unit and its target.

When a unit with **First Strike** makes a voluntary [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** against a target, it delivers both [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and any [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") it may possess *before* the target gets to deliver its own Melee Damage and Touch Attacks.

As a result, a unit with **First Strike** can kill one or more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
in the target unit just prior to the target's retaliation. Any figures
killed this way do not deliver any retaliatory damage. This gives the **First Strike** unit defensibility through offense.

**First Strike** is a fairly-common ability, possessed by no fewer than 13 units by default. 8 of these are [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") from different [Races](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Race "Race") (particularly [Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cavalry "Cavalry")), 3 are [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), and 2 are powerful [Champions](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Champion "Champion"). All units with **First Strike** are quite fast-moving, an important property required to utilize the **First Strike** ability in battle.

There is no known method of adding **First Strike** to a unit that does not possess it by default.

Note that there is another unit ability, called [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike"), whose only purpose is to prevent **First Strike** from working at all.

## Description

One of the primary advantages of mounted units is their ability to
charge into an enemy formation at high speed. This allows the unit to
control the engagement, dash in, and inflict as much damage on the enemy
as possible before the enemy can even react. The vast majority of units
can only ready themselves and hope that they survive this initial
onslaught so that they can return some damage to the cavalry that
charged them.

Not every horse-mounted unit in the game possesses this ability, but it is mostly common among [Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cavalry "Cavalry")
units and other similar mounted troops. The speed and agility of a
horse is all but required in order to perform this complex maneuver.

## Effect

**First Strike** is automatically used whenever the unit makes a *voluntary* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** against an enemy unit. It is not used when the **First Strike** unit is [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to an enemy assault.

**First Strike** does
not increase the unit's damage output in any way. Its only effect is
that it rearranges the order of the combat round, allowing the **First Strike** unit to deliver its damage *before* the enemy can retaliate.

During combat where **First Strike** is *not involved*, the exchange of damage is handled in the following order:

1. The attacker delivers its [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack** or [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**, if it has one.
2. The attacker delivers its [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), if it has any.
3. The defender delivers its [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), if it has any.
4. Both attacker and defender exchange [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") with each other, as well as any [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") they might have. This is done simultaneously.

Any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
in the attacking and defending unit that survives any of the above
stages gets to continue to the next stage and deliver the appropriate
attacks. Figures that die during any stage do not proceed to the next
stage, and thus their unit's overall damage output is reduced for that
stage.

When **First Strike** is utilized, the order of combat is radically altered, giving the attacker a serious advantage:

1. The attacker delivers its [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack** or [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**, if it has one.
2. The attacker delivers its [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), if it has any.
3. The defender delivers its [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), if it has any.
4. The attacker delivers its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**, and any [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") it might have.
5. The defender delivers its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**, and any [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") it might have.

Essentially, **First Strike** added another step along the process, allowing the attacker to deliver its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and/or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") first. Once again, enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** killed during this new stage do not reach stage #5 alive, and thus cannot retaliate with their any [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")!

The overall result is (usually) that the strength of the enemy's [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") is significantly reduced - assuming the attacker has managed to kill some enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** during its charge. Even better: if the enemy unit is entirely destroyed during step #4, it delivers *no [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")* at the **First Strike** unit (apart from [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), if at all available, which are delivered during steps #2 and #3).

The result, therefore, is reduced risk to the attacker. The attacker might even suffer *no damage at all*, removing the primary drawback of Melee combat compared to Ranged combat.

### Example

:   Lets imagine a unit of [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas") attacking a unit of [Gnoll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Spearmen "Gnoll Spearmen"). Both units are at full health for the purposes of this example. The [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas") unit possesses the **First Strike** ability, as well as a [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") ability of strength 4.

:   If the [Gnoll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Spearmen "Gnoll Spearmen") makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** against the [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas"), the normal order of combat is used. Thus, the exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") between the two units occurs simultaneously, as well as the [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas") causing extra [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") to the [Gnoll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Spearmen "Gnoll Spearmen"). The [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas") unit stands to bear the full strength of the Spearmen's attack, which can potentially amount to roughly [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7.2 points of Damage** - easily enough to kill one of the Nagas.

:   However, if it is the [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas") who make the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** against the [Gnoll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Spearmen "Gnoll Spearmen"), then **First Strike** is triggered, and it changes the order of combat. In this case, the [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas") deliver both their [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") *and* [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") first.

:   The [Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas") can be expected to deliver a total of roughly [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **8.8 points of Damage** - easily enough to kill most or all of the [Gnoll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Spearmen "Gnoll Spearmen"). Only those spearmen who actually survive the attack may deliver their own Damage back at the Nagas. If the *entire* [Gnoll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Spearmen "Gnoll Spearmen") unit is killed this way, it delivers *no damage whatsoever* back at the Nagas!

### Negate First Strike

Several types of units possess a "counter-ability" specifically designed to prevent **First Strike** from working. It is unsurprisingly called "[Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike")".

When a unit with **First Strike** attacks a unit with [Negate First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Negate_First_Strike "Negate First Strike"), the **First Strike** ability is *not utilized*
at all. It does not trigger, and does not change the order of the
combat round. Thus, combat proceeds normally, with both units exchanging
[Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") simultaneously.

This is a classic maneuver that can grant considerable survivability to
the **First Strike** unit.

## Acquiring First Strike

There is no known method of adding a **First Strike** ability to any unit that does not already possess it.
