**Cold Immunity** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

When a unit with **Cold Immunity** is struck by [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage") from any source, its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is temporarily raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for purposes of blocking this damage. This means that it is exceptionally difficult (though still possible) to inflict [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage") to this unit.

**Cold Immunity** is one of the more common immunities in the game. It is possessed by all [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** realm, including the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"). It is not possible to add **Cold Immunity** to a unit that does not already have it, except by turning that unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.

## Description

Extreme cold temperatures are very dangerous to most living things.
Not only is heat necessary for the survival of most creatures, but cold
itself can disable and even destroy living tissue. As a result, attacks
that drastically lower a target's temperature can cause it severe damage
or even kill it.

Naturally, creatures that are not actually alive do not have much
to fear from the cold. Their bodies are already rotting, decomposed, or
not even there anymore - and so they have no need for heat and no
problem with being chilled. Though very-extreme cold can still cause
additional damage, it is unlikely to harm the creature to any serious
degree.

## Effect

**Cold Immunity** is a passive ability that protects a unit specifically from [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage").

Whenever a [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage") attack is performed against a unit with **Cold Immunity**, the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") rating is immediately raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. Therefore, the unit can make 50 [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls, blocking on average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **15 points of Damage**
- an impressively-high amount. It is unlikely for any attack to
actually get through this much Defense, and normally results in the unit
taking no damage whatsoever.

There are currently only 2 known sources of [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage"), and both sources are spells from the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature** Realm: [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") and [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm"). [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") is completely unthreatening to units with **Cold Immunity**, and will practically never cause any damage to them due to its low [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** output. [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") can be a little more threatening, but its caster would need to spend a *hefty* amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to make the spell strong enough to threaten such a unit - and even then is extremely unlikely to cause more than a scant few [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **points of Damage**, if any!

Note that the **Cold-Immune** unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is raised *only* for the purpose of blocking damage from a [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage") attack. Once the unit has made its defense rolls against this specific attack, its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is restored to normal.

## Acquiring Cold Immunity

There is only one method in which to add **Cold Immunity** to a unit: by turning that unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.

It is not a coincidence that all of the units with default **Cold Immunity** are [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** creatures: these creatures are not alive, and thus do not react to the cold. Similarly, units that die and are brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units are also **Immune to Cold**.

Units can be turned into [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") in several different ways:

* The [Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels") spell will turn a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by [Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain") damage is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by an enemy unit with [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle and then targeted by [Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the caster of this spell.

Regardless of which method is used, the unit comes back with most of its original abilities intact - but now possesses the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property which, among other effects, also gives the unit **Cold Immunity**.

Note that the icon for **Cold Immunity** is not added to the unit's details panel. However, the presence of the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property icon is always sufficient to indicate that **Cold Immunity** is indeed in effect.
