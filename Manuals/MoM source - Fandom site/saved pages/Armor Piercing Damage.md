:   *This article is about a Damage Type called "Armor Piercing Damage". For the unit ability with a similar name, see [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing").*

**Armor Piercing Damage** (or **AP Damage** for short, and sometimes called **Lightning Damage**) is a type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") that can be delivered by various units as part of their attacks, as well as by various spells. An attack delivering **Armor Piercing Damage** halves the effectiveness of the target's armor. Thus, any attack containing **AP Damage** will inflict more damage to its target, on average.

**Armor Piercing Damage** is actually a much more complex concept than it sounds, and can be difficult to understand as a separate [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") since it inflicts *no actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** on the target* whatsoever. An attack delivering **AP Damage** must deliver it in tandem with other damage types, commonly just [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"), as these will be the components that actually hurt the target. **AP Damage** only makes it easier to inflict that damage, by reducing the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score temporarily.

**Once the attack is over, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense")**Defense **score returns to normal.**

**Armor Piercing Damage** is fairly common as a default component in the attacks of several [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") and even some [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), thanks to their innate [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") ability. Furthermore, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may be equipped with "Lightning" weapons. These will add an **AP Damage** component to one of the Hero's attacks.

Units with [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") also deliver **Armor Piercing Damage** as a component of this specific attack type. Finally, at least one lightning-base combat spell delivers an **AP Damage** component, and several others may as well (research is pending).

## Effect

**Armor Piercing Damage** is responsible for halving the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
score temporarily, making it easier for an attack to get through the
target's armor. It delivers no actual damage on its own, relying on
other components of the attack to do so. The target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** is restored once the attack is concluded.

### Defense Penalty

Whenever an attack containing an **Armor Piercing Damage** component strikes any target on the battlefield, the target's ability to defend against this attack is halved and rounded down.

In technical terms, the target may only make half as many [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls against the **AP Damage** attack, and is thus more vulnerable to the other [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") that this same attack also delivers, if any.

**For example**, lets take a target unit with [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **7 Defense**. Normally, it would make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **7 Defense Rolls** against any incoming [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), potentially blocking a fairly large amount of the incoming [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). However if the same attack includes an **AP Damage** component, that target can only make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **3 Defense rolls** against it. Thus, the target will suffer much more of the incoming [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (on average) from this enhanced attack.

The same principle would apply when using **Armor Piercing** [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), which deliver [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") and **AP Damage** together. The target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is halved by **AP Damage**, allowing the [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") (or [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage")) to get through more easily.

The [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** penalty is percentage-based: exactly 50%, rounded down. Therefore, the higher the target's original [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score, the more effective the **AP Damage** is against that target. Dropping a unit's Defense from [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5**
is a significant penalty, and can potentially make a tough opponent
more readily defeatable. On the other hand, reducing a unit's Defense
from [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **1** has very little effect - unless of course the attacker is also quite weak, in which case every little bit helps.

**The reduction in the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense")**Defense **applies only for the duration of the attack.** Therefore, as explained below, an attack *must* deliver **AP Damage** in tandem with at least some amount of physical damage in order to exploit the target's reduced [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. An attack that delivers *only* **AP Damage** would be useless: it lowers the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score, and then does nothing to exploit it before finally removing the penalty.

### No Actual Damage

**This is a crucial part to understanding how **Armor Piercing Damage** works, so please read carefully.**

**Armor Piercing Damage**
is distinct from all other damage types in that it delivers no actual
damage on its own. It is responsible solely for reducing the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rating - whereupon a [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") component needs to actually *hurt the target*. Therefore, all attacks delivering **AP Damage** must deliver it in tandem with [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") (which causes [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **physical harm** to the target) in order to exploit the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** penalty!

Thus, it should not be surprising that **Armor Piercing Damage** is always added to a unit's *existing* attacks. Most commonly, it is added directly to the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"): Such an attack would deliver the **AP Damage** first, and then inflict [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") on the target to exploit its reduced Defense.

**Armor Piercing Damage** is added in the same way to a unit's [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (if it possesses one), which would then deliver **AP Damage** followed by [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"). It is theorized, but not yet proven, that the same applies to the unit's [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** and [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks**, if any.

The "secondary" [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") delivered by the attack has to be [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") - any damage type which forces the target to make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls against it. Otherwise, the Defense reduction would not really help. For example, a hypothetical attack delivering **AP Damage** along with [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") does not benefit, since [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") forces the target to make [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls - not [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls. Since it doesn't exploit the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** penalty from **AP Damage**, the attack is not overall more effective.

These cases are very rare, resulting always from magical effects
that mess with a unit's attacks. For a good example, imagine an attacker
possessing the [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") ability whose [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** strength has been reduced to [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **0** by some spell or effect. This attacker still delivers **AP Damage** during its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), but will cause no damage to its target since it delivers 0 [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). This attack would be useless with or without **AP Damage**, of course, but illustrates a possible situation in which **AP Damage** is completely useless.

## Immunities

Naturally, **Armor Piercing Damage** has no effect on units whose [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is already [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** - such as the [Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors"), [Phantom Beast](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Beast "Phantom Beast"), any unit affected by the [Berserk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Berserk "Berserk") spell, and so forth. These units already cannot make any [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls, so halving their Defense score would be meaningless ([Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** / 2 = [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**...).

No immunity can protect the target from **AP Damage**. The effect of halving the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score always applies - even against targets possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity"), and so forth. Thus, it is effective against virtually any target in the game.

However, as explained above, **Armor Piercing Damage** does not actually cause damage to the target. It relies on a "secondary" damage component, such as [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"), to exploit the reduction in [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** and thus harm the target more severely. Whatever the other damage component (or components) is, it *may or may not* be blocked by the target's various immunities, as per the rules pertaining to that specific component.

It's also important to note that the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** penalty from **AP Damage** is applied *before* the target's immunities are triggered. Since most immunities work by raising the target's Defense score to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**, **AP Damage** has no effect whatsoever against those targets.

### Example

[Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks**, though rare, are a good example for this. These attacks normally hurt their target by inflicting [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") on it. However, when a Ranged Missile Attack is used against a target with [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity"), the immunity raises the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** - and will thus completely block all but the strongest Ranged Missile Attacks.

Now, if an **Armor Piercing Damage** component was added to the same Ranged Missile Attack, it *would* lower the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score as normal - but the target's [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") then kicks in and raises it right back to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. Thus, while **AP Damage** does work as intended against this target, its effect is immediately undone by the target's immunity being triggered.

**AP Damage** simply did not help in this case - you could actually say that it was "blocked" by the [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") simply because it is part of a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack**. For example, a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** would've gotten through [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") with no problem, so **AP Damage** on *that* attack would work as intended when directed at Missile-Immune units!

Thus, while **Armor Piercing Damage**
has no immunity that actually blocks it from taking effect, the
target's immunities (if any) can still render it useless - depending on
what other [types of damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") the **AP Damage** attack delivers (if any).

## Sources of Armor Piercing Damage

**Armor Piercing Damage** is a component in many different attacks and spells. Its most common source is the [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") ability, which adds **AP Damage** to each of a unit's attacks. [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may equip themselves with [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") possessing the "Lightning" enchantment, which adds **AP Damage** to one of the Hero's attacks.

Furthermore, some [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** spells seem to deliver **Armor Piercing Damage**. And finally, the [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Lightning Breath** ability gives a unit a separate [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") that delivers **Armor Piercing** [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage").

### Units with Armor Piercing

:   *Main article: [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing")*

The [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") ability adds an **Armor Piercing Damage** component to the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). If the unit also possesses a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), **AP Damage** is added to *that* attack as well.

It is currently unknown whether **Armor Piercing Damage** is also added to the unit's [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**s or [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack**s, for units that have these attacks. The working assumption is that it is *probably* added to them as well, but more research is needed to confirm this assumption.

The benefit of **Armor Piercing Damage**
depends greatly on the unit's existing attacks types and their
strength, with stronger attackers generally benefiting more from **AP Damage** - though the weakest [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") units are still quite powerful thanks to it. Even more importantly of course, it's usually the *target's* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score that makes the greatest difference, since it will always be *halved*.

Refer to the article on [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing#Units_with_Default_Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") for a full list of all units possessing this ability.

### Lightning Weapons

Another source of **Armor Piercing Damage** comes from weapons imbued with the "Lightning" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). This enchantment can be placed only on weapons - including [Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace"), [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"), [Bows](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"), [Wands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand"), and [Staves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff"). When the enchanted item is equipped on a Hero, it adds an **AP Damage** component to the attack associated with the item. Therefore, a [Sword](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Mace](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace") or [Axe](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") only add an **AP Damage** component to the Hero's [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), while a [Bow](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"), [Wand](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand") or [Staff](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") only add **AP Damage** to the Hero's [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

During the custom [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting") process, the "Lightning" bonus is only available if you possess at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**5 Chaos** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). Naturally, it's always possible to find a weapon containing this bonus as a reward for defeating [Encounters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter").

Note that, as explained above, this does not change the actual strength of the attack nor the [types of damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") it delivers. The only difference is that the attack will now deliver **AP Damage** to halve the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score as described above.

The Hero's attack will only deliver **AP Damage** while the Hero is holding the enchanted item. If the item is removed or lost, the [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s attack no longer delivers **AP Damage**.

### Lightning-based Spells

There are three different spells from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm** and one from the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature Realm** which deliver **Armor Piercing Damage**. They are the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")[Lightning Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Bolt "Lightning Bolt"), [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")[Warp Lightning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Lightning "Warp Lightning"), [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")[Chaos Rift](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Rift "Chaos Rift") and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")[Call Lightning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Call_Lightning "Call Lightning") spells. All four spells deliver this damage in tandem with [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") (which is responsible for inflicting the actual harm on the target).

The first spell, [Lightning Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Bolt "Lightning Bolt"), is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10**, it will unleash a single attack delivering 5 [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") along with **AP Damage** at the selected target. This spell's [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") is adjustable: up to [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **40 extra Mana** may be invested into it, with each [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** increasing the amount of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") the spell delivers by 1 point. The spell's **AP Damage** component is unaffected by this investment.

[Warp Lightning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Lightning "Warp Lightning") is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **35**, it will strike a target with 10 successive [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") attacks of decreasing strength, starting with a strength [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **10** attack. Each of the 10 attacks delivers **AP Damage**. Note that [Warp Lightning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Lightning "Warp Lightning") is also one of the possible effects of the [Call Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Call_Chaos "Call Chaos") spell.

[Chaos Rift](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Rift "Chaos Rift") is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Town Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Curse "Town Curse") that may be cast on an enemy town. While in effect, it will direct 5 lightning bolts each turn, with each bolt delivering 8 [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") points, at random targets in the town's garrison stack (if any). It is assumed that these bolts deliver **AP Damage**, but is yet to be conclusively proven.

Finally, [Call Lightning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Call_Lightning "Call Lightning") is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment")
that will deliver between 3 and 5 lightning bolts each combat round, at
random enemy targets on the battlefield. Each bolt delivers 8 [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") points. These bolts also deliver **AP Damage**.

### Lightning Breath

:   *Main article: [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath")*

Only one unit in the game - the [Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake") - possesses an ability called [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath"). This ability allows the [Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake") to make a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") whenever it voluntarily engages any enemy unit in Melee combat. This Breath Attack delivers 20 points of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"), in tandem with an **Armor Piercing Damage** component.

This attack can be very devastating, even to heavily-armored targets. This makes the [Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake") extremely useful in taking out powerful enemies. The only units that can avoid massive damage from this [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") attack are those possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), as it renders the **AP Damage** component useless, and raises the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** at the same time.
