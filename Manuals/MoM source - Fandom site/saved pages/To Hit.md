**Chance To Hit** (often called "To Hit", for simplicity) is a game concept used in *Master of Magic* to determine the accuracy of all [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") and [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") attacks. In essence, it helps determine how much damage such an attack would inflict on its target.

The attacker, be it a spell or a unit, has a certain **To Hit** score which is a primary component in determining how many [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **points of Damage**
will be inflicted on the target. The process of inflicting that damage
involves one or more random rolls, meaning that an attacker's **To Hit** score only represents statistical probabilities of inflicting [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**.

During a [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") or [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") attack, the attacker is required to make a number of **To Hit** rolls, which determine how many hits are registered against the target. The defender may or may not be allowed to make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls in order to deflect some or all of these registered hits. Each hit that is not deflected will cause [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 point of Damage** on the target.

A unit's **To Hit** score is represented on this wiki with an unofficial icon that looks like a golden cross-hairs: [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit"). This icon is based on the icon used to represent **To Hit** bonuses in the game.

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
values are measured on this wiki in increments of 10%, which correlates
with the statistical chance they represent. The default [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
score of any unit or spell is 30%, though units often have bonuses or
penalties that increase or decrease this value. The higher the value,
the better chance that unit or spell has to inflict more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** on this target, though it is only one of many factors involved in determining damage output.

## Concept

The **Chance To Hit** of an attack is a measure of its accuracy.
Whether it represents how well a unit uses its weapons, or how accurate a
spell is in hitting its designated target, the **To Hit** value has similar implications regardless of the source of the attack.

The use of **Chance To Hit** in *Master of Magic* adds an important aspect of randomness to [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") and [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") attacks (which are usually the most common [types of damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type")
in the game). It means that the results of such an attack depend not
only on the attacker's strength or the defender's properties, but also
on a powerful random element that makes luck an important factor.

As a result, even a powerful attacker may, occasionally, inflict
little or no damage - or alternatively inflict more damage than average -
on the same target. Note also that this is not the only random factor
involved in such attacks, since the defender's [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls, which are quite similar to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls, have a similar importance.

## The Mechanism

The **To Hit** value comes into play whenever a [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") or [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") attack is performed by any unit or spell against any target. **To Hit** is used as a percentage chance of inflicting "Registered Hits" on said target, which will later be translated into actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to that target, if they can get through the target's defenses.

### Registered Hits

Whenever a [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") or [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") attack is performed, against any target, the attack's source must make a series of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
rolls in order to determine how effective the attack was. The number of
rolls made differs based on the source of the attack, as does the
chance of success with each roll.

The number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls is equal to the strength of the attack. For example, a unit with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3 Melee Attack strength** which makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") on any target will make 3 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls. Spells work similarly, so a spell with a strength of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **5** will make 5 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against its target. Note that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** may make more rolls against the target, since each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the unit attacks the target simultaneously.

Each roll results in a value between 1 and 100. This is then compared to the attacker's current [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score (with modifiers taken into account) in order to determine whether that roll is successful. If the roll is *equal to or lower than* the attacker's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score, then that roll was successful.

After making all the necessary rolls, the game tallies up all
successful rolls. This is the number of "Registered Hits" that the
attack managed to inflict on its target. At this point, the target often
(but not always) gets to make a set of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls to reduce the number of "Registered Hits". After this, any Registered Hits remaining are immediately translated into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **points of Damage** and inflicted on the target. Each Registered Hit equals [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 point of Damage**.

The maximum possible number of Registered Hits on the target is
therefore exactly equal to the strength of the attack. Returning to the
example above, a unit with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** may make anywhere between 0 to 3 Registered Hits on its target, depending on how many [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls were successful. If the unit's **To Hit** score is [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100%**,
it will always inflict 3 Registered Hits, since it will automatically
succeed in each and every roll. Conversely, if the unit's **To Hit** score is [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **0%**,
the unit automatically fails each roll and therefore inflicts 0
Registered Hits. Values in between give the unit a chance to succeed or
fail in each roll, and thus will inflict differing numbers of Registered
Hits each time it attacks, in this case anywhere between 0 and 3.

This allows us to estimate the ***average*** number of
Registered Hits that a unit or spell will inflict on its target, which
can be done by multiplying the unit/spell's attack strength by its [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score. For example, if the attack has a strength of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3**, and a **To Hit** score of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **60%**, then it will (on average) inflict [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** \* [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **60%** = 1.8 Registered Hits on its target. Note that this is *before* the target gets to try to [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **deflect**
those Registered Hits, so the final result will often be very
different. Nonetheless, comparing the attacker's statistical average to
the defender's blocking ability gives a good indication of how much
damage can be expected.

For a run-down of the entire process, including important details and caveats, please read the articles on [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") and [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage").

### Chance To Hit Score

By default, every unit and direct-damage spell has a **To Hit** score of exactly [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%**.

For units, a variety of special effects (often bestowed by innate properties or [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment")) may alter this value up or down as appropriate. Some units, for example [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experienced** [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), may reach [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100% To Hit** (or higher!). In certain situations, a unit may drop to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **10%**, but no lower than that.

A unit's actual [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score is not displayed anywhere. However, modifiers to a unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score *are* usually displayed in the unit's details panel - specifically, in the unit's [Abilities List](#The_Abilities_List)
at the bottom of that panel. The modifier is displayed as a number next
to an icon of a yellow cross-hair on a golden background:

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit")

Modifiers are measured in-game in increments or decrements of 1.
For example, a unit may have a modifier of +1, +2, +3 or -1, -2, -3 and
so on, indicating relative adjustment of the unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score above or below the default value as appropriate. Note however that on this wiki, [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** is measured in increments of 10%, so a +1 bonus in game is translated into a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** bonus on this wiki.

When no such icon appears in the game, it means that the unit is at the default **To Hit** score, which is exactly [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%**.

Again, all direct-damage spells and effects have exactly [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30% To Hit**, and their accuracy cannot be altered in any way.

**Note:**  On this wiki, **To Hit** is classified as one of the primary unit attributes - i.e. the same as [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack Strength**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**, [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** and so forth. It is not considered a [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") despite the fact that the indicator appears in the unit's Abilites List.

#### Chance To Hit > 100%

:   It is sometimes possible, especially with highly-[Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experienced** [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wielding very powerful [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), for a unit to achieve a **To Hit** score higher than [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100%**.

:   Such a score has no practical benefit over a **To Hit** score of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100%**, since at 100% the unit is already guaranteed to succeed in any [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** roll it makes.

:   However, having more than [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100% To Hit** can serve as a "buffer" to protect the unit against ill-effects that might lower its **To Hit** score. If the unit's **To Hit**
    score is high enough, the ill-effect would not be able to drop it below
    100%, meaning that the unit's combat performance is not adversely
    affected.

:   Even more importantly, units using a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attack** will often suffer [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties when attacking distant targets. With a **To Hit** score above [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100%**, such a unit can ignore distance penalties altogether, remaining just as effective when firing clear across the battlefield.

:   For example, imagine [Alorra the Elven Archer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alorra_the_Elven_Archer "Alorra the Elven Archer"). When firing across the battlefield, **Alorra** will always suffer a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-30% To Hit** penalty due to the distance involved, making her shots less effective. However, when she reaches a very high [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level** and is equipped with a powerful [Bow](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"), she can achieve a **To Hit** score of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **130%**. This means that even when suffering those distance penalties, her score is reduced only to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100%**, allowing her to be just as effective as she would be against a nearby target.

## To Hit Modifiers

As mentioned above, units in the game often enjoy (or suffer from) various modifiers that alter their **To Hit** score from its default value of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%**.

Raising a unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
value means that the unit will inflict, on average, a greater portion
of its attack strength on the enemy, while lowering the value means that
the unit will struggle to inflict as much damage as it normally would.
Therefore, beneficial spells and effects are geared towards raising the
value, while maledictions and [Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Curse "Curse") often lower it.

When a unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
score has been modified from the default, an icon will appear in its
Abilities List to indicate this. To view this, right-click the unit and
examine the list of abilities and modifiers in the bottom half of the
window that pops up for you.

Note that modifiers do not always appear in the unit's details
window. They only appear if they apply equally to each of the unit's [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type").

The most common sources for [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** modifiers on a unit include the following:

* Innate unit bonuses that are permanent and immutable.
* Innate [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") that this unit or its target possess by default.
* [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") that improve with [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level").
* [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") of a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") or [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit").
* [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") and [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") affecting the unit or its target.
* [Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") and [Global Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") currently in effect.
* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical weapons** (or better) currently held by a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit").
* [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") with certain [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") currently held by a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

Furthermore, when using a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attack**, [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties may be applied based on the distance to the target.

The icon in the unit's Details Panel will indicate only the *total* modifier to the unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
score, and does not list each and every effect. This may sometimes make
it harder to determine why the unit is enjoying/suffering a certain
amount of modification. Careful study of the unit's properties and
external effects may be necessary to determine this.

### Innate Unit Bonuses

A large number of units, primarily [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature"), enjoy an innate bonus to their [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score, which represents superior or super-natural accuracy. In addition, some [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") possess a similar innate bonus thanks to [Racial](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Race "Race") benefits.

Such a unit will always have a certain base modifier to its **To Hit** score, above the default [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%**,
regardless of what else affects it. The innate modifier cannot be
removed, and will never be missing for any reason, regardless of how the
unit was brought into the game.

Most units of this kind enjoy only a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus. A scant few, very powerful units indeed, enjoy an innate bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20% To Hit** or even [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+30% To Hit**, making them incredibly dangerous.

* Most [High Elven](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Elves "High Elves") [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") except the [Catapult](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult") and [Trireme](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Trireme "Trireme") racial [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** (for a base [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **40% To Hit**).
* [Elven Lords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elven_Lords "Elven Lords") and [Minotaurs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Minotaurs "Minotaurs") enjoy a bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20%** (for a base [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **50% To Hit**).
* Most [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") possess a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit Bonus** (for a base [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **40% To Hit**).
* Most [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") possess a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20% To Hit Bonus** (for a base [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **50% To Hit**).
* Most [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") possess a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+30% To Hit Bonus** (for a base [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **60% To Hit**).

These innate bonuses apply to all [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") attacks performed by the unit, including its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") and [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") (if any of these are available, of course).

### Innate Unit Abilities

Two [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") in the game will alter the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score of an attacker or defender as appropriate. These are [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") and [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility").
A few units possess these abilities by default, meaning that the
ability is permanent and immutable, and cannot be added or removed
throughout the unit's existence.

The [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") ability, among its other effects, gives the unit a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit Bonus** in each and every [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") attack it makes. This may seem like a small amount, but can have a very strong impact for many of the units possessing it.

The [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") ability works somewhat differently. Instead of augmenting the unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score, it actually *decreases* the **To Hit** score of any opponent that attempts to attack it, by [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%**. This makes the unit harder to kill. Note, however, that opponents possessing [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") or affected by [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") will not suffer this penalty at all.

* All [Halfling](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halflings "Halflings") [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), except the [Catapult](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult"), possess the [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") ability by default.
* The vast majority of [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may pick [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") as one of their [Random Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities") at the start of the game.
* The [Dark Elven](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_Elves "Dark Elves") [Nightblades](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nightblades "Nightblades") possess [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") by default.
* The [Air Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Elemental "Air Elemental") and [Night Stalker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Night_Stalker "Night Stalker") possess [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") by default.
* [Shin Bo the Ninja](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shin_Bo_the_Ninja "Shin Bo the Ninja") possesses [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") by default.

**Note:**  [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility")
can also be added to a unit that does not possess it by default, though
the use of one of two different spells. This is described below.

### Hero Abilities

Several [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") in the game possess the [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") [Hero Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Ability "Hero Ability"). This ability provides a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+5% To Hit** bonus for each of the Hero's current [Experience Levels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level"). Therefore, a [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") Hero with [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **8 Experience Levels**, for example, receives a bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+40% To Hit**.

[Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") is a fairly uncommon [Hero Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Ability "Hero Ability"), possessed by default only by a few Heroes. However, due to the process of selecting [Random Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities"), a single game may feature more or fewer Heroes with this ability.

Even rarer than this is the [Super](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Super_Ability "Super Ability") [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") ability. This works the same as the "regular" ability, except it bestows a bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+7.5% To Hit** per [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level**.

Note that [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster")'s bonuses are smaller than [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** per level. Since the total bonus is rounded down to the nearest number divisible by 10 (since [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
is normally measured only in increments of 10%) this means that the
Hero will not enjoy an actual benefit with each level. For example, a [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") Hero with [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **9 Experience Levels** has a bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **5%** \* [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **9** = [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+45%**, which is immediately rounded down to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+40%** - the same bonus he/she would have at [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level 8**. No bonus was gained, since the new total is not divisible by 10%.

* [Alorra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alorra "Alorra"), [Mortu](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mortu "Mortu"), [Shalla](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shalla "Shalla"), [Shin Bo](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shin_Bo "Shin Bo"), [Shuri](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shuri "Shuri") and [Tumu](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tumu "Tumu") all possess [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") by default.
* All of the above may randomly acquire [Super](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Super_Ability "Super Ability") [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") as one of their [Random Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities").
* Over a dozen other [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may randomly acquire [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") or even [Super](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Super_Ability "Super Ability") [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") as one of their [Random Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities").

Note that the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus from [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") and [Super Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Super_Ability "Super Ability") applies equally to the [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") and [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), whichever are available.

### Experience Level

A unit's current [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") may grant it several different bonuses to its basic combat properties, and one of these bonuses is a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus that grows as the unit's level increases. Only [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") and [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") accumulate [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**.

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses are given only at the higher [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Levels**.
By the time a unit reaches such a level, it is already significantly
more dangerous than it was when first created, and these bonuses can
serve to turn even a very weak unit into a rather dangerous one.

[Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") usually receive a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus once they have reached [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite** level. With the [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort") or [Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade") spell, [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") can reach [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Ultra-Elite** level to gain [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20% To Hit**. With both [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") and [Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade") in effect, [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") may reach [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Champion** level, gaining the maximum bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+30% To Hit**.

[Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") begin receiving [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses at [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Captain** level, and the bonus will grow every 3 levels after that. In other words, they receive [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** at [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Captain** level, [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20% To Hit** at [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Lord** level, and [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+30% To Hit** at [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Demi-God** level. Note of course that [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may also gain bonuses from the [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") ability at various levels, as explained earlier. These are cumulative with the regular bonuses from [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level**.

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses from [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") apply equally to the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") and [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), whichever are available.

### Unit Enchantments and Curses

A good way to influence a unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score artificially during the game is to cast certain [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") or [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") upon it (for a beneficial or detrimental effect, respectively).

There are three different spells of this category that affect [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**,
and each does so in a completely different fashion. Nonetheless, they
can have a crucial impact on a unit's combat performance as a result.

In all cases, however, the modifier applies only while the spell
remains in effect. Enchantments and Curses can be dispelled by enemies
or even cancelled by the caster himself, so they are not truly
permanent.

[Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon"), a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") from the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm**, will equip the targeted [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons**. This has several effects, one of which is a flat [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus, which applies to the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") and [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") (if any are available), but not to its [Magical Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Ranged_Attack "Magical Ranged Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") or [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"). Furthermore, note that the bonus from [Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon") is actually *cumulative* with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons** acquired in other ways, so a unit possessing both will have [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20%**.

[Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)"), a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") from the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery Realm**, will add the [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility")
property to the targeted unit. This has many important effects; but as
described earlier in this article, it will give any opponent that
attacks this unit a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** penalty. Opponents possessing [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") or [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") ignore this effect.

[Vertigo](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vertigo "Vertigo"), an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Unit Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") from the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery Realm**, is targeted at enemy units to make them *less* effective in combat. The affected unit receives a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20% To Hit** penalty, along with [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **-1 Defense**. Note that the targeted unit may [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** the spell, and thus suffer no penalty at all, and that [Vertigo](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vertigo "Vertigo") lasts only until the end of combat.

### Combat and Global Enchantments

[Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") and [Global Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") are also capable of affecting units' [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** scores. However, unlike [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") and [Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"), they do so in a blanket fashion - affecting a large number of units simultaneously. This makes [Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") and [Global Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") extremely cost-effective, and potentially quite dangerous.

Again, these spells will apply a temporary modifier to the unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** value, which persists only as long as the spell remains in effect. For [Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment"), this is until the end of the current battle or until the enchantment is dispelled. [Global Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") have no set duration, and can either be dispelled by the enemy or cancelled by their own caster.

[Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade"), a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") from the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm**, has been discussed earlier in this article. When it is in effect, every friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") receives one bonus [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level**. As a result, units require fewer [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience Points** to reach [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Level** level, whereupon they receive their first **To Hit** bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%**. [Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade") also allows units to reach [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Ultra-Elite** Level for [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20% To Hit**. If the wizard also possesses the [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), units reach these levels even earlier, and can reach the maximum level of [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Champion** for [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+30% To Hit**.

[Holy Arms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Arms "Holy Arms") is another important [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") from the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm**. While it is in effect, every friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") enjoys the same effects bestowed by [Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon") (discussed earlier), which give the unit a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapon**. One of the effects is a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus to all the affected units, which is cumulative with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons** acquired through other means. As with [Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon"), this bonus applies *only* to the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") and [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack"), but not to its [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") or [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack").

[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer") and [High Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer") are two non-stacking [Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") from the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm**, which provide a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus to all friendly units on the battlefield, among other bonuses. If both are in effect, units still receive only [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%**. [Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer") is an [Uncommon Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell"), while [High Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer") is a [Very Rare Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell"), with the latter providing much better ancillary bonuses than the former.

[Warp Reality](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Reality "Warp Reality"), a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**, has a more tactically-complicated effect. It will apply a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20% To Hit** penalty on *all* units on the battlefield, friend or foe, that are not associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. This normally applies to all [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), and non-[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creatures "Fantastic Creatures").

Finally, [Mass Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Invisibility "Mass Invisibility") is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") from the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery Realm**, which applies the same effect as the [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)") spell on all friendly units on the battlefield. As a consequence, any enemy unit attacking a friendly unit receives a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** penalty, unless that enemy unit possesses [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") or [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight").

### Magical Weapons

[Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") produced in a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") containing an [Alchemists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild") are always created with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons**, or better. One of the effects of these weapons is to give the unit a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus.

The modifier from [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons**
is not displayed in the unit's Details Panel, because it does not apply
to all of the unit's attacks. It only applies to the following attack
types:

* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**
* [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack**
* [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attack**

The bonus does not apply to the following attacks, if the unit even has them at all:

* [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack**
* [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**
* [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath Attack**
* [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Lightning Breath Attack**

Note that although [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") carrying [Magical Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") enjoy some of the benefits of a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit")'s [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons**, they do not receive a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus for that weapon.

### Magical Items

[Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")
may be equipped with a variety of items that can boost their combat
performance in a myriad of different ways. Some items can be imbued with
a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus of a certain magnitude, which is then applied to the [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") holding that item.

Only certain types of [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can receive a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus, including all weapon types as well as [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry"). Armor may not have any [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses imbued into it, and no armor with such a bonus will ever be found in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure") piles.

[Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") with a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus have an important caveat: they will only apply that bonus to *some* of the Hero's attacks, but not all of them:

* The [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus on a [Staff](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") [Staff](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") or [Wand](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand") [Wand](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand") only applies to the Hero's [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attacks**.

* The [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus on a [Bow](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow") [Bow](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow") only applies to the Hero's [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks**.

* The [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus on a [Sword](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword") [Sword](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword") or [Mace](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace") [Mace](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace") only applies to the Hero's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attacks**.

* The [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus on an [Axe](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") [Axe](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") only applies to the Hero's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attacks**. Even though attack strength bonuses *do* apply to the Hero's [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** as well, the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses do not.

* The [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus on a piece of [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") only applies to the Hero's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attacks** and [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (of any kind), but not to [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** or [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath** attacks.

The bonus remains in effect as long as the Hero is wearing the item.
If the item is removed, the bonus is lost. The bonus cannot be removed
in any other way.

#### Possible Item To-Hit Bonuses

:   During [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting"), several important factors determine which [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses, if any, may be placed on the item. This includes the spell being used to craft the item (be it [Enchant Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchant_Item "Enchant Item") or [Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact")), the type of item being created, and the amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** the caster wishes to spend.

:   Items created with [Enchant Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchant_Item "Enchant Item") cannot contain any [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses whatsoever, regardless of type. Only items created with [Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact") may contain such bonuses at all.

:   [Wands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand") may only contain a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus. [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") and [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") may be imbued with either [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** or [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20%**. [Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace"), [Bows](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow") and [Staves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") may be imbued with anywhere up to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+30% To Hit**.

:   For weapons, each [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** bonus increases the weapon's value (and thus the amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** required to create it) by [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **400**, making [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** one of the most expensive bonuses that can be placed on any item. For [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") this is even more expensive, at a rate of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **800** per [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%**. A [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") item with nothing but a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20% To Hit** bonus costs a whopping [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1650 Mana**!

### Ranged Penalties

When a unit attacks using its [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attack** (but not when using a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack**), it may suffer a certain [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalty based on the distance to the target.

The penalties are as follows:

| Range | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** Penalty |
| --- | --- |
| 1 Tile (adjacent)\* | No Penalty |
| 2 Tiles | No Penalty |
| 3,4 or 5 Tiles | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** Penalty |
| 6,7 or 8 Tiles | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20%** Penalty |
| Etcetera | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-30%** Penalty |

*\* "Adjacent" means the attacker and the target are right next to each other (including diagonals).*

*Remember of course that a unit's final **To Hit** score can never drop below [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **10%**, after* all *modifiers have been taken into account.*

This means that units possessing a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
that's subjected to such penalties will have a strong incentive to
close the distance to its target before using that attack. Only powerful
ranged attackers can afford to pelt the enemy from far away.

Also note that the presence of the [Long Range](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Long_Range "Long Range") ability on a unit puts an absolute cap on these penalties. Such a unit will not suffer more than [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** from distance to its target, so a shot from 8 tiles away or even more is just as effective as a shot from 3 tiles away.

### Counterattack Penalty

If a unit undergoes more than two attacks within one round, then its counterattacks receive a temporary **To Hit** penalty. It's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** for the third and fourth attack, [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20%** for the fifth and sixth attack, and so on. Note that this penalty will not affect the unit, if it initiates the attack.

**Example:**

:   A [Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra") is attacked by 2 units of [Gnoll Bowmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gnoll_Bowmen "Gnoll Bowmen") and 4 units of [Wolf Riders](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_Riders "Wolf Riders").
    The higher speed of the Wolf Riders will be used to initiate the attack
    twice and simultaneously in one round. This has not only the advantage
    that the Hydra can't use its [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath** attack, its counterattacks will also become weaker against the later Wolf Riders.

:   A Hydra has an innate [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10 % To Hit** bonus which means that each [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icon will usually have a **40 %** chance of working. The 2 units of Gnoll Bowmen will attack first with their [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Attack**.
    This will unlikely cause any damage. However, it will help to reduce
    the damage inflicted to the Wolf Riders: After these 2 incoming attacks,
    the Hydra will already suffer a temporary [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%**
    penalty to its upcoming counterattacks in this counterattack round.
    Now, the first unit of Wolf Riders will initiate the attack twice: In
    both counterattacks, the Hydra's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons will only have a **30 %**
    chance of working thanks to the previous attacks of the Bowmen. After
    these 2 further incoming attacks, the Hydra will suffer a temporary [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20%** penalty to its upcoming counterattacks.

:   Now, the second unit of Wolf Riders will initiate the attack twice: This time, the Hydra's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons will only have a **20 %** chance of working in both counterattacks, and in the aftermath the temporary To Hit penalty is increased to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-30%**. Now, the third unit of Wolf Riders will initiate the attack twice: The Hydra's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons will only have a **10 %**
    chance of working in these 2 counterattacks. Since the success chance
    of a roll cannot fall below 10 %, each further temporary To Hit
    penalty will basically have no effect: If the fourth unit of Wolf Riders
    attack, the Hydra's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons will still have a **10 %**
    chance of working. If the Hydra should survive all these attacks, then
    the temporary To Hit penalties are removed at the start of the Hydra's
    turn. So, each of its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Sword** icons (and [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath** Breath icons) will have the usual **40 %** success chance again.

**Note:**  [Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur") [Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur") doesn't affect [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**. Instead it makes 10% of otherwise successful enemy hits barely miss. This effect is weaker than a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** penalty. [Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon") [Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon") doesn't affect [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** either. Instead it reduces the opponent's ability [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** by -10%.

**Note:**  The [unofficial 1.40 Insecticide patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") makes some changes regarding To Hit effects:

* Magical Weapon ([Alchemy retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemy_(Retort) "Alchemy (Retort)") or [Alchemists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild")) and [Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon") increase **To Hit** of [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** attacks by [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** each.
* [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") and [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") increase **To Hit** of [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** attacks.
* [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") increases **To Hit** of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath** attacks.
* [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") and ([High](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer")) [Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer") will **not** decrease enemy's **To Hit** by [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** anymore in melee combat.

This makes Halfling units less valuable in v1.40. Opposite is true for Barbarian units and heroes with [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") attacks. These changes are also present in the [unofficial 1.50 patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").
