**Fire Damage** is a type of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") that is delivered by several different units, spells and spell effects, particularly those belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**.

**Fire Damage** is similar to basic [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") types (such as [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")) in the way it harms the target: The attacker must make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine how many times it has hit the target, and then the target must make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to determine how many hits it managed to block. All unblocked hits are then translated into simple [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** which is applied to the target, potentially killing one or more of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**.

**Fire Damage** is considered a sub-type of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"). This means that it is almost entirely ineffective against targets possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). On top of this, **Fire Damage** is almost always useless against targets possessing [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity").

## Effect

**Fire Damage** behaves the same way as [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"),
except it may trigger two different kinds of immunities possessed by
the target (if any), making it ineffective against such a target.

### To Hit and Defense Rolls

Like other types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), **Fire Damage** attempts to inflict actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** on the target. To do so, the attacker must make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine how successful the attack was, and the target may make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to determine how much damage it managed to block.

The attacker makes a number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls equal to the strength of the **Fire Damage** attack. For example, an [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") spell attacks with a strength of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **6**, so it makes 6 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls. Each of these rolls results in a number between 1 and 100. For each roll that comes up *equal to or lower than* 30, the game registers one hit against the target.

The target then makes a number of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls equal to its current [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. Each roll results in a number between 1 and 100. For each roll that comes up *equal to or lower than* the target's current [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** score (which is usually [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30** for most units), the game reduces the number of hits against the target by 1.

At the end of this process, the game tallies up the number of
unblocked hits (equal to the number of registered hits minus the number
of blocked hits). It then applies [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to the target accordingly - [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** per unblocked hit.

As with all other types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), if the target has lost one of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** due to this damage it may make another set of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to try and block more damage. The process only ends once all hits have been translated into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** or have been blocked by these Defense rolls.

For a more thorough explanation of the process, see [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage").

## Immunities

**Fire Damage** is a subset of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"). This means that whenever the target of a **Fire Damage** attack possesses [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") or [Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness") , that target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is raised to a whopping [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**, for purposes of blocking the **Fire Damage** component of the attack.

Thus, when the target possesses this ability, the attack needs to
be exceptionally powerful - otherwise it would have no chance of
busting through [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. For example, the [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") spell *can* be this powerful, assuming a lot of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** has been invested into it. Most other **Fire Damage** attacks, however, have virtually no chance to affect such a target.

**Fire Damage** is different from other [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") types in that it can also trigger *another* immunity called [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"). In other words, if the target possesses [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"), its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is also raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** whenever struck by a **Fire Damage** attack. Again, this makes the target almost entirely impervious to any but the strongest spells or attacks.

If the target possesses both [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") and [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"), it still gets only [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** - but again this is usually enough to prevent all incoming **Fire Damage** anyway.

## Fire Damage vs. Immolation Damage

Several fire-based effects do not actually deliver **Fire Damage**. Instead they deliver a damage type called [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage"), which (depending on circumstance) can behave exactly like **Fire Damage** or completely different from it.

When [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") is used against a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**, it behaves exactly like **Fire Damage**. The process is explained above in this article, and works the same for both damage types.

However when used against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") is significantly more potent than **Fire Damage** due to the way it is processed. Instead of making a single set of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against the target, and allowing the target to make a single set of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") will actually strike *each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit independently of the others*.
Each target figure also makes its own attempts to block the hits
inflicted directed at itself. Only after all rolls have been completed
is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** applied to the target unit as a whole.

The end result is that against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") can inflict *far* more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** than would a **Fire Damage** attack of similar strength. This is why a [Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball") spell (delivering [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage")) is often much stronger against such units than the cheaper [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") spell (delivering **Fire Damage**). Against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure units**, [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") is much more economic, causing just as much harm for a much-lower price.

Nonetheless, in all cases the [immunities](#Immunities) that apply against **Fire Damage** work the same way against [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage").

For a thorough explanation, read the [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") article.

## Sources of Fire Damage

**Fire Damage** is a fairly common [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), and comes from exactly two common sources: [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, which several different units possess, and fire-based spells which all belong to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**.

### Units with Fire Breath

[Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath** is a fairly-common unit ability, possessed by no fewer than 12 different units. Many of these are [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") from the [Draconian](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Draconian "Draconian") race, though the list also contains one [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

When a unit possessing the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") ability makes a voluntary [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against any opponent, it will first unleash a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") delivering some amount of **Fire Damage**. In fact, each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** within the attacking unit makes a separate [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), so the more figures the unit contains, the more **Fire Damage** it delivers overall.

[Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack")
are executed at the very beginning of the Melee combat process, before
any other attack or damage is delivered by either of the combatants.
Therefore, the more enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
killed in this attack, the fewer remain to deliver retaliatory damage
later in the combat process. This means that successful use of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath** can reduce retaliatory damage to the attacker - a sort of preemptive strike.

On the other hand, [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") do not work when the unit is [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). It must initiate the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") voluntarily in order to be able to use its [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack").

For each unit possessing it, the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath")
ability has a different strength. This is indicated next to the ability
icon in the unit's details panel. This indicates the amount of **Fire Damage** delivered by the attack, per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**. [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") can grow in strength due to many factors and magical effects, including when the unit gains [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Levels**.

For a complete list of all units possessing this ability, as well as the attack strength of each unit, see [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath").

### Fire Bolt

[Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. For a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5**, this spell will deliver 5 **Fire Damage** points at a selected enemy target on the battlefield.

At casting time, the [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") spell can be infused with additional [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** in order to make it stronger. With every extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** invested into the spell, it will deliver an extra 1 **Fire Damage**.

The spell can take up to [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **20 extra Mana**, meaning that its strength can be increased up to [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **25** (i.e. delivering 25 **Fire Damage** points). Such a strong attack is highly likely to kill or at least heavily-injure a powerful [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**.

Of course it is always important to remember that **Fire Damage** is much more effective against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure units**, due to the way that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** defend themselves. That is why a [Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball") spell, though costing much more [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** but delivering [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage") is often preferable to [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") when the target contains [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multiple Figures**.

This leaves [Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt") useful primarily against weaker, or at least less-armored [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure units**, or when sufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to cast one of these other spells is not available.

### Fire Storm

[Fire Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Storm "Fire Storm") is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. For a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **250**, this spell will strike each and every enemy unit within a given overland map tile with an attack delivering 8 **Fire Damage** points.

This spell is particularly useful against large enemy armies, since the more units present the more **Fire Damage** is delivered overall. However, an attack of only [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **8**
is relatively weak, and is unlikely to kill any targets. It does,
however, injure the enemy army to some extent, and thus is often used to
weaken an enemy army prior to actual battle.

### Meteor Storm

[Meteor Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meteor_Storm "Meteor Storm") is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. It has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **900**, and requires an extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** to be paid each turn for its [maintenance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"), otherwise the spell dissipates.

One effect of the [Meteor Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meteor_Storm "Meteor Storm") is to strike each and every unit on the map (except units garrisoned inside [Towns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town")) with 4 **Fire Damage**
points. It will do this at the start of each and every overland turn,
so the longer this spell remains in effect, the more units it can
potentially kill off. 4 **Fire Damage** is not much, but a unit repeatedly stricken by this damage may eventually be completely destroyed. If kept going long enough, [Meteor Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meteor_Storm "Meteor Storm") can literally clean the map from units - again except those that remain inside [Towns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") and of course those that have [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") or [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity").
