**Hit Points** measure a unit's remaining capacity for taking [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. *Master of Magic*
does not actually track this attribute. Instead, it is a calculated
value used mainly to inform the player about the status of units (both
own and enemy). The game records only [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** taken, which is essentially the inverse of this statistic.

The term **Hit Points** is often confusingly used, even by the game's own documentation, to refer to a unit's *[Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure*** value, also covered in this article. However, while **Hit Points** generally describe a unit's *current* state, [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** represent each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s *maximum* capacity for surviving [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. This serves as the basis for most procedures that involve dealing or healing [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

## Concept

The use of **Hit Points**
originates from classic role-playing games, particularly Dungeons &
Dragons. However, the concept has been adopted with multiple other
names, such as "health", or "life", and has appeared even before. It
represents both an object's or creature's structural or physiological
integrity, and its ability to withstand loss thereof. It typically goes
hand in hand with a system capable of rationing damage in a way that
some attacks are lethal, but most are not. These also often incorporate
attributes that enable entities to reduce or avoid damage from certain
sources, such as armor or magic resistance.

## Effect

[Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** and **Hit Points**, while aspects of the same concept, have different practical effects. However, they are both represented by [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Bright Heart** icons, both in the game, and on the wiki. Since [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** form the basis of calculating **Hit Points**, they are examined here first.

## Hits Per Figure

Units in *Master of Magic* are comprised of individual entities called [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. Some contain only a single one, while others can have up to nine. [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**, or more precisely, "[Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**", is one of their base attributes. It shows how many [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") it takes to slay or destroy an individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit. For [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** units, it naturally also equals their total health, or maximum **Hit Points**.

[Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** are shown in the *unit statistics window* as a row of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Heart**
icons, equal in number to the attribute. When the unit is first
recruited or summoned, and when it is at full health, all of the icons
are bright. However, if the unit suffers Damage Points, some of these
icons can get [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **darkened** out. This indicates what is often called the "[top figure damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Top_Figure_Damage "Damage Points")". The overall amount of icons remains the same however, still showing the amount of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**.

### Losing and Regaining Figures

When a unit suffers [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** equalling its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, it will lose one of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. This usually destroys [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** units outright, but [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** ones have an advantage here, as they can remain operational as long as they have at least one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** still standing. However, their offensive potential progressively diminishes as they lose [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, which happens at even intervals of suffering [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") matching their [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**. Similarly, as these units heal, whether naturally or by magical means, they also regain [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** as soon as their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** becomes lower than one of the tresholds indicated by their [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

#### Example

:   A [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3 Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, and a maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4**. Suffering [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3 Damage Points**, equal to their [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**, will slay one cavalryman. If another [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** are inflicted on them, for a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **6**, another one will fall. Then the third one at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **9**, until finally the unit is destroyed at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **12**. However, if the last [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** survives with only [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **11**, and then starts healing up, then a second [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** is restored at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **8**, a third at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5**, and the last one at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2**. Both losing and regaining the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** happens at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** point intervals, equal to the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value.

### Role in Damage Resolution

[Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** play a role in resolving most [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"). During [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks, defending units are allowed to perform additional [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") every time they log an amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** equal to their [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**. Looking at the [Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cavalry "Cavalry") from above, if a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against them scores 5 "successful hits", but they only manage to block [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**, then they can make another roll after recording [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** of the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4** points. This simulates the idea that each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** has its own [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Armor**
and other mitigating factors, so if an attack is strong enough to
affect more than one of them, then they can all defend against it.
Although, this implementation may seem counter-intuitive in that it
ignores "top figure damage" when defending against attacks made by enemy
units. That is, these attacks are always assumed to target healthy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

The situation is slightly different for attacks marked as dealing [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"). Here, a separate attack is performed against each standing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a target unit. However, this type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can characteristically only deliver as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") as each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**. Naturally, this means that for all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** except the "top" one, [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** also defines the maximum amount that Area Damage effects can inflict on them.

Finally, [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** plays an important role in resolving all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"). These attacks force their targets to make an amount of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks depending on the type of the effect. [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") typically require one for each *attacking* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, while spells and [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") call for one per *defending* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. Regardless though, every failed check results in [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** equal to the target's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, effectively slaying one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

### Modifiers

There are only a handful of ways to increase a unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, the most common of them being the effect of [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**. There is no method for *decreasing* the attribute, magical or otherwise. Additional [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** granted in a temporary fashion are usually denoted by [Icon Hits Gold](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/b/b5/Icon_Hits_Gold.png/revision/latest?cb=20150104003008) **Golden Heart** icons in the *unit statistics window*. These either depend on the presence of a certain enchantment, or last only until the end of a battle.

#### Experience

:   This effect is only applicable to [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), as [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") are unable to gain [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**. The former gain [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** upon reaching [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite** level, which usually happens when they accumulate [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **120 Experience Points**, or are enchanted with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Heroism](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heroism "Heroism")**. Units created in a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") with an [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Altar of Battle](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Altar_of_Battle "Altar of Battle")** already start out with this much as well. Another [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1** may also be gained (for a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+2**), should a unit become [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Ultra-Elite**. However, this requires both the [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), and an active [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade")** [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") to be possessed by the controlling [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard"); in addition to the same amount of [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience Points** by the unit.

:   Heroes gain [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** much faster, at a rate of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1** per level, up to a maximum of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+8** at [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Demi-God**. This is much needed though, considering they are all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** units. In addition, many of them can possess the [Constitution](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Constitution "Constitution") [Constitution](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Constitution "Constitution") ability, either by default, or as a [Random](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities") "Fighter" perk. This grants another [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1** per level. Because it already starts giving a benefit at the first level though, it can provide a total of up to [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+9**.
    Over half of the Heroes that have access to it may also occasionally
    get the super (\*) version of this ability. This grants even more: 150%
    of the original, rounded down, up to a maximum of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+13** at the final level.

#### Unit Enchantments

:   Only two [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") are capable of enhancing [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**. The first one is the [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Lionheart](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lionheart "Lionheart")**, which can be cast both overland, for a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **200**, or during a battle, costing only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **40** in this case. It grants a rather powerful [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+3** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, but requires an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **4** per turn to maintain overland.

:   The second spell is somewhat counter-productive. This is the [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")**, which may only be cast overland. It has a base Casting Cost of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **100**, with an upkeep of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** per turn. While it increases [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** by [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1** for as long as it is maintained, it also has a permanent "side effect". [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") targeted by this spell become [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"),
    an irreversible change that will not go away with the removal of the
    enchantment. These units are no longer able to heal naturally or be
    targeted by healing spells, meaning that any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") they suffer can only be removed by [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") or the use of [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage").

#### Charm of Life

:   [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Charm of Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charm_of_Life "Charm of Life")** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") and very powerful [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment"). It has a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,250**, although its [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") is only a comparatively low [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** per turn. As long as it is in effect, this spell increases the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value of *all* of its owner's units by **25%**, with a minimum improvement of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **+1** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. This ensures that even the weakest [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") will benefit from the spell.

#### Magical Items

:   Unfortunately for [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** is *not* among the base attributes that can be enhanced by [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). However, the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Lionheart](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lionheart "Lionheart")** enchantment does have an [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") version called [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Lion Heart](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lion_Heart_(Item_Power) "Lion Heart (Item Power)")**. This requires the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact")** spell and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[6 Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") to imbue. Its cost is a base [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,000**, and it can only be placed on [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry"). As [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Lion Heart](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lion_Heart_(Item_Power) "Lion Heart (Item Power)")** is one of the three Item Powers that do not appear on any of the game's pre-fabricated items, it always requires custom [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting").
    As such, it's worth noting that two instances of this power are not
    cumulative, and neither does this enchantment stack with the actual
    spell (although it does have the advantage of not being dispellable).

#### Life Stealing Damage

:   Units executing attacks that deal [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") stand to gain temporary extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** if the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** they inflict matches or exceeds their [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count. This can only happen if they are fully healed first though. If this is the case, they are granted [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** equal to the remaining healing ("overhealing"), divided by their (maximum) [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
    count. These are temporary, and last until the end of the battle, at
    which point they will disappear. They do not carry over to the overland
    map.

#### Combat Healing Bug

:   In the last official game version (v1.31), and also in [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide"), a bug exists that can grant the same type of temporary extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** that are gained through Life Stealing. This triggers whenever a combat healing effect restores a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in an injured unit, but only if the unit's new "[top figure damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Top_Figure_Damage "Damage Points")" is at least as much as its maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
    count. In this case, the process described in the above paragraph is
    exectuted, except the "top figure damage" is substituted for the
    overhealing amount. Thus, the unit will gain ["top figure damage" ÷
    maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count], rounded down, in [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**. This bug is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

## Hit Points

 [Unit cards in the overland unit window feature colored health bars to display current Hit Points.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/d/d8/Health_Bar_Unit_Cards.png/revision/latest?cb=20181120035746)

Unit cards in the overland *unit window* feature colored health bars to display current **Hit Points**.



The term "**Hit Points**" is itself used in two slightly different contexts, in addition to sometimes referring to the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value. Firstly, it can represent the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** required to destroy a unit or [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. This is its most common interpretation. However, it is also the classic unit of measurement for healing effects.

### Maximum Health

A unit's maximum health always equals its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** multiplied by its maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** count. Both of these are base attributes, characteristic to each unit type, although as noted above, [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**
can actually be improved by a few effects. Maximum health is used to
calculate "top figure damage" when inspecting units overland, and when
entering combat. It also determines the amount of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** that the unit can heal naturally between overland turns, as described in the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Natural_Healing "Damage Points") article.

### Top Figure Health

The inverse of the "top figure damage", this is the value represented by the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Bright Heart** icons in the *unit statistics window*. It equals [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** - "top figure damage". Since only the foremost [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
in the unit can ever become "injured", this information can be used by
the player to accurately gauge the remaining strength of a unit. The
value is also used during the resolution of [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"), where it sets the maximum [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that can be inflicted on the foremost [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**; whereas for all other [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the unit, this limit is more easily described as simply the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** amount.

### Current Health Total

 [The active unit window displays a health bar for the currently selected unit.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a8/Health_Bar_Active_Unit_Window.png/revision/latest?cb=20181120035714)

The *active unit window* displays a health bar for the currently selected unit.



The current total health of a unit can also be expressed in [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, and is often easier to understand than the internal representation used by the game. It is calculated as [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** × [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **figures** - "top figure damage". This value is highlighted as a colored bar, both on unit cards overland, and the *active unit window* and *combat unit display*
in battle. The bar is filled proportionately to the unit's current-
versus maximum health. On the unit cards, a grey or transparent bar
indicates full health. This changes to green if the unit has suffered
some [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**,
but is still above 70% of its maximum health. Yellow indicates current
health between 40% and 70% of the maximum, while the color changes to
red below 40%. The combat displays are slightly different in that full
bars are also green for better visibility.

### Healing

 [The combat unit display also shows a health bar when mousing over a target unit.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/0/06/Health_Bar_Combat_Unit_Display.png/revision/latest?cb=20181120035731)

The *combat unit display* also shows a health bar when mousing over a target unit.



**Hit Points** are typically used as the unit of measurement for healing spells and abilities. Although technically, these effects reduce [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") instead, the end result is essentially the same. For example, the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing "Healing")** spell can be described either as removing up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5 Damage Points**, or healing up to [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **5 Hit Points**. Both are correct, and yield the same total health. However, as health is traditionally expressed and tracked as **Hit Points**, the latter representation is arguably easier to get accustomed to.

On the other hand, because *Master of Magic* internally tracks [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") rather than **Hit Points**,
a description of healing effects is not included on this page. Instead,
these are explained in detail in the linked article under the heading [Removing Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Removing_Damage_Points "Damage Points").
