**Damage Points** represent the injuries sustained by a unit or [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** at any given time. **Damage Points** are, essentially, lost [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points"). They are represented in several different ways in the game, most prominently by the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Darkened Heart** icons that replace the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Bright Hearts** in the *unit statistics window*. This icon also signifies both [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points**, and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** in general throughout the wiki.

Units can suffer **Damage Points** in a multitude of ways, collectively termed "attacks". Should their amount equal or exceed the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, that unit is completely destroyed. Thus, the primary way to kill a unit is to inflict a sufficient number of **Damage Points** on that unit. [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units further consist of multiple individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. At regular intervals of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** taken, they will lose one of these [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, weakening their offensive combat capabilities.

[Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** can be healed in several ways, particularly by a process known as "natural healing", whereby the **Damage Points**
sustained by the unit are reduced by a certain amount at the beginning
of each overland turn. Units can also be healed artificially with magic,
or thanks to the powerful [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") ability.

## Concept

**Damage** is the loss of structural integrity. As such, it is
often not measured itself beyond the amounts that directly result from
attacks. Instead, most games choose to measure the integrity itself. On
one end of the scale, in role-playing games there is the physiological-,
or bodily integrity of creatures or characters. This is sometimes
called simply "health" or "life" but, more often, the term used is [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**,
or "HP". This originates from the fact that this attribute determines
how many "hits" the character or creature can take before being
incapacitated or slain. Which means it really measures the ability to
take **Damage** in the first place.

On the other end of the scale are strategy games. After a certain
point, the differences between the sturdiness of individual creatures
or soldiers become just too insignificant when considering the power of
hundreds or thousands of these entities. Sure, it may still be a listed
attribute, but what really matters is the amount of troops that are
still battle-ready. Here, **Damage** is typically a measure of the soldiers lost from a unit.

Whatever the scale though, most games place a higher emphasis on the *remaining* health or troop count, and this is what is stored or written down. *Master of Magic* does the exact opposite: it only actually records *damage taken*. In other words, **Damage Points**. Remaining [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, as well as [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
counts and "top figure damage", are always a function of this
information, combined with the unit's base statistics and any applicable
special effects.

## Effect

Units in *Master of Magic* consist of individual entities called [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. Some, most notably [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), ships, war machines, and powerful summoned creatures, contain only a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure**. Others, like early [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), and the more common [Fantastic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") ones, have somewhere between 2 and 8. The 9 heads of the mighty [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** are also considered to be [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** for all intents and purposes. The maximum amount of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in a unit is one of its base attributes. This will also be their initial count whenever the unit is recruited or summoned.

Each unit also has a separate statistic called [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** or, more accurately, *[Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure***. The total (starting) "health" of an entire unit is thus its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** times its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure count**. Naturally, for [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** units, such as Heroes, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** health translates directly into unit health. In either case though, this value represents the amount of **Damage Points** it takes to slay or destroy the entire unit. Other than increasing risk though, **Damage Points** have no individual effect until certain thresholds are reached, at which points [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** loss and, ultimately, the destruction of the unit will occur.

### Top Figure Damage

By design, each unit in *Master of Magic* is limited to a single injured [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. All others are considered to be at full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** at all times. [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** inflicted on this "top" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** is thus often called the "top figure damage". This is the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** visible in the *Unit Statistics Window* as a number in combat, and is what is indicated by the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Darkened Heart** icons replacing the bright [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** icons, whenever the unit is inspected. This number allows the player to determine how much **Damage Points** the unit has suffered, or approximate how long it would take for it to recover naturally.

 [In combat, the unit statistics window also displays "top figure damage" as a numerical value, labeled simply "Damage".](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/5/53/USW_Damage_Display.png/revision/latest?cb=20181120035559)

In combat, the *unit statistics window* also displays "top figure damage" as a numerical value, labeled simply "Damage".



On the overland map, the game calculates the "top figure damage" in a
very straightforward manner. It is the remainder of the recorded **Damage Points**, divided by the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** statistic of the unit. However, in combat, it has its own variable, and is actually tracked separately from [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** taken.

### Figure Loss

Whether overland or in combat though, if the "top figure damage" reaches a unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit will fall. For [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** units, this naturally means the destruction of the unit itself. [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units, on the other hand, will survive, unless it was the last [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. However, as their offensive power comes directly from their numbers, the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** a unit loses, the less capable it becomes at inflicting **Damage Points** on the enemy. Granted, having a large amount of initial [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** can greatly reduce the impact of losing a single one.

 [The](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/9/92/Hydra_Head_Count.png/revision/latest?cb=20181120035625)

The [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** is indicated by the amount of Heads it has, rather than by its graphics, like for other units. This is shown in the *unit statistics window*.



The amount of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** that are still standing in a unit is only indicated visually in the game. The only exception is the afore mentioned [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**, whose graphics remain the same until the end, and its "head count" is shown as a number instead. For all other units, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** need to be counted while looking at them in combat, or by checking their image in the *unit statistics window*.
While selecting units overland, their unit cards also display an
overall health gauge, but this is often not enough to get an accurate [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count.

#### Example #1

:   A [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Stone Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Giant "Stone Giant")** is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** unit. It has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **20 Hit Points** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, so the unit as a whole also has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **20 Hit Points**. This means it can take up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **19** **Damage Points** before dying. However, none of that [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
    is going to hinder its combat performance in any way, although the risk
    of the giant falling to the next attack does keep steadily increasing
    as it is taking more and more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Once it suffers the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **20th** point though, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, and along with it, the entire unit, is destroyed.

#### Example #2

:   A fresh [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Recruit** [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit contains [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures**, and has a [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** statistic of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3**. The overall "health" of this unit is thus [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3** × [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **12**. Its base [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") is [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **4**, which means that at full strength, it can execute 4 attacks at strength [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **4** each. Taking [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** or [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** **Damage Points** will not change this situation, although the *unit statistics window* would reflect this both in its row of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** icons, and separately as a "Damage" number in combat.

:   Should the cavalrymen take a third point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, they will lose a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. At this point, the image displayed when opening the *unit statistics window* will now only show 3 cavalrymen. At the same time, a full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3** icons return, indicating that the "lead"-, or "top" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, has all of its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**
    fully intact. However, should the unit engage in battle now, it can
    only make 3 attacks instead of 4, although its Attack Strength is still [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **4**. That is, it has lost 25% of its offensive capability.

:   The next two **Damage Points** repeat the first procedure, and no [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** are lost. The only impact is the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** visible in the *unit statistics window*. Then, at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **6**, another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** dies, reducing the unit's effectiveness to a mere 50% of its original. This cycle then keeps repeating until finally, at [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **12**, the last [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** perishes, and the unit is destroyed.

## Sources of Damage

[Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** can be caused in a myriad of ways, collectively called *attacks*.
These generally use one of two common mechanics, that differ both in
the way they are resolved, and the defensive attribute that might be
used mitigate their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

### Conventional Damage

:   *Main article: [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage")*

Conventional attacks are by far the most common sources of **Damage Points**. [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath Attacks**, and even [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **direct damage** spells all use the same generic procedure to determine their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. They start with an [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), that sets out their *damage potential*.
An amount of 10-sided dice equal to this value are then used to find
out how much of this potential is realized with that specific attack.
This is called an [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll"), and it introduces the concept of "raw", or "unmitigated" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, sometimes also called "successful hits". This is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that is not yet fully processed, and as such is typically not ready to be applied directly as **Damage Points**.

The reason for this is that in most cases, Conventional Damage can be reduced, or possibly avoided entirely, using the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
attribute. This allows a defending unit to use their own set of
10-sided dice, equal in number to this statistic, to also perform a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll"). Every die that is deemed "successful" here (the base chance of success is **30%** per die in both sets of rolls) will reduce the incoming "raw damage" by [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 point**. If the remainder is more than the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, then that much is slated to be delivered, and a new [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") can commence to further reduce whatever is left over. This can repeat as many times as necessary, until all the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is accounted for. Only then is it final, and ready to be applied as actual **Damage Points**. However, as noted below, the process is still not entirely complete.

Confusingly enough, Conventional Damage resolution can either
ignore, or fully consider "top figure damage". That is, the second
Defense Roll will take place either after a full [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** worth of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
has been dealt - or after only as much as the "top figure" had
remaining. Which of these will occur depends on whether the attack was
made by a unit or a spell. [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") are always directed at a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** with maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, even if the unit does not have such a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** any more. They have to record at least that much in [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
before they will allow another Defense Roll. In contrast, spells always
target the actual "top figure" - the unit will get to defend again as
soon as it takes enough [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to destroy this [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

### Special Damage

:   *Main article: [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage")*

The other major group of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** sources are [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistable** spells. These are far less common than "[Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage")" attacks. They are typically defended against with the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
statistic, although in a completely different manner. While this
mechanic also uses 10-sided dice, in this case, the target number is
actually the unit's attribute score. If the roll is equal to or lower
than this, then it was successful. If it is above the score however, the
check was a failure. This means that the higher the score, the better
the chance that the unit "resists" these sources of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Many effects therefore apply a temporary [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** modifier, to make the checks either easier or more difficult to pass. However, with a modified score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** or higher, the unit will always succeed with no random chance involved.

Most Special Damage attacks deal [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. That is, on a failed [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll, the target unit suffers as many **Damage Points** as its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, effectively slaying one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. How many [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks are required depends on the attack. [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), for instance, activate once for each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** delivering one. [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and spells, on the other hand, call for one roll for each standing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the *target* unit.

There are also a few Special Damage attacks that affect units as a whole. [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") forces the target to make one [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll for every point of its strength, and inflicts [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** on the unit for each failed one. [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") causes as many **Damage Points** as the target fails a single [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check by. Finally, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Disintegrate](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegrate "Disintegrate")** and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call")** do not even allow for a roll. The former will automatically work so long as a unit *can* fail its check against it, while the latter has a flat 25% chance to succeed unless the target is [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** or [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal") [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal"). Both of these inflict a fatal [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **200** **Damage Points**, more than enough to destroy even the most powerful units in the game.

## Applying Damage

There are a few more quirks that may be worth remembering about **Damage Points**. First and foremost is that during battles, the game tracks *three different types* of **Damage Points**, not just one. When exactly are the points applied, and how much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can be dealt to a single unit, are also not trivial questions to answer.

### Types of Damage Points

On the overland map, the game only tracks **Damage Points**
in general. However, in combat, there are also two more types that can
be applied by certain spells and abilities. "Regular", or "normal" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
is the generic kind. This is the type dealt by the majority of attacks,
and is the one tracked overland. Whenever a battle ends, the other two
types are automatically converted into regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.
This means that any effect that they may have will not be carried with
the unit from battle to battle, and is only considered during the one
fight that they are applied in.

However, if either of their main effects do trigger, those are
entirely permanent. This is because they only activate if the unit is
destroyed during the battle. Essentially, these two types of **Damage Points** control whether the unit can be brought back from the dead, and if so, how.

#### Create Undead Damage Points

:   Injuries caused by [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage"), and the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**- and [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") attack of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Ghouls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ghouls "Ghouls")**, has the power to bring slain units back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") under the control of their attacker. This mechanic is implemented by using special **Damage Points**
    that are only applicable during combat. Should a unit be destroyed in a
    way that is considered to be primarily "Create Undead Damage", the
    effect activates, and has the following consequences:

    * Even if the unit has the [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") ability, it will no longer return to life if its side wins the battle.
    * If the unit's original owner loses the battle, it will rise as an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit, under the control of the victorious player. It should be noted though, that this specific effect does not work on [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** units (including those that are already [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")).

:   To trigger any of the above, the amount of "Create Undead" **Damage Points** must at least equal the regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** suffered by the unit, and must be at least one point *higher* than any Irreversible **Damage Points**. To assist with this though, if a unit has both "Create Undead" and regular **Damage Points** inflicted on it, then all combat healing effects are set to remove the regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** first. However, that does not mean that "Create Undead Damage" can not be healed. For example, if a unit has suffered [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** "Create Undead" **Damage Points** and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** points of regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, a [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing "Healing")** spell cast on it will still remove all [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5**.

:   "Create Undead Damage" also does not work against units that possess [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). That is, all such **Damage Points** are automatically converted to regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** before being applied. The [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") also extends this mechanic to units with [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity").

#### Irreversible Damage Points

:   Some attacks cause injuries so severe that they can not be
    recovered from while in combat. No healing spells or effects, not even
    the [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") ability can remove these "Irreversible" **Damage Points** during the battle. Should the unit survive though, they can be healed normally afterwards, as they are converted to regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. On the other hand, units that are destroyed primarily by [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** like this are lost forever. This effect triggers if the amount of Irreversible **Damage Points** dealt to the unit are *at least as much* as both the regular and any "Create Undead Damage". That is, if it is the highest (or equal) out of the three types.

:   Units destroyed this way are no longer valid targets for either [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Raise Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Raise_Dead "Raise Dead")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead")**. [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration")
    will also not bring them back, even if their side wins the battle.
    Naturally, since the requirements conflict with those of "Create Undead
    Damage", that may not take effect either, and neither can the unit be
    affected by [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Zombie Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Zombie_Mastery "Zombie Mastery")**. If a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") is slain this way, not only do they become unavailable for [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Resurrection](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resurrection "Resurrection")**, but any [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item")
    they carry are also destroyed along with them, and can not be recovered
    after the battle. However, it still remains possible to summon [Torin the Chosen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Torin_the_Chosen "Torin the Chosen") again using the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Incarnation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Incarnation "Incarnation")** spell, and he does get to keep his level and abilities.

:   Irreversible [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is only available from [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") sources, nearly all of which are [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based. That is, they inflict [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** equal to the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value for every failed [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check. The only two effects that don't conform to this are those of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Disintegrate](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegrate "Disintegrate")** and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call")** spells, both of which destroy their target by dealing [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **200** Irreversible **Damage Points**.

### Simultaneous Damage

**Damage Points** are often
not applied immediately after resolving an attack. Or, to be more
precise, it is actually not all that common for an individual attack to
to be performed by itself. This practically only happens when single
target [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage")-, or unit-based [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") spells are cast; or when [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure** units perform [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") with no "added effects".

In all other scenarios, the target unit will be hit by multiple
attacks at the same time. As a general rule of thumb, the game will
always resolve *all* of these attacks before applying *any* of their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. For example, when executing conventional attacks, all eligible [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit will perform that attack. However, no **Damage Points** are registered to the defender until all of these are resolved. The same is true for [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that are delivered as "added effects". The Special Damage is also evaluated before the Conventional is dealt.

The situation is slightly different when two units engage in [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**. This is a multi-stage process by itself, and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** does get applied at the end of each of its phases. However, units are *not* destroyed here regardless of the amount **Damage Points** they took. The entire sequence is always played out as if both units were still present. Since slain [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** can't perform any attacks, this does not matter in terms of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** caused to the surviving side, but it can make all the difference when considering the types and amounts of **Damage Points** done to the destroyed unit, as illustrated in the examples below.

The [Melee Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Sequence "Melee Attack")
is also typically finished with a mutual attack phase, where both
combatants strike at each other simultaneously. In this stage, *all* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from both sides, whether Conventional or Special, is delayed until every standing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** of each unit has concluded all of their available attacks. For example, two unhurt units of [Nightblades](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nightblades "Nightblades") fighting each other would make 12 individual [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** attacks and another 12 [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") attacks before either of them suffers any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from the other.

### Overkill Damage

**Damage Points** are by no
means limited to the total health of a unit. The game does not stop
tracking or applying them just because there's already enough inflicted
on a unit to destroy it. This is most prominent during [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** combat, where a unit that perishes to short-range attacks ([Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown**, [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath**, or [Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")) will still be dealt [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Damage**.
However, it also happens almost every other time a unit is destroyed,
and can be very important to consider when trying to create [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units through [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage"). The only exception is [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"), which is incapable of inflicting more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** on a unit than it has remaining [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**.

In any case though, **Damage Points** do have a hardcoded limit of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **200** each, to prevent excessive amounts from wrapping around and actually reducing the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** inflicted. This is more than the double of the total [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**, the unit that has by far the most [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** in the game (twice as much as any other contender).

### Examples

The following examples should help understand most of the quirks of applying **Damage Points**. For the sake of simplicity, [Conventional](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage")- and [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage")
attacks are grouped together throughout them. In reality though, the
resolution order is "added effects" followed by the conventional attack
for the first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, then the same order for the next [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, and so on, until every attack has been concluded. Because of the simultaneous application of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
however, the actual order of execution has absolutely no practical
relevance, as the outcomes are always exactly the same as in the
illustrations below.

#### Stoning Touch vs Gaze

:   A unit of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** is facing off a [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")** in a [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** (**+2** to all stats for both units). Since neither has a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), they have to duke it out in [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** combat. Both can attack the other, and who initiates the engagement has no impact on the outcome. The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")**'s [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") goes first, as it always happens before mutual [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** in the sequence.

:   This is an ordinary [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), which means it is performed as an "added effect" to an otherwise hidden short-range attack that matches the [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") of the [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") itself. Because it is enhanced by the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")**, it has an [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **3**. The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")** rolls 3, 4, and 9 which, counting in its universal [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1 To Hit**, means it registers 2 "raw" points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** have a natural [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **3**, which is also boosted to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5** because of the [Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node "Node"). They make their [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll"), and get 2, 5, 6, 6, and 9. There are no modifiers [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** in play, so only one of these rolls is "successful", blocking [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**, and leaving the other [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** slated to be delivered.

:   Before that however, the Gaze effect takes place. It has an innate modifier of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-1**, but again the Node Aura grants [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+2**, bringing the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")**' score to a total of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **8** against the attack. Since Gaze Attacks require as many [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks as the target unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** remaining, the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")**
    have to make 4. The dice come up as 2, 3, 6, and 9. That's three
    successes and one failure, meaning that the attack will deal one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s worth of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** in [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. In this case, this is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**, as this attribute is *not* enhanced by the Node.

:   This concludes the Gaze Attack, and the short-range attack phase also ends with it. The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** take a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**, which is now applied. They lose one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, leaving them with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **3** for the rest of the sequence. The extra [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** "top figure damage" does not have any effect on their performance.

:   Continuing to the actual [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** phase, the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")** has a modified Attack Strength of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **17**. Rolling a fairly average series, it scores 7 "successful hits". The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** roll better than last time though, and with their 1, 3, 3, 6, and 10, manage to block [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**. However, the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4** is still above their [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, so the computer records [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** for delivery, and makes another Defense Roll against the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**. This time, the beasts roll 2, 3, 4, 7, and 8. Although this is enough to block [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2**, it unfortunately can not affect [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** already slated for delivery. That is, it blocks only the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**, with one success becoming effectively wasted. Thus, the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** will take is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**.

:   Before that can happen though, they also get to strike this time. With their remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **3**, they perform 3 attacks at an enhanced [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **6** each, against the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")**'s also boosted [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **6**. Unlucky for them, even with their [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1**, only one of them manages to get through, with [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** left after the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")**'s Defense Roll. Despite this however, all three of them also get to execute their [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch"). It is not required for their conventional attack to do [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** for this to happen, it simply has to be *capable* of doing so (i.e. must have an Attack Strength of at least 1).

:   The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")** has a natural [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **7**, enhanced to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **9** by the Node. However, the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** impose a penalty of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-3** on their [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), bringing this down to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **6**. The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")** has to make 3 saves, one against each attacking [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s Stoning Touch. It rolls a rather unlucky 6, 8, and 9, meaning it fails two out of the three rolls. Since it has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **30** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, this means it will suffer a whopping [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **60**.

:   As all attacks are now resolved, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can be applied to both units. The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** suffer [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**, and lose one more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. They emerge from the battle with a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7** **Damage Points**, out of which the *unit statistics window* will show **1**. The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")**, on the other hand, is completely annihilated, as it takes a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **62** versus its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **30**.

:   It may also be worth noting that a simple [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements "Resist Elements")** spell could have helped the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")** avoid more than half of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** they took. It would have made them immune to the Gaze effect entirely by raising their [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **11**, while also providing an extra [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** against the hidden [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") component.The same spell cast on the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")** would still only have resulted in [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **9**, meaning that over the course of multiple engagements with multiple enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, it would still have had a fair chance of being slain.

#### Multi-Gaze

:   [Tauron](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tauron "Tauron") is trying to crack open a [Tower of Wizardry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower_of_Wizardry "Tower of Wizardry") with his [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**, so he can get some [Settlers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Settlers "Settlers") over to the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power**-rich [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror"). His scouts report a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** inside. Unfortunately, when he enters the battle, he is greeted by an additional 8 [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fire Elementals](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Elemental "Fire Elemental")** supporting the beast. This instantly foils his grand plan of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireballing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** it into oblivion, as he will need to save his [Spell Casting Skill](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Casting_Skill "Spell Casting Skill") to deal with the elementals.

:   Confident in the spawn though, he sends it forward, stopping just so that it can get the jump on the advancing [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**. The elementals can't attack the [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** spawn, so there's no need to worry about them for the time being. Sure enough, the 9-headed monstrosity steps right up to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**, and in so doing exhausts its [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"). Thus, the spawn can attack first without having to worry about any incoming [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath").

:   Unlike the single [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") of other creatures, the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**'s "Multi-Gaze" does not require a hidden conventional attack to attach the gaze effect to. This is because the [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") already delivers [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"), which [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") attacks can be added to. So much so, that even the creature's [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") is triggered from it. That will not be considered in this example though, as it has no effect on any of the enemies here.

:   As the spawn is attacking, the [Melee Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Sequence "Melee Attack") starts with its Gaze Attacks. These can not be applied yet though, as along with this attack come two more gaze effects (and a [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") attack that fails to do any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** because of the opponent's high [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**).

:   First is the [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze"). While the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** has a rather high [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **11**, the spawn also applies a penalty of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-4** to this attack, bringing that down to only [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **7**. Having [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **9 figures** (each head counts as one), the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** is in big trouble, and has to make 9 individual [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
    checks. It rolls fairly well: 1, 1, 3, 5, 6, 6, 7, 8, and 10. Only the
    last two of these are failures, meaning it will suffer two [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s worth of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** in [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Since each of its heads has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10**, this is a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **20 Damage Points**.

It has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures** remaining, so it can attack 4 times with a strength of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **6**. The first head thus deals no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, the second [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2**, the third again none, while the last one does [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**, for a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5**. Since there are no more attacks, these [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5 Damage Points** are applied to the spawn, while the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** does not get hurt this time.

:   Tauron, trusting the monster's lack of intelligence, decides to use the remaining [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**0.5** of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")** to withdraw instead of commencing with another attack, and the combat turn ends. The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**, unfazed by its injuries, follows the retreating enemy, again sacrificing the advantage of its [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath Attack**. This reduces its total recorded [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **53**, but does not allow for any of its heads to regrow.

:   It is again the spawn's turn, and it moves in to attempt a finishing blow. Its Doom Gaze deals the same [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4** unblockable **Damage Points**. However, this time, the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** only has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures** left for the other two Gaze Attacks. Unfortunately for the beast though, that's still two times four [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks, as any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
    done by one gaze will not be applied until both are resolved. It rolls
    4, 6, 8, and 9 for the Death Gaze; and 2, 2, 4, and 8 for the Stoning
    Gaze. Alas, that's two failures on the first set, and one on the second,

Although it does avoid the spawn's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** attack entirely again, it also fails to inflict any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** on it. Even though it manages to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** both of the other gazes, the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** done to it is now [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **91**, and its last head falls at the end of the short-range attack phase. As it has no more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** left, it can inflict no [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Damage** to the spawn, who survives the "duel" with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10 Hit Points**
    remaining. Aided by Tauron's entire Casting Skill, the elementals are
    now no match for it despite their numbers and immunities, leading to the
    swift capture of the Tower for the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard").

#### Undead Through Life Stealing

:   [Rjak](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rjak "Rjak") has just arrived on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror"), conquering a [Tower of Wizardry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower_of_Wizardry "Tower of Wizardry") with his [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")**. Two turns later, while scouting the vicinity, he spots a small [Dwarven](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarves "Dwarves") [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") nearby. Although his [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")** have sustained some [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** in the previous fight, and are logging [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4 Damage Points**, they are very strong against [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), so he decides to attack the hamlet anyway. It is defended by 5 units of [Halberdiers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halberdiers "Halberdiers"): two [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Veterans**, two [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Regulars**, and a single [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Recruit**.

:   Since the Dwarves don't have any [Ranged](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")- or short-range attacks, the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")** are free to pick and choose their targets. To maximize the chances of gaining an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") garrison, Rjak starts the battle with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Prayer "Black Prayer")**, inflicting penalties of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **-1**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **-1**, and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-2**
    on all of the Halberdiers. Since most of his other spells would only
    hinder his goal in this scenario, he dumps the rest of his [Spell Casting Skill](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Casting_Skill "Spell Casting Skill") into a [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** instead. It's just enough to fully empower the spell. He targets one of the [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Veteran** Dwarves, the same one he intends to attack first with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")**.

:   [Dwarf Halberdiers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarf_Halberdiers "Dwarf Halberdiers") have a base [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **8**, the highest of any [Race](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Races "Races"). [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Veterans** also receive [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+2** on top of this. However, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Prayer "Black Prayer")** effectively cancels this out with its [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-2** penalty, and a fully powered [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** carries a further [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-8**. This brings the Dwarves' overall [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** against this attack down to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **0**, meaning that they will take [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
    regardless of what they roll: they can not possibly succeed the check.
    Fortune does not favor them either, as the die comes up as a 7, and they
    take **7** - [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **0** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7** points of "Create Undead Damage". The third set is even better and gets 2, reducing the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to a single point. Before it is applied though, there is still the matter of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")**' [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal"). If cast by [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") though, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** does also heal them much the same way as the ability would.

:   The Halberdiers have a final score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **8** + [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **2** - [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **2** - [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **3** = [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **5** against this Life Stealing attack, which is executed once for each attacking [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. In this case, that is [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4**, so the Dwarves have to make 4 [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** checks. #### Melee Overkill

:   The dragon is surrounded by a host of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors")**,
    but Merlin knows they will be of little significance if he manages to

Paying no mind to its "followers", the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake")** quickly closes the distance between the two armies to only a single tile. Merlin prepares his tactical play, and moves his [Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spearmen "Spearmen")

But fortune smiles on Merlin this day, as the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake")** takes the "bait", and swoops right down on the brave, but suicidal Spearmen.     and so does one more down the line. Thus, overall, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** points get blocked, while [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **13** are ultimately delivered.

    for every [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** recorded, a new Defense Roll can take place. Even though this is followed by another two sets with zero successes ([Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7** dealt, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0** remains); if this was the only attack, the unit would actually have survived despite their very low [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. Granted, their single remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** would not have been very useful on the battlefield, but the unit may have lived to tell the tale, and gain some [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**.

    range, with no moves left for an attack. This gives the Berserkers a

## Removing Damage Points

**Damage Points** are
mostly transient. They can be removed in several ways, most commonly
through an overland process called "natural healing". In addition, some
spells and [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") can also accomplish this feat. However, most restorative effects do not work on [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units and creatures associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). They neither heal naturally, nor are they affected by healing spells. The only exception in the latter group is [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")**, which can still remove **Damage Points** through the additional effect of the [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") it deals.

### Natural Healing

All units in *Master of Magic* (apart from [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** ones, as stated above) will lose the **Damage Points**
inflicted upon them over time. How quickly this happens depends mostly
on where the unit ends its turn. This is because natural healing always
occurs between two turns - after one ends, but before the next one truly
begins. Units out in the open heal very slowly (roughly 5% of their
total health per turn), while those in a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") with an [Animists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animists%27_Guild "Animists' Guild") regain their strength in the shortest amount of time (healing up to around 16.67%, or 1/6th of their [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** per turn). Either way though, only at most as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can be removed as the unit has sustained. Negative **Damage Points** have no meaning and will simply be zeroed out.

Natural healing is a two stage process. First, the unit automatically loses an amount of **Damage Points**
equal to their total health divided by an arbitrary value, determined
by where the unit has ended its turn. Next, if the previous division had
a remainder (sometimes that's all there will be), there is a random
chance of losing [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** **Damage Point**.
To decide this, the computer generates a number between 1 and the
threshold value (the divisor from above). If the result is equal to, or
lower, than the remainder, the point is removed. Otherwise, it is not.
This essentially simulates the fractions that would otherwise occur
during percentage-based healing, since the game does not store these.

The threshold values are [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **20** for units in the field, [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10** for units in a Town, and [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **6** for the lucky ones in Cities with an Animists' Guild. This roughly translates to regaining 100% ÷ [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **20** = 5%; 100% ÷ [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10** = 10%; and 100% ÷ [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **6** = 16.67% of the units' total health. There is also a bug related to [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") [Healers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") (see below), that can set this value out of turn to an awfully high [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **66**, which will result in units altogether failing to heal naturally the majority of the time. Their chance to regain a single [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** is [total health in 66], and they may not regain more than that until moved out of the [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") Healer's stack. Granted, for units with totals over [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **4**, the ability is typically stronger than their natural healing, so the trade-off may still be worth it in the end.

The table below lists the natural healing amounts for the most common total health values. ### Healer Ability

[Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") is a unit ability possessed by all [Shamans](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shamans "Shamans") and [Priests](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Priests "Priests"), in addition to three similarly themed [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heroes "Heroes"): [Serena the Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Serena_the_Healer "Serena the Healer"), [Elana the Priestess](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elana_the_Priestess "Elana the Priestess"), and [Roland the Paladin](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Roland_the_Paladin "Roland the Paladin"); although the latter two of these can only be hired by [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") possessing at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[1 Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
The effect of the ability shares some of the characteristics of natural
healing. Namely, it is applied between overland turns, and does not
work at all on [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** and [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units.

When the effect activates, all units stacked with the [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") Healer lose **Damage Points**
equal to 20%, or 1/5th of their total health, rounded down; or as many
as they have sustained, if it is less. Because of the rounding, the
Normal Units with this ability will typically not benefit from it
themselves, unless they achieve [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite**
level or are enchanted in some way. This healing is also independent of
any other factors. It is not an added percentage to natural healing. It
is also not cumulative, and will only be applied once per turn,
regardless of the amount of [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") Healers in the stack.

In fact, because of this (and an unfortunate oversight on part of
the developers), the ability can also greatly reduce the natural
healing of some stacked units. The same variable that sets the natural
healing threshold for units is also used to mark those that have already
received benefit from a [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer")
Healer. Namely, by setting the value to 66. However, the ability is
evaluated along with the processing of natural healing, in the same
order that units have been created in. That is, when the [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer")
Healer's turn comes up, it will also utilise the ability on all units
it is stacked with. Because of this, any such unit, that was recruited *after* the [Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healer "Healer") Healer, will have its threshold set to [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **66** by the time the game gets to process its natural healing. As a result, it will most likely not heal at all from that source!

### Overland Spells

There are three spells that can remove all **Damage Points** from units on the overland map. The simplest of these is [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature's Cures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature%27s_Cures "Nature's Cures")**, an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") that, for a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **75**, fully heals an entire stack of units on a selected map tile. Next is [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Stream of Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stream_of_Life "Stream of Life")**, a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Town Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Enchantment "Town Enchantment") with a base cost of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **300** and an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **8** per turn. It heals all units inside the enchanted [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") at the beginning of every turn. Finally, the [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Herb Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Herb_Mastery "Herb Mastery")** [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") removes all [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** sustained by *all* of the caster's units between overland turns. It has a Casting Cost of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,000** though, and requires [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** per turn to maintain. Neither of these spells affect [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** and [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units.

### Regeneration

[Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") is a unit ability that provides both full healing overland, and constant removal of **Damage Points** in combat. Its effect can be duplicated by both the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration_(Spell) "Regeneration (Spell)")** [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), and the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration_(Item_Power) "Regeneration (Item Power)")** [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). The former is a [Very Rare Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") that takes a base [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **300** to cast overland (or [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **60** in combat), and a hefty [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** per turn to keep in play; while the latter requires [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[6 Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact")** spell, and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,500** to imbue, and can only be enchanted onto (or found on) [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry"). All three sources share the same restorative effect, which is one of only two methods available for removing **Damage Points** from units associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm").

On the overland map, regenerating units are fully healed not only
between turns, but also after every victorious battle. In fact, unless
the unit is destroyed mainly by Irreversible- or "Create Undead Damage",
it will even return from death as long as its controller wins the
battle. In addition, such units are impervious to [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") on the overland map, regardless of the scale of the spell causing it. However, they do take [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") as normal, and can still be destroyed by it.

In combat, a regenerating unit will lose [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Damage Point** at the beginning of each of its controller's turns, so long as it has sustained any; although Irreversible **Damage Points** may not be removed, and regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is always healed before any "Create Undead Damage". In the official and [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") versions of the game, regaining a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** through [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") Regeneration can trigger the "combat healing bug", described further below. However, this is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

### Life Stealing Damage

[Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage"), when caused by a unit, has the power to either heal that unit, or grant it temporary maximum health in the form of extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. It is the only other way besides [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") to remove **Damage Points** from [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures and [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units. This [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") is characteristic of the [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") ability, where it got its name from. However, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can also utilize it by equipping a [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Vampiric](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vampiric_(Item_Power) "Vampiric (Item Power)")** weapon, or casting the [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** spell. The [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") requires [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[5 Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact")**, and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **800** to enchant, and can only be placed on [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** weapons ([Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"), [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace"), and [Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword")).

When a unit successfully deals Life Stealing Damage, an amount of **Damage Points**
equal to what it inflicted will be removed from it. The unit is
effectively healed by the life force it stole. If this causes it to lose
all of its **Damage Points**, or if it didn't have any to begin with, then this effect can instead grant it temporary strength. For this to happen, the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** caused must also at least match the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count of the unit. If it does, the unit gains extra maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** equal to the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** divided by the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
count, and rounded down. These persist until the end of the battle,
during which time the unit is considered to have more than its usual
total health for all intents and purposes.

It should be noted that both of these effects occur on a
per-attack basis. If multiple attacks are performed at the same time,
such as a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit executing [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") Life Steal [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), then its the individual attacks' [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that will determine whether extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** are gained, not the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** of all of the Life Stealing attacks. Furthermore, both effects are trigged during the *evaluation* of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and not on its actual application. This again matters mainly for Touch Attacks delivered in [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**, where no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is dealt until every attack is processed. This means that in a mutual [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**, the unit is healed *before* it takes [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from the opponent, if any.

Regaining a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** through the removal of **Damage Points**
by a Life Stealing attack may also trigger the "combat healing bug", as
described below. In addition, the healing effect of Life Stealing
Damage has another bug that also works in favor of the Life Stealing
unit. Although it can not remove Irreversible **Damage Points**, it allows the unit to regain [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** regardless, without actually decreasing its recorded [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Not only does this often trigger the previous bug, causing the unit to gain extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**, it also allows getting more when at full [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, as if the unit was actually undamaged.

### Combat Healing Spells

[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing "Healing")** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") that, for a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **15**, can remove up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5** **Damage Points** from any non-[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** unit. It also has a battlefield version, the [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Mass Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Healing "Mass Healing")**. This instead targets all friendly units with the same effect, for a base cost of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **60**. In addition, the first spell is available as a unit ability called [Healing Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing_Spell "Healing Spell") [Healing Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing_Spell "Healing Spell"), possessed by all [Priests](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Priests "Priests"). It is also a default spell always known by the [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") [Serena the Healer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Serena_the_Healer "Serena the Healer"), [Elana the Priestess](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elana_the_Priestess "Elana the Priestess"), and [Torin the Chosen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Torin_the_Chosen "Torin the Chosen"); although [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") able to recruit the latter two will often have access to it anyway.

### Regaining Figures

As **Damage Points** are removed, a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit will potentially regain any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** it has lost, one by one, until eventually it returns to full strength (having as many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** as it had when first created). Because there can only be one damaged [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit at a time, this always occurs after the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is sufficiently reduced to fully heal the previous [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. The next point restored will then revive a missing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

:   For example, take a [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit again, with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 Figures** and [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3 Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. The unit has just suffered [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7** **Damage Points** in a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fire Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Storm "Fire Storm")**, losing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **2 Figures**, with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** #3 also slightly injured. It finds shelter in a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") and, after spending its turn there, is healed of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Damage Point**. This will go towards the injured cavalryman, who is now back to full health.

:   Staying in Town for another turn removes another [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**. However, because the remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** are now at full health, this will actually restore one of the dead cavalrymen, who returns with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1**. Subsequent healing will now go towards bringing this cavalryman back to full health ([Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3**).
    Once he's fully healthy, any additional healing will first restore the
    last missing cavalryman, and then work towards bringing him back to full
    health. In the end, the unit will have all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures** back, with each one being fully healthy ([Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3**) when the unit reaches [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0** recorded **Damage Points**.

Regaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** overland is very straightforward, as the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** counts of units are always dynamically calculated from their base statistics and the **Damage Points** they have logged. In combat though, the game also stores the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
count separately in its "battle unit" records, in addition to the "top
figure damage" also having its own entry. Unfortunately, the interaction
between all of the variables and the [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage")
effect can result in some scenarios that have seemingly been overlooked
by the developers, leading to at least two different bugs related to
regaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in combat. Both of these work in favor of the unit whose [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** are being restored.

#### Life Steal vs Irreversible Damage Bug

:   One of the purposes of Irreversible **Damage Points** is that they are meant to be unhealable during the battle. The restorative effect of [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") is no exception: it can not remove these **Damage Points** either. On the other hand, by design, the restriction that normally disables healing beyond the removable **Damage Points** does not apply in this case. As a result, when all healabe [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is gone, the Life Stealing unit is still allowed to get to the code branch that restores [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in combat. Even a single point of "healing" will then increase the unit's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count, likely also triggering the "combat healing bug" described below.

:   The next instance of "healing" will then immediately restore another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, if there are still more missing, and so on, until the unit regains its full count. Naturally, if it suffers reversible [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** inbetween, that needs to be removed before the bug will trigger again. However, at full [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, the intended effect of [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") also starts working again, and the unit can continue accumulating extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**, despite the fact that it should not actually be at full health, as it has recorded Irreversible **Damage Points**.

:   This makes the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** tracking of the unit inconsistent with itself, and can lead to unpredictable behaviour. The *unit statistics window* will also no longer correctly show [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, as its display is based on "top figure damage". This is not adjusted by the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") attacks that deal Irreversible Damage. At the same time though, removing the healable **Damage Points**
    does reduce it, eventually leading to the unit being apparently
    undamaged. While it may have been intended that Life Stealing units
    should be able to regain [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** or be granted extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** even after losing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** to Irreversible Damage (such as [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Dispel Evil](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil "Dispel Evil")**), there is no indication of this in the game code.

:   Based on the observed interactions between the variables used,
    it is much more likely that this scenario was simply never considered.
    As such, the current efforts to fix this issue focus on disabling [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") Life Steal's ability to heal over the amount of **Damage Points**
    that are actually removable (unless that's all there is), as opposed to
    creating code that would properly implement either of the above
    features. Should a fix like this be applied, it would mean that once the
    unit has suffered any Irreversible Damage, it will no longer be able to
    gain extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** for the remainder of the battle, nor regain any irreversibly lost [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

#### Combat Healing Bug

:   As described above, when executing a [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") attack that brings the unit's **Damage Points** down to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0**, a check is normally performed with any remaining healing. If it is more than the unit's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count, extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** can be granted, equal to this "overhealing" divided by the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count, and rounded down. These are temporary, and last until the end of the battle.

:   However, due to an oversight, the code branch responsible for adding these additional [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** is inadvertantly also executed every time a combat healing effect (including spells and the [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") ability) restores a lost [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
    in a unit. Except in this case, the "top figure damage" is essentially
    substituted for the overheal amount instead. These variables are
    logically the same. If the value is positive, it can be interpreted as
    "top figure damage", whereas a negative number indicates overhealing
    above the unit's maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count. To calculate the amount of extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits**, an absolute value is taken, which allows both signs to exert the same effect in the end.

:   Thus, when the bug triggers, the unit gains [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** equal to its new "top figure damage" divided by its maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**
    count. Naturally, this means it may only have an actual effect for
    units that can have "top figure damage" at least as much as their
    maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count, such as the regenerating [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** with its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10 Hits** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **9 heads**, or the Life Stealing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death Knights](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Knights "Death Knights")** with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8 Hits** but only [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures**. The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**, for example, will gain an extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit** every time a head is regenerated in combat.

:   This bug also causes a discrepancy in recording the unit's **Damage Points** as well. If the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** being restored is not the last one that can be, then the actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** logged (and taken) by the unit becomes less than what is indicated by the "top figure damage", and shown in the *unit statistics window*. Each extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit** gained causes a difference of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** for each remaining restorable [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. To stay with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")**, regaining its 4th head means it has sustained [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10** × [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **60** **Damage Points**, which are just reduced to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **59**. However, because of the bug, 5 missing heads with a "top figure damage" of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **9** is now [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **11** × [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **5** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **9** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **64**. The difference is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5**, one for each head still missing.

:   Despite this though, the unit actually becomes stronger in terms of how much more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** it can take. This is because each extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit** also acts as an immediate effective [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** for each existing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, which can be interpreted as the unit having healed more than what it should have. Looking at the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hydra](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hydra "Hydra")** once more, where it previously had a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **30**, it will effectively have [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **11** × [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **3** + [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **35** after triggering the bug. That's exactly [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **4** more than what it should have gained from [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") Regeneration - one point for each standing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, including the one just restored.
