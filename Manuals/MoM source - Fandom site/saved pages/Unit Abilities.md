**Unit Abilities** are distinguishing properties of a unit, which differentiate it significantly from other units. There is a total of 54 **Unit Abilities**
in the game (or 53, depends on exact definition). Each ability gives
the unit a different advantage. As a result, a unit's set of **Abilities** determines how that unit would perform in various situations, compared to other units.

A list of all 54 **Unit Abilities** can be found [at the bottom of this article](#List_of_Unit_Abilities).

**Note:**  **Unit Abilities** are distinguished from [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") in several ways, particularly that [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") are exclusive to [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), and can be picked as [Random Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities"). **Unit Abilities** are either possessed or can be acquired by almost any unit in the game - including [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

## Concept

In *Master of Magic*, each unit type has a set of properties
that define its strengths and weaknesses. This serves to determine how
well the unit would do in different situations.

The most mundane (and ubiquitous) properties are called
"attributes", and nearly every unit has the same set. Attributes include
[Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**, [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**,
and so on. They are listed clearly at the top of a unit's details
panel, and in many other places too. The value of each "attribute"
determines how well the unit will fare in basic combat.

Additionally to these "mundane" properties, many units also have a set of assorted **Abilities**. These abilities give the unit an "unusual" advantage that most other units don't get at all. The variety of **Unit Abilities** is quite large - including 54 different types of abilities - and each of them gives a different advantage!

For example, one ability may enable a special action for all
units possessing it. Another might give special bonuses that other units
don't get. Yet another ability might even change the way the unit moves
(whether in combat, on the map, or even both).

Thus, a unit's set of **Abilities** often gives it a distinctive advantage in specific situations. For example, a unit whose **Abilities** give it a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
bonus against a specific kind of attack will be very useful when
fighting enemy units that use this attack. A unit that can move quickly
through a certain type of terrain is often used to lead other, slower
units through the same kind of terrain.

With 54 **Unit Abilities** available, studying a unit's **Abilities**
further helps to determine which units you need to get for your army -
and also to figure out the proper uses of each unit you own, in combat
or otherwise.

### A Unit and Its Abilities

A unit gets a set number of **Unit Abilities** the moment it is created. The number and type of these **Abilities** is determined by the game's rules, based entirely on the unit's type.

For example, a newly-created [Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake") unit *always* gets the abilities "[Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity")", "[Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity")" and "[Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") 20", because the game data dictates that it should. The same works in reverse: a newly-created [Troll Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Troll_Spearmen "Troll Spearmen") unit will *never* have [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") or [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath"), because the game data says that it shouldn't.

Once the unit is created, it will normally keep all of its initial **Unit Abilities** for as long as it continues to exist. Of course, many spells and magical effects are designed to add new **Unit Abilities**
to a unit (whether temporary or permanent). Some effects can
temporarily disable one of an enemy unit's abilities. But only one known
effect, in one specific scenario, will actually remove one of a unit's
default abilities (and only to immediately replace it with another
ability!).

Since a unit's **Abilities** (or lack thereof) will often
determine its usefulness to a great extent, it's necessary to understand
how they affect the unit's performance. This is a key factor in all
tactical and strategic considerations in *Master of Magic*.

### The Abilities List

In order to determine which abilities a unit has, all you need to do
is look at the unit's details panel (right click on the unit). The
bottom half of this panel is called the "Abilities List", since it is
mostly dedicated to displaying the unit's **Abilities**, as well as any additional magical effects that the unit is currently enjoying / suffering from.

***Note:**  For [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), the first page of the list shows [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") and [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). Click the "arrow down" button to scroll to the Abilities list.*

Each item on the list includes an icon, a label, and possibly a numeric value. **Unit Abilities** are distinguishable from all other effects in that their icon has a gold-colored background.

The label indicates the name of the ability. The value, if it
exists, indicates the strength of the ability (read more on this below)
where applicable.

A right-click on any ability icon will bring up a short in-game
tooltip. Beware - they are commonly inaccurate, vague, or ambiguous.
Refer to the various [Ability articles](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Abilities "Category:Abilities") for more accurate explanations.

## Unit Abilities vs. Hero Abilities

The game makes a clear distinction between two different categories of **Abilities**: **Unit Abilities** and [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities"). While similar in many ways, the two have several fundamental differences. This article discusses only **Unit Abilities**, so please refer to the [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") article for more information on that subject.

In total, there are 67 **Abilities** in the game - but 13 of these are quite different from the others, *mainly* in that only [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can ever have them (as well as a few other distinctions). They are thus called [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities"), and are discussed separately.

The remaining 54 **Abilities** are called **Unit Abilities**, and are discussed in this article. It's harder to define what **Unit Abilities** are; instead we define them as "not [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities")". They are the exception to the rule, despite being much more numerous.

When looking at a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s **Abilities List** (see above), it is very easy to distinguish the Hero's **Unit Abilities** (if any) from his/her [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") (if any). [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") always have a rust-red background for their icon. **Unit Abilities** have a golden background. There are two exceptions regarding the [Merging](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merging "Merging") and [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") abilities, but that's not very important.

When looking at a non-[Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s **Abilities List**, there will never be any red-backed icon there, since these units cannot ever have [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") (again, [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") is an unusual exception).

## Effects

As repeated several times above, there are 54 **Unit Abilities** and each one has a different effect on the unit possessing it. Thus, each **Unit Ability** gives a different advantage, and makes the unit better than normal in specific situations as a result.

**Unit Abilities** can
be informally grouped into sub-categories based on the type of effect
they give. Many are similar enough to one another that they can be
grouped this way, though again this is informal and subjective.

### Special Attacks and Special Damage

A large number of **Unit Abilities** will give the unit a new [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that it did not previously have. The unit will automatically utilize this attack whenever it makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** against an opponent. Some [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") can also be used when the unit is [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to an enemy assault.

Most [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") are vicious, and will either add a lot of damage output or just force the target to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist**
or die. Very often, the strength of the attack is important (and
possibly variable!), and would normally be indicated after the ability's
name on the [Abilities List](#The_Abilities_List).

Some abilities don't add a new [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") per se, but rather modify the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and/or [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") so that they deliver extra [types of Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"). This makes the basic attack itself more dangerous, in one way or another.

### Immunities

Protective **Unit Abilities** are also quite common, and are actually called "Immunities". Most often, they will boost the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score by a large amount - but only when struck by a certain [type of Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") or a certain [type of Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type").

Some immunities, particularly [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), prevent enemy spells from targeting and/or harming the unit at all. These can be extremely valuable - and suitably rare.

### Modifying Movement

There are a few **Unit Abilities** whose purpose is to change the way the unit moves during combat or on the overland map. They usually do not alter the unit's [Movement Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Type "Movement Type") nor its [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"), instead changing movement costs and/or allowing the unit to ignore certain obstacles.

Very often, such abilities also allow the unit to lead other
units through rough terrain on the overland map - increasing the
movement speed of an entire stack. These units may be valuable in a
strategic sense, providing other units with faster transportation.

### Group Bonuses

Other than stack movement changes (explained above), a few abilities
give bonuses both to the unit itself and to every other unit stacked
with it. An entire army can become more dangerous thanks to just one
unit within it that gives bonuses to everyone else.

In most cases, these bonuses are non-cumulative, which means that
if two or more units in the same army possess that same ability, the
army only enjoys one of their bonuses (the highest).

### Extra Actions

Certain abilities allow a unit to perform an action that is simply
unavailable without that ability. For example, in order to construct a
new [Settlement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Settlement "Settlement"), a unit *must* possess the [Create Outpost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Outpost "Create Outpost") ability. Otherwise, this action is not possible at all.

Units possessing such abilities are often extremely important for properly developing an empire.

### Other Effects

Finally, some abilities have an effect that is so unique that it simply cannot be lumped into the above categories. [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") and [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") are good examples for this, as are [Scouting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Scouting "Scouting"), [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster"),
and so on. They are often very complex to explain, and sometimes
difficult to understand. Nonetheless, they are often also quite
powerful.

## Acquiring Abilities

As stated earlier, each unit is created with a pre-defined set of
default abilities based on its type. However, there are many effects in
the game that can add a new **Unit Ability** to a unit that did not possess it by default.

Not every ability can be added - some are unique to those
creatures that have them by default. For example, all Extra Action
abilities (see list, above) are unique; you'll never be able to add them
to any unit except those that already have them be default.

The most common way to add a new ability to a unit is through [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). When such a spell is legally cast on a unit, it will bestow it a new **Unit Ability** which lasts as long as the spell continues to exist. Most often, it is necessary to pay a per-turn [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") to ensure that the spell does not dissipate. An ability added by a [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") will have a purple background on the [Abilities List](#The_Abilities_List).

Another common way is to add a spell-like [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") to a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), and then give that item to a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). While the [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") is holding this item, he/she acquires the new **Unit Ability**. The ability is lost if the item is ever removed. An ability added by a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") usually doesn't show up on the [Abilities List](#The_Abilities_List) at all, but when it does it will have a brown background.

A less common method includes turning a unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit or a [Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")
unit (there are various ways to do either). In both cases, the unit
receives one or more permanent abilities that it may or may not have had
previously. It is impossible to remove those extra abilities, since it
is impossible to revert a unit from [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") or [Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels") status.

## To Hit Bonus: Not a Unit Ability!

One of the many indicators appearing within a unit's [Abilities List](#The_Abilities_List) is labeled "[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit")", followed be a number, and preceded by a yellow icon of crosshairs:

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit")

When this icon appears, it indicates that the unit is benefiting from a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus, raising its To Hit score above [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%** by the specified amount. Conversely, some ill effects (such as [Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Curse "Curse")) can cause a negative number to follow the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") label, indicating a penalty that has dropped the unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") value below [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%**.

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit"), however, is classified on this wiki as one of the primary unit attributes - i.e. the same as [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack Strength**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**, [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** and so forth. It is even indicated with a custom icon, [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit"), which was designed based on the large yellow icon seen in the game.

When the unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") score is [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30**, it is not displayed at all. Thus referring to it as a **Unit Ability** would put it at odds with all other **Unit Ability**,
making it an uncomfortable exception. It is much easier to treat it as
an attribute - particularly because the vast majority of units have [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30% To Hit** by default, without any icon displayed.

Thus, [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") is not listed above, and is not referred to as a true **Unit Ability**. Instead of saying that a unit has [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%** with a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") ability of strength +2 (which is how it appears in the game), we simply say it has a To Hit score of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **50%**.

## Ranged Attack Ammo: Not a Unit Ability!

Similarly to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") bonuses, as explained above, the number of [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
a unit can make per battle (known as its "Ammunition") is also
displayed as a unit ability, with its own icon, label, and number. The
icon is that of a quiver on a brown background:

[Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")

When this icon appears, it indicates that the unit may only make a limited number of [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") per battle, and the number following the label indicates that exact limit.

Not all units capable of a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") have this icon in their Abilities List. This is because many (but not all!) [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attackers** use their own [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool to pay for their Ranged Attacks, instead of ammunition.

As a result, ammunition is treated on this Wiki as being a unit property. The concept of "ammunition" is explained on the [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") page, as it only applies there.
