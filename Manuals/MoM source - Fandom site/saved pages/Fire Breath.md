[Ability FireBreath](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/8/81/Ability_FireBreath.png/revision/latest?cb=20120128201709)

**Fire Breath** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") found in *Master of Magic*. With this ability, a unit making a voluntary [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") starts this attack with a blast of [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"). It is one of only two [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") available in the game (the other one being [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath")).

The [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of the **Fire Breath** ability is noted after its name (e.g. "Ability FireBreath **Fire Breath** **30**"). This indicates the maximum [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that it can cause (per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit), though this is subjected to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls and the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls.

There are 12 units in the game that possess a **Fire Breath** attack by default. 7 of these are [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") (almost all from the [Draconian](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Draconian "Draconian") [Race](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Race "Race")), 4 are [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**, and one is a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Great Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Drake "Great Drake")** has the strongest **Fire Breath** by far - at a whopping [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **30**! The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** spell can also be cast on a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), and may have some chance of adding the **Fire Breath** attack to that unit. However, [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") cannot acquire **Fire Breath** if they don't already have it.

There are many different modifiers in the game that can increase the strength of a unit's **Fire Breath**. The available modifiers differ based on the unit's type, however, none of them can modify the Attack Strength of **Fire Breath** gained through [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**.

## Description

Fire-breathing monsters have long haunted the nightmares of humans in our world - but in the world of *Master of Magic*
they are quite real. Not only that, but certain races (evolved from
fire-breathing dragon ancestors) take this ability for granted.

**Fire Breath** offers a
great way for a combatant to start its attack. Only those enemies who
aren't burned to a crisp by this deadly breath will be able to stand up
to their attacker in hand-to-hand combat. Thus, the creature delivers a
preemptive strike that can weaken its opponent considerably.

## Effect

**Fire Breath** is automatically used whenever the unit *voluntarily* makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against an opponent. Before the unit commences its actual attack, it will unleash a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") delivering a certain amount of [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"). This occurs before any other exchange of damage or other attacks between the unit and its target. It precedes [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") and any exchange of actual [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage").

As a result, any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the enemy unit that is killed by the **Fire Breath** does not get to retaliate *at all*
- it is already dead by the time its unit begins the retaliation (that
is, if the unit even survived). This confers a great deal of protection
to the **Fire Breath** unit: if it can kill a large number of enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** (or all of them) with the **Fire Breath**, the target can return very little damage at it.

The Breath Attack itself is carried out in a similar fashion to a regular attack. The **Fire Breath** unit makes a number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls equal to the strength of its breath attack. The target then gets to make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls to try and block some of the incoming damage.

Note that **Fire Breath** is *not* used when the unit is [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")
to an enemy assault. It only works when the unit is the one initiating
the Melee Attack. On the other hand, this ability allows a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Ground** unit to engage a [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit in melee combat, even if said unit is actually immune to fire.

### Immunities

Since **Fire Breath** delivers [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"), it is somewhat inferior to [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"): Fire Damage can easily be blocked by either the [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") or [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") abilities. If the target possesses either of these, its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is temporarily raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for the purposes of blocking damage from this attack. Thus, even the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Great Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Drake "Great Drake")**'s impressive [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **30 Fire Breath** would cause only little damage to such a unit. Furthermore, **Fire Breath** is associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**, and thus may be blocked partially or completely by the spells [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements "Resist Elements")**, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")**, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**. This last [Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") actually has the same effect against **Fire Breath** as the immunities mentioned earlier.

## Acquiring Fire Breath

The only way to add the **Fire Breath** ability to a unit that does not already possess it is via the [UnitEnchantment ChaosChannels FireBreath](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/f/fb/UnitEnchantment_ChaosChannels_FireBreath.png/revision/latest?cb=20120201231842) [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Channels** effect. This can be obtained either through the spell of the same name, or as a result of the [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")**.

### Chaos Channels

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") that can be cast on friendly [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"); all other units, including the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"),
and units already affected by Chaos Channels, are invalid targets. The
spell may only be cast on the overland map, for a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**. It has two effects on its target:

1. Adding one (and only one) of three powerful bonuses at random.
2. Permanently associating the unit with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**.

One of the three available bonuses is **Fire Breath** **2**. It is only available for selection by the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** spell if the unit does not already possess a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") or **Fire Breath** ability - otherwise another bonus will be selected.

On the other hand, if the unit had the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability before this change, a new **Fire Breath** ability will *replace this entirely*. This may be seen as somewhat disappointing, since there are no immunities that avert [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack** damage, and they are also easier to improve. However, it's worth noting that players using the unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch do not have to worry about this oversight, as it forces the game to choose a different effect for [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") Thrown units instead (as it normally does for every other type of ranged attack).

Once a unit has been altered by [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**, its new **Fire Breath** ability is entirely permanent, with a fixed [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of **2**. The spell requires no additional [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"), and the change to the unit may never be undone.

### Doom Mastery

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") that can be cast on the overland map for a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,100**. While this spell is in effect, whenever a new [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") is produced in any of the caster's towns, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")** will automatically apply [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Channels** on that unit - for no extra charge. The resulting effects on the unit are identical to those described above.

## Improving Fire Breath

There are several methods of improving a unit's **Fire Breath**. The availability of these depends on the type of unit involved, and may affect either the ability's [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**, or both.

### Experience

First and foremost, for [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), the strength of the **Fire Breath** ability will improve as the unit gains [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**. It is one of the only [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") in the game that does so. For Normal Units, **Fire Breath** improves at a rate of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+1** for every 2 levels - meaning [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+1** at level 2 ([Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Regular**), [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+2** at level 4 ([Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite**), and [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+3** at level 6 ([Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Champion**). For [Fang the Draconian](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fang_the_Draconian "Fang the Draconian"), the **Fire Breath** ability improves by [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+1** for every [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level** (above first).

Experience further improves these units' chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**, and this will also apply to **Fire Breath**. Each [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1** represents an extra 10% chance per [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") to cause [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** (on top of the base 30%). For Normal Units, this attribute improves by [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1** at every level above 3rd (only the first of which can be obtained without the [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") retort or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade")** spell); while Heroes gain a natural [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1** on every 3rd level instead (up to a maximum of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+3** at [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Demigod** level). Unlike modifiers to Attack Strength, modifiers [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** do apply to the **Fire Breath** of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** units, and present the only practical way of improving this attack.

### Heroes

The [Might](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Might "Might") [Might](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Might "Might") ability *does not* increase the strength **Fire Breath**. However, [Leadership](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Leadership "Leadership") [Leadership](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Leadership "Leadership") does help - it can increase not only a Hero's own **Fire Breath**, but also that of any Normal Unit or other Hero accompanying them into battle. However, Leadership has no effect on either [Fantastic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** units, making [Fang the Draconian](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fang_the_Draconian "Fang the Draconian") the only actual Hero whose **Fire Breath** can ever benefit from it.

With the basic version of [Leadership](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Leadership "Leadership") Leadership, this bonus is [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+1**, and starts to apply once the Hero reaches Level 6 ([Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Lord**). It does not improve beyond +1. Heroes with [Super](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Super_Ability "Super Ability") Leadership attain the same bonus at Level 4 instead ([Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Commander**), and can improve its magnitude to [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+2** if they manage to reach Level 8 ([Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Superhero**).

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses from the [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") [Blademaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blademaster "Blademaster") (or its [Super](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Super_Ability "Super Ability") version) and [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") abilities both apply to a Hero's **Fire Breath** attack. Akin to natural [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** modifiers, this holds true even for **Fire Breath** gained through [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**.

Most [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") have no effect on **Fire Breath**. The exception is [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry"),
which can be enchanted to provide an all-round Attack Strength boost.
This affects every type of attack that the Hero makes, including [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**. On the other hand, [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses on such an accessory *do not* apply, unless the game is played using the [unofficial 1.40 Insecticide patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") (or [later](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50")).

### Node Aura Bonus

Whenever a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** Realm enters battle in a [Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node") or its area of influence, it will enjoy an Aura Bonus granted by the [Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node "Node"). This also applies to any [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") mutated by the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**
spell (see above). The bonus is transient however: it applies only
while the unit is doing combat within the vicinity of the Node. It is
not applied when doing combat anywhere else.

This Node Aura bestows a **+2** bonus to many of the properties of any [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** Creature affected by it. This includes the unit's **Fire Breath**, if it has one. It's a small bonus, but is nonetheless welcome - and can be quite beneficial to **Fire-Breathing** [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, for whom the bonus is applied to each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s attack.

### Chaos Surge

[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Surge](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Surge "Chaos Surge")** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") with a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,000**, and a relatively high [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **40** per turn. This is for good reason though: as long as this spell is active, any [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** (or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**) creature entering battle anywhere in the game world (on either plane) receives a **+2** bonus to all of their [Attack Strengths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), including a [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **+2** to **Fire Breath**.
This effect is fully cumulative with the Node Aura described above,
effectively doubling its magnitude with respect to Attack Strength.

The only downside of this spell is that it indiscriminately
applies to both friendly and enemy units. However, two instances of the
spell (cast by different [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard")) do not stack, the effect will only be applied once per battle (but will always affect both sides).

## Hurting an Enemy's Fire Breath

There are several spells in the game that can reduce the strength of an enemy's **Fire Breath**, thus reducing the danger from that enemy unit. This is the only area where [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** **Fire Breath** actually has an advantage: its fixed [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of **2** is not subject to any of these reductions either.
