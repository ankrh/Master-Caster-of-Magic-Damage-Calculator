**Blur** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") belonging to the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery Magic** realm. For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25**
it may be cast during combat to give a lasting effect.
It blurs the outline of all friendly units making them more difficult to
hit. There is a 10% chance that each point of combat damage done by
enemy units will miss. This happens before shields are applied. Any [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") and [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") is affected by **Blur**.

This effect is weaker than inflicting a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** penalty. For example consider a unit with a 50% hit chance ([To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+20% To Hit**).
With -10% To Hit penalty, hit chance is reduced to 40% = 50% - 10%.
With Blur, hit chance is reduced to 45% = 0.9 \* 50%.

## Usage

**Blur** may be cast during combat for a rather high [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25**. It will immediately affect all eligible enemy units.

After casting the spell, its icon will appear in the
"Enchantments" window underneath the casting wizard's name. The spell
will remain in effect until the end of battle, or until dispelled by the
opponent. Since it does not carry on after the end of the battle, **Blur** has no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

## Effect

**Blur** inserts an extra step into the resolution of any combat attack damage against friendly units. This may cause some of the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to be averted (negated) before any [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense Rolls** are actually made. This extra step is executed every time an enemy unit performs an [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") to determine the unmitigated [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that its attack can cause to a friendly target. **Blur**'s effect thus occurs between the Attack- and the [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll").

When **Blur** is triggered, the game generates a random number between 1 and 10 for each point of raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that the attack would inflict. Each "roll" that comes up as a "10" (10% chance) will negate that specific point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**,
reducing the total by one. The success chance of these rolls may
unfortunately not be modified in any way, and is always 10% regardless
of any other effects or circumstance.

This spell strictly only affects the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** of combat attacks performed by opposing units. It works against [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attacks** (including [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")); [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (all types: [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack"), [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack"), and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack")); and short range attacks ([Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire-** and [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Lightning Breath**, and the hidden component of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")).

**Blur** does not work against [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze"), [Chaos-powered](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_(Item_Power) "Chaos (Item Power)") weapons, or any other source of [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage"), which means it will also not protect units under the effect of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Sleep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sleep "Black Sleep")** from any type of attack (even those listed above). It will also not negate any type of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage** or [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"), even if that [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is caused by an ability possessed by an opposing unit (such as [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation")). Similarly, it has no effect on [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** caused by direct damage spells even if they are cast by enemy units rather than a [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") (e.g. [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")).

On the other hand, **Blur** has no conflict with other "tandem damage" types ([Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") and [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing")), and will work against attacks using these components so long as they fall into the categories mentioned above.

## Acquisition

As an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** spell, **Blur** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least one [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is almost never guaranteed.

Customized Wizards possessing [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**11** Spellbooks at game creation may choose **Blur** as one of their starting spells, in which case it will be available for casting immediately on the first turn.

Wizards who possess at least [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**8** Spellbooks, or Wizards with [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**11** Spellbooks who did not select **Blur** as a guaranteed spell, will be able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") this spell at some point during their campaign. Wizards with fewer than [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**8** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to Research it. The chance for this spell to appear increases with the number of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or obtains during gameplay.

**Blur** has a base [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **300**.

With at least [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**1** Spellbook, the **Blur** spell *may be* acquired as a reward for winning encounters in creature [Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lair "Lair"), [Towers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower"), et cetera, or when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival wizard who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

**Blur** has a rather small effect for its cost. [Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")
only costs 5 mana more, but typically provides at least twice the
defense boost, plus offense and resistance boosts as well. However, **Blur** comes from a different realm and has less than half the research cost of **Prayer**.
For that matter, the spell is accessible to Sorcerers with Death
spellbooks in their profile (whereas they will not be able to use **Prayer** under any circumstances).

In an important battle, or with no other defensive spell options, **Blur** is still definitely better than nothing.

## Known Bugs

In the official game (and also in [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")), **Blur** suffers from two different bugs. First, every time **Blur** successfully averts a point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**,
it will skip the roll for the next point. This means that it can, at
most, block only 50% of the total, no matter how lucky the rolls
actually are.

Second, **Blur** is (rightfully) supposed to be ignored by units with [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"). However, when checking for this, the game looks for the immunity on the *defending* unit instead of the attacker, as it should. This essentially means that friendly units with [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") Illusions Immunity do not gain any benefit from **Blur** at all, while the opponents' immunities are ignored completely.

The [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") corrects both of these bugs (W156FIX.TXT).
