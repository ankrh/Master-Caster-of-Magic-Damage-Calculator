**Blizzard Damage** is one of the many [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") in *Master of Magic*.

**Blizzard Damage** is essentially [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"). This type of damage is unique in that it makes separate [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against *each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure*** in the enemy unit, instead of attacking only the "lead" figure (as would happen with [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") types). The entire process is extremely complex, but the end result is that the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** there are in the enemy unit, the more damage it will suffer on average.

**Blizzard Damage** is distinct from the other [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") type, called [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage"), in that it will trigger a target's [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") (if the target possesses this immunity), and will not trigger a target's [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"). Note that either of the two [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") types will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity").

The name **Blizzard Damage**
was chosen for this damage type because it best described its effect -
despite the fact that the term "Blizzard" is not used anywhere in the
game.

**Blizzard Damage** is delivered only by one source: the [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") spell.

## Effect

**Blizzard Damage** is a type of [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"), and as such is processed in a very complex way. Its purpose is to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target, but the way in which the game determines how many [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** are inflicted is not easy to explain. For a more thorough explanation, see [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage").

The results of this damage greatly depend on whether or not the target is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**, and if so, it depends on how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** the target contains.

### Against Single-Figure Units

When **Blizzard Damage** strikes a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure Unit**, it behaves identically to [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") (in particular, a sub-type called [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage")).

This starts with a single set of X [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against the target unit, where X is the strength of the **Blizzard Damage** attack. Each roll that comes up *equal to or lower than* 30 is considered a "registered hit" against the target. The number of registered hits is tallied up and recorded.

Next up, the target unit makes a single set of X [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, where X is the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. The result of each roll is compared to the target's [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** score, with each roll coming up *equal to or lower than* that score being considered a "successful block". The number of successful blocks is tallied up and recorded.

The game then subtracts the number of successful blocks from the number of successful hits. This is the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** caused to the target. If the total amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to a target (including damage carried over from previous injuries) surpasses the target's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health**, then the target unit is destroyed.

For a more thorough overview of this process, check out the article on [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). Note again that this applies only when the target is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**!

### Against Multi-Figure Units

**Blizzard Damage** behaves very differently whenever it strikes a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** target. In this case, it behaves almost as though several separate attacks were directed against *each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit* separately! The process is very complex to understand, and so is covered in full in the [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") article. Nonetheless we'll attempt to explain it briefly below.

To start, the attack makes X [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit, where X is the strength of the **Blizzard Damage** attack. Each roll that comes up *equal to or higher than* 30 is considered a "registered hit", but only against the specific [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** it was directed against. Already this is very different from [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), where all [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls are directed against the "lead" figure in the target unit. The game records the number of hits successfully registered *against each individual figure*, separately.

Next up, each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit makes X [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, where X is equal to the target unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. The result of each roll is compared to the unit's [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** score, with each roll that comes up *equal to or lower than*
that score being considered a "successful block". Again, the game
remembers the total number of successful blocks made by each figure,
separately.

To calculate [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**,
the game subtracts each figure's successful blocks from the number of
hits registered against this specific figure. This is why hits and
blocks were recorded separately. The result is the amount of "unblocked
hits" directed at each specific figure. The game also makes sure to
limit each of these results to between 0 and the figure's current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health** (no figure can suffer less than [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0 Damage**, and no figure can suffer more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** than it has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**).

Finally, all of these "unblocked hits" are pooled together. The sum of these unblocked hits is the total amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to the target. This is applied one point at a time, starting with the "lead" figure in the unit. It will kill off [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as necessary, and will stop only when either all "unblocked hits" have been applied, or once the target has lost all of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**.

The result is usually massive damage to the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**. If the **Blizzard Damage** was powerful enough, and the rolls involved were not particularly in the target's favor, **Blizzard Damage** can wipe out an entire Multi-Figure unit instantaneously - something which [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") is highly unlikely to achieve.

Again, for a full analysis of this process, refer to the article on [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"), which explains this in much greater detail, including examples.

## Immunities

**Blizzard Damage** is similar to [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage") in that it will always trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") or [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), if the target possesses either of these.

In case the target possesses *only* [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is temporarily raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for purposes of blocking this **Blizzard Damage**. This will likely block any **Blizzard Damage** directed at the unit. The chance of any damage from an [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") getting through this immunity is astronomically low.

If the target possesses [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") however (with or without [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity")), then **Blizzard Damage** will cause absolutely no harm to that target under any circumstances.

## Sources of Blizzard Damage

There is exactly one source of **Blizzard Damage** to be found in the game. It is the [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") spell, a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") belonging to the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature Realm**.

For a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **200**, this spell will deliver a **Blizzard Damage** attack against each and every enemy unit inside a targeted overland map tile. Each unit is struck with exactly 6 points of **Blizzard Damage**.

This spell is particularly useful against large enemy armies, since the more units present the more **Blizzard Damage** is delivered overall. It is even more powerful if the enemy army contains several [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, since these tend to suffer much more from **Blizzard Damage** than you'd normally expect. However, an attack of only [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **6** is fairly weak against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure units**, and is unlikely to kill any of them, particularly heavily-armored ones. [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") is often used to weaken an enemy army prior to actual battle.
