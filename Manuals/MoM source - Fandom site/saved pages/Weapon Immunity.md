[Ability WeaponImmunity](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/5/5a/Ability_WeaponImmunity.png/revision/latest?cb=20120121180202)

**Weapon Immunity** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

When a unit with **Weapon Immunity** is subjected to non-magical [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") is raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** for the purpose of blocking this [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. This makes the unit more resilient against most attacks made by such units.

There are many effects that can alter a unit's [Weapon Quality](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Normal_Weapons "Attack Strength") to allow it to get through **Weapon Immunity**. Even without these, setting the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** may not be much of an improvement, making this a comparably weak immunity. However, the fact that it scales well with the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** counts of both the attacker and the defender still makes it quite powerful in the early game.

**Weapon Immunity** is not a very common immunity, but is still possessed by 7 different [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), almost all from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**. It may also be acquired through the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form")** spells, or the [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") of the same name (in the case of [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")).

## Description

Though cold steel weapons are often dangerous to anyone at the
receiving end, the existence of mystical entities and defensive spells
will occasionally render simple steel insufficient. Whether a creature
is magically protected, or simply is not tangible enough to be hurt by a
normal weapon, it will be hard to kill such an opponent without magic
of one's own.

Creatures with **Weapon Immunity**
therefore hold a serious advantage against "mundane" enemies, taking
little or no damage from their attacks. Fortunately, there is more than
one method to imbue weapons with magical energy, so that they can hurt
their target properly.

## Effect

**Weapon Immunity** is a passive ability that protects a unit from regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. The majority of [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") deliver only this type by default, with no other special properties attached. When such units make a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-**, [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile**, or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder** attack against a target possessing **Weapon Immunity**, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is raised to exactly [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** (if it is lower). This allows the defender to make 10 [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls, nullifying, on average, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** from each attack.

[Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** is actually a high amount, even compared to the toughest [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), but it is not normally enough to block all damage from any serious attack. Many experienced high-tier [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") can deliver more than [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, and can thus get through the Immunity. Still, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** is better than what most units have by default.

Note that the **Weapons-Immune** unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is raised *only* for the duration of resolving this [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, and will return to normal immediately afterward. This ability also does not apply at all against [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, or spells of any kind. However, if the attacker is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit, the increase in [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** does work separately against each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**'s attack.

From the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") onwards, **Weapon Immunity** also works against [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**. However, due to a still persitent bug, four generic Normal Units will still ignore it on *all* of their attacks. These are [Triremes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Trireme "Trireme"), [Galleys](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Galley "Galley"), [Warships](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warship "Warship"), and [Catapults](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult").

### Defeating Weapon Immunity

As stated above, **Weapon Immunity** only triggers against [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") - [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") and [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") bypass it by default. There are several ways to improve the quality of a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit")'s weapons, thus enabling their attacks to ignore **Weapon Immunity** entirely.

Below is a list of effects that will alter a unit's weapons this way:

* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons**, [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Mithril Weapons**, or [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Adamantium Weapons**, which require the [Alchemy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemy "Alchemy") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or an [Alchemists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild");
* [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")**, a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") castable both in- and outside of combat;
* [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Flame Blade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flame_Blade "Flame Blade")**, an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") Unit Enchantment, also castable both in- and outside of combat;
* [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Metal Fires](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Metal_Fires "Metal Fires")**, a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") that will affect all friendly units;
* [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon")** (Common), or its [Global](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") version [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Arms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Arms "Holy Arms")** ([Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell"));
* [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")** (Uncommon), [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** (Uncommon), and a number of various [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death**-related spells and effects (most of which result in creating [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")), which all turn Normal Units into [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature").

Note that in all of these cases, the unit is not actually delivering [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") (which would be blocked by [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity")), but rather a special kind of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") that simply does not trigger the effect of **Weapon Immunity**. All of these effects apply to every [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") that the unit can make.

## Acquiring Weapon Immunity

There are several ways to add **Weapon Immunity** to a unit that does not already have it. This includes:

* Casting [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** on the unit.
* Casting [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form")** on the unit.
* Equipping a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") with a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") that contains the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability_(Item_Power) "Invulnerability (Item Power)")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraithform](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraithform_(Item_Power) "Wraithform (Item Power)")** [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power").
* Casting the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Lycanthropy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lycanthropy "Lycanthropy")** spell on [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), turning them into [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves")**.

Each of these methods adds the same kind of **Weapon Immunity** described above in this article.

### Invulnerability Unit Enchantment

[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). When cast on a unit, it will add the **Weapon Immunity** ability to that unit, as well as another (much more important) effect.

When cast on the overland map, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **200**. It has a lingering effect, so that the targeted unit will keep its [Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability") [Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability") effect so long as the spell continues to exist. While it exists, the spell requires an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5** to be paid at the start of each turn.

Alternatively, it is possible to cast [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** during combat as an impromptu Unit Enchantment. In this case the spell's basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") is reduced to [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **40**,
but its effects will only last until the end of the battle. Since
neither the spell nor the effect will carry over to the overland map,
there is no associated Upkeep Cost with this version of the spell.

Note that a unit with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** also receives a [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** point *reduction* from any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** inflicted on it (with the exception of [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage")) - in fact, this is the *primary* benefit of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")**.

### Wraith Form Unit Enchantment

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form")** is another [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). This also combines the **Weapon Immunity** ability with another effect which, in this case, is the addition of the [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal") [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal") trait to the enchanted unit.

When cast on the overland map, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form")** has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **150**. It has a lingering effect, so that the targeted unit will keep its [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") effect so long as the spell continues to exist. To stop it from dissipating, the spell requires an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **3** to be paid at the start of each turn.

Alternatively, it is possible to cast [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form")** during combat as an impromptu Unit Enchantment as well. In this case the spell's basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") is only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **30**, but its effect will always end when the battle is finished. As such, this version has no Upkeep Cost either.

### Item Powers

A permanent version of either the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form")** spell effects can be imbued into any [Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shield "Shield"), [Chainmail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chainmail "Chainmail"), [Platemail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Platemail "Platemail") or [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") piece. When a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wears this item, he/she receives the **Weapon Immunity** ability (as well the additional effects of these spells noted above).

During [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting"), [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability_(Item_Power) "Invulnerability (Item Power)")** is only available to [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") possessing at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**5 Life** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), while [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Wraithform](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraithform_(Item_Power) "Wraithform (Item Power)")** requires [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**4 Death** books instead. Otherwise, they may not be selected for enchanting. Of course, it is always possible to be offered an item by [Merchants](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant "Merchant") that already possesses one of these traits (finding them in [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") typically imposes much worse Spellbook requirements in v1.31, as listed in the articles of these [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power")).

The **Weapon Immunity** ability bestowed by items is transient: it will disappear if the Hero ever removes or loses the enchanted item.

### Lycanthropy and Werewolves

This is not a true method of adding **Weapon Immunity** to an existing unit, but should be mentioned nonetheless.

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Lycanthropy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lycanthropy "Lycanthropy")** is an [Uncommon Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell"), that is a unique mix of a [Summoning Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell") and an [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell"). When cast on a friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") on the overland map, it will permanently turn that unit into [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves")**.

After transformation, the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves")** are essentially an entirely different unit - which possesses **Weapon Immunity** among its many other abilities. Therefore, instead of actually adding **Weapon Immunity** to a Normal Unit, it replaces the unit entirely with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves")** - who always possess **Weapon Immunity**.

Still, when fighting enemies relying primarily on Normal Units without access to [Alchemists' Guilds](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild") (as will always be the case with [Raiders](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Raiders "Raiders"), for example, or opponents relying heavily on [Mercenaries](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mercenaries "Mercenaries")), a bunch of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves")** may fare better against them than most Normal Units, thanks primarily to **Weapon Immunity**.

## Known Bugs

Due to a coding error in the official versions of the game, **Weapon Immunity** has no effect against [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** attacks, despite the fact that it is supposed to. For example, a [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Recruit** level [Barbarian Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Barbarian_Spearmen "Barbarian Spearmen") unit will barely hit [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves")** with its [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). On the other hand, its [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** attack will have fairly high chances of inflicting [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to them anyway. This bug is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") (W152FIX.TXT). Unfortunately, the combination of this patch with [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")'s flawed fix of the next bug has an unintentionally drastic outcome with regards to [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** attacks versus **Weapon Immunity** (when it applies).

In the official game, if a unit with **Weapon Immunity** also possesses [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity"), the unit's Defense will only be raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** against normal [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Missiles**, instead of the expected [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**, as **Weapon Immunity** mistakenly overwrites the stronger ability in this case. The unofficial patch [1.40](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") fixes this bug, but introduces a new one in exchange: when **Weapon Immunity** is applied against non-[Melee](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") attacks, it will raise Defense to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** instead of its usual [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10**. This will affect any [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder** attacks and, in v1.50, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** attacks, made by units with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Normal Weapons**. This bug is, in turn, fixed in v1.51 (W637FIX.TXT).

Finally, **Weapon Immunity** has no effect against attacks made by "generic" units ([Triremes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Trireme "Trireme"), [Galleys](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Galley "Galley"), [Warships](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warship "Warship"), and [Catapults](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult")), regardless of [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type"). Yet, it does work against the same attacks made by [Air Ships](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Ship "Air Ship"), [Steam Cannons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Steam_Cannon "Steam Cannon"), and [Golems](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Golem "Golem"). This oversight is also corrected in v1.51 (W635FIX.TXT).
