[Ability Invisibility](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/2/23/Ability_Invisibility.png/revision/latest?cb=20120121180155)

**Invisibility** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

Rivals cannot see an **Invisible** unit on the overland map. During combat, the presence and position of an **Invisible**
unit is normally unknown to the enemy as long as the unit is at least 1
tile away from any enemy unit (i.e. not adjacent). This allows **Invisible** units to maneuver easily around the enemy lines. Furthermore, the **Invisible** unit cannot be targeted by any [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") or spells, at least while its position is still unknown.

Finally, whenever an **Invisible** unit is attacked, the attacker suffers a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** penalty, reducing overall damage output.

There are many special rules regarding this ability, particularly how it behaves when enemy units on the battlefield possess [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"). Please read the article below carefully.

4 different units possess **Invisibility** by default. 2 of these are [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), one is a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), and one is a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

It is possible to add **Invisibility** to any unit that does not possess it, by casting the [Invisibility Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)") on that unit. Certain [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can be imbued with **Invisibility** and given to [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") to turn them invisible as well.

## Description

Illusion is one of the most basic forms of magic. While causing
something to disappear can be seen as a neat trick, in the world of *Master of Magic* it is not only a viable tactic, but a potential strategic tool.

**Invisible** creatures make excellent scouts, operating in
enemy territory without being noticed. On the battlefield, they can
sneak around stronger enemy units to hit weaker targets, then disappear
back into thin air - leaving the enemy baffled and uneven. A single **Invisible** unit can thus make a great difference.

## Effect

**Invisibility** is one of the more complex abilities in the game,
since it has many different effects. Furthermore, its interaction with
enemy units possessing [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") merits its own discussion.

In general, **Invisibility**
prevents the enemy from spotting a unit, both on the battlefield and on
the overland map. Until it is right on top of the enemy, the enemy
doesn't even know it's there. Furthermore, even when combat erupts with
an invisible unit, its translucence makes it much harder to hit.

### Undetectability

An **Invisible** unit is undetectable by the enemy, especially on the overland map. Normally, an **Invisible** unit is also undetectable on the battlefield, but must reveal itself when preparing to attack.

While on the overland map, an **Invisible** unit is completely hidden from the enemy. No sort of skill or magical effect can reveal such a unit. Even if the **Invisible**
unit is in an army stack, any rival looking at the stack cannot see it
in the unit list. Note however that if an enemy army moves into a tile
containing an **Invisible** unit, battle will ensue as normal.

During battle, the **Invisible** unit is hidden from the enemy
as well - but only as long as it is not adjacent to any enemy unit
(including diagonals). Therefore, as long as the **Invisible** unit
stays far enough from enemy units, it remains undetected. Since its
position is unknown, the enemy cannot chase it, attack it, or target it
with any spells.

Whenever the **Invisible** unit is adjacent to an enemy unit,
it becomes semi-transparent. The enemy army can now see its position,
and can direct its units to move closer and engage. A semi-transparent
unit can be targeted by spells, but cannot be targeted by any [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (more on this below).

### No Targeting

On the overland map, an **Invisible** unit cannot be directly
targeted by any enemy spell. The game does not acknowledge the unit's
presence, so it is not a valid target.

During combat, the same rule applies as long as the unit is completely invisible. Once the **Invisible** unit moves adjacent to an enemy unit, it becomes semi-transparent and may be directly targeted like any other unit.

In both cases (combat and overland), spells that do not directly
target the unit will still affect it. This includes spells that target
the tile underneath the unit, target all units of a certain type, or
have a global effect that applies to all units on the map/battlefield.

Furthermore, during combat it is not possible for any unit to make a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") at an **Invisible**
or even semi-transparent unit. If you attempt to make such an attack,
the game will throw a red error box, saying that the attack is not
possible.

**Invisible** units may be targeted by [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), but as explained below, this still carries some penalty.

### To Hit Penalties

Whenever a non-[Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell** attack is made against an **Invisible** unit, the attacker suffers a temporary penalty of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit**.
This effectively reduces the attacker's average damage output.
Depending on the strength of the attack, it can be quite a substantial
reduction.

Attacks that do not make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit rolls**, such as [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") or [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze"), are naturally not affected by this penalty.

### Illusions Immunity

The [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") ability, whether innate or bestowed by the [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") spell, will negate the effects of **Invisibility** during combat:

* If any live enemy unit possesses [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"), the enemy can see the position of all **Invisible** units on the battlefield. Any **Invisible** unit appears semi-transparent, and can be targeted by spells. If all enemy [Illusions-Immune](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") units are killed off, the **Invisible** units regain their **Invisibility** as per the rules explained earlier.
* An [Illusions-Immune](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") unit can make [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") against any **Invisible** unit - disregarding the targeting rules explained above. However, units without [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") still cannot target the **Invisible** unit.
* When an [Illusions-Immune](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") unit makes any attack against an **Invisible** unit, it does not suffer the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties described above.

As a result, access to [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") or the [True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight") spell are very important when engaging in warfare against wizards who like using **Invisible** units. Even one [Illusions-Immune](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") unit in an army can make a big difference.

## Units with Default Invisibility

There are exactly 4 units in the game that possess **Invisibility** by default. They include 2 [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), one [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), and one [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"):

[Air Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Elemental "Air Elemental") [Air Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Elemental "Air Elemental")
[Night Stalker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Night_Stalker "Night Stalker") [Night Stalker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Night_Stalker "Night Stalker")
[Nightblades](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nightblades "Nightblades") [Nightblades](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nightblades "Nightblades")
[Shin Bo the Ninja](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shin_Bo_the_Ninja "Shin Bo the Ninja") [Shin Bo the Ninja](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shin_Bo_the_Ninja "Shin Bo the Ninja")

**Invisibility** works the same way for all the above. Both the [Air Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Elemental "Air Elemental") and [Shin Bo](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shin_Bo "Shin Bo") can be quite dangerous thanks to this ability.

## Acquiring Invisibility

There are three known ways of adding **Invisibility** to a unit that does not already possess it by default:

* By casting the [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") on a unit.
* By casting the [Mass Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Invisibility "Mass Invisibility") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") during battle.
* By equipping a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") with a [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") piece imbued with the "Invisibility" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power").

All methods provide the same kind of **Invisibility** as described above in this article.

### Invisibility Unit Enchantment

[Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)") is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") belonging to the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** realm. When cast on a unit, it will add the **Invisibility** ability to that unit.

When cast on the overland map, **Invisibility** has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **175**. It has a lingering effect, so that the targeted unit will keep its **Invisibility** ability so long as the spell continues to exist. While it exists, the spell requires an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** to be paid at the start of each turn.

Alternatively, it is possible to cast **Invisibility** during combat as an impromptu [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). In this case the spell's basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") is only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **35**,
but its effects will only last until the end of the battle (or until
dispelled by the enemy). Since neither the spell nor the effect will
carry over to the overland map, there is no associated [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") with this version of the spell.

### Mass Invisibility Combat Enchantment

[Mass Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Invisibility "Mass Invisibility") is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") belonging to the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** realm. It is cast on the battlefield rather than at any specific unit. While active, this enchantment gives **Invisibility** to each and every friendly unit on the battlefield.

[Mass Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Invisibility "Mass Invisibility") has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **80**, regardless of how many units will feel its effect. Thus, it is cheaper (and faster) to cast this spell than to cast **Invisibility** separately on three or more units. If you only want to enchant one unit, use [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)") (see above).

[Mass Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mass_Invisibility "Mass Invisibility") lasts only until the end of combat, and as such has no associated [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

### Invisibility Item Power

A permanent version of the [Invisibility Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)") can be imbued into a [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") piece. When a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wears this item, he/she receives the **Invisibility** ability.

During [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting"), it is only possible to select the "Invisibility" bonus if you possess at least [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**5 Sorcery** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
Otherwise, "Invisibility" is not available. Of course, it is always
possible to find an item already enchanted with "Invisibility" as reward
for defeating an [Encounter](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter").

The **Invisibility** ability bestowed by an item is transient: it will disappear if the Hero ever removes or loses the enchanted item.
