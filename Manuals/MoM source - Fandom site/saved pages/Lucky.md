**Lucky** is the name of two identical abilities in *Master of Magic*. One is a [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability"), while the other is a [Hero Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Ability "Hero Ability") which might randomly appear, but they have the same exact effect.

A unit or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") possessing the **Lucky** ability receives a bonus of exactly [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit**, [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **+10% To Block**, and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1 Resistance**. Any [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") on the **Lucky** unit is also carried out at [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** (a separate and distinct effect from the unit's own bonus **To Block**). **Lucky**
therefore enhances the unit's chance to succeed in various die rolls it
might be required to perform, slightly increasing both damage output
and survivability.

Only 6 [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") possess the **Lucky** ability by default, all belonging to the [Halfling](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halfling "Halfling") [Race](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Race "Race"). Any [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") that has at least one [Random Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Ability "Random Ability") pick may also occasionally acquire **Lucky** (25 Heroes in all).

The **Lucky** ability does not improve with [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience"), but its percentage-based bonuses mean that improving the unit's attack and defense ratings (whether through [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") or by other means) also improves the total benefit from this ability.

There is no known way of adding **Lucky** to a unit that did not start the game with this ability.

## Description

Authorities on cosmic laws suggest that the race is neither to the
swift, nor the battle to the strong, but that sometimes all it takes is
just pure luck. In *Master of Magic*, the **Lucky** ability could be a mark of divine favor or a naturally-developed gift for making the most of circumstances.

[Halflings](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halflings "Halflings")
are inherently lucky creatures. In a straight reenactment of fantasy
tradition, they can in fact be led by a wizard to steer the course of a
war. From time to time, lucky heroes may also petition to join a
wizard's cause who, like the Halflings, also seem to have a strange
accord with the wheels of destiny, one that drives their abilities
beyond their normal limits.

## Effect

The [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") and [Hero Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Ability "Hero Ability") called "Lucky" work in exactly the same way. These abilities tip various random rolls in favor of their owner during combat.

### To Hit Bonus

Any unit possessing the **Lucky** ability receives a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus. This means that whenever the unit has to make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** delivered to a target, the unit will win more of these rolls than it would without the **Lucky** ability, thus inflicting more damage on average.

[To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls are used primarily when calculating the damage output of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") and [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") also require [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls for damage calculations, but it is currently unknown whether the **Lucky** ability influences these as well.

A unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") score starts at [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%** by default, and may be adjusted by various magical effects, [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level"), [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") (for [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")) and innate bonuses. A unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") score is always measured in increments of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **10%**.

When the unit attacks, it makes a number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
rolls equal to its attack strength. Each roll results in a number
between 1 and 100. For each roll that comes up equal to or lower than
the unit's **To Hit** score, the unit inflicts 1 registered hit on the target - causing [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 point of Damage** if the target fails to (or cannot) block it.

As a result, adding [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** allows the unit to inflict more damage, since it will statistically win more **To Hit** rolls. Also, if the unit's attack strength is increased, the overall benefit from the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonus increases as well, since it is essentially a percentage-based bonus.

Note that this bonus can raise a unit's **To Hit** score above 100%. Values over 100% are effectively the same as [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100%** - meaning that the unit delivers exactly as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** as its attack strength. Points above 100% are only good for redundancy.

### To Block Bonus

Any unit possessing the **Lucky** ability receives a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **+10% To Block** bonus. This means that whenever the unit has to make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls to determine the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** it has blocked from any direct-damage attack, the unit will win more of these rolls than it would without the **Lucky** ability, thus averting more damage on average.

[Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls are made whenever an attack delivers [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to the unit (though some exceptions apply). The unit makes a number of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls equal to its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. Each roll results in a number between 1 and 100. For each roll that comes up equal to or lower than the unit's [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** score, the unit averts [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 point of incoming Damage**. It can potentially block *all* incoming damage this way.

Almost all units possess a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30% To Block** chance, which means that they will normally block an amount of damage equal to 30% of the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. **Lucky** is one of the very-few effects that change a unit's [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** score. A unit's **To Block** score is always measured in increments of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **10%**.

As a result, adding [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **+10%** allows the unit to avert more damage, since it will statistically win more [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls. Also, if the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is increased, the overall benefit from the [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** bonus increases as well, since it is essentially a percentage-based bonus.

### Enemy Melee To Hit Malus

Whenever a unit possessing the **Lucky** ability is attacked, that attack is subject to a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit**
penalty. This means that whenever a lucky unit's assailant rolls for
hits, less of these rolls will succeed, and the lucky target needs to
mitigate fewer [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** with its own [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score and accompanying rolls To Block.

This is sort of a mirror-image of the lucky [To Hit Bonus](#To_Hit_Bonus) described above, applying as a penalty to the attacker rather than a bonus to the lucky unit. It only applies to all [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") rolls made during [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") (including [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")). [Ranged](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") and [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") are **not** affected. Although reduced To Hit values do not change an attack's maximum potential damage, they lower the *average* damage output of that attack.

For instance, most units have [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30% To Hit** by default, so **Lucky** will cause those units to deliver only 20% of their maximum damage potential on average.

This aspect of **Lucky** is also distinct from the lucky [To Block Bonus](#To_Block_Bonus)
described above, and will be effective in situations where blocking
might be less effective or not even occur at all, such as under an [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") or [Illusionary](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") assault. Moreover, this hit penalty affects creatures with high [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** and even [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), who otherwise present very difficult targets for direct penalties of this kind.

Note that this effect was removed in the [unofficial 1.40n Insecticide patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide").

### Resistance Bonus

Finally, the **Lucky** ability gives the unit a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1 Resistance**
bonus. This will help the unit's chances of succeeding any Resistance
roll it is forced to make when trying to fend off enemy spells or
certain [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack").

A large number of spells, including primarily [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"), will force a unit to make one or more [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls. Each roll results in a number between 1 and 10. If the number comes up equal to or lower than the unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
score, the unit is said to have won the roll, and has therefore averted
the ill effect that the enemy was trying to inflict upon it.

Thus, the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1** bonus given by the **Lucky** ability will statistically improve a unit's chance of surviving [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), warding off [Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Curse "Curse"), and averting certain types of spells.

Units can have very different [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
scores by default, with some already having a score so high that it
will avert any ill effect even without additional bonuses. The majority
of units, however, will find a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1 Resistance** bonus, albeit small, quite welcome.

### Improvement Table

Both the [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") and the [Hero Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Ability "Hero Ability") named "**Lucky**" do not improve with [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level"). In fact, **Lucky** is one of the few [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") that does not improve with [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience"). Nonetheless, as explained above, both the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**
bonuses given by this ability are percentage-based bonuses, and will
give an overall greater benefit as the unit's attack strength and/or
Defense scores improve - which they normally will, thanks to [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") and other effects.

## Acquiring Lucky

There is no known method of adding **Lucky** to a unit that does not possess it when the game begins.

However, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may occasionally pick the **Lucky** ability as one of their [Random Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities"). This is not available to [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), of course.
