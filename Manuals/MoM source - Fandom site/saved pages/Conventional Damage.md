**Conventional Damage**, also commonly called "[Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage")", is one of the two main mechanics to cause harm to units in *Master of Magic*.
It follows a generic set of rules using dice rolls to determine the
amount of successful hits, which the target may then reduce based on its
[Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** statistic (again using dice rolls), to arrive at an amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** suffered. This is opposed to [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"), which is defended against with the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** statistic, and typically deals fixed amounts of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, such as equal to an individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**'s worth of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, or [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**.

**Conventional Damage** is by far the more common mechanic. It is dealt by all [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **direct damage** spells. **Conventional Damage** may carry a variety of "*damage type modifiers*", termed simply "[Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type")" on the wiki, which may alter certain characteristics of its resolution, such as the amount of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** that a target unit may employ to reduce it. In fact, a single **Conventional Damage** attack can have multiple different Damage Types all at once, and some may even cancel out others!

Dealing **Conventional Damage** is normally a multi-stage process. It starts with an "*attack*", that determines the amount of unmitigated [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, followed by a "*defense*", which can reduce this value. Anything left over is then suffered by the target unit in the form of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points").

If a unit has multiple [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**, or can perform multiple different actions with a single command (such as a [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack** along with a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**), then each and every one of these attacks counts as an individual and separate source of **Conventional Damage**. For example, a [Barbarian Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Barbarian_Spearmen "Barbarian Spearmen") unit ordered to attack an adjacent target will perform a whopping 16 individual attacks, provided that all of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** are still alive.

The same holds true for **Conventional Damage** marked with the [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") type: a separate attack is performed against each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**
in the target unit. However, in this case, the defense is resolved
slightly differently, as Area Damage can not "overspill" onto other
defending [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. Here, each attack's [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is strictly limited to the (current) [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**.

In contrast, without the Area Damage property, if a **Conventional Damage** attack deals more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** than a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** has either current or maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** (depending on whether it deals [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell-** or non-Spell [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**), it will kill off that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, and carry on with the remaining raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** against another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**.

## The Attack

Every attempt at dealing **Conventional Damage**, regardless of source, starts with an *attack*. This is the procedure that translates *potential damage*, called [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), into raw, or unmitigated, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. Apart from attacks flagged with the unique [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") "[Doom](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage")" (also known as "[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_(Item_Power) "Chaos (Item Power)")"), this invariably involves performing an [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll").

#### Modifiers

:   [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") can have a multitude of different modifiers based on various circumstances. A full list of these can be found [here](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Table_Of_Attack_Strength_Modifiers "Attack Strength"), at the end of the main article. They are also typically shown in the *unit statistics window* for unit attacks and abilities. However, one notable exception are [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wielding weapons with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_(Item_Power) "Chaos (Item Power)")** property, for whom the Attack Strength will be *half* of the listed value, rounded down. In addition, variable-damage spells have an Attack Strength that depends on the [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** spent on their casting, although they always have a minimum [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost"), with a corresponding base strength.

### Attack Roll

:   *Main article: [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll")*

The Attack Roll is the series of dice rolls that determine how much of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** potential is realized by a **Conventional Damage** attack. For each point of the [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"),
a 10-sided die is cast. Ignoring modifiers for a moment, the die is
"successful" in landing a "hit", if the rolled result is 8, 9, or 10,
translating into a statistical **30%** chance of success, otherwise known as the *base chance* [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**. Thus, a 10-point **Conventional Damage** attack (Attack Strength of 10) will achieve, on average, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** raw hits.

#### Modifiers

:   *Main article: [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit")*

:   For [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**, the chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** can not be altered by any effect in the game. That is, all direct damage spells always use the same [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%** regardless of circumstance. This applies to every spell from every [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") that deals **Conventional Damage**, whether it is single- or multi-target. In addition, the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") effect, while technically available as a [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability"), also abides by this rule. It is considered to deal [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**, and is not affected by any attributes of the unit possessing it.

:   Basic unit attacks, on the other hand, can be improved by a separate ability aptly termed [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit"). [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") naturally acquire this ability by gaining [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**. Some, most notably [High Elves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Elves "High Elves") and [Halflings](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halflings "Halflings"), even possess it by default. [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") summoned by spells of at least [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity") also nearly all have this ability, with higher Rarity creatures typically possessing increasingly better [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** modifiers. There are also various spells and other effects that can increase (or decrease) a unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** value. A comprehensive table of these can be found [here](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit#Table_of_To_Hit_Modifiers "To Hit"), in the main article.

:   Modifiers [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** are expressed in-game as positive or negative integer values. Every [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+/-1** represents a whole [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+/-10%**
    chance for each die of the Attack Roll to succeed. Because the dice are
    10-sided, there are no partials: the increments always come in whole
    multiples of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **10%**.
    However, to make understanding the modifiers easier, they are often
    written as percentages on the wiki. Either way, the same 10-strength
    example attack from above made with [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1** instead would deliver, on average, a raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**, rather than [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**.

 [These Spearmen actually have a](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/b/b4/ToHit_NoDisplay131.png/revision/latest?cb=20180928192421)

These Spearmen actually have a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **50% To Hit** chance, even though there is no direct indication of this in the *unit statistics window*.



:   Unfortunately, exactly how much [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** a certain attack will benefit from can be somewhat ambiguous when looking at the *unit statistics window* in the original game. This is because only "overall" modifiers are calculated into the listed [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") ability. That is, any benefits that do not apply to every **Conventional Damage** [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") that a unit can make are omitted by default. This includes some rather common modifiers, such as those conferred by spells, [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magic Weapons**, or [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), for example. The unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch alters the *unit statistics window* by adding a separate [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** display to make things a little easier for the player, and this is further refined in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") by separating [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** and [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") **Ranged** modifiers.

 [The , on the other hand, does properly display](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/1/1c/ToHit_FullDisplay150.png/revision/latest?cb=20180928192514)

The [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50"), on the other hand, does properly display [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** modifiers, although it also removes them from the actual ability list.



:   It may be worth noting that the chance for each die to succeed can never be lower than **10%** (a roll of 10 ***always*** succeeds), or higher than **100%** (an attack can never do more raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** than its Attack Strength), regardless of the amount of negative or positive [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** modifiers. Still, neither is entirely meaningless, as they can still cancel out situational effects. For example, a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** Hero with [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+90%** is able to hit targets with 100% accuracy from a greater distance than one with only [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+70%**.

#### Doom Damage

:   There is one [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") that, when applied, will skip the Attack Roll mechanic entirely: [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage"). Attacks bearing this modifier do not roll dice: the Attack Strength translates directly into raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. In fact, because this type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** also skips the *Defense* mechanics (see below), it will typically inflict this amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** in the end too. However, some sources of Doom Damage come from spells, and these can be negated by [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**, in which case they will deal no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** at all. In addition, as mentioned previously, the *listed*
    Attack Strength of Heroes with this Damage Type is halved when using
    the affected attack(s), and will thus cause only half as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. It's also worth noting that attacks made against units under the effect of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Sleep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sleep "Black Sleep")** always deal Doom Damage, which is why these units suffer maximum [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** from every **Conventional Damage** unit attack.

### Blur

:   *Main article: [Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")*

[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")** is a [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") with a unique effect that inserts an extra step into the resolution of unit-vs-unit **Conventional Damage**. Whenever a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown-**, [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath-**, or [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") is performed against a unit protected by [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")**, the game rolls another 10-sided die for each point of the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** that resulted from the [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") above. Each of these dice that come up as 1s reduce the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** by **1** (an average 10% reduction). This does not affect attacks marked as dealing [Doom-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") or [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**, but it does apply against the hidden **Conventional Damage** component of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack").

Unfortunately, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")** has two noteworthy bugs in the official game. First, attackers possessing [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity")
are supposed to ignore its effect, but the game checks this on the
wrong unit (the defender), meaning that units with this immunity are
unable to benefit from [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")** altogether, while attackers won't see through the enchantment based on their own abilities alone. The second error is that the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**
reduction is actually lower than it should be, as after every
successful roll, the computer discards an additional die if there are
any more left. This means that it will roll less dice than there is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, and there is absolutely no chance of fully avoiding anything over [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**, which the rules otherwise do allow even if the chance would be very low.

## Nullifying Immunities

There are two types of immunity mechanics in *Master of Magic*. One will raise the defending unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** (and/or [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**), while the other nullifies the incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** entirely. Confusingly enough, both can apply to **Conventional Damage**.
In fact, the very same immunity can exert either type of effect based
on a single condition. "Reductive" immunities, that increase [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**, are discussed further below.

The two effects that can nullify **Conventional Damage** are those of [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**. Both of them can only do this under one circumstance: the attack must deal [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**. If this is the case, than the nature of both effects is switched from a reductive to a nullifying immunity, and *all* of the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is instantly discarded. Naturally, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")** also has a stricter requirement of the attack having to belong to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm").

## The Defense

Once raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** has been fully determined, the target typically gets a chance to mitigate some, or all of it. The only exception is [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage"),
as this type may not be defended against. Otherwise, the process is
similar to the attack, only using a different set of attributes.

### Defense Score

:   *Main article: [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense")*

All units in *Master of Magic* possess a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** statistic, a measure of how well they can avoid incoming **Conventional Damage**. This is displayed in the *unit statistics window* as a row of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shield** icons, where the total number of icons corresponds to the attribute score. [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** sets out the *potential reduction* of raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, before it is applied as [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points"). Modifiers to a unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score can be classed into two categories: either they affect the attribute across the board, regardless of the incoming [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"); or they provide a bonus only against attacks marked with one or more specific Types.

#### Universal Modifiers

:   [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
    can be improved in many ways in the game, and it may also be reduced by
    several effects. Any modifiers that apply (in theory) to all attacks
    against the unit will also show up in the *unit statistics window*, either as gold (positive effect), or greyed out (negative effect) [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shield** icons. A list of these "universal" modifiers can be found [here](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense#Unmitigated_Changes "Defense"), in the main article.

#### Conditional Modifiers

:   Some other effects that improve [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** don't do it across the board. They only provide their benefit if the source of the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** satisfies certain criteria. As such, these modifiers will not be directly indicated in the unit's visible [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score; although the effects that grant them generally do have an icon, and corresponding help description, in the *unit statistics window*.

:   In all cases, conditional modifiers apply on a per-attack basis.
    Any benefit (or penalty) is gone as soon as that individual attack is
    resolved. Granted, when [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** are fighting each other, the effects will typically work for each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**'s attacks or defense, but this is certainly not the case if a unit has multiple different *types* of attacks, such as [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath** before [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**.

:   The order in which conditional modifiers are applied can also make a huge difference in the final [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** value, so the subsections below are organized according to this order. A condensed table of these can also be found [here](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense#Order_of_Operations_for_Resolving_Defense_and_Immunities "Defense"), along with an outline of the triggering conditions for each effect.

:   [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") is a **Conventional** [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), or modifier. When an attack is flagged with it, the target's total [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** is cut in half (rounded down) while resolving the attack, consequently halving its potential [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** reduction. Because of its placement in the resolution order, Armor Piercing has no effect against reductive immunities or the [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls") bonus, but it does halve any other [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** modifiers, conditional or otherwise. At the same time, it is always superceded by [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage"), and sometimes also [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage"); in which case it is rendered useless. This can be important when deciding which powers to choose during [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting").

:   The [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") ability affects all [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") that its possessor makes, except for those that deal [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**. In contrast, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wielding weapons imbued with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Lightning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_(Item_Power) "Lightning (Item Power)")** only gain this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") when using attacks associated with the item. The same applies to the listed spells and [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Lightning Breath Attacks** which, while themselves are Armor Piercing, do not grant this property to the rest of a unit's attacks.

#### Reductive Immunities

:   Unlike "nullifying" immunities, when a "reductive" immunity applies against a certain attack, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** is simply raised, rather than the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** being negated entirely. With the exception of [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") however, this actually results in an otherwise nigh-unreachable [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**.
    While not completely impenetrable, this typically does ensure that only
    the strongest of attacks can hurt the unit, providing an average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** reduction of **15**. Although this [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** may not be [Pierced](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage"), likely as a result of a programming oversight, it *can* be negated by [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage").

:   [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") is notably weaker than the rest of the "reductive" immunity effects. It only raises [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** (if it is lower otherwise), rather than the usual [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**, and its triggering condition is not exactly straightforward either. It is based on the attacking unit's *weapon quality*: a property not directly visible in the game. It is automatically ignored by *all* [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") and [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), including any [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**
    units; and can also never trigger against attacks that are considered
    inherently "magical" in nature. In the official game, a bug also
    prevents it from working against [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Weapons**.

:   To bypass the immunity, a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") needs to either possess an upgraded *weapon quality*, or be affected by one or more of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")**, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Flame Blade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flame_Blade "Flame Blade")**, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Metal Fires](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Metal_Fires "Metal Fires")**, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon")**, or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Arms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Arms "Holy Arms")**
    spells. Weapons can not be upgraded for already existing units, short
    of turning them into Fantastic Creatures. The property is set when the
    unit is first created, and will only be non-Normal if the recruiting [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") houses an [Alchemists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild"), or its controlling player possesses the [Alchemy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemy_(Retort) "Alchemy (Retort)") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"). It may also be worth noting that neither of these conditions ever apply to units offering up as [Mercenaries](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mercenaries "Mercenaries"), and as such these troops will always be equipped with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Normal Weapons**.

:   In addition, [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") has some further [bugs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity#Known_Bugs "Weapon Immunity") in the original game, the most notable of which is that it accidentally overrides the much stronger [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") in cases where both would apply. While this is corrected in [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide"), that patch introduces new bug instead, and makes [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") grant [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** instead of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** against non-[Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** attacks. This, in turn, requires the also unofficial [v1.51](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") patch to fix.

#### City Walls

:   *Main article: [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls")*

:   During sieges of walled [Towns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town"), all units inside the 4×4 walled area of the Town proper enjoy a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** bonus against any attack made by a unit outside this area. Units adjacent to an intact wall section receive [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3**, while those standing in the middle squares, or next to a destroyed section, get [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1**. This applies to every attack not flagged as dealing [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**, meaning that it is invalid for [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") and **Conventional Damage** spells (even if cast by units); but works against all [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-** and [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") otherwise. Considering its placement in the order of resolution, this extra [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** is naturally unaffected by either [Armor Piercing-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") or [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage").
    It may also be worth noting that this benefit is side-independent: if
    an attacking unit makes it inside the walls, it will get the same bonus
    should a defender target it from the outside.

### Defense Roll

:   *Main article: [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll")*

The Defense Roll is the game's mechanic for turning *"potential mitigation"* (the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score) into actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** reduction. Provided that the *effective* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** of the target was not brought down to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** during the previous calculations, it can normally attempt to reduce the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** that resulted from the [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll"). The only time this may not happen is if the attack is flagged with the [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") type: in this case, no Defense Rolls are allowed, and the game proceeds directly to [calculating the damage](#Calculating_The_Damage).

The actual Defense Roll is very similar to the Attack Roll. The computer takes a number of 10-sided dice equal to the target's *effective* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**,
and rolls them in a series. Ignoring modifiers again, any dice that
come up as 8, 9, or 10, are "successful", leading to a statistical
reduction chance of **30%** per die, also known as the *base chance* [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**. The "result" of the Defense Roll is the total amount of successes, which is then deducted from the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**.

#### Modifiers

:   Unlike the many ways of altering an attack's chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**, there are only 3 effects in the game that can influence a unit's chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**. The first of these is the [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") ability, available as a racial for [Halflings](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halflings "Halflings"), and a random ability for [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). This grants an overall [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **+10%** against *all* **Conventional Damage** attacks. A bonus of the same magnitude can also be gained from the [Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[High Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer")**, treated as a single effect due to the fact that they are not cumulative with each other, although they do stack with [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky"), if available.

:   The last modifier is a negative one. It is applied when a unit enchanted with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")** makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-** or [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**, or [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** against another unit. For the duration of resolving such an attack, the target's chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** is *reduced* by [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **10%**, cumulative with any other modifiers. It's important to note that this penalty does not apply to any other [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type")
    besides the ones listed here. That is, if the enchanted unit has other
    types of attacks, those will not benefit from this effect, and the [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **-10%** is strictly only active during the resolution of the affected attacks.

#### Invulnerability

:   *Main article: [Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")*

:   Besides granting its target [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity"), this [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") also has a unique effect that triggers every time the unit would be allowed to make a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") to reduce incoming **Conventional Damage**. "Would be", because [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** still works even if the target is otherwise reduced to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**,
    and would normally have no dice to roll. The only time this effect does
    not work is if the Defense Roll is prohibited in the first place:
    against [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage"). When triggered, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** reduces the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** by a flat [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2**, roughly equivalent to an extra [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+7**, except there are no rolls involved.

## Calculating The Damage

**Conventional Damage** has two different mechanics for determining the final [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done to the target unit. Which one is used depends on whether the attack has the [Area](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type").

### Non-Area Damage

The majority of **Conventional Damage** attacks do not do Area Damage. That is, they hurt the target one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** at a time. If the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** exceeds the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, it can "overspill", and move on to another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**.
Thus, it is entirely possible to wipe out an whole unit with a
"single-target" attack. However, every time there is an "overspill" of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, the "next" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** gets to "Defend" against it (even if there is no actual next [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**).

The reason this is not treated as a separate attack altogether is
that it omits an entire stage in the process: the "Attack" itself. No
new [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") is performed, the "overspill" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** simply becomes the new raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, and the game jumps directly to the "Defense" stage for the next [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**. This keeps repeating until finally the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is no longer higher than a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**'s worth of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, meaning that the process can be concluded.

For all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** other than the first, subsequent Defense Rolls always trigger when the unit registers exactly its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** value in [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. However, for the "lead" or "top" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, the game uses two different mechanics. Unit attacks, such as [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), ignore "top figure damage" entirely, and are always considered to be directed at full health [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**,
even if the unit does not have such any more. Thus, the second Defense
Roll is consistent with all the other ones down the line: it will occur
after registering a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**'s full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**' worth of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**.

In contrast, all (non-Area) [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage** treats the *actual* "top" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** as the primary target. That is, against direct damage spells, the second Defense Roll occurs at the point where the registered [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** equals the *current* [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** value of this [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, as shown in the *unit statistics window*. While this is often less effective in comparison, especially against targets with a high [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")**, it is also more intuitive - the next [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** "steps up" to defend itself when it would normally be expected to.

The final [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** of the attack is the sum delivered to each (theoretical) [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, including the last (or only) chunk that is always less than or equal to a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**'s [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** value. For example, if a defender with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** takes [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**, then an "overspill" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**, and finally an "overspill" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2**, then the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done to the unit as a whole would be [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **8**. In fact, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** in *Master of Magic* is always applied to units as a whole, regardless of type, and never to individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. The game actually calculates the amount of remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** from the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** taken by units.

### Area Damage

:   *Main article: [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage")*

Arguably an easier process to understand is delivering Area Damage. This is because attacks flagged with this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") can easily be subdivided into several attacks instead. To be more precise, one attack with the same [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") is performed separately against each defending [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**. If, after the "Attack" and "Defense" stages, there is raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** left to deal, the game compares this amount with the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**. If it is greater, it is reduced to that exact value instead. In other words, each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit can suffer at most as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** as it has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**.

The final [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** dealt by the Area Damage attack as a whole is the sum of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done to each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**. However, it is important to note that when the game applies this [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, its context changes from being delivered to single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**, to a unit as a whole. This is because units in *Master of Magic* are designed to only have at most one injured [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** at a time. For example, a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fireball](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fireball "Fireball")** spell delivering a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5** across [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6 Figures** of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3** each will always yield one dead and one injured [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** regardless of how the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** was originally distributed among them.

## Damage Points

:   *Main article: [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points")*

As noted above, the game always applies [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to units as a whole, and not to individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. In fact, the game does not even keep track of the current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of either [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** *or* units. It only records [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** taken. Actual [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** counts, when needed, are always calculated by combining this information with the unit's base statistics.

*Master of Magic* has 3 types of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points"),
only one of which is actually used outside of battles. If a unit
survives while carrying any of the other two types, they are
automatically converted to *"regular damage"* when combat ends. The purpose of the other types is to determine what happens or, rather, what *can* and *cannot* happen, if the unit dies during the battle.

### Create Undead Damage

The [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") and [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") abilities, along with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** spell and the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Vampiric](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vampiric_(Item_Power) "Vampiric (Item Power)")** [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"), are capable of inflicting a type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** that, under the right circumstances, can cause units to rise as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
after the battle. For this to happen, the unit must belong to the side
that has lost the battle, meaning that only the victorious side can ever
raise units this way. In addition, the amount of "Create Undead Damage"
the unit has suffered must be *higher* than the amount of Irreversible Damage, and *at least as much* as the regular [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**.

### Irreversible Damage

The third type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points**
recorded by the game serves a completely different purpose: it may not
be healed during battle. No spell or effect that restores lost [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** in combat can heal these injuries. In addition, should a unit die after having suffered *at least as much*
Irreversible Damage as both of the other two types, that unit is lost

If a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") carrying one or more [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") dies this way, the items themselves are also destroyed, and may not be recovered by the winning side either. However, [Torin the Chosen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Torin_the_Chosen "Torin the Chosen") may still be re-summoned with the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Incarnation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Incarnation "Incarnation")** spell even if he is slain by Irreversible Damage.

### Melee Combat

:   *Main article: [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")*

Unlike spells and [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Damage**
is typically not applied immediately upon resolving an attack. This is
because during Melee, the fighting units are expected to strike at each
other simultaneously. Thus, if a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**'s or unit's [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** was registered before the opponent could retaliate, the side whose [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** was delivered first would always be at a significant advantage. Of course, there are tactical abilities, such as [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") or [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath"), that facilitate exactly this behaviour, but normally, the [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") takes place *before* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is dealt to the defender.

This is even more important when one or both of the units engaged in Melee are [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**. In this case, *all* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** of *both* units will conduct their attack before [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is registered to either combatant. The computer keeps track of the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done to each unit separately, but applies it only once every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** had a chance to swing at the opponent. However, it should also be noted that executing a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") is itself also a multi-stage process, of which the actual, mutual Melee engagement is only the last step. [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**
is still fully applied between the phases, and this is exactly how the
aforementioned abilities provide their tactical advantage.

### Simultaneous Attacks

There are no such things as truly simultaneous attacks in *Master of Magic*. However, as already evidenced above, the game does have a way of delaying the application of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. While [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-** and [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") are the main situations where this has real practical relevance, it also happens in two other scenarios.

#### Multi-Figure Units

:   When performing most types of attacks, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** actually conduct that attack as many times as they have remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. Slain or destroyed [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** naturally cannot participate. However, applying [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is always delayed until all eligible [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** have finished their attack. This is true even for non-[Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** attacks.

:   There are also two situations where not all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** will attack. The first are the [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") components of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), which are only activated once, regardless of the amount of remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. That single activation will in turn affect all enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. This makes the main effect of Gaze Attacks scale only with the target unit's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**, although in the original game this fact is only really relevant for [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Gorgons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons")**, as all other Gaze Attack units are [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figures**.

:   The other exception is facing opponents with [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") in [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Combat**. Units engaging a target with this ability must [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** on a per-[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** basis, and any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** failing this check is unable to deliver their [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**.
    It should be noted though, that this ability is severely bugged in the
    official version of the game, and requires at least the [v1.40](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch to really exert a meaningful effect.

#### Touch Attacks

:   *Main article: [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")*

:   There are several abilities in the game that deliver an "added effect" along with **Conventional Damage** attacks. These are called [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), and are executed immediately after the physical attack. They even occur in the same stage in the *Melee Attack Sequence*, and thus their effect can be considered simultaneous. However, this also means that in the mutual [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** phase of that sequence, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done by these attacks is only applied after the opponent had a chance to [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

:   Touch Attacks conferred by [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") are typically added to *all* **Conventional Damage** attacks that their possessor can make (with the exception of those that deal [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**). In addition, some Touch Attack effects are also available as [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"), that can be imbued onto [Magical Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). In this case, the effect only works with attacks that are associated with the type of the weapon: [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** for [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace") and [Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"); [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** and [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** for [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"); [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** for [Bows](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"); and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical** for [Staves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") and [Wands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand"). For other [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type"), the effect simply doesn't trigger.

:   Each attack delivers the effect exactly once. That is, in a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Unit**, it is exectuted as many times as there are remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. The sole exception to this is the hybrid [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") attack, which only triggers once. [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** that do not attack (because of [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear") [Cause Fear](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear "Cause Fear"), for example) do not perform any Touch Attacks either. On the other hand, there is no limit to the amount of *different* effects that can be delivered by a single **Conventional Damage** attack. In this case, all of them are fully resolved before *any* of their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is applied to the target, whether they occur during a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** engagement or otherwise.
