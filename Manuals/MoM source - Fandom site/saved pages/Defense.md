The *unit statistics window* displays all of a unit's attributes, and allows enchantments or the entire unit to be dismissed out of combat.



**Defense**, or [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Armor**, is a unit property indicated by a row of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shield** icons in the *unit statistics window*. It signifies the unit's ability to block or reduce incoming [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"), such as caused by most [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attacks**, [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), and a variety of offensive spells. Each point of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** has a base chance of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30%** to block a point of the incoming raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, although a few effects may increase or reduce this chance.

## Concept

A unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
score indicates its ability to block, dodge or otherwise avert incoming
damage. Both armor and agility are factored into this score, so both
nimble and well-armored targets may have a high [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. Naturally, in a world filled with magic, enchantments and [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can also further enhance one's ability to defend themselves.

## Conventional Damage

:   *Main article: [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage")*

Conventional Damage is the most common [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") in the game, and is dealt by [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **direct damage** spells like [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt")** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt")**. Other sources include [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath Attacks**, and occasionally a few others.

Conventional Damage is distinguishable from other types of damage because it forces the attacker to make an [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") in order to determine the accuracy of the attack. This is done using a number of 10-sided dice equal to the [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"). Each successful roll registers one "hit" on the target, and will potentially cause [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** if it goes unblocked.

Fortunately, [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") also allows the defender to make a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll"),
in an attempt to block, dodge or otherwise avert any successful hits
from the attacker. This is also done using 10-sided dice, this time
equal to the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. However, each successful roll made by the defender will avert only [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, so a unit requires a high **Defense** score to completely block any powerful or particularly-successful attack.

### Defense Rolls

:   *Main article: [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll")*

As noted above, each [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shield** allows the target unit to make a single roll which - if successful - will avert [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to the unit. Therefore, a unit with [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** may use 10 separate dice - potentially averting up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **10**. However, due to the random nature of these rolls, it is more likely that the unit will block somewhere *between* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0** and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **10**.

A unit's chance of success with each die is called the chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **"To Block"**. By default, it is exactly [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30%**, and only a scant few effects can alter it. For each die, the program randomizes a number between 1 and 10. If it comes up *equal to or higher than 8*, the roll is considered "successful" and will block [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**. If the roll comes up *lower than 8*, that particular roll is failed and will not block [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

#### Example

:   During a [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") attack, the attacker registers 10 successful hits, or "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** on some target. If no Defense Roll was allowed, that target would suffer exactly [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **10**.
    Assuming that no special conditions apply however, the normal rules of
    Conventional Damage allow the target unit to make a Defense Roll. This
    particular target possesses a **Defense** score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10**, and has no modifiers to its [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Block** chance.

:   The target therefore rolls 10 random numbers, each between 1 and
    10. Any roll that comes up 8 or higher will negate exactly one hit. If,
    for example, three rolls are successful, then the number of incoming
    hits is reduced by **3**. Therefore, the target will only suffer **10 - 3 =** [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7** points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

:   If all 10 rolls are successful, the target unit suffers no
    damage - having averted all hits! Conversely, if all 10 rolls fail, the
    target will suffer all 10 hits, translated into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **10** points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, the exact same result as if the Defense Roll was not made at all.

### Multi-Figure Unit Defense

A [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit may be able to make more [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") against a single attack. This occurs only when one of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the unit is killed off during an attack - allowing the next [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** to step up and make a whole new series of rolls.

The process goes as follows:

1. The attacker makes the [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") to determine its accuracy, resulting in a number of hits registered against the target unit, also called "raw", or unmitigated [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.
2. The first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the target unit makes a Defense Roll using an amount of dice equal to the number of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shields** possessed by the unit.
3. Any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that has not been averted will hurt this specific [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.
4. If sufficient [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** has been caused to this [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, it will die. The next [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** then becomes the first one, and can try to avert the remainder of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.
5. This new first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** now makes a new Defense Roll, again using an amount of dice equal to the number of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shields** possessed by the unit.
6. Any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that has not been averted will now hurt this specific [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.
7. And so on and so on, until all remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** has either been applied or averted.

In short: with each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** it loses, the unit can make more and more [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense Rolls**, repeatedly reducing the incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

The common result of this process is that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units are significantly more durable than their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
score might indicate. They can be quite hard to kill using a single,
powerful attack - often requiring several attacks to destroy the entire
unit. This gives them great survivability comparable to heavily-armored [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure** units. Of course, the caveat is that with each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** the unit loses, it also becomes much weaker offensively. This means that while the unit is more survivable, just a few lost [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** can make it ineffective. It might survive overall, but will no longer contribute as much to the battle.

Confusingly, the game uses two different mechanics to determine when the first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** has taken "enough damage" to be slain for the purpose of proceeding with the next Defense Roll. Unit attacks, such as [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), are always considered to be directed at a healthy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, meaning that the second [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s roll can commence only after a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**' worth of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** has been recorded by the unit. This is true even if the unit no longer has such a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

On the other hand, [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage** allows for a second Defense Roll as soon as the unit has taken enough [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to destroy the actual "top figure", whose current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** are visible in the *unit statistics window*. This makes direct damage spells somewhat less effective against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** with an injured "top figure", especially if the target unit has a high [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score.

In any case though, the rules converge after the second roll, so a
third or any subsequent Defense Rolls will always happen after
recording an amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** equal to the defending unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value - the maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.
It's worth noting though, that these mechanics remain in place even if a
unit gets destroyed in the process. In fact, they even extend to [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure Units**. They also get to make further Defense Rolls against excessive amounts of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
the exact same way, even if they are technically already slain by that
point. However, this fact can only ever become relevant for units that
have recorded *Irreversible-* or "Create Undead" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points"), as it may alter the resolution of their effects. In all other scenarios, "overkill" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** will have no practical effect.

#### Example

:   A [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit is being attacked. Lets assume that this unit is undamaged, meaning that it still has all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures** present. Each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in this unit has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3 Hit Points**. The unit's **Defense** score is [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2**.

:   The attacker gets 7 successes on the Attack Roll, scoring a total of 7 hits. Potentially, this should cause [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7** to the [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry"), killing two of the Cavalrymen and slightly injuring another one.

:   The "lead" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit makes its [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense Roll**. Since the unit has a **Defense** score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2**, this cavalryman may roll 2 separate dice. Each roll has a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30% chance** to avert one hit.

:   The cavalryman rolls two numbers: 9 and 2. Only one of these
    rolls is higher than or equal to 8, so we can say that the cavalryman
    achieved one success on its Defense Roll. This means that the number of
    inflicted hits is reduced to **7 - 1 = 6**.

:   Since the poor cavalryman only has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3**, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3 Damage points** are inflicted on him, killing him on the spot. The next cavalryman then steps up, ready to absorb the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**.

:   This next cavalryman then gets to roll 2 **Defense**
    dice of his own! He rolls better than the previous man: 8 and 10. Both
    of these rolls are equal to or higher than 8, so he can be said to have
    made two successes on his Defense Roll, blocking exactly [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** of the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points"). This leaves [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Damage** unblocked, which is now inflicted on this cavalryman.

:   Therefore, despite having a **Defense** score of only [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2**, the unit has rolled a total of 4 **Defense**
    dice. 2 were used by the first cavalryman, who failed to protect
    himself and died valiantly. The second cavalryman then got a fresh
    chance, using an additional 2 **Defense** dice and saving himself. Had this cavalryman also been killed, the next one would also have been able to use 2 **Defense** dice.

:   As a result of this mechanism, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units are often considered to be significantly more resilient than [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure** units. The more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** are present, the more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can potentially be blocked from any attack, thus increasing the number of attacks required to destroy the whole unit.

:   On the other hand, each Cavalryman the unit loses will reduce its offensive strength by 25%. Therefore, although the [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit survived this powerful attack relatively intact, it did lose [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **1 figure** and with it a significant chunk of the unit's combat effectiveness.

### Block Chance Modifiers

As noted previously, there are only a few effects in the game that can alter a unit's chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**. The first one of these is the [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") ability. This is one of the racial traits of [Halfling](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halflings "Halflings") units, and is also a [Random Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Random_Abilities "Random Abilities") for [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). It improves all rolls of a unit by **+1**, or **+10%**, with the exception of [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") made for [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**. That is, it grants [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1 To Hit**, [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1 Resistance**, and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **+1 To Block**.

A similar effect is available in combat through the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** spell, that also improves all rolls by **+1**. This [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") affects all friendly units. It also has a more powerful version called [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[High Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer")**.
However, while this latter spell also gives other benefits, the roll
improvements from the two are not cumulative. On the other hand, they do
stack with [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky"), which results in the highest possible block chance per die in the game: [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **50%**.

There is also one way to *reduce* the chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**. This is only a conditional modifier however, and applies only during the resolution of certain attacks. Its source is the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")** [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), which can only be cast on [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").
Whenever the enchanted unit makes an attack that the game considers to
be a "weapon attack", the effect is triggered, and the target
temporarily loses [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **10%**, cumulative with any other modifiers. The only [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") this works with are [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-** and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, and [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks**. Nothing else will activate the effect, and the penalty is strictly only present while resolving the attack that triggered it.

### Invulnerability

[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")**, and its [Item Power version](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability_(Item_Power) "Invulnerability (Item Power)") provide an effect that, while does not increase **Defense** directly, provides analogous [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** reduction nonetheless. This effect is triggered every time the unit is allowed to make a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll"). Regardless of the outcome of the roll, the enchantment reduces the incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** by an additional [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2**. Although it doesn't work against [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") since no Defense Roll is possible there, the effect does trigger if the unit's **Defense** is otherwise reduced to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**. During [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** **Defense**, it also activates with *every Defense Roll*, not just the first one, making it one of the most potent defensive enchantments in the game.

## Anti-Armor Damage Types

Some attacks have an easier time getting through a target's **Defense**. That is, they possess a [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), or modifier, that changes the resolution of **Defense**. There are three such Damage Types in the game, although one of these ([Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage")) also alters the "Attack" stage of [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") mechanics, and is significantly different than the other two. [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") and [Armor Piercing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") simply reduce or eliminate the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score temporarily, just long enough for the attack to get through and severely hurt the target.

### Illusion Damage

:   *Main article: [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage")*

Illusion Damage can be added to a [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attack in one of two ways: Either the attacker has to possess the [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") ability, or it must be a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") holding a [Magical Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") imbued with the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantasmal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantasmal_(Item_Power) "Phantasmal (Item Power)")** [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). The result is the same in both cases. ## Area Damage

:   *Main article: [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage")*

Area Damage is another [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage")
type that significantly modifies the resolution mechanics. It is
delivered by a variety of spells and effects, most of which are
available only to [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard").
Its name stems from the fact that instead of executing a single attack
against a target unit, it performs as many as there are [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in that unit. Each one gets its own [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll"), and must be defended against separately, with each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** making its own [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll"), using as many dice as the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**.

However, Area Damage has one serious limitation. It can ultimately only deal as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** as they have actual [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**. That is, each "sub-attack" is targeted at a specific [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, and any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** above their current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** is *discarded* instead of carrying on to hurt other [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. This means that in the outline above, the process always ends step #4, recording only the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** done up to that point. Naturally then, the worst case scenario is the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** dying, which yields [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** equal to its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**. If the unit already has a damaged [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, its current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** total will be used, not the maximum.

Once each separate attack is evaluated, the game tallies up all of the unblocked hits to all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the unit, translates these hits into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and applies the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to the unit as a whole - killing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** one by one in order. Because of this, the same amount of final [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** will always have the same result, regardless of how the points were originally distributed among the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

## Immunities

Many units in the game possess abilities known as "Immunities", that
are meant to make the unit possessing it much more resilient (or
completely invulnerable) to one or more [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") or spells. When it comes to [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"), immunities have two different methods of action: they either negate the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** entirely ("nullifying" immunity), or simply raise the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** statistic by a large margin ("reductive" immunity). The former group contains only two effects ([Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**), although both of them are actually also part of the latter group. That is, they nullify the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** if, and only if, it is [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**. For [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**, it also has to come from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm").

Reductive immunities instead raise the unit's **Defense** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** when they are triggered. This provides a rather high average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** reduction of **15**, assuming there are no modifiers [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** in play. The only exception is [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity"), which only raises **Defense** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10**. In either case though, nothing happens if the unit's **Defense** is already higher than what the immunity could provide; although this is nigh-impossible for the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** ones.

Immunities also don't stack if more than one would apply. In this case **Defense** will normally be set to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**.
However, the original game contains an unfortunate bug that is
expressed in exactly this scenario. If an attack would trigger both [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity")- and [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity"), the weaker effect overrides the other, and the unit will only either get [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10**, or no increase if it has that much already. [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") fixes this problem, but introduces a new one instead, as [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") will now grant [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** against any non-[Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** attacks that trigger it. This bug is ultimately corrected in [v1.51](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50").

## Conditional Defense

[Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") and immunities are not the only mechanics that can alter a unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** in a way that does not work all the time. There are a three more spells and a [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") that exert a similar effect, and are only triggered against certain kinds of attacks.

### Large Shields

:   *Main article: [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield")*

The [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield") [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield") ability gives a unit a bonus of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+2 Defense** when struck by [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"), but only if that damage is **not** delivered by a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**. This gives the unit holding the Large Shield better survivability against such attacks, so long as they deliver [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). Many [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can also get this ability, simply by equipping a [Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shield "Shield") of any kind.

:   **Large Shield:**  [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+2**

### Bless

The [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** enchantment protects its target against [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). This is fairly straightforward for spells and [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attacks**, as their color is defined by the Realm of the spell or creature making the attack. For other [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") however, which Realm they belong to is preset, and *does not depend on the attacking unit*.

Although [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** would technically work against [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") delivered by [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** spells, this omitted from the table below because there are simply no [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** spells in the official game that deal this type of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**. They all deliver [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"), against which [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** grants a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** bonus instead.

In addition to the spell and [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") of the same name, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")** is also available to [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") that can equip [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** weapons with the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Avenger](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Avenger_(Item_Power) "Holy Avenger (Item Power)")** Item Power, which also contains this effect.

## City Walls

[City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls") [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls") are a unique [Town Building](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Building "Town Building")
in that they provide an almost purely combat oriented benefit, rather
than an economical one. In addition to significantly changing the
tactics likely to be employed by both the attacker and the defender
during a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") siege, they also grant very tangible protection to the units inside.

 [This diagram shows the 4x4 Town Proper area and the 12 segments of the](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a6/Screenshot_CityWalls_Schema.png/revision/latest?cb=20120430000225)

This diagram shows the 4x4 Town Proper area and the 12 segments of the [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls").
 **W = Wall**
 **T = Tower**
 **G = Gate**



The walls surround a 4×4 tile area on the combat map commonly called
the "town proper". On each corner is a tower tile that is impassable for
units on either side, while a single dedicated section serves as the
"gate" that allows walking through. The entire structure is illustrated
by the image on the right.

Units standing *adjacent* to any of the wall or gate sections receive a bonus of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** against nearly all attacks originating from *outside the walled area*. The only exception is [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell Damage**,
which is never affected. However, each section of the City Walls can be
individually destroyed by the opponent during the battle. Should this
happen, the **Defense** bonus of *that particular section* is immediately reduced to only [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1**.
Regardless of its magnitude though, this protection is not exclusive to
the defending side. If an attacking unit manages to outmaneuver the
defenders and get inside the walls, they will enjoy the same benefit
when attacked from the outside. It may also be worth noting that [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** and [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal") [Non-Corporeal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Non-Corporeal "Non-Corporeal") units, while able to bypass the walls themselves during their movement, *do not negate* any **Defense** bonus granted to their opponent.

Units stationed in the middle of the Town, that are not adjacent to the walls, also get [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1**. In this case though, it can only ever apply against [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), since these units are too far away to be engaged in [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** from the outside. However, this bonus is increased to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** in the unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch.

Finally, City Walls are also the source of an obscure bug that requires the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") to fix. In nearly every battle that does *not* take place at a Town, units standing in the 4×4 area where the town proper *would be*
actually gain the benefit of a destroyed City Wall section. This is
even more pronounced in Insecticide, as units in the inner 2×2 square
gain an overall [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3**
against all Ranged Attacks from the outside. Essentially, the combat
map is treated as if City Walls were present, but all destroyed, except
that there are no visuals to indicate this fact.

## Unmitigated Changes

 [The combat unit display shows Defense as a numerical value with all unmitigated changes already factored in.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/3/34/Combat_Unit_Display_MaxDefense.png/revision/latest?cb=20181017192715)

The *combat unit display* shows **Defense** as a numerical value with all unmitigated changes already factored in.



**Defense** can be both
improved and lowered in many ways in the game, although the majority of
these effects have limitations on the types of units they can affect.
The base score is indicated as a row of bright [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shield** icons in the *unit statistics window*, and also includes improvements gained from [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience** and [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities"). Any other "universal" bonus that applies across the board will extend the row with golden icons ([Icon Defense Gold](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/1/15/Icon_Defense_Gold.png/revision/latest?cb=20150104003756)). Reductions, on the other hand, darken, or grey out, some of these icons instead ([Icon Defense Lost](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/0/00/Icon_Defense_Lost.png/revision/latest?cb=20150104003838), [Icon Defense Gold Lost](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/f/f8/Icon_Defense_Gold_Lost.png/revision/latest?cb=20150104003815)), always starting with any enhancement icons. The sole exception is the effect of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Berserk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Berserk "Berserk")** spell, which removes the entire row of icons rather than darkening them out, effectively hiding the unit's original score.

A full list of the effects that unconditionally modify **Defense** is compiled in the table below. It is evident from it, that [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") have the easiest time increasing this attribute, while [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") are more or less limited to "aura" effects and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** spells. If enabled in the game's Settings, the *combat unit display*, shown in the upper right corner when mousing over a unit, displays **Defense** as a single numerical value, with all unconditional modifiers already included.

### Examples and Quirks

Events lower (thus later) in the order of operations will supersede
the earlier ones. The procedure usually generates combat results that
make perfect sense, but will also also yield some that are unexpected.
Ranged attacks and counters, in particular, may be difficult to predict
due to color associations. The following examples may help illustrate
how these defensive buffs function.

* *[Sprites](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sprites "Sprites") [Sprites](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sprites "Sprites") fire a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** at a unit with both [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements "Resist Elements")** and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")***

:   :   Sprites use a [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** aligned ranged attack. [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")** will trigger first, adding [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+10** to the target's score, and prevent [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements "Resist Elements")** from taking any effect.
