**Haste** is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") belonging to the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery Magic** realm. It may only be cast during combat, for a [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**, and must be targeted at a friendly unit. The affected unit becomes twice as fast in everything it does: it has a doubled [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"), and makes two attacks (whether [Ranged](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") or [Melee](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")) whenever ordered to do so. The unit even [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") twice when assaulted by an enemy.

As a "combat-only" [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), **Haste**'s
effects will remain active only until the end of battle - or until the
enchanted unit dies, whichever comes first. The spell dissipates
automatically, and no trace of it remains when returning to the overland
map. Therefore, it has no further effect, does not need to be
dispelled, and has no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") to pay each turn. To give it any continuous effect, the wizard must cast this spell again and again whenever entering combat.

## Effects

Haste doubles the speed of the unit's movements, and doubles the
number of attacks the unit performs each time it is ordered to do so.

### Doubled Movement Allowance

**Haste**'s first effect is to double the [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") of the enchanted unit.

This allows the unit to move twice as far during each combat turn
(assuming no obstacles to navigate around). The added maneuverability
can be staggeringly effective, especially if the unit already had a high
[Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") to begin with.

Note that since Movement Allowance is only reset at the start of each of the unit's turns, casting **Haste**
upon a unit does not immediately change its remaining Movement Points
for this turn. The unit will only receive double its normal Movement
Points at the start of the subsequent turn (and any turn thereafter,
unless **Haste** is dispelled).

### Doubled Ranged Attacks

Whenever the enchanted unit is ordered to make a [*Ranged Attack*](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") *that uses ammunition*, it will instead perform *two* attacks in a row. When a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") is ordered, the unit uses up *2*
points of ammunition instead of one - and delivers about twice as much
damage - on average - with what visually appears to be a single attack.

This happens because *two* [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") have been dealt in a row, instead of just one. The unit makes a set of [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") rolls based on its [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Attack Strength**,
then makes another set of rolls to inflict further damage! Therefore,
damage isn't simply doubled; instead the unit actually makes two
separate attacks in rapid sequence, causing twice as much damage *on average* as a result.

Note that **Haste** doesn't give the unit twice as many [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") *in total* during combat, as the ammunition is also depleted twice as fast. It only means that the unit would deliver all of its [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") in a much shorter span of time. Unfortunately, it also means that the unit should be pickier about its [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") targets, as it *has* to spend two ammunition points on each target it picks.

This double ranged attack still costs the same amount of Movement
Points as a single attack would. It may still be performed even if the
unit has as little as 0.5 Movement Points remaining this turn.

This doubling effect does not apply when the unit is using a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Ranged Attack** that uses the attacker's own [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool. In these cases, only one Ranged Attack is made, as normal, and the caster's Mana pool is reduced by only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **3** as normal.

### Additional Ranged Attack Opportunity

[Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") usually cost a unit all of its Movement Points. However, in fact, [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
actually cost only 10 Movement Points - it's just that this is not
noticeable during normal gameplay because most units don't have that
many points.

[Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can sometimes gain a very high [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") exceeding 5 Movement Points per turn, primarily thanks to speed-boosting [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). When such a hero is enchanted with **Haste**,
his/her Movement Allowance is doubled and exceeds 10 Movement Points.
This opens the door to what is probably an unintended feature, but can
be exploited for the player's benefit.

When such a Hero makes a Ranged Attack, he/she spends only 10
Movement Points and can then use the remaining points to move and/or
attack a second time! Remember that with **Haste**, any Hero that uses ammunition (see above) will also get 2 [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
for the price of one. Therefore, such a Hero could make up to 4 Ranged
Attacks per turn (at the cost of depleting their ammo in 2 turns)!

### Doubled Melee Attacks

Whenever the enchanted unit is ordered to make a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), it will instead perform *two* attacks in rapid succession. However, instead of executing the entire [Melee Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Altering_the_Sequence "Melee Attack") twice (which would give the opponent two chances to [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")), **Haste**'s additional attacks are actually worked into the sequence itself. This has the interesting consequence that some [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") may be performed multiple times, while others can not.

[Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** and [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks**, at the start of the sequence, will both be executed twice. Consequently, [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") that can be affixed to these will also be delivered twice. On the other hand, [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"),
and their own related Touch Attacks are only performed once, giving a
distinct edge to the first two types of Special Attacks when used in
combination with **Haste**.

[Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") will also be dealt twice, with the added quirk that [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") will only affect the first of these, meaning that the second attack will occur concurrently with the opponent's [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") regardless of this ability. [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") that can be affixed to Melee Damage will be delivered with both strikes, making it possible for them to trigger up to *four* times during the entire sequence.

[Fear Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cause_Fear#Fear_Attack "Cause Fear")
are also only executed once, and apply any effect they have to both
iterations of Melee Damage, rather than being delivered separately
before each.

Note that the Movement Points cost of making a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") is increased along with the [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") while under the effect of **Haste**.
Therefore, the unit still spends the same proportion of its total
Movement Allowance (50% to be exact) when ordered to attack. Counting in
the extra attacks bestowed by **Haste**,
if a unit spends all of its movement points on Melee Attacks, it will
make a total of 4 attacks instead of the normal 2. The only caveat is
that it has to attack each target twice (i.e. it cannot select 4
different targets).

### Doubled Counter Attacks

During a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") initiated by an enemy unit, the **Hasted** unit also [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") twice.

In other words, when [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is being dealt by the attacker and the target, the **Hasted**
unit delivers its damage, and then makes another series of Melee Damage
rolls to inflict even more damage upon its assailant. Both of these
will also trigger Melee [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), if the unit possesses any.

On the other hand, just like when attacking, [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") can still only be performed once during the sequence.

## Usage

**Haste** may only be cast during combat, for a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**.
The spell will only affect the targeted unit until the end of the
battle - at which point it will dissipate. Since its effects do not
carry over to the campaign map, there is no need to pay a per-turn [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost")
to maintain the spell - but it does mean that you must re-cast the
spell every time you enter combat if you wish a unit to enjoy its effect
repeatedly, as would be the case with other [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment").

Like all other [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), **Haste** must targeted at a friendly unit.

Once the spell is in effect, the unit will glow with a [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Blue** aura, unless there is a more powerful spell also affecting it.

**Haste** may not be
voluntarily removed from a unit once it's been cast. There should be no
reason to do so - as once the spell is cast it is purely beneficial, and
costs nothing extra to keep (it goes away by the end of combat anyway).

## Acquisition

As a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** spell, **Haste** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**2** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is not guaranteed unless the Wizard acquires at least [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**10** Spellbooks.

Customized Wizards possessing [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**11**
Spellbooks at creation time may choose this spell as one of their
default spells before starting the game, in which case the spell will
already be researched and available for casting immediately on the first
turn.

Wizards with [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**2** to [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**9** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") **Haste** during the game. The chance for this spell to appear for research increases with the number of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or obtains during gameplay. With [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**10** or [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**11** Spellbooks, the spell is guaranteed to appear for Research at some point, if it is not already available for casting.

**Haste** has a base [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **1,700**.

With at least [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**2** Spellbooks, the **Haste** spell *may be* acquired as a reward for winning encounters in creature [Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lair "Lair"), [Towers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower"), et cetera, or when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival wizard who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

**Haste** has two primary
functions: to allow a unit to maneuver more easily across the
battlefield, and to boost up its damage-dealing potential by a factor of
2. In essence, one could say that most units actually do become twice
as effective on the battlefield as a result of this spell, making it one
of the most potent [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") available.

Any unit targeted by this spell will find it extremely beneficial, though it is often best to place it on high-powered [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
creatures who can put it to good use: charging straight into enemy
lines and causing massive damage. Powerful "suicide" units are some of
the best targets for this spell, as the spell does not increase the
unit's protection in any way. [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") units, sadly, do not benefit quite so much.
