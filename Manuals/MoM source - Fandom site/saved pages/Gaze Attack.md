A **Gaze Attack** is one of the many [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") Types featured in *Master of Magic*. A **Gaze Attack** is only executed as an integral part of a unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack").

**Gaze Attacks** are only available to units possessing one of the three [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") that bestow such an attack, including [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze"), [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze"), and [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze"). Each of these abilities provides the unit with a **Gaze Attack** that delivers one specific kind of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"). The type of damage delivered by the attack determines its effect, and it can be quite different from one **Gaze Attack** to the next. Units that do not possess one of the **Gaze Attack** abilities by default will never be able to use a **Gaze Attack**.

**Gaze Attacks** are executed nearly halfway through the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") sequence, before any [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is delivered. Thus, the more enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** a **Gaze Attack** can kill, the less danger there will be to the **Gaze Attacker** once the target gets the chance to retaliate. Note that [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") and/or [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") will be executed before any **Gaze Attacks** take place.

**Gaze Attacks** may be executed by either or both of the participants in a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). However, if both units are capable of a **Gaze Attack**, the initiator of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") gets to go first, followed by the target of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") (if it is still alive by that point).

Two of the three different kinds of **Gaze Attacks**, namely [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") and [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze"), will always trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") (if present). If this occurs, the target will feel no harm from the **Gaze Attack** whatsoever. A [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") attack, however, does not trigger [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity").

The strength of a **Gaze Attack**
is indicated in a unit's details panel as the value of its relevant
gaze ability. For example, a unit's details panel may list "[Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") -4", "[Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze")
10", and so forth. This value can have different meanings depending on
the type of damage delivered by the attack. For example, "[Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") -4" inflicts a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-4 Resistance** penalty on the target. To understand how these values work, it is best to read the article explaining the relevant ability.

The strength of a unit's **Gaze Attack**
will likely stay the same throughout the unit's existence, and normally
cannot be modified by any spells or effects. Again, specific types of
gaze-related abilities may behave differently from others, so please
read the relevant ability articles for more information.

Possession of a **Gaze Attack** allows a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit to attack any enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit.

## Concept

Ancient mythology, and particularly Greek mythology, contains various
creatures who can cause harm to anyone making eye-contact with them.
Most famous of these were the Gorgons, and primarily the Medusa, whose
stare could turn any creature into a solid stone statue. In the ancient
tale, the only way to avoid this effect was to approach and attack the
creature while observing it only through a mirror (specifically, a
reflective shield).

In the world of *Master of Magic*, various creatures possess
a similarly-deadly ability of killing purely by looking at the target.
In the game, sneaky tactics involving polished shields may not be used,
so any unit attacking a gazer opponent is grievously risking itself.
Because only eye-contact is required, the **Gaze Attacker**
can harm its opponent from a relatively safe (though not overly long)
distance, well before physical combat ensues between the two units.
Therefore, the more enemies are killed or harmed by this gaze attack,
the fewer remain to harm the **Gaze Attacker** when physical contact finally occurs.

## Effect

The effect of a **Gaze Attack** depends entirely on the type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") that it delivers. All **Gaze Attacks** deliver one damage type, and the exact type is determined by the [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") that gave the unit this kind of attack.

The following table shows the three available gaze-related [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities"), and the type of damage delivered by their respective **Gaze Attacks**:

| Icon | Name | Gaze Attack Damage Type |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") | [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") | [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") |
| [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") | [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") | [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") |
| [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") | [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") | [Stoning Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Damage "Stoning Damage") |

[Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") and [Stoning Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Damage "Stoning Damage") are very similar, forcing each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** or die instantly. The "strength" of a [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") or [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") ability indicates a temporary [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
penalty inflicted on the target to make it harder for those figures to
survive the attack. The real difference between these two damage types
is that each will trigger a different immunity, if present.

[Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") operates very differently - causing a precise number of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target. Unlike [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") or [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage"), it does not involve any [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls by the attacker, nor any [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls by the target. Thus the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** inflicted will always be the same. That amount is indicated by the strength of the [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") ability.

### Gaze Attacks and Multi-Figure Units

[Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") and [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") affects all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** when targeted at a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**. That is, each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**
in the target unit has to resist the effect individually, with the same
penalty (if applicable), regardless of the amount of remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in either the attacking or defending unit.

In contrast, [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") is more of a direct damage ability, and will only resolve its effect once, against the lead [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** of the target unit.

What little research has been carried out on this subject indicates that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** execute only one **Gaze Attack** upon their target, regardless of how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** they has left. Thus, as long as the unit has at least [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **1 Figure** remaining, its **Gaze Attack** is as deadly as it was when the unit was still fully healthy.

This also means that confronting an enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** **Gaze Attack** unit is very deadly even if that unit has lost most of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. Try to finish it off from a distance (with [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") or spells) if possible, instead of risking a unit in Melee combat.

## Immunities

Any [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") or [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") attack performed against a target possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") is completely ineffective. These **Gaze Attacks** will trigger that [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), causing all damage from the attack to be completely ignored.

[Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") is an exception: it will not trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") at all, and thus will hurt a Magic-Immune target just as easily as it harms any other target.

## Execution and Sequence

The **Gaze Attack** is one of the [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") types. This means that it cannot be executed on its own, but rather is always a part of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") executed by one unit upon the other.

A unit may use its **Gaze Attacks** regardless of whether it is the initiator of Melee combat or its target. If both attacker and target possess a **Gaze Attack**, *they may both use it* during the fight. Therefore, a unit with a **Gaze Attack** is lethal both when attacking and when attacked.

**Gaze Attacks** are always executed after [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") or [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), and always before either combatant gets to deal [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack").

| Stage | Attacker | Defender |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") / [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") | -- |
|  | **Gaze Attack** | -- |
| 2 | -- | **Gaze Attack** |
| 3 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") |

As the table above shows, if both attacker and target possess a **Gaze Attack**, then the initiator of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") gets to deliver its own **Gaze Attack** first. If the target survives, it may unleash its own **Gaze Attack** in retaliation. In either case, **Gaze Attacks** occur before either unit may inflict [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") upon the other unit.

The position of the **Gaze Attack** on the above table gives it an important tactical advantage: if a **Gaze Attack** can successfully kill off one or more enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**, the enemy unit will be weakened by the time stage 3 (the exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack")) is reached. This potentially reduces the harm caused to the **Gaze Attacker** by the enemy at the end of the Melee process. If a **Gaze Attack** manages to kill off *all* enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** simultaneously (a not-uncommon event), then the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") process abruptly ends with victory for the **Gaze Attacker**!

## Ground vs. Air

The normal rules of combat prohibit any [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit from initiating a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against any [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** opponent. Such units may only [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to this opponent, allowing the [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** enemy to maneuver freely around them without fear of being attacked.

However, possession of a **Gaze Attack** by a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit allows that unit to break the rules: it may freely and voluntarily execute [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units.

Therefore, while such a unit is not actually [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying**, it can still engage enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units on relatively even terms during combat.

## Hidden Attack Strength

**Gaze Attacks** have 2 damage components whereas this article ignored one component so far (except for [Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")
where it is replaced by Doom Gaze). The one already mentioned component
does damage based on the opponent's resistance: each enemy figure has
to make a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll (or two rolls in case of Chaos Spawn); if it fails this roll, the enemy unit loses [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** worth of a whole figure.

The so-far-unmentioned component behaves like conventional [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") instead: The unit has a certain [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), each figure with the Gaze ability makes an [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") (rolls as many dies as the attack strength indicates), and the enemy unit will make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") to avoid some of the incoming damage. All in all, this component works like [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** and [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath** Attacks, whereas it is also applied in [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") (but a little later than Thrown and Breath).

The Attack Strength of this component is usually hidden though.
In case of a Chaos Spawn, it corresponds to the strength of its Doom
Gaze. All other units have a default Attack Strength of 1.

As said before, the default Attack Strength is **1** for all units
except Chaos Spawns. So any attack bonus will still have only a
marginal effect. Usually, stronger enemy units are able to avoid most
damage thanks to their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**, and the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **resist-or-die**
component is the one dealing the major damage. However, there's a
special case where the hidden Attack Strength can have a tremendous
effect. The [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **resist-or-die** component only works in tandem with the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **conventional damage** component. If the hidden [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") is reduced to 0, then the other component will also stop working.

This can become the case, if [Black Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Prayer "Black Prayer"), [Mind Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mind_Storm "Mind Storm") or [True Light](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Light "True Light") are in effect. For example, a single [Black Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Prayer "Black Prayer") spell will disable the **Gaze Attack** of all enemy [Night Stalkers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Night_Stalker "Night Stalker"), [Basilisks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk") and [Gorgons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons"), provided there's no other effect boosting the hidden Attack Strength. This situation is similar to [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") only working in tandem with conventional [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") (usually [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")), e.g. [Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices") will lose their [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch"), if their Attack Strength is lowered to [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **0** (note that Stoning Touch and Stoning Gaze behave differently regarding applicable [Melee Phase](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Attack_Sequence "Melee Attack") and number of affected figures).

**Examples**

* [Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk") vs [Wolf Riders](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_Riders "Wolf Riders") without any other effects: Let's examine the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **conventional damage** component first. The Basilisk's hidden Attack Strength is **1**,
  so it will make a single roll which has a 40 % chance to succeed
  (+10 % To Hit). Now, the Wolf Riders will use their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shields**
  which will cancel a single damage point in at least half of the times.
  All in all, there's roughly a 14 % chance that the Wolf Riders will
  suffer [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Damage**. However the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **resist-or-die** component will have usually a significant effect. Even on [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Veteran**
  level, each Wolf Riders figure will fail the resistance roll in
  50 % of the cases (note that the Basilisk's Gaze lowers Resistance
  by [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-1** temporarily). The Wolf Riders unit will suffer [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5 Damage** for each failed roll. All Wolf Riders figures that died during Gaze Phase will not be able to [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

* [Gorgons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons") vs [Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors") under [Nature Node Aura](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node#Nature_Node_Unit_Bonus_Aura "Nature Node") (combat near Nature Node): Let's examine the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **conventional damage** component first. The Aura boosts the Gorgons' hidden Attack Strength to **3**. Each Gorgons figure will thus make 1.5 damage in average (+20% To Hit, 1.5 = 3 \* 0.5). The Phantom Warriors have no [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
  to reduce the incoming damage, and, when considering overkill damage
  and assuming 4 Gorgons figures, will suffer 6 points of damage in
  average. Phantom Warriors have [Stoning Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Immunity "Stoning Immunity"), so the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **resist-or-die** component will have no effect. Thanks to the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **conventional damage** component almost no Phantom Warriors figure will survive the Gaze Phase, thus the [Gorgons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons") will barely suffer any damage in the subsequent [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

* [Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn") under effect of [Mind Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mind_Storm "Mind Storm"): The Chaos Spawn's hidden Attack Strength is **4** and the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **conventional damage** component is replaced with [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") dealing a fixed amount of damage (independently of the enemy's defense). [Mind Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mind_Storm "Mind Storm") lowers the Attack Strength to **0**. This not only interrupts the Doom Gaze, the whole Gaze Phase is skipped. This means that there isn't any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **resist-or-die** component either ([Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") and [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze")). The Poison effect is also skipped in Gaze Phase (also true for Melee Phase because Melee strength is [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **0**, too). The [Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn") just became a punchbag - not being able to inflict any damage at all.
  The situation is differently, if [Chaos Surge](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Surge "Chaos Surge") is in effect. In this case the Chaos Spawn's hidden Attack Strength is just lowered to **1** (1 = 4 + 2 - 5). While Doom Gaze will just inflict [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Damage**,
  Stoning Gaze and Death Gaze can still inflict considerable damage (and
  there's also Poison): Stoning Gaze and Death Gaze will still kill an
  enemy unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **9** crosses half of the times thanks to their innate [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-4**
  Resistance penalty which is unaffected by Mind Storm. Note that a unit
  consisting of 8 figures will have to make 8 Stoning rolls and 8 Death
  rolls (both at [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-4**)
  and that all damage is summed up before calculating how many figures
  died (so a whole unit may still die, even if multiple figures manage to
  resist both rolls at the same time).
