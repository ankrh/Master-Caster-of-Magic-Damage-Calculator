**Life Stealing Damage** is a type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") that can be delivered by various units as part of their [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and/or [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). An attack delivering **Life Stealing Damage** stands a good chance of causing extra [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to the target, and may even heal the attacker himself in the process.

When a target is struck by **Life Stealing Damage**, it does not make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls. Instead, it makes a single [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll. The result of this roll determines how much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** the target takes, and how much of the attacker's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health** is restored. A successful [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll by the target will completely avert any damage.

Aside from damaging the defender and healing the attacker, **Life Stealing Damage** also has an additional effect: If any unit is killed primarily as a result of **Life Stealing Damage** inflicted upon it, it might rise as an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit once the battle is concluded - and will now be under the command of the enemy.

**Life Stealing Damage** is commonly found as a component in the attacks of powerful [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** Creatures, thanks to their [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") ability. [Ravashack the Necromancer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ravashack_the_Necromancer "Ravashack the Necromancer") also possesses [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal"), and thus delivers **Life Stealing Damage** with each of his attacks. Other [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may also add a **Life Stealing Damage** component to their [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") by equipping a [Magical Melee Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") that contains the "Vampiric" enchantment. Other than these, no other units or attacks will deliver **Life Stealing Damage**.

## Effect

**Life Stealing Damage**
has three distinct effects: It may cause damage to the target, it may
heal the attacker, and it may cause the target to rise as an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") at the end of the battle. Each of these effects depends primarily on a single [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll made by the target in response to the attack.

### Rolling for Damage

Whenever an attack containing a **Life Stealing Damage**
component strikes any target on the battlefield, that target must
immediately make a single die roll - resulting in a random number
between 1 and 10. This number is then compared to the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score (with possible modifiers which are explained below).

If the rolled number is *equal to or less than* the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score, then the target takes no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** whatsoever, and will also ignore the other two effects described below.

On the other hand, if the rolled number is *higher than* the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
score, the difference between these two values directly determines the
amount of damage inflicted by the attack. The following formula is used:

```
Damage = Rolled Number - Target's Resistance
```

Thus, if the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score is 4, and the rolled number came up 7, then the target suffers 7-4 = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3 points of Damage**.

This [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is directly applied to the target, lowering its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** and killing off [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** as necessary. The target *may not* make any [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls or any additional [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls to avert this damage.

#### Resistance Modifiers

:   Each attack that delivers **Life Stealing Damage** inflicts a certain penalty on the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
    score. The higher this penalty, the less chance the target has to "win"
    the roll and avert the damage altogether. Also, a higher penalty
    increases the potential amount of **Life Stealing Damage** that can be inflicted, and the greater the statistical chance to inflict an amount of damage closer to that potential.

:   The [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty varies from attacker to attacker, with the "strongest" penalty being that inflicted by a [Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord")'s attacks: [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-5**! This attack has no problem affecting units with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **5** or less (it will never fail to inflict damage on them), and can occasionally affect targets with Resistance scores as high as [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **14**.

:   The weakest attacks, those possessed by [Ravashack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ravashack "Ravashack") as well as other [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") who acquire a "Vampiric" weapon (see below), inflict no [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty on the target at all. If the target has [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** or more, a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s **Life Stealing Damage** will always fail to affect it.

### Healing the Attacker

Whenever **Life Stealing Damage** actually manages to hurt opponent, the attacker is also immediately healed by a number of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** corresponding to the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** inflicted.

For most [Life Stealing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") units, it seems that the attacker is healed by *half* the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** inflicted on the target. [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") delivering **Life Stealing Damage** seem to be healed by an amount equal to the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** they caused.

For example, if a [Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord")'s **Life Stealing Damage** component inflicts [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4 points of Damage** on a target, the [Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord") is healed by [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2 Hit Points**. If [Ravashack the Necromancer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ravashack_the_Necromancer "Ravashack the Necromancer") inflicts [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**, he is healed by [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **4**.
The reasons for this disparity are currently unclear and unexplored.
You may or may not experience the same results in your game.

Furthermore, it seems that some units ([Demon Lords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord") in particular) can actually gain a boost to their maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health** as a result of this effect. If this occurs, the unit will only keep its extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**
until the end of the battle. The mechanism behind this effect is not
understood at all, and any further information will be helpful.

### Creating Undead

When a battle is concluded, the game runs through each and every dead
unit on the battlefield, checking what type of damage killed that unit.
If the majority of the damage that killed the unit is found to be **Life Stealing Damage**, and it is the enemy army that just won the battle, the unit will rise again as an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit, and will switch allegiance. It is now under the control of the master of the enemy army.

For example, imagine a battle where several [Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths") assault an enemy [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit and completely destroy it. If the majority of the damage inflicted on the [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") unit was **Life Stealing Damage** from the [Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")' attack, and the [Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")' army went on to win this battle, then the [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") returns as an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit, serving the [Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths")' master.

Remember that for this to happen, the majority of the damage must have been **Life Stealing Damage**. Most often, an attacker will end up delivering **Life Stealing Damage** alongside regular [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"). If we go back to the example above, the [Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths") will probably deliver a lot of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") in their attacks in addition to the **Life Stealing Damage**. The [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry") will only be raised as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") if the amount of **Life Stealing Damage** delivered was greater than the amount of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage").

For more information about what happens to a unit when it is raised in this way, see the article on [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead").

## Immunities

**Life Stealing Damage** is considered to be associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** realm. As a result, it will never hurt any unit that possesses [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity"). Since no **Life Stealing Damage** is inflicted, no [Healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing "Healing") will occur, and the target will never rise as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead").

Furthermore, **Life Stealing Damage** is also entirely blocked by [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") as described above.

Finally, any unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10 Resistance** or more may be completely invulnerable to **Life Stealing Damage**, as it cannot possibly fail its [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll. Of course, it's always important to remember that **Life Stealing Damage** may inflict a penalty on the target's Resistance, lowering it below [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** and thus making it possible to hurt that target.

Note that these immunities will only block the **Life Stealing Damage** component of an attack. If the attack also delivers any [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") or any other type of damage, that damage may or may not go through the target's immunities unimpeded.

## Sources of Life Stealing Damage

**Life Stealing Damage** is a fairly rare [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"). It can only come from either the [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") ability or from a [Magical Melee Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") containing the "Vampiric" enchantment.

### Units with Life Steal

:   *Main article: [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal")*

The [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") ability is the most common source of **Life Stealing Damage**. Whenever a unit possessing this ability makes any type of attack ([Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") or even [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")), it will also execute a separate [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") that delivers **Life Stealing Damage**.

As a [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), this occurs simultaneously with the exchange of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") between the attacker and the target.

The strength of a [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") ability is very important: it indicates how much [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty is inflicted on the target. The greater the penalty, the greater the chance of inflicting any **Life Stealing Damage** on the target, as well as the average damage expectancy and maximum damage output.

### Vampiric Weapons

The other source of **Life Stealing Damage** comes from weapons imbued with the "Vampiric" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). This enchantment can be placed only on a [Sword](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Mace](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace") or [Axe](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"). During the custom [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting") process, the "Vampiric" bonus is only available if you possess at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**5 Death** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). Naturally, it's always possible to find a weapon containing this bonus as a reward for defeating [Encounters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter").

In theory, when such an item is equipped on a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), that Hero's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") should now deliver **Life Stealing Damage** in addition to any [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or other damage types it has. The process is handled exactly the same as with the [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") attack described above - there is no real difference. Note that the **Life Stealing Damage** delivered by the Hero's attack does not inflict any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalties on the target.

The Hero's attack will only deliver **Life Stealing Damage** while the Hero is holding the enchanted item. If the item is removed or lost, the [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s attack no longer delivers **Life Stealing Damage**.
