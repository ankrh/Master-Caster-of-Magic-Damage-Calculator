**Berserk** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") belonging to the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Magic** realm. The spell may only be cast during combat, for a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **30**. It must be targeted at a friendly unit. The targeted unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") strength is raised to double its current value, though the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is reduced to exactly [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**. The spell lasts only until the end of combat, and has no [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"). There is no lasting, overland version of this spell.

## Effects

**Berserk** drives a unit
wild with combat frenzy, allowing it to attack with twice as much
ferocity as normal. However, the unit no longer gives any regard to its
own safety, and so loses any chance to block incoming damage to itself.

### Doubled Melee Attack

While **Berserk** is affecting a unit, that unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") strength is raised to exactly double the amount the unit would have without **Berserk**.

This allows the unit to potentially inflict twice as much damage
as normal - and subsequently increases the average amount of damage the
unit will inflict with each attack by 2x.

This doubling is applied *after* any bonuses or penalties which the unit receives from any other source.

For example, a unit with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** and affected by the [Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels") spell is targeted by **Berserk**. The unit's current Melee Attack is [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** + [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **2** = [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **5**. **Berserk** doubles this *final* value, so the unit now has a Melee Attack with a strength of exactly [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **5** \* 2 = [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **10**.

Note that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** benefit greatly from this, as *each individual figure's* Melee Attack strength is doubled. However, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure Units**
tend to have very high Melee Attack strengths to begin with, and thus
can end with ridiculously-high attack values themselves. Therefore most
targets will benefit from this effect to a great extent.

### Nullified Defense Score

While **Berserk** is affecting a unit, that unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is reduced to exactly [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**.

This means that whenever this unit is attacked, it cannot make any [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") rolls at all to block incoming damage. Of course the unit may still use its [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") score to block certain effects - just not ones that apply direct damage.

The [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is absolutely nullified regardless of any [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") or effects that may be increasing it at the time. While **Berserk** is active no effect can raise the unit's Defense score above 0.

For example, a unit under the effect of [Iron Skin](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Iron_Skin "Iron Skin") should get a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") bonus of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+5**, but while **Berserk** is in effect the unit's Defense score is still exactly 0. The bonus is simply ignored.

## Usage

**Berserk** may only be cast during combat. It has a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **30**, and must be targeted at a friendly unit on the battlefield.

The spell remains in effect until the end of battle, or until dispelled by the opponent. When **Berserk**'s effect ends, the unit's properties will return to their normal state.

Once the spell is in effect, the unit will glow with a [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**purple** aura, unless there is a more powerful spell also affecting it.

## Acquisition

As an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** spell, **Berserk** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least one [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is almost never guaranteed.

Customized Wizards possessing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**11**
Spellbooks at creation time may choose this spell as one of their
default spells before starting the game, in which case the spell will
already be researched and available for casting immediately on the first
turn.

Wizards who possess at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**8** Spellbooks, or Wizards with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**11** Spellbooks who did not select **Berserk** as a guaranteed spell, will be able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") this spell at some point during their campaign. Wizards with fewer than [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**8** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to Research it. The chance for this spell to appear increases with the number of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or obtains during gameplay.

**Berserk** has a base [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **720**.

With at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**1** Spellbook, the **Berserk** spell *may be* acquired as a reward for winning encounters in creature [Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lair "Lair"), [Towers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower"), et cetera, or when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival wizard who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

**Berserk** can turn any unit into an absolutely terrifying [Melee Attacker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), especially if this unit is already very powerful. It even doubles the effect of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")-
increasing bonuses that are currently affecting the unit, and can
potentially increase its attack strength to ridiculously-high levels.

On the other hand, it makes the unit incredibly weak defensively.
Any attack made upon this unit can cause massive damage to it, which
means that the unit may be destroyed quite easily.

The best times to cast this spell would be when an enemy unit has plenty of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense"),
and thus your units will benefit from an increase in their Melee Attack
strength in order to overcome this defense. This is especially true
when the enemy's Defense score is [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** or higher, which few other units can match.

The best targets for **Berserk** are obviously units with [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike"),
since the lost defense won't hurt you if the enemy is destroyed before
getting a chance to strike back. Other viable choices are units which
already have a very high [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
score, since the higher it is the more benefit you get from doubling
it; and units that already have poor or no defense (such as [Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves") or [Phantom Beasts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Beast "Phantom Beast")) - they stand little to lose from the spell, only gaining its advantages!

Remember that **Berserk** can easily turn your unit into a target - especially if the enemy is using plenty of [Ranged Attackers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (who will turn it into a pincushion rapidly!). Therefore, units with plenty of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**
make good targets, as they will tend to survive for a longer period of
time even while taking substantial damage. You could also delay casting **Berserk**
until the very last moment - allowing the targeted unit to defend
itself up until it reaches the enemy, and only then explode with
destructive rage on the target.

In most cases, expect your **Berserking** unit to either take plenty of damage or be destroyed entirely. This is one of the reasons why [Regenerating](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") units, such as [Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves"),
make great targets for this spell - it doesn't really matter if they
die, because they will come back to life as long as you actually manage
to win the battle. Whenever they make contact with the enemy, hordes of **Berserking** [Regenerating](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") units are exceptionally effective.
