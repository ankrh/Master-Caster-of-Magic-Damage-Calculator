[Ability NonCorporeal](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/d/df/Ability_NonCorporeal.png/revision/latest?cb=20120114063015)

**Non-Corporeal** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*. The **Non-Corporeal** ability gives a unit several advantages - but it is also one of the few abilities that also gives a disadvantage.

For starters, on the overland map, a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit with **Non-Corporeal**
can travel across both land and water tiles, and will always pay
exactly 0.5 Movement Points to enter any tile, regardless of its [Terrain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Terrain "Terrain"). This allows **Non-Corporeal** units to act as fantastic scouts, particularly when they have a high [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"). During combat, **Non-Corporeal** units can move through [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls") unimpeded.

All **Non-Corporeal**
units are also completely immune to several spells - particularly those
which hinder movement or target the tile underneath the unit.

On the downside, **Non-Corporeal** denies the unit any benefits from traveling along an [Enchanted Road](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchanted_Road "Enchanted Road"). Thus, such units cannot move as rapidly across the land during the later stages of the game (esp. on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror")).

Exactly 6 units in the game are **Non-Corporeal** by default. All six are [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit").

It is possible to make any unit **Non-Corporeal** by casting the [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") on it. Furthermore, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may be equipped with [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") possessing a [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power").

## Description

In the world of *Master of Magic*, a creature does not require a
true physical form in order to exist. Some creatures have the ability
to exist in a partially ghost-like form, which does not interact with
physical obstacles or indeed with the ground itself. Such units, called "**Non-Corporeal**"
("without body") can perform things that would seem otherwise
impossible, such as passing through sheer walls or gliding straight over
a chasm as though it was land.

## Effect

The **Non-Corporeal**
ability confers two important advantages to a unit possessing it: the
ability to move freely and quickly through obstacles and any type of
terrain, and the ability to shrug off effects that would slow it down.
On the other hand, a **Non-Corporeal** unit is also unable to utilize the benefits of [Enchanted Roads](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchanted_Road "Enchanted Road").

### Freedom of Movement

A [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** **Non-Corporeal** unit enjoys two benefits related to freedom (and speed) of movement.

On the overland map, such a **Non-Corporeal** unit can move across both land and water tiles. It essentially possesses both a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** ability and a [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** ability at the same time. While the unit's [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") remains unchanged, it is no longer restricted to using either water *or* land.

Furthermore, entering any type of [Terrain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Terrain "Terrain") on the overland map costs the **Non-Corporeal** unit only 0.5 Movement Points. This allows the unit to essentially move a number of tiles equal to twice its [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"), each turn. Increasing a **Non-Corporeal** unit's [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance"),
therefore, can allow it to travel vast distances very quickly. The
effect is identical to that of a "regular" unit traveling down a long [Road](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Road "Road"), except the **Non-Corporeal** unit does not need an existing road, and can even travel this fast across [Oceans](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ocean "Ocean")!

Some freedom of movement also applies during combat, since the **Non-Corporeal** unit is unhindered by [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls"). It can pass through them just like a [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit would. Note that this applies only to [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls"): the unit will still suffer if it tries to pass through a [Wall of Fire](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Fire "Wall of Fire"), due to the magical nature of such a wall.

#### Exceptions

:   Note that [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** **Non-Corporeal** units do not enjoy either of the above benefits, since the [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** ability overrides the **Non-Corporeal** ability in this regard.

:   For starters, [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** **Non-Corporeal** units must pay 1 Movement Point per tile when moving on the overland map, as all [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units do.

:   Furthermore, since [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units already ignore most battlefield obstacles (i.e. [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls")), the same capability given by the **Non-Corporeal** ability is redundant.

:   This is somewhat of a disadvantage, but it's not without compensation: as explained below, [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** **Non-Corporeal** units may utilize [Roads](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Road "Road"), balancing things out a little.

:   Nonetheless, it means that [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** **Non-Corporeal** units do not make ultra-fast scouts.

### Immunity to Certain Spells

The **Non-Corporeal** ability also confers total immunity to several types of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature** spells that either hinder movement or cause damage to the terrain underneath the unit.

* The [Web](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Web "Web") spell or [Web Spell Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Web_Spell "Web Spell") cannot be targeted at any **Non-Corporeal** unit. The game will throw a red error box if this is attempted, and request a different target.
* The [Cracks Call](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cracks_Call "Cracks Call") spell will do nothing when targeted at a **Non-Corporeal** unit (wasting its entire [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost")).
* The [Entangle](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Entangle "Entangle") spell will not slow down any **Non-Corporeal** unit on the battlefield.
* The overland spells [Earthquake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Earthquake "Earthquake") and [Nature's Wrath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature%27s_Wrath "Nature's Wrath") will cause no damage to **Non-Corporeal** units whatsoever.

Strangely, the mud created by an [Earth to Mud](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Earth_to_Mud "Earth to Mud") spell *does* in fact slow down **Non-Corporeal** units as it would any other unit. This contradicts the idea behind **Non-Corporeality**, and is assumed to be an oversight by the game's developers.

### No Use of Enchanted Roads

Unfortunately, **Non-Corporeality**
comes with a price, which may seem unimportant at first but can become
very important during the late game, especially for empires located
primarily on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror").

**Non-Corporeal** units do not enjoy the benefits of an [Enchanted Road](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchanted_Road "Enchanted Road"). When entering a tile containing such a road, they pay the same [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**0.5** they would for entering any other tile, completely disregarding the [Enchanted Road](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchanted_Road "Enchanted Road").

#### Work-Arounds

:   Fortunately, the limitation can be circumvented entirely by grouping a **Corporeal** unit with the **Non-Corporeal**
    creature. The corporeal unit's overland movement restrictions are
    considered by the game to be tighter than those of its companion. They
    will, as a result, override the **Non-Corporeal** property, and the group can travel the enchanted road at unlimited speed.

:   Furthermore, as mentioned above, the ability to [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Fly** overrides **Non-Corporeality** with regards to movement costs and restrictions. While this means that [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** **Non-Corporeal** units do not move as fast as [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** units, it does mean that they can use [Roads](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Road "Road") as normal.

:   Thus, if a unit has both [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** and **Non-Corporeal**, it can still enjoy speed benefits from [Roads](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Road "Road"), but will not be sped up on other types of terrain like other **Non-Corporeal** units would be.

## Acquiring Non-Corporeal

The **Non-Corporeal** ability can be added artificially to a unit by one of two means:

* Casting the [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") on the unit.
* Imbuing a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") with the "Wraithform" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"), and equipping it on a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

Both methods give the unit the same **Non-Corporeal** ability described above in this article - except for the fact that it is not permanent.

### Wraith Form Unit Enchantment

[Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") is a [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** realm. When cast on any unit, it will add the **Non-Corporeal** ability to that unit. The ability remains while the spell is in effect, and behaves exactly as described in this article.

When cast on the overland map, [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") has a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **150**. It has a lingering effect, so that the targeted unit will keep its **Non-Corporeal** ability so long as the spell continues to exist. While it exists, the spell requires an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **3** to be paid at the start of each turn.

Alternatively, it is possible to cast [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") during combat as an impromptu [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). In this case the spell's basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") is only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **30**,
but its effects will only last until the end of the battle (or until
dispelled by the enemy). Since neither the spell nor the effect will
carry over to the overland map, there is no associated [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") with this version of the spell.

Note that while enchanted by [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form"), a unit's details panel will show the **Non-Corporeal** icon with a purple background and the label "Wraithform". Despite the label, this is functionally identical to the **Non-Corporeal** ability.

### Wraithform Item Power

A permanent version of the [Wraith Form](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith_Form "Wraith Form") spell can be imbued into any [Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shield "Shield"), [Chainmail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chainmail "Chainmail"), [Platemail](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Platemail "Platemail") or [Jewelry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry") piece. When a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wears this item, he/she receives the **Non-Corporeal** ability.

During [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting"), it is only possible to select the "Wraithform" bonus if you possess at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**4 Death** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
Otherwise, "Wraithform" is not available. Of course, it is always
possible to find an item already enchanted with "Wraithform" as reward
for defeating an [Encounter](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter").

The **Non-Corporeal** ability bestowed by an item is transient: it will disappear if the Hero ever removes or loses the enchanted item.
