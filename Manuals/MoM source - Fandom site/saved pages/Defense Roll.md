A **Defense Roll** is a series of d10 rolls made to determine how much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** a target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** can reduce from an incoming attack. It may only be rolled once per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** per attack, but can occur multiple times as a result of a single attack; if it manages to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** multiple [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** (i.e. does "excess" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** over what is required to kill the first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**).

The term "d10" originates from classic role-playing games, where
"d" stands for "die" or "dice", and the number is used to denote the
amount of sides on this dice. In computer gaming, a roll is a randomly
generated number within certain confines (i.e. a roll of the figurative
dice). Thus, a d10 roll is, in essence, a randomly generated integer
between 1 and 10.

For a **Defense Roll**, the amount of "dice" rolled corresponds to the effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** making it. This is determined on the spot, using the same statistic of the unit (that the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** is part of) as a baseline. Thus, a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** with an effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** of **5** would roll "5d10".

Each individual die is either a "success" or a "failure" based on the number it rolled, with the base chance of success being **30%** (3-in-10). [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** reduction is then measured by the amount of "successful" rolls out of the total, and is applied directly to the incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

The term **Defense Roll** is sometimes also used to refer to the individual dice cast during this process, e.g. by saying that a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** with [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **4** will make **4** **Defense Rolls** when subjected to [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). This article considers that entire *series* of rolls to be a single **Defense Roll** instead, in an attempt to simplify and include the calculation of *effective Defense* in the same process as "rolling the dice".

## Concept

Can the shining armor of the kinght deflect the claws and fangs of
the hounds of hell? Will he raise his shield in time to defend against
their breath of infernal flames? These are the kinds of questions that a
Defense Roll is supposed to address. The answer may not be trivial, and
it probably won't always be the same either. However, it is easy to see
how certain factors, such as the knight's combat experience or quality
of armor can have a direct influence on his chance of succeeding.

Unlike in some popular RPGs (e.g. Dungeons & Dragons or the d20 system games), [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** in Master of Magic is an *active characteristic*.
That is, it is rolled for, every time it is used, instead of simply
serving as a target number or damage modifier. Given the way it is
rolled, each point of the attribute represents a separate chance at
mitigating incoming damage. This effectively provides a linear function
of damage reduction, that can encompass everything from armor and
shields, through experience and dexterity, to all kinds of natural armor
or magical protections.

Defense in MoM generally applies to all types of conventional damage, and **Defense Rolls** may be made against both Physical and Magical Damage. [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
is instead used against effects that would outright kill or disable a
creature, and a few other effects for which most physical protection
would not be helpful at all (i.e. Poison and Life Stealing). This is in
contrast to some other RPGs where Physical and Magical damage form two
distinctive categories that have their own defensive attributes, as seen
for example in the Final Fantasy series, or World of Warcraft.

## Effect

**Defense Rolls** may be performed as a reaction against nearly all types of [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"). They are always made by individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** (as opposed to units), and their results directly reduce the incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

There are two criteria that must be met for a damaging attack to trigger a **Defense Roll**. First and foremost, the attack must allow for one to be made. This separates [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") from Physical Damage, as this type does not allow for any **Defense Rolls** (nor does it require [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll")). There are a handful of spells and abilities that deliver Doom Damage, and units under the effect of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Sleep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sleep "Black Sleep")**
spell will always have any Conventional Damage directed at them
converted to this type instead. Damage transformed this way, along with
damage done by [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") and [Chaos (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_(Item_Power) "Chaos (Item Power)") [Chaos Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_(Item_Power) "Chaos (Item Power)"), are not subject to [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**, or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")**
either; making them essentially unblockable. However, this does not
apply to other sources of Doom Damage (i.e. spells), which are
completely blocked by the first two of these defenses.

The second criteria for **Defense Rolls** is that the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** taking damage needs to have an effective Defense higher than [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**. [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") and similar spells aside, this criteria also has a [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") that is designed to nullify it: [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage"). A [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** struck by this type of damage will have its Defense reduced to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** during the calculation of its effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**, unless the unit it is part of possesses [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity").
Due to the order in which conditional modifiers are applied to Defense,
Illusion Damage even overrides other immunities (more on this below).
However, it has no effect on the bonus granted by [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls"), if any, which may still offer some protection even though the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s own Defense is otherwise lowered to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**.

### Effective Defense

:   *Main article: [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense")*

The baseline [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** of each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a unit is equal to the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
attribute of the unit itself. This is displayed in various places in
the game, both in and out of combat. However, there are often
conditional modifiers to this score, granted by spells or abilities,
that only affect certain types of attacks, or only protect against
certain types of damage. These modifiers are applied only during, and *strictly only for the duration of*, resolving these types of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Thus, they only really alter effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**.

Most immunities fall into this category, along with the [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield") [Large Shield](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Large_Shield "Large Shield") ability, a few protective [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), and even [Armor Piercing-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing_Damage "Armor Piercing Damage") and [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage").
The order in which these modifiers are applied is always the same, and
makes a lot of difference with respect to the final effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. The complete list and order of execution, with examples of specific quirks, can be found in the [Order of Operations for Resolving Defense and Immunities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense#Order_of_Operations_for_Resolving_Defense_and_Immunities "Defense") section of the main article.

In short, defensive non-immunity effects are applied first, which
are followed by Armor Piercing. Next are any immunities, including [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")**, that will raise Defense to either [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** or, more commonly, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. This may then be negated by Illusion Damage, after which the game applies the protection granted by [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls"), if any. Finally, the effect of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Sleep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sleep "Black Sleep")** and [Chaos (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_(Item_Power) "Chaos (Item Power)") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_(Item_Power) "Chaos (Item Power)") weapons are considered - these both circumvent the **Defense Roll** entirely when they apply.

### Chance To Block

Once effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** has been determined, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** can proceed with making the **Defense Roll**. A number of "10-sided dice" are rolled equal to the effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**.
Each die yields either a "success" or a "failure" based on the number
rolled, with the amount of "successes" setting out the actual result of
the **Defense Roll**.

*The base chance for each die to succeed is **30%***, or 3 in 10, which is termed on this wiki as the **"Chance** [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block****"**. Similar to the chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**,
the success chance of Defense dice can also be altered. However, unlike
Hit chance, there are very few effects that can modify the chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**. *3*, to be exact: the [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") ability, the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** spell (or its beefed-up version [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[High Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer")**), and the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")** enchantment.

#### Lucky

:   [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") Lucky is a racial ability of [Halflings](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Halflings "Halflings"), and a random ability for [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). It improves all basic rolls made by the unit (or [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** thereof) by 10%, that is: rolls [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**, rolls [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**, and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls. Two of these are visible in the unit statistics window (as [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+1** and [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1** respectively), but the success chance of **Defense Rolls** only appears for players using the unofficial [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")
    patch or later. In the official 1.31 version of the game, this
    statistic is hidden from the player, although the base chance can be
    found in both the manual and the strategy guide, and the ability tooltip
    does give an accurate description of the bonus.

#### Prayer

:   [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[High Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer")** are both [Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment"), that have a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **30** and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **60**
    respectively. They are not cumulative, the more expensive version
    simply adds more bonuses on top of those granted by the basic spell. [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** grants the same benefit that [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") Lucky does (+10% on all basic rolls) to *all friendly units* on the battlefield. This is not the same effect as [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") Lucky though, and these *do* stack. In fact, the combination of these two effects yields the highest chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** achievable in the game: **50%**.

#### Eldritch Weapon

:   [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")** is a [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") that may be cast on friendly units both in- and outside of combat. Instead of raising their own chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** however, this spell reduces the success chance of **Defense Rolls** made by targets attacked by the enchanted unit. This is the only effect in the game capable of lowering the chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**, and it does not work on every type of attack either.

:   [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")** will reduce the opponent's chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** by 10% when the enchanted unit makes either a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**, or [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack**. It does not work with any other type of attack, ability, or spells cast by the enchanted unit, but it does also affect any [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") it may make.

Modifying the success chance of **Defense Rolls** provides an effect that is obviously proportional to the actual [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** of a unit. This is also independent of, and cumulative with, any direct bonus or penalty to the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** attribute itself. For example, an [Agility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Agility "Agility") [Agile](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Agility "Agility") Hero gains increased benefit from also being [Lucky](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lucky "Lucky") Lucky, whereas a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** attack has a higher chance of penetrating [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") if it is made by a unit enchanted with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")**.

## Defense Rolls and Damage

It is not possible to discuss **Defense Rolls** completely separate from the process of dealing [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, as it is in the case of [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll"). This is partly because if there is no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, there is no need for a **Defense Roll** either; but more importantly, [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") is handled in a manner that calls for a new roll every time a certain amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** has been actually dealt.

### Single Target Damage

When an attack dealing non-[Area](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") strikes a unit, it deals [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** of the unit one by one until the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is exhausted. The unit (or [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
thereof) being destroyed has no bearing on this process. It is entirely
possible to kill a whole unit multiple times over with a single
Conventional Damage attack. The resolution of this is normally as
follows:

| Stage | Step | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Attack | **1** | Determine (situational) [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"). |
| **2** | Using this value, make an [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") to determine raw (unmitigated) [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. |
|  | | |
| **Defense** | **3** | Calculate effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. |
| **4** | Make a **Defense Roll**. |
| **5** | Subtract the result of the roll from the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. |
|  | | |
| Excess | **6** | If the remainder is greater than the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points****\*** of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** being attacked, another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** will also be hit. Store the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** value as actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** (the first time this happens), or add it to the already accumulated number (on subsequent occasions). Reduce the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** by this value and jump back to step #4. |
|  | | |
| Resolve | **7** | Add any remainder (this will be less than or equal to the target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, and can also be zero) to any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** accumulated by step #6: this will be the total [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** done to the target *unit*. |
| **8** | Apply the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to the unit as a whole, and recalculate remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** based on unit health. This concludes the attack. |
|  | | |
| **\*:** For the first [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **spell damage** uses its *current* [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** here. However, for unit attacks, and for all subsequent [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in any case, the *maximum* [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** (i.e. the [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value) are used. | | |

There are a few quirks of this system that may be worth taking note of. First, as mentioned earlier, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count of the defending unit is not considered anywhere during the above process. A new **Defense Roll** is made every time there is enough unmitigated [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** left after the previous roll to kill a full [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. This applies even to [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-figure** units.

Because the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
is not actually dealt until the end of the process, a defending unit
may also keep making further reductions to it even if all of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
are technically dead by that time. In fact, the entire process has no
requirement for the defending unit to be still alive to begin with.
Normally this would not matter, but there are several effects in the
game that rely on a "majority damage type", and excess [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** of one type or another may make or break these effects (more on this below). [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** dealt to a unit after it has already perished will count for all of these effects.

Second, Conventional Damage from unit attacks is dealt in what is essentially a reverse [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** order, as the second (and any subsequent) **Defense Roll** is made after killing an *unhurt* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, completely ignoring any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** suffered by a "lead" or "front" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. This is somewhat counter-intuitive, as in this case, it makes displaying lead-figure [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** relevant only in terms of the player being able to calculate the total remaining [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of a unit. However, [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **spell damage** does consider this value, and will allow the second **Defense Roll**
to be made earlier if there is recorded "top figure damage", although
any further rolls can still only occur after dealing the full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** amount in [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to the unit.

Finally, despite attacks affecting [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** one at a time, and them making **Defense Rolls** on a per-figure basis, the resulting [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is still applied to the unit *as a whole*, rather than actually slaying [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** one by one. The remaining count is instead calculated at the end, from the precept that there can only ever be one damaged [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a unit. In fact, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** taken is stored by the game *for the unit*, rather than the individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, although "top figure damage" does also get tracked in combat.

#### Example

:   To illustrate these perks, lets pit a unit of [Minotaurs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Minotaurs "Minotaurs") against a unit of dwarven [Hammerhands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hammerhands "Hammerhands"). Lets say both groups are [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Veterans**. Minotaurs come in pairs ([Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **2 figures**), and their stats are [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **13**, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5**, and [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **12**. They also have an innate modifier of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+2 To Hit**, which grants each dice of their [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") an extra **+20%** to succeed. [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Veteran** [Hammerhands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hammerhands "Hammerhands") have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6 figures**, [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **9** [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength"), [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5**, and [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **4** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

:   Neither combatant has any [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), which means they have to duke it out in melee combat. All [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
    will be dealt in the melee phase, and which unit goes first has no
    relevance on the outcome of this battle. Let's look at how each of their
    attacks are resolved in turn, starting with the Minotaurs.

:   Both Minotaur [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
    attack separately. The first one rolls its dice, and manages to succeed
    10 out of its 13 rolls (remember, Minotaurs have a base 50% for their
    dice to succeed). This translates to **10 points** of raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** against the dwarves. The first Hammerhand rolls his **5** [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** dice, and scores 2 successes. This reduces the incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to **8**. Since this is higher than his Hit Point total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **4**, the attack deals "excess" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and will hit another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** as well.

:   The computer stores the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**, which corresponds to the total [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** of the Hammerhand [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, and then subtracts it from the remaining raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, leaving another [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**.
    This is what the next Hammerhand has to defend against. This dwarf is
    even luckier than the first, and manages to succeed 3 out of his 5
    rolls. That blocks [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** out of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4 points**, leaving only a single [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **point**. This is now below the Hammerhands' [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** value, meaning there is no further excess [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

:   The game adds this [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** to the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4** stored earlier, and concludes the first Minotaur's attack as having caused [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5** points of damage altogether. However, as this happens in a melee phase, the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** does not get applied immediately: the outcome of every attack needs to be determined first.

:   The second Minotaur is much less successful than the first: it
    only scores 5 successes on its Attack Roll. Again, a Hammerhand steps up
    to take the hit, but is even more unlucky than the Minotaur, and all of
    his [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** dice come up as failures. This means he will suffer all [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5** points, or at least as much as he can, which is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**. The game stores that as actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and subtracts it from the total. This reduces the remaining raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to **1**, but as this is still excess, it gets directed towards another [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**.

:   Yet another Hammerhand steps up to defend itself. This time, 3 out of the **5** [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** dice are successes, which means the dwarf could, in theory, reduce the remaining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** by **3**. However, there is only [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** left. Unfortunately for the dwarves, this doesn't mean that the already stored [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is reduced: the extra 2 successes are simply discarded instead. Still, the excess [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is completely blocked, resulting in a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4 damage** from the second Minotaur's attack.

:   It does not matter that the first Minotaur's attack caused [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** that, when applied, would yield a "lead" Hammerhand [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** that does not have its full [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**. In fact, this wouldn't matter even if the attacks occurred outside of a melee phase (i.e. if [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** was fully applied between each attack). The second **Defense Roll** would still only trigger after the Hammerhands took [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**, rather than any smaller value ([Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** in this illustration). This should become even more clear during the Hammerhands' [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

:   Speaking of which, it is now the dwarves' turn to swing back at
    the bull-men. Before looking at any dice rolls though, its worth
    considering something about the numbers. The Hammerhands have a Melee
    Strength of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **9**, while the Minotaurs have a health of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **12** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. Even if a dwarven [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** realized its full [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** potential, there would never be a second **Defense Roll** on the Minotaurs' part. The reason for this is simple: any subsequent **Defense Roll** (after the first) requires the attack to deal *more* raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** than an unhurt [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**. In this case, that would be [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **13+**, which a single Hammerhand's attack can not achieve here, even if the opposing Minotaur failed its **Defense Roll** completely.

:   With that in mind, the Hammerhands make their 6 separate [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll"),
    using 9 dice each. They roll fairly lucky, with success counts of 5, 2,
    3, 4, 1, and 4. Each of these attacks triggers a single **Defense Roll** from the Minotaurs, using 5 dice to represent their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5**.
    Their rolls are average, and generate, in order: 1, 0, 2, 3, 2, and 1
    success. Subtracting these from the previous set, we get [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** values of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4**, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2**, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1**, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0**, and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3**. Note that the fifth pair of rolls (1 - 2) would result in negative [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. In this case, the extra successes generated by the **Defense Roll** are discarded, as actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can never be lower than 0.

:   Finally, after every attack has been processed, [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can now be applied to both units. The Minotaurs' attacks yielded [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **9**, this is inflicted on the Hammerhands. They have [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **4** each, for a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **24**, which means the unit will have [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **15** remaining. This is enough for 3 full health [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, and a fourth, "lead" [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **3** (one [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") darkened heart and 1 point of "Damage" shown in the unit statistics window).

:   At the same time, the dwarves' [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is tallied up, and applied to the Minotaurs. [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0** + [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **3** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **11**, just below the full health of a Minotaur [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. Thus, both bull-men remain standing. The unit statistics window will display [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **11**, and respectively "Damage 11" when inspecting this unit. However, if another round of melee ensues, both [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** may still attack with full efficiency.

:   Furthermore, even though the "lead" Minotaur now only has a single [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** left, there would still be no second **Defense Roll**
    against the dwarves' attacks in the following turn, despite the fact
    that it would now be possible for them to kill the first Minotaur [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a single hit. Again, this is because the game always considers the defending [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** to be an unhurt one when resolving unit attacks. This remains true even for [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure** units that took [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** previously, and is also the case for a damaged last [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** of a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit.

### Altering the sequence

There are two effects that can alter the sequence described above. Both of these simply insert an extra step into the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** resolution process under certain circumstances.

#### Blur

:   When this [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") is active, friendly units will insert an extra step into the sequence whenever they take [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") as a result of an attack performed by an enemy unit. Between steps #2 and #3, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")** will make an extra "roll" for every point of raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** yielded by step #2. These rolls provide a flat 10% chance to negate each particular point of this [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. There is a catch, however: in the official game, every time [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")** blocks a point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, it will skip the roll for the next point, meaning that it can, at most, negate 50% of the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

:   [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")** only applies against damaging attacks made by enemy units, and will not protect against either spells, or [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**-based attacks. It also does not work against any source of [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage").

#### Invulnerability

:   A defending [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** under the effect of this [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") will perform an extra action after step #5: *"reduce the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** by 2"*. Essentially, every **Defense Roll** made by the enchanted unit is treated as if it generated an extra 2 successes, which directly reduce the incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. This works even if the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** has an effective Defense of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**, although it does not apply against attacks that do not allow a **Defense Roll** (i.e. it applies against [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") but not [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage")).

### Melee Sequence, Multi-Figure-, and Destroyed Units

As mentioned above already, a unit does not necessarily need to be alive to take [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Similarly, it can also keep mitigating (reducing) incoming [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** even after all of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** have perished. The most common scenario in which this happens is when the attacker is a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit. Once the attack has been initiated, all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
will perform one, and its highly unlikely that it will always be the
last one delivering the finishing blow. For some reason, the developers
decided not to implement any checks to see whether the defending unit
has any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** left before processing [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. This results in unit destruction always occurring only at the very end of an attack sequence.

This also has a profound impact on the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
sequence, where units may be capable of performing multiple (possibly
different) attacks within a single run of the sequence. Although checks
are in place before each of these attacks to prevent a destroyed unit
from *delivering* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, there is nothing stopping a unit from *suffering*
any. Thus, the sequence will be executed fully even if one of the
combatants perishes halfway through. For example, a defending unit
destroyed by a [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attack** will still take (and defend against) the onslaught of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") that invariably follows.

Under normal circumstances, this would likely have no relevance,
as the destroyed unit can't fight back and how dead it is at the end of
the process should technically make no difference. However, as noted
already, the game tracks multiple distinctive types of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") done to a unit, that determine the effectiveness of certain spells and abilities. There are 3 such [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** types to be exact: regular-, *Irreversible-*,
and "Create Undead" damage, and the two groups of effects that rely on
these have to do with bringing back the dead - either alive, or as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"). The first category consists of [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration"), [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Raise Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Raise_Dead "Raise Dead")**, and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Resurrection](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resurrection "Resurrection")**; while the second contains [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") and [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage").

The sole purpose of *Irreversible Damage* is to stop
creatures from being brought back to life by any means. For a unit to
come back from the dead at all, it must meet the criteria of having
suffered *less* Irreversible Damage than either of the other types.
If that is not the case, then it is permanently destroyed, and none of
the above effects will work on it. Other than this, Undead may be
created from units that suffered at least as much "Create Undead-" as
regular Damage Points, and units may regenerate or be raised from the
dead otherwise. These are mutually exclusive: if the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
suffered was equal, no regeneration or resurrection will occur, even
for units on the winning side of a battle: they can only come back as
Undead (if appropriate).

### Area Damage

[Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") is resolved with a simpler sequence. However, since it makes a separate attack against every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the target unit, steps #1 through #6 are performed that many times before any actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is dealt.

| Stage | Step | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Attack | **1** | Determine (situational) [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"). |
| **2** | Using this value, make an [Attack Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") to determine raw (unmitigated) [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. |
|  | | |
| **Defense** | **3** | Calculate effective [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. |
| **4** | Make a **Defense Roll**. |
| **5** | Subtract the result of the roll from the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. |
|  | | |
| Excess | **6** | If the remainining [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is higher than the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, reduce it to that value. Store the actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** for each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. |
|  | | |
| Resolve | **7** | Add up the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** accumulated by step #6: this will be the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** done to the target *unit*. |
| **8** | Apply the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to the unit as a whole, and recalculate remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** based on unit health. This concludes the attack. |

The sequence for Area Damage is exactly the same as the conventional
one up to the point of determining whether there is "excess" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and what to do with this. Here, it is discarded, whereas for non-Area [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") it initiates what could be described as a new [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** process - although one for which the raw [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** is already set rather than rolled for, skipping steps #1 and #2.

Since all Area Damage is always considered to be [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **spell damage** (even that caused by the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") ability), it will never trigger the extra step of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Blur](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blur "Blur")**. However, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** also equally affects Area Damage, and is executed with the same timing within each sequence (after step #5 for each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**).
