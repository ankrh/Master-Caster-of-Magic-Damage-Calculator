**Poison Immunity** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

A unit with **Poison Immunity** is completely unaffected by any attack that delivers [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage"). When such an attack strikes the unit, it does absolutely nothing to that unit.

**Poison Immunity** is by far the most common immunity, possessed by no fewer than 21 different units by default. Nearly all **Poison-Immune** units are [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature"), including (among others) all creatures associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**. The exception is the [Golem](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Golem "Golem"), a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit").

The only way to add **Poison Immunity** to a unit that does not already have it is to turn it into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.

## Description

There are plenty of venomous creatures in the world of *Master of Magic*,
and they can be quite dangerous indeed. Venom and poison can cause
severe damage to a target, coursing through its veins and breaking down
its body from the inside.

Fortunately for some creatures, their physical or magical natures
prevent poison from having any effect. Some creatures are simply very
tough and can shrug off a venomous bite. Others have no internal systems
to be attacked by the poison, and others yet are protected by divine
forces that can neutralize poison before it has any effect.

## Effect

**Poison Immunity** is a passive ability that protects a unit specifically from [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage").

Normally, attacks that deliver [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") force the target to make several [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls, causing [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Point of Damage** for each failed roll. Units with low Resistance scores are particularly vulnerable to this type of damage, and [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** scores do nothing to reduce it.

The **Poison-Immune** unit, on the other hand, has absolutely nothing to fear from such an attack. It will never have to make any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls against [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage"), and will not suffer any harm.

[Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") is caused only by the [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") ability, which many creatures possess. Such creatures deliver [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") simultaneously with [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). The most powerful of these is the [Great Wyrm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Wyrm "Great Wyrm"), which delivers a [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") attack of strength 15 - potentially causing up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **15 points of Damage** to the target!

Note that **Poison Immunity** protects *only* against [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage"). If an attack delivers several types of damage simultaneously, the **Poison Immunity** ability will only shrug off the [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") component of that attack.

## Acquiring Poison Immunity

There is only one method in which to add **Poison Immunity** to a unit: by turning that unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.

It is not a coincidence that most of the units with default **Poison Immunity** are [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death**
creatures: these creatures are not alive, and thus have no internal
organs and delicate nervous systems for the poison to attack. Similarly,
units that die and are brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units are also **Immune to Poison**.

Units can be turned into [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") in several different ways:

* The [Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels") spell will turn a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by [Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain") damage is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by an enemy unit with [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle and then targeted by [Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the caster of this spell.

Regardless of which method is used, the unit comes back with most of its original abilities intact - but now possesses the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property which, among other effects, also gives the unit **Poison Immunity**.

Note that the icon for **Poison Immunity** is not added to the unit's details panel. However, the presence of the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property icon is always sufficient to indicate that **Poison Immunity** is indeed in effect.

### Immunity to Poison by Other Means

[Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") forces the target to make [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls at no penalty, where each failed roll results in the target taking damage.

Since no penalty is involved, a unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10 or higher** will never fail its rolls, and thus will suffer no damage. The end result is therefore identical to **Poison Immunity**.

The only difference of course is that a unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score can be lowered below [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** due to enemy [Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Curse "Curse") or other ill effects. **Poison Immunity** cannot be removed from a unit that possesses it, and thus is permanently protecting the unit from [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") regardless of circumstances.
