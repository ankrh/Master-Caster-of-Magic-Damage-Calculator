**Resist Elements** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") from the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25**, it may be cast on a friendly unit on the overland map to give it two transient bonuses: [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+3** against [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") and ill effects associated with the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** and [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Realms; and [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** against all direct-damage attacks from the same Realms. This benefit can be maintained with a [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** per turn [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

Alternatively, a cheaper version of the same spell may be cast for only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5**
during combat, in which case it will temporarily bestow the same
bonuses on a unit for the duration of the battle (with no permanent
effects or Upkeep Cost).

## Effects

**Resist Elements**
protects a unit against specific types of magic. It bestows two separate
bonuses of the same magnitude, both of which apply conditionally
against attacks associated with either the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). However, neither bonus is cumulative with those granted by the similar, but more powerful, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")** spell.

### Defense Bonus

The second benefit is a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** bonus, that applies whenever the unit is targeted by any [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attack associated with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**. Naturally, this only works if the attack allows a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") (i.e. is not a [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") attack). It's also worth noting that this bonus can be halved by [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing") [Armor Piercing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armor_Piercing "Armor Piercing"), or negated by [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion"),
if applicable. Otherwise, it grants 3 extra dice on the Defense Roll,
making it more likely that the enchanted unit will suffer less, or no [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from these attacks.

## Usage

**Resist Elements** may be cast both overland and in combat, for the basic [Casting Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25** and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **5**
respectively. If the casting is successful, the game will prompt for a
friendly unit to be selected as the spell's target. On the overland map,
this typically requires clicking on the army stack that contains the
unit which, in turn, brings up a list view of all units in that stack,
allowing for the target to be picked individually. Only units not
already under the effect of **Resist Elements** may be chosen. Once the spell is cast, a glowing [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[green](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** outline appears around the unit (unless it is overridden by a stronger enchantment); and **Resist Elements**, along with its icon, will be displayed in the *unit statistics window* when inspecting this unit.

The overland version of **Resist Elements** has an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1**
that must be paid at the beginning of every turn to keep the spell in
effect. This is automatically deducted by the game from the owner's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool as long as the enchantment remains in existence. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
in the pool to pay for the spell, then it will dissipate, ceasing its
effect immediately. It may also be cancelled manually by left-clicking
its name in the *unit statistics window* of its target unit. However, this action can only be performed outside of combat, during the caster's own turn.

The combat version of **Resist Elements**
does not carry over to the overland map: it will disappear
automatically as soon as the battle is over. As such, it has no Upkeep
Cost; but at the same time may not be manually removed by its caster
either (as enchantments can not be cancelled during a battle).

## Acquisition

As a [Common Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") of the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Resist Elements** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**1** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With none, the spell can naturally not be learned during the campaign.
This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Resist Elements** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 30% (with [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**1** book), which gradually increases with the amount of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**7** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **40**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master"), and/or [Nature Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Mastery "Nature Mastery") [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"); or a bookshelf containing [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**8** or more.

**Resist Elements** may also be selected as a starting spell by any Wizard with at least [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**2** books. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

## Item Power

**Resist Elements** can also be duplicated as an [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") and imbued onto a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). This requires either the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Enchant Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchant_Item "Enchant Item")** or the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Create Artifact](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Artifact "Create Artifact")** spells, and has the advantages of being non-dispellable, and requiring no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"). While enchanting [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements_(Item_Power) "Resist Elements (Item Power)")** onto an item can't be done unless the [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") possesses at least [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[2](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), knowledge of the actual spell is *not necessary*. Selecting this Power increases the item's total value by [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **100**.

[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements_(Item_Power) "Resist Elements (Item Power)")** can be enchanted onto any type of Magical Item. Naturally, this means that it is available to any [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), as all of them possess at least one [ItemSlot Misc](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/d/d8/ItemSlot_Misc.png/revision/latest?cb=20120702054432) miscellaneous [Equipment Slot](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item#Equipment_Slots "Magical Item").
If an item with this Power is created and given to a Hero, then they
will benefit from the effect described above for as long as they keep
the item equipped. Since [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Resist Elements](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements_(Item_Power) "Resist Elements (Item Power)")** is present on [11](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Elements_(Item_Power)#Overland "Resist Elements (Item Power)") pre-fabricated Magical Items, it is also possible to occasionally find an item already imbued with this Power in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be offered one for purchase by a wandering [Merchant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant "Merchant").

## Strategy

**Resist Elements** is an excellent early-game protective spell against enemies that use either [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, or some form of petrification. It can be especially useful against neutral opponents, such as [Dark Elven](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_Elves "Dark Elves") [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") if the player starts on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror"); or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Sprites](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sprites "Sprites")**, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")**, or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Basilisks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk")** guarding [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") on either [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane").
The abundance of the latter creatures in lairs in the official game
usually makes this spell a good investment, possibly even as a starting
one.

**Resist Elements** works best on [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units with high [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** counts, at least when defending against [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Attacks** or [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze"), in which case it may still be useful in the mid- to late game. Because the effect doesn't stack with that of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")** however, it will typically get superceded by the stronger spell, at least on units that participate in many battles.

While [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+3** is a comparatively small bonus, it may be just enough to push a unit with a fairly high [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** over the threshold for being immune to a certain attack too. That is, if the resulting score is [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**
or more (considering, of course, any penalty applied by the attack),
then the unit can no longer be affected, completely eliminating the risk
of taking [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from that attack. For example, if a unit with a base [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** is engaging [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices")**, whose [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") carries a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-3** penalty, then casting **Resist Elements** can tip back the scale, and make the unit immune to the [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") again.

It's also worth noting that the low [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of **Resist Elements** is a double-edged sword. On one hand, even the lowest level spellcasting [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")
are able to cast this spell at least once in every battle, which makes
it convenient to use situationally when required, instead of maintaining
it overland. On the other hand though, it also makes the enchantment
more prone to being dispelled, which may matter when going up against
the forces of a rival [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard").
