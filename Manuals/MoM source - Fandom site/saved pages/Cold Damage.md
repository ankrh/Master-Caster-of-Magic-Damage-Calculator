**Cold Damage** is a type of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") that is only delivered by the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature** spell called "[Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt")".

**Cold Damage** is similar to basic [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") types (such as [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")) in the way it harms the target: The attacker must make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine how many times it has hit the target, and then the target must make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to determine how many hits it managed to block. All unblocked hits are then translated into simple [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** which is applied to the target, potentially killing one or more of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**.

**Cold Damage** is considered a sub-type of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"). This means that it is almost entirely ineffective against targets possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). On top of this, **Cold Damage** is almost always useless against targets possessing [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity").

## Effect

**Cold Damage** behaves the same way as [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"),
except it may trigger two different kinds of immunities possessed by
the target (if any), making it ineffective against such a target.

### To Hit and Defense Rolls

Like other types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), **Cold Damage** attempts to inflict actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** on the target. To do so, the attacker must make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine how successful the attack was, and the target may make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to determine how much damage it managed to block.

The attacker makes a number of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls equal to the strength of the **Cold Damage** attack. Only the [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") spell delivers **Cold Damage**, but its strength is adjustable by investing more [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** into it at casting time. For example, an [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") spell with no extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** invested attacks with a strength of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **5**, so it makes 5 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls.

Each of these rolls results in a number between 1 and 100. For each roll that comes up *equal to or lower than* 30, the game registers one hit against the target.

The target then makes a number of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls equal to its current [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. Each roll results in a number between 1 and 100. For each roll that comes up *equal to or lower than* the target's current [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** score (which is usually [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30** for most units), the game reduces the number of hits against the target by 1.

At the end of this process, the game tallies up the number of
unblocked hits (equal to the number of registered hits minus the number
of blocked hits). It then applies [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to the target accordingly - [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** per unblocked hit.

As with all other types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), if the target has lost one of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** due to this damage it may make another set of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to try and block more damage. The process only ends once all hits have been translated into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** or have been blocked by these Defense rolls.

For a more thorough explanation of the process, see [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage").

## Immunities

**Cold Damage** is always delivered by the [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") spell. Since it results from a spell, it will be completely blocked by any target possessing the [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") ability. Such a target will suffer no damage from the spell, regardless of how much extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** was invested into it.

**Cold Damage** is different from other [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") types in that it can also trigger *another* immunity called [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"). In other words, if the target possesses [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** whenever struck by a **Cold Damage** attack.

Thus, when the target possesses this ability, the attack needs to
be exceptionally powerful - otherwise it would have no chance of
busting through [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50**. The [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") spell *can* be this powerful, but you would need to invest a large amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** into it, and even then the chances of getting through [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") are slim.

## Cold Damage vs. Blizzard Damage

The [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") spell does not actually deliver **Cold Damage**. Instead it delivers another unique damage type called [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage"), which (depending on circumstance) can behave exactly like **Cold Damage** or completely different from it.

When [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage") is used against a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**, it behaves exactly like **Cold Damage**. The process is explained above in this article, and works the same for both damage types.

However when used against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage") is significantly more potent than **Cold Damage** due to the way it is processed. Instead of making a single set of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against the target, and allowing the target to make a single set of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage") will actually strike *each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit independently of the others*.
Each target figure also makes its own attempts to block the hits
inflicted directed at itself. Only after all rolls have been completed
is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** applied to the target unit as a whole.

The end result is that against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage") can inflict *far* more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** than would a **Cold Damage** attack of similar strength. This is why [Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm") is so powerful (on top of the fact that it can be delivered against several targets simultaneously...).

Nonetheless, in all cases the [immunities](#Immunities) that apply against **Cold Damage** work the same way against [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage").

For a thorough explanation, read the [Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage") article.

## Sources of Cold Damage

The game contains exactly one source of **Cold Damage**: The [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") spell. This is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Combat Instant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") from the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature** realm, whose only purpose is to inflict a certain amount of **Cold Damage** on the selected target.

The amount of damage inflicted is variable - controlled by the amount of extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** invested into the spell when it is being cast. The "basic" [Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt") spell, cast with no extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**, delivers only 5 **Cold Damage** points. The fully-powered spell, cast with an extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **40**, delivers 45 **Cold Damage** points. The ratio is [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** for each extra 1 **Cold Damage**.
