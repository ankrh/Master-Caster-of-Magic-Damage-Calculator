**Disintegration Damage** is a type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") that can be delivered by [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") as part of their [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and/or [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). An attack delivering **Disintegration Damage** stands some chance of killing the lead [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the enemy unit. Targets destroyed by **Disintegration Damage** cannot be restored to life.

When a target is struck by [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage"), it does not make any [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls against it. Instead, it makes a single [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
roll. The result of this roll determines whether the lead figure in the
target unit is killed instantly or left completely unharmed.

**Disintegration Damage** is quite rare: it is only found as a component in the attack of [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") who are equipped with a [Magical Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") imbued with the "Destruction" enchantment. No other unit, spell or effect delivers this type of damage.

**Note:**  The [Disintegrate](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegrate "Disintegrate") spell *does not* deliver **Disintegration Damage**. It works completely differently than what's explained below - and is much more powerful than **Disintegration Damage**.

## Effect

**Disintegration Damage** forces its target to make a single [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll. If the target fails this roll, it loses one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** instantly. Target units that are completely destroyed after suffering sufficient **Disintegration Damage** cannot be restored to life by any spell or effect.

### Disintegrating the Lead Figure

Whenever an attack containing a **Disintegration Damage**
component strikes any target on the battlefield, the target must make a
single die roll - resulting in a random number between 1 and 10. This
number is then compared to the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score.

If the rolled number is *higher than* the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score, the lead [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit is immediately killed off - regardless of how many [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** it has remaining or how high its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is. The figure is simply destroyed.

If the rolled number is *equal to or lower than* the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**, the target suffers no damage or ill effect whatsoever.

Note that, unlike other similar [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") types like [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") or [Stoning Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Damage "Stoning Damage"), **Disintegration Damage** does not inflict any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
penalty on its target. This makes it inferior in terms of the chance it
has to kill its target, and makes many high-end or enchanted units
completely immune to the effect (see below).

### Targets Destroyed Forever

When a unit is destroyed, the game checks to see how many of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** it lost were killed off by **Disintegration Damage**. If **Disintegration Damage** caused the death of over half of the unit's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, that unit is considered utterly destroyed. It cannot be restored to life in any way, whether during or after combat.

A "disintegrated" unit cannot be the target of any [Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead") or [Raise Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Raise_Dead "Raise Dead") spell. Furthermore, "disintegrated" [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") will not come back as [Zombies](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Zombies "Zombies") while the [Zombie Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Zombie_Mastery "Zombie Mastery") spell is in effect.

A "disintegrated" [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") will never reappear in the game under your employ. He/she will not appear for hire, will never appear as [Prisoners](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prisoner "Prisoner"), do not reappear on the [Resurrection](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resurrection "Resurrection") list, and cannot be resummoned with [Summon Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summon_Hero "Summon Hero") nor [Summon Champion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summon_Champion "Summon Champion").

It is currently unknown exactly how many of the enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** must die from **Disintegration Damage** for this effect to apply. Naturally, any [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure units** will always be utterly destroyed if killed by **Disintegration Damage**, but with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** things are not so clear. None of the official sources explain this subject, so only testing can tell for sure.

The standing theory is that **Disintegration Damage** must kill over half of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in the unit during a single battle, as supposedly is the case with [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") and other similar abilities. It is unknown whether the game remembers the number of **Disintegrated** figures in the unit from battle to battle, and if so, does that tally decrease as the unit regains lost [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** over time? Also, does **Disintegration Damage** need to kill half of the unit's *maximum* number of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, or half of the figures that were alive when the battle began?

Testing for these answers may be very difficult, but will need to
be done sooner or later. If you have concrete information, please
discuss it in the comments section below.

## Immunities

Units possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") can never be affected by **Disintegration Damage**. They will never be hurt by it.

Furthermore, since **Disintegration Damage** inflicts no [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty on its targets, it cannot affect any unit that has [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** or higher. Such units will never fail their Resistance rolls, and thus will not be affected in any way.

Note that these immunities will only block the **Disintegration Damage** component of an attack. Virtually all attacks delivering **Disintegration Damage** will also deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") or any other type of damage, which may or may not go through the target's immunities unimpeded.

## Sources of Disintegration Damage

**Disintegration Damage** is extremely rare, having only one source: [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") wielding a [Magical Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") containing the "Destruction" enchantment.

### Weapons of Destruction

The only source of **Disintegration Damage** comes from weapons imbued with the "Destruction" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). This enchantment can be placed only on weapons - including [Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace"), [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"), [Bows](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"), [Wands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand"), and [Staves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff"). When the enchanted item is equipped on a Hero, it adds a **Disintegration Damage** component to the attack associated with the item. Therefore, a [Sword](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Mace](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace") or [Axe](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") only add a **Disintegration Damage** component to the Hero's [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), while a [Bow](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"), [Wand](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand") or [Staff](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff") only add **Disintegration Damage** to the Hero's [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

During the custom [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting") process, the "Destruction" bonus is only available if you possess at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**5 Chaos** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). Naturally, it's always possible to find a weapon containing this bonus as a reward for defeating [Encounters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter").

The Hero's attack will only deliver **Disintegration Damage** while the Hero is holding the enchanted item. If the item is removed or lost, the [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s attack no longer delivers **Disintegration Damage**.
