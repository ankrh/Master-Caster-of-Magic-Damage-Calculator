A **Thrown Attack** is one of the many [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") Types featured in *Master of Magic*. A **Thrown Attack** is only executed as an integral part of a unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), and always delivers simple [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). Its primary purpose, aside from hurting the enemy, is to try and kill of as many enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as possible before the rest of the Melee combat process (including the enemy's [retaliation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")) can take place.

**Thrown Attacks** are only available to units possessing the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability. This includes 4 of the primary [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") from the [Barbarian](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Barbarian "Barbarian") race, as well as three [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). Units that do not possess the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability by default may not acquire it in any way, and thus will never be able to execute a **Thrown Attack**.

**Thrown Attacks** are executed at the very beginning of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") sequence. Thus, the more enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** a **Thrown Attack** can kill, the less danger there will be to the **Thrown Attacker** once the target gets the chance to retaliate.

**Thrown Attacks** are only executed when the attacker is voluntarily initiating Melee combat. They do not work when [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to an enemy assault.

The damage delivered by a **Thrown Attack** is *always* very basic [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), with no additional properties. It will attempt to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target, and will not trigger any of the target's immunities.

The strength of a **Thrown Attack** is indicated in a unit's details panel as the value of its [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability. On this wiki, the strength of a **Thrown Attack** is indicated by a non-official icon of an axe within a blue box: [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"). This icon does not appear in the game, but is based upon the icon representing the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability. The strength of a [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack** indicates how much [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") it delivers.

The strength of a **Thrown Attack** will normally grow with [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience** at the same rate as the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") strength. Most effects that improve a unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") will also improve its **Thrown Attack**. Furthermore, any [Axe](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") containing attack-improving bonuses and held by a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") will improve both the Hero's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") *and* his/her **Thrown Attack**, if present.

Possession of a **Thrown Attack** allows a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit to attack any enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit.

## Concept

A **Thrown Attack**
consists of a unit hurling specially-designed weapons at the enemy from
some distance away. This includes weapons like throwing-spears and
throwing-axes. These weapons are meticulously balanced in order to
maintain air stability, which they usually achieve by rotating around a
pre-defined axis. Therefore, they can maintain a rather constant
trajectory through the air, and are thus sufficiently accurate to strike
their target. Since these projectiles are much heavier than arrows or
bolts, they also tend to cause far more damage to anything they strike.

On the other hand, thrown-weapons lack the range of dedicated
ranged-weapons like bows and slings. They cannot be used against
far-away targets. Instead, they are quite effective at just beyond
hand-to-hand combat range, and thus are used as a "first-strike" weapon
preceding a powerful charge. In other words, these weapons are hurled
just before a unit charges into an enemy formation. They kill a few of
the defenders, and thus make it easier to mop-up the rest.

Because of the short distance involved, the **Thrown Attack** occurs at the very start of any [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). It will preceded any form of retaliation by the enemy. The desired outcome of a **Thrown Attack** is the death of as many enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
as possible, so that once the two units are engaged in combat, the
enemy has less strength and thus will pose less of a risk to the
attacker. It is, essentially, a preemptive strike meant to weaken the
enemy prior to actual engagement.

## Effect

All **Thrown Attacks** deliver a very basic form of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), with no additional effects or special properties.

[Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") is meant to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target - with the intent of killing as many enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as possible before the attacker and defender engage each other up close.

The amount of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") delivered by a **Thrown Attack** is equal to the strength of the attacker's [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability, as indicated in its details panel. This is the maximum potential number of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** that might be caused to the target - though the actual amount is usually much lower. On this wiki, the strength of any **Thrown Attack** is indicated by the axe icon: [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"). Therefore, an attack with a strength of [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **4** delivers 4 points of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), and can potentially cause up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4 points of Damage** to the target.

To calculate the actual amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to the target, the game runs through the [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") process - a long and complicated process that's explained in detail in the [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") article. In essence, the attacker makes a series of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine the accuracy of the attack, and the defender makes a series of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
rolls to determine how effectively it blocked and/or dodged the attack.
When the results of these rolls are compared, we get the total amount
of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to the target. Target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** are killed as appropriate, before moving on to the next step of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") process (usually the exchange of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") between the attacker and the target).

It is currently unknown which [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses apply when processing a **Thrown Attack**, and testing is quite difficult. However, it is certain that some bonuses, such as those received from [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"), certainly do apply to the **Thrown Attack**. These will improve the accuracy of the attack, and thus help it deliver more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** on average.

On the other hand, it is almost certain that no extra properties or [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") can be added to a **Thrown Attack**, so it will always deliver *only* [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") (with the above effect).

### Multi-Figure Thrown Attackers

When a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit** uses a **Thrown Attack**, each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** executes a separate **Thrown Attack**
against the target, in rapid succession. Each of these is treated and
processed as a separate attack, involving the entire routine of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls, [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls and [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** distribution as described above, from start to finish.

Each of these attacks has the same strength, equal to the value of the unit's [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability. Thus, a unit with "[Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") 3" and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **4 Figures** will execute 4 separate **Thrown Attacks** against the target, each with a strength of [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **3** (i.e. delivering 3 points of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") at the target).

Therefore, the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** a unit contains, the stronger its **Thrown Attack** is overall. Nonetheless, a "weaker" **Thrown Attack** made against a heavily-armored target may still fail to cause any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to that target even with many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
participating. This is because each of the individual sub-attacks is
weak, and thus each has an equal chance of failing to penetrate the
armor.

#### Example

:   A unit of [Barbarian Swordsmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Barbarian_Swordsmen "Barbarian Swordsmen") has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **6 Figures** when fully-healed. By default, this unit possesses the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability of strength [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **1**.

:   When the [Barbarian Swordsmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Barbarian_Swordsmen "Barbarian Swordsmen") unit is voluntarily attacking, each of the 6 **Swordsmen** makes a separate **Thrown Attack**, and each of these attacks has a strength of [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **1** (i.e. delivering 1 [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") point at the target). Therefore, as per the rules of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), each **Swordsman** makes a single [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** roll against the target, and can potentially inflict [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Point of Damage**. If all attacks are successful, the target will suffer a total of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** \* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **6** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **6 points of Damage**.

:   However, almost all targets are sufficiently-well-armored to withstand this damage, *because* it is delivered in [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **1-point** "packets", instead of a single [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **6-point** attack.

:   For example, an enemy unit with only [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **1 Defense** - extremely light armor - has only a 25% chance of suffering damage from *each* of these [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **1-point**
    attacks. In this case, having 6 separate attacks does help, since a 25%
    chance with each of six separate attacks means roughly a 75% chance
    that the target will suffer at least [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 point of Damage**.

:   On the other hand, if the target has a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **5**, the chance of each [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **1-point** attack inflicting *any* damage on it is remarkably slim - only about 5% per attack! This gives only a ~25% chance of hurting the target *at all*. For comparison, a single [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **6-point** attack would've been significantly more useful against the same target, having roughly 45% chance of hurting it to some degree.

:   Thus, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** with a low [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**
    strength can indeed be dangerous to lightly-armored enemies, simply
    because of the multitude of attacks being executed one after the other.
    However they are still far less useful against heavily-armored targets
    than would a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **single** powerful **Thrown Attacker** delivering the same total amount of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") in one go.

## Execution and Sequence

The **Thrown Attack** is one of the [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") types. This means that it cannot be executed on its own, but rather is always a part of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") executed by one unit upon the other.

**Thrown Attacks** are executed only by the initiator of a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). For example, if unit A attacks unit B, and both units have the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability, only unit A gets to make a **Thrown Attack** since it initiated combat. Unit B may not use its **Thrown Attack**. If both units survive of course, then once unit B's turn begins it can initiate a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against unit A, in which case unit B gets to use a **Thrown Attack** and unit A does not.

**Thrown Attacks** are always executed at the very beginning of the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
process, before any other attack or damage is delivered. This is one of
its primary advantages: the attacking unit strikes with its full force,
before the target gets any chance to hurt the attacking unit and reduce
its strength in any way.

| Stage | Attacker | Defender |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Thrown Attack** / [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") | -- |
|  | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") | -- |
| 2 | -- | [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") |
| 3 | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") | [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Touch Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") |

As the table above shows, the **Thrown Attack** occurs in stage 1, and shares this stage with the [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"). Note that the rules of the game state that a unit may not have both a **Thrown Attack** *and* a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), so only one of these will be executed as appropriate.

Because of its position in stage 1, the **Thrown Attack** also has an important tactical advantage: any enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**
that are successfully killed off in this attack cannot participate in
any of the following stages. Thus, enemy figures killed by a **Thrown Attack** will not deliver their own [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), nor any [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")
back at the attacker. This provides the attacker some security: by
weakening the enemy, the attacker stands to suffer less retaliatory
damage.

## Immunities

**Thrown Attacks** deliver only basic [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). As far as we know, this means that no immunity will ever be triggered by a **Thrown Attack**.
This means that there is no good way to prevent this attack from doing
damage, except of course when the target innately has a high [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") rating.

## Ground vs. Air

The normal rules of combat prohibit any [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit from initiating a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against any [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** opponent. Such units may only [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") to this opponent, allowing the [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** enemy to maneuver freely around them without fear of being attacked.

However, possession of a **Thrown Attack** by a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit allows that unit to break the rules: it may freely and voluntarily execute [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units.

Therefore, while such a unit is not actually [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying**, it can still engage enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units on relatively even terms during combat.
