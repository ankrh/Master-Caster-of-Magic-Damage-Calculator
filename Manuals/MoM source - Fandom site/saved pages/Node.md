A **Node** is a type of [Terrain Special](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Terrain_Special "Terrain Special"), which doubles both as a potential [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** source and a high-level [Encounter Zone](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone"). **Nodes** also represent unique terrain types that are not found elsewhere (apart from other **Nodes**), and give different benefits than the generic terrain that the **Surveyor (F1)** reports them to be on.

There are three types of **Nodes** in the game. Each type corresponds to a specific [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") of magic, and behaves differently as a result:

1. [Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")**,
2. [Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node") [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")**,
3. [Sorcery Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node") [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")**.

There are no **Nodes** associated with the Realms of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**, which relay their power through religious institutions instead.

Initially, all **Nodes** are guarded by a contingent of [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") that belong to the **Node**'s Realm. Once these creatures have been removed, any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") may send a [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Magic Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit")** or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")** to [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") with the **Node**, thus acquiring a constant input of [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** based on the **Node**'s coverage area.

30 **Nodes** are generated across the Planes at the start of each game. These **Nodes** cannot be removed or altered, and no new **Nodes** will be added during the course of the campaign. The 14 **Nodes** found on the [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane") of [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror") produce much more [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** than the 16 found on [Arcanus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcanus "Arcanus") – but are also better defended.

**Nodes** cause (and represent) oddities in the magical field. During combat at a **Node**, these interfere with the casting of any spell that is not from the **Node**'s own Realm. The aura of magical energy emanating from the **Node** also boosts the abilities of all [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") that belong to the **Node**'s Realm during any battle within the **Node**'s area of influence.

[Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** output from a **Node** is determined by the size of its influence zone, a random field spanning 5 to 10 tiles for Arcanus **Nodes** and 10 to 20 for Myrran ones, roughly centered around (and always including) the tile that the **Node** itself is on. The *Magic Intensity* Setting chosen when starting the game also has a direct effect on the generated [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power**. **Nodes** can be subverted using the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Warp Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Node "Warp Node")** spell, and random "Conjunction" type [Events](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event "Event") may temporarily alter the amount of [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** they provide.

## Description

A **Node** is a location where the barrier between one of the magical Realms ([Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**, or [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**), and the primal planes ([Arcanus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcanus "Arcanus") and [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror")), is weak, allowing [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** to seep through in great quantities. There are three types of **Nodes** in the game, and each corresponds to a different magical [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm").

[Nature Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node") lead to the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**. They are defended by [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** creatures, and interfere with non-[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** spells. They appear as magical forests of glowing green trees. The tiles containing these **Nodes** always show up as [Forests](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Forest "Forest") in the **Surveyor (F1)**,
but these enchanted woods are much more than mere Forests. Their trees
bear a wide variety of fruits, and their shades are teeming with a
multitude of animals, creating small, contained patches of paradise.

[Chaos Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node") lead to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**. They are defended by [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** creatures, and interfere with non-[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** spells. They appear as volcanoes with a wide, glowing red caldera. The tiles containing these **Nodes** are initially always [Volcanoes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Volcano "Volcano").
In fact, every active Volcano on either Plane will have a Chaos Node in
it at the beginning of the game. Warlocks jealous of this power may
acquire the means to eventually raise Volcanoes of their own, but these
creations will always pale in comparison (and will never contain any **Nodes**).

[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node") lead to the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**. They are defended by [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")** creatures, and interfere with non-[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")** spells. They appear as lakes with bright-blue waters. Yet, the **Surveyor (F1)** will always claim that the tiles containing these **Nodes** are [Grassland](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Grassland "Grassland").
In reality, these fertile magical waters not only attract an abundance
of wildlife, but are so refreshing, that armies marching through will
feel no more encumbered than they would were they crossing a flat
landscape.

If a **Node** is [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") [Melded](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") with by a [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Magic Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit")** or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")**, the **Node** tile, and any tile within its zone of influence, will glow with magical [Sparkles](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a5/Sparkles.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140121231841)sparkles of the same color as the empire that sent that spirit.

## Distribution and Terrain

When creating the world for a new campaign, the game randomly places 30 **Nodes** at various locations on both [Planes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane") (16 on [Arcanus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcanus "Arcanus"), and 14 on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror")). The overall number of **Nodes** always remains constant between games. However, the types of **Nodes** created is different, and there is no guarantee that any type of **Node** will appear on either of the Planes. **Nodes** may not be created less than 3 tiles away from any edge of the map (including the invisible horizontal boundary), or any other **Node**.

The [Terrain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Terrain "Terrain") underneath a **Node** is always determined by the **Node** itself: the actual tiles will be converted to special terrain types that are unique to [Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nodes "Nodes"). Unfortunately, this is not directly reflected by the **Surveyor (F1)**, which will always show [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** as [Forests](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Forest "Forest"), [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** as [Volcanoes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Volcano "Volcano"), and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** as [Grasslands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Grassland "Grassland"). In any case, it is not possible to change the terrain of any tile containing a **Node**, with one exception: in games played without the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50"), the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Gaia's Blessing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaia%27s_Blessing "Gaia's Blessing")** spell can alter the appearance and [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") bonuses of a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** Volcano tile by turning it into a [Hill](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hill "Hill") instead (while leaving the actual **Node** intact).

A **Node**'s tile is an invalid location for constructing a new [Outpost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Outpost "Outpost"), although it is possible to build a [Road](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Road "Road") or [Enchanted Road](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchanted_Road "Enchanted Road") over it. On the other hand, Towns may be built *next to* a **Node**, and such Towns will enjoy the full benefit of the **Node**'s special terrain type, even if the **Node**'s defenders have not yet been vanquished, or the **Node** belongs to another Wizard. If the Town is also within the **Node**'s zone of influence, then [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") of the corresponding [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") will also fight with increased effectiveness here. This may be worth considering when settling next to an unconquered **Node** that can still generate [Rampaging Monsters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rampaging_Monster "Rampaging Monster").

The special terrain types that come with **Nodes** generally grant more powerful benefits than the tile type indicated by the **Surveyor (F1)**, and are almost always worth including in the catchment area of any Settlement. The Volcanoes of a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** are the only ones for which the game gives an accurate description: these tiles provide the same [Production](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Production "Production") **5% production** bonus that [Mountain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mountain "Mountain") tiles do, which is the biggest such benefit available from a single tile.

The Grassland under [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** provides [Food](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Food "Food")**2** instead of the displyed 1.5, which puts it on par with [River](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/River "River") and [River Mouth](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/River_Mouth "River Mouth") tiles without the hindrance that those tiles present to [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** units. Finally, the enchanted Forests of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** yield the game's highest possible [Food](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Food "Food")**Food** available on a single tile: [Food](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Food "Food")**2.5**. Regular Forest tiles can only match this amount with the [Wild Game](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Game "Wild Game") [Terrain Special](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Terrain_Special "Terrain Special"), which, unlike the **Node**, is susceptible to destruction (although at the same time it also increases actual [Food](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Food "Food")**Food** output, not just its availability).

### Node Generation

The official *Strategy Guide* claims that the terrain surrounding the location picked for a **Node** has some influence over the type of the **Node**
that will appear there. While this indeed used to be the case in the
earliest versions of the game, this information is now outdated and is
no longer true from v1.1 onwards. In the more recent versions, **Node** generation is based mostly on chance. The initial type is determined by a set of random "bias" variables, that result in [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** ~60% of the time (3,370 in 5,625), [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** ~22% of the time (1,240 in 5,625), and [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** in the remaining ~18% (1,015 in 5,625).

The **Nodes** are then put through an "equalizing" procedure, which randomly converts [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** to a different type if their amount on either Plane exceeds a certain threshold. This limit is 9 on [Arcanus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcanus "Arcanus"), and 4 on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror"). If there are more [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** than this, the game will keep randomly converting them, first to [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")**, then to [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")**,
until there is at least a certain amount of each of them on the Plane
in question. Arcanus will then have at least 6 of those two types, while
Myrror will have at least 3; irrespective of the remaining amount of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** in either case.

Generally, this yields pre-set ratios of 6-6-4 and 3-3-8 ([Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** – [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** - [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")**)
on Arcanus and Myrror respectively, as is clearly visible in the tables
below, that illustrate the statistical distribution of the **Nodes** from each Realm. The first table for each [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane") lists the probabilities for each **Node** type's individual count on that Plane, while the second tables show the chances of particular [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** / [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** combinations.

These numbers also show how differently the "equalizing" procedure behaves on the two Planes by virtue of the amount of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** required to activate it. On Arcanus, where the average count of this **Node**
type is just above the function's threshold, there is a fair chance
that it will not be invoked at all, leaving intact whatever amount of **Nodes** were initially generated. Once it does kick in however, it drastically reduces the amount of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** (by at least 6), while roughly doubling the other two.

In contrast, the function will nearly always trigger on Myrror (the average [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")**
count being over twice as much as the trigger threshold). However, in
this case, the presets are very close to the average values, and there
may either not be any actual "equalizing" at all, or it will be limited
to very small number of conversions, mostly to a single other type.

As a direct consequence of the different thresholds, Myrror is much less likely to feature no **Nodes**
of one type or another, although the likelihood of this happening on
either Plane is already extremely low to begin with. At the same time,
Myrror will generally have a higher amount of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** despite it having a lower total **Node** count, as this will nearly always be the case when the Arcanus **Nodes** are "equalized". In addition, with respect to [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** **Nodes**, the only way to have very few (or none) of one type is to have an excessive amount of the other, at least enough to bring the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** count below the limit of triggering the "equalization".

While as mentioned above, terrain does not normally play a role in **Node**
generation in the more recent game versions, there is actually one
exception. When the game selects a set of random coordinates to check if
a **Node** may be created there, there is a good chance (especially on lower *Land Size* Settings) that the tile is not an actual land tile: i.e. it is an [Ocean](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") or a [Shore](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shore "Shore"). Typically, the program will then evaluate whether to go through with creating the **Node** there (since the tile will be altered anyway) using another random roll.

On Arcanus, such a tile has a 1-in-40 (2.5%) chance of being accepted, and a **Node** is then created on it. However, the function suffers from a major bug with regards to Myrran **Nodes**. The code calls for a 1-in-25 (4%) chance for allowing **Node**
creation, but when the coordinates are actually checked against the
tile type, the Arcanian terrain is used instead of the Myrran map. That
is, if the Myrran coordinates point to a land tile on Arcanus, the **Node** *will* be created on Myrror without ever considering the Myrran terrain. This results in a much higher amount of Myrran **Nodes** being created in Oceans than intended. The [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") corrects this behaviour, and players using it will find significantly less **Nodes** on 1-2 tile islands on Myrror (which also greatly reduces the overall existence of such islands).

## Encounter Zone

:   *Main article: [Encounter Zone](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone")*

 [An example Node scouting dialogue.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/5/58/Encounter_NatureNode_Dialog.png/revision/latest?cb=20120515220315)

An example **Node** scouting dialogue.



When initially created, every **Node** designates an Encounter Zone. That is, a randomly generated group of neutral creatures defending some sort of [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure").
The value of this Treasure is inexorably tied to the strength of its
guardians, i.e. the stronger the monsters, the better their Treasure (on
average at least, considering that it is ultimately random). The
following sections explain how the game chooses these creatures and the
rewards for defeating them.

### Defenders

Each Encounter Zone can contain up to 9 defending units. These are always [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature"), and any single **Node** can feature up to two different types of them. However, they will always be of the same magical [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") (matching that of the Node itself).

The exact types and amounts of the guardians are determined
through several steps, the first of which is setting out an "encounter
budget", which defines the total possible value of the defending units.

If the encounter budget is insufficient for any creature, the **Node**
has no defenders and the encounter is defeated the first time any unit
attempts to enter the square. Otherwise, the game divides the final
budget by a random integer in the range of **1 - 4** (or **1 - 6** in patches v1.50 and later), and selects the most expensive unit which costs *less* than this value. This will be the "primary" creature featured at the **Node**.
If no monsters qualify, a new random number will be chosen, repeating
the process up to 200 times to ensure that if the budget is high enough
to afford any creatures, there *will* be some.

Once the primary creature type is chosen, the game divides the budget by the cost of this unit, rounding down, to determine *how many* of these monsters will actually be in the **Node**. However, this will never be more than **8** (or **6**
in the v1.50+ patches), and if it is more than 1, there is a 50% chance
that it will be reduced by 1. These limitations significantly increase
the likelyhood of encounters featuring more than one type of monster.

The type of the primary creature(s) guarding the **Node** can be revealed by any scouts that move onto its square. This information will then subsequently be available through the **Surveyor (F1)** tool. [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") also vaguely denotes the amount of these creatures by adding the word "many" to the dialogue if there are 4 or more. The [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50")
takes this a step further, using "few" for 3 or 4 units, and "many" for
5 and 6 (the maximum amount in this patch) instead. Neither patch
carries these quantities over into the **Surveyor (F1)** however, which is thus limited to showing the creature type in all game versions.

#### Secondary Creatures

Finally, the game calculates the remaining budget by subtracting (*number of primary monster*) × (*cost of primary monster*)
from the initial budget. If this is sufficient for any creature other
than the primary monster, it will then proceed to add "secondary"
defenders to the **Node**. These units won't show up in the scouting dialogue, but if a battle is initiated at the site, their type *will* be displayed by the **Surveyor (F1)** afterwards (provided that any of them survives).

Similar to the primary guardians, secondary monsters are also
chosen using a divisor roll which, just like for primary defenders, can
be rerolled up to 200 times to ensure that as long as the budget is high
enough for them, secondary creatures will also be present. However, the
range for this random number is not pre-set, as it is for the primary
guardians. Instead, the game subtracts the amount of primary monsters
already added to the **Node** from **10** (or **9** in v1.40 and later), and uses this value as the maximum range of the random function. For example, if the **Node** already has **3** primary creatures, the secondary divisor will be in the range of **1** to **7** (or **1** to **6** in v1.40+).

Again, the most expensive unit (other than the primary monster), which costs *less*
than the remaining budget divided by the random number, will be
selected. The rest of the encounter is then populated with this
creature, up to the maximum of 9 total units (including the primary
monsters), or until the remaining budget is exhausted. That is, the
amount of secondary monsters will be the lower of either (9 - *amount of primary monsters*), or (*remaining budget*) / (*cost of secondary monster*).

### Multiple Battles

If, during an assault on a **Node**, the invading army
manages to kill a defending unit, but then loses the battle or retreats,
that unit will not be restored unless it has the [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") ability. This means that it is possible to "whittle down" the **Node's** defenders with several subsequent battles, instead of trying to kill all of them at the same time.

However, any guardians that are not destroyed completely are fully healed at the end of each battle, and will have all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
restored to life appropriately. Thus, it is not possible to kill one
unit by injuring it repeatedly in each battle - it must be killed
completely to ensure that it does not reappear in the next battle.

### Treasure

:   *Main article: [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure")*

The rewards for conquering a **Node** depend largely on its defenders: the stronger the creatures guarding it, the better the Treasure. Supreme rewards, such as new [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), or [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"),
are only found behind the strongest monsters. The game decides the
types of Treasure that will eventually be awarded immediately after
setting out the monsters, meaning that conquering the **Node** in multiple battles will *not* reduce the Treasure found inside. On the other hand, because Treasure *specifics* are only chosen at the time of the victory, saving before the (final) battle can be useful for rerolling them.

#### Treasure Budget

:   To determine the base value of the Treasure, the game first
    tallies up the guardians. The full cost of the primary monsters is
    converted into Treasure "points", while secondary creatures only
    contribute half of their cost. Thus, the formula is (*cost of primary monster*) × (*amount of primary monster*) + (*cost of secondary monster*) × (*amount of secondary monster*)
    / 2. In v1.50 or later, the cost of the secondaries is no longer
    halved, and the full creature cost is used as the base Treasure "budget"
    instead.

:   In v1.31 and v1.40 (but *not* in v1.50), this base "budget" is then put through what is essentially a reverse function of the *Difficulty* adjustment used to scale the creature budget (see above), so that Treasure value is more or less unaffected by the *Difficulty* Setting (as in the original game *Difficulty*
    is only supposed to scale down monster strength). That is, unless the
    game is played on the "Impossible" Setting, for which the Treasure is *not* adjusted *downward*, meaning that the higher creature budgets here result in more valuable Treasure than on any other Setting.

:   Finally, the budget receives a percentage adjustment. In the official game and [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide"), this modifier is random depending on the [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane") where the **Node** is located: on [Arcanus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcanus "Arcanus"),
    the final budget will be 50% - 125% of the previously obtained value,
    while Myrran sites will have between 76% to 175% of that. [v1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") uses flat values instead of the random percentages: Arcanian **Nodes** in this patch will have exactly their total monster value to spend on Treasure, while those on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror") receive 125% of this. However, as this patch also deals with [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone")
    that have no guardians differently than the original game, the final
    budget is further increased by 30 in v1.50, and by 75 in
    v1.51+ (note that the v1.50 value *can* occasionally result in no Treasure being found).

:   In the official game (and also in Insecticide), if the **Node** contains no creatures, or if their total cost is below 50, the **Node's** Treasure budget will be set to exactly 50, which allows for a single Treasure roll (see below). This can yield either [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **10** - [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **50**, [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** - [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**, or a single spell with a *default* [Spell Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity") of Common. Unfortunately, spells can no longer be found in empty sites starting with v1.50.

    type results of the d15 are immediately rerolled instead. v1.50 removes
    this behaviour altogether, and can not overwrite an already set rarity
    with another. Neither version will try to award a lower *default* rarity if the original roll does not "Qualify".

#### Treasure Specifics

* [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **Gold** and/or [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana Crystal** piles can appear multiple times in the **Node's** Treasure, and their amounts are determined at the start of the game (added together).

* The quality of a **Magical Item** reward is supposed to scale
  with remaining treasure points, but in version 1.31, the program only
  manages to constrain the item's quality in the case of a ["Failed Special"](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure#Failed_Special "Treasure"). Typically, then, the wizard only needs the [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") ranks that an item demands, for it to be eligible to (randomly) appear in a **Node**. Full lists of pre-fab items and their arbitrary rank requirements can be found [here](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure#Spellbook_Requirements "Treasure"), in the article on Treasure. The articles on [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") and [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") also examine in detail how the items are actually chosen.

* A **Prisoner**, a most uncommon find, might also be held at the **Node**. This individual will be one of the 25 non-champion [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") in the game, drawn at random from those who are not already in the [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard")'s
  service or defeated. The captive V.I.P. will offer to join for no
  initial cost, out of gratitude for being rescued (however, their upkeep
  won't be free unless they bear the [Noble](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Noble "Noble") [Noble](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Noble "Noble") trait). Before v1.50 however, if the victorious army stack is 9 units deep, or the Wizard already controls 6 Heroes, **"Absolutely Nothing"** will appear in the **Prisoner's** place.

* For **Spell** rewards, the game will first enumerate the [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") available to the [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") based on their Spellbooks, and choose one randomly out of these. The [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**
  Realm was supposed to always be valid for this for any Wizard, however a
  bug in the program (before v1.50) only allows these spells to be found
  if the player possesses either of the (completely unrelated) [Alchemy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemy_(Retort) "Alchemy (Retort)") or [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"). A random spell is then chosen from the selected Realm matching the *default* [Spell Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity") set out at game creation (see the [Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone#Spell "Encounter Zone")
  section of the Encounter Zone article for exceptions). If this spell is
  already known, the game will sequentially try to award all other spells
  of the same Realm and rarity. If that also fails, it increases the
  rarity (wrapping around from Very Rare to Common), and checks each spell
  in succession until it finds one that is not yet known. Only if *all*
  spells of the chosen Realm are known will MoM pick another color to
  try. There is no replacement reward for Wizards who already know all of
  the spells available to them based on their books.

* When a **Special** is generated, there is a 74% chance that it will be a [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), and a 26% chance that it is a [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"). Retorts that cost 2 picks can only appear at a site with 2 **Specials**, and will cost both **Specials**. The [Myrran](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrran "Myrran") Retort cannot appear in Treasure, and the prerequisites for all other Retorts are ignored. Spellbooks found in a **Node** will always be of the **Node**'s own Realm. Because a Wizard cannot possess more than 13 Spellbooks and 6 Retorts in a single campaign, any **Special** reward above these will instead be replaced by a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") with a maximum value of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,200** (or [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **2,000** per "pick" lost in v1.50+).

### Rampaging Monsters

:   *Main article: [Rampaging Monster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rampaging_Monster "Rampaging Monster")*

Any **Node** that has not yet been cleared of defenders is a possible source for [Rampaging Monsters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rampaging_Monster "Rampaging Monster").

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Difficulty | Budget | Frequency |
| Intro | Turn x 0.4 | ~1/50 turns |
| Easy | Turn x 0.4-0.8 | ~1/30 turns |
| Average | Turn x 0.4-1.2 | ~1/20 turns |
| Hard | Turn x 0.4-1.6 | ~1/14 turns |
| Impossible | Turn x 0.4-2.0 | ~1/10 turns |

Starting from turn 50, there is a chance for [Rampaging Monsters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rampaging_Monster "Rampaging Monster") to be generated from a random still-populated non[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**life** [Encounter Zone](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone"); although only **Nodes** that are on the same continent as a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town")
controlled by any player are valid for this procedure. Both the
frequency of this event, and the strength of the monster group created,
are random, with limits controlled by the game's *Difficulty* Setting. The frequency is also increased if the only remaining Neutral Cities are *not* on the same continent as any Wizard's Towns (resulting in the failure of creating [Raiders](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Raiders "Raiders")).

The [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm")
of the Rampaging Monsters will always match that of the source
Encounter Zone, and the process uses a creature "budget" similar to that
of generating site guardians. This budget is determined by the turn
number and *Difficulty* and, in the original game, is halved if the Rampaging Monsters are spawned on the same continent as an AI wizard's [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") (provided that the human player's capital is not also on that continent). The [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") patch increases the budgets by 25% in general, and provides a new option to further *double* them (*"Monsters Gone Wild"*).

Unlike site guardians, the process used for generating Rampaging
Monsters does not use divisors, and is not limited to 2 types of
monsters. The game will simply select random monsters one by one from
the matching Realm until it uses up the allocated budget.

## Combat in a Node

 [A as it appears during combat, and a few defending creatures.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/3/39/Screenshot_NatureNode.png/revision/latest?cb=20120511211523)

A [Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node") as it appears during combat, and a few defending creatures.



Whenever combat occurs on the tile of a **Node**, or even within its vicinity, special rules come into play that may have a bearing on the outcome. Generally speaking, [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who use spells from the same [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") as the **Node** will have an easier time doing battle within its area, whereas other wizards may have problems when doing so. As a result, the **Node** can be a strategic asset, or a dangerous area that needs to be avoided.

During battle at the **Node**'s own tile, the **Node**
is capable of countering any combat spells that do not belong to its
Realm, while they are being cast. In addition, when combat takes place
anywhere within the **Node**'s area of influence, all [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") from the **Node**'s Realm receive powerful bonuses to their combat attributes.

Both effects are visible when clicking the "*Info*" button
during battle, and this is the only way of telling whether a tile falls
within a zone of influence without actually melding with the **Node**.

### Node Dispelling Aura

The massive output of magical energy from the **Node** can interfere with the casting of combat spells. During battles that take place on the same tile that the **Node** itself is located on, any attempt to cast a combat spell that is *not* from the **Node**'s own Realm must face a dispelling attempt that may interrupt and counter the spell.

This dispelling attempt occurs immediately upon selecting the
spell for casting – before any target can be chosen. If it is
successful, the spell will fizzle with no effect, wasting both [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** and the "Spell" action of the caster. The strength of this dispel is always [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**, thus, the formula for calculating its success rate is as follows:

```
Dispel Chance = 50 / (50 + TSCC) * 100,
```

where "TSCC" is the total [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of the spell, including any reductions for [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort") or high [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") counts.

For example, when attempting to cast a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Fire Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Bolt "Fire Bolt")** spell in a [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** with a total of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **12** invested, the chance of it being dispelled will be as follows:

```
Dispel Chance = 50 / (50 + 12) * 100
= 50 / 62 * 100
= 0.80 * 100
= an 80% chance to dispel this spell as it's being cast.
```

Spells from the **Node**'s own Realm will bypass this effect entirely. They will never be dispelled by the **Node** regardless of their type or Casting Cost. The same [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")** from above will completely ignore an [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt")**, [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Giant Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Giant_Strength "Giant Strength")**, or [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Entangle](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Entangle "Entangle")** spell for example, allowing [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**-wielding wizards to cast with impunity in its presence.

The only way to counter this effect is through the [Node Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node_Mastery "Node Mastery") retort. This allows a Wizard (and any units under their control) to cast spells from *any* Realm inside *any* **Node**, without them being subjected to its dispelling effects. This gives Wizards with this retort a clear advantage in **Node** combat. It may also be worth noting that, while invoked using the "Spell" button, the [Web Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Web_Spell "Web Spell") [Web Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Web_Spell "Web Spell") ability of [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Giant Spiders](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Giant_Spiders "Giant Spiders")**, and the [Summon Demons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summon_Demons "Summon Demons") [Summon Demons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summon_Demons "Summon Demons") ability of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Demon Lords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord")**,
are not considered actual spells, and may not be countered or
interrupted as such. However, these are the only exceptions, and any
other action taken through the use of the "Spell" button is subject to
dispelling based on the Realm of the effect. Spells cast through the [Spell Charges (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Charges_(Item_Power) "Spell Charges (Item Power)") are also affected.

### Node Unit Bonus Aura

During combat on any tile that is inside the **Node**'s area of influence, all [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") from the **Node**'s Realm receive a set of very important bonuses:

* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+2 Melee Attack Strength**
* [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") **+2 Ranged Attack Strength**
* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+2 Defense**
* [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+2 Resistance**

Although the bonus to Melee and Ranged [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") applies only if the unit possesses the appropriate attacks by default, the [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") bonus applies to *all types* of Ranged Attacks, including short range ones ([Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown**, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, and [Lightning Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lightning_Breath "Lightning Breath") **Lightning Breath**, [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze"), and the hidden physical component of other [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")).

*All* [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") from the **Node**'s realm are affected, regardless of their owner, while other units are unaffected in any way. For example, a [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** will give these bonuses to *any* [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** creature fighting within its area of influence. If two armies containing such creatures fight one another within the **Node**'s vicinity, they *both*
receive these bonuses. Note that units summoned into the battle do not
receive the bonus on the turn that they are summoned, but will receive
the bonus on the following and subsequent turns.

These bonuses are extremely potent, and have several strategic
implications. For starters, this means that the original creatures
guarding a **Node** (which are all from the **Node**'s own Realm by definition) are much harder to defeat than similar creatures encountered in, say, a [Tower of Wizardry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower_of_Wizardry "Tower of Wizardry").

Furthermore, the fact that this effect also applies in the vicinity of the **Node** means that the entire area is a great place for some Wizards to mount a defense or an attack. The area around a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")**, for example, confers great benefits to any army comprised mostly or entirely of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** creatures. Similarly, assaulting an enemy army comprised of such creatures in the vicinity of a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** is typically a bad idea.

Finally, this means that any Wizard with access to [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")** magic is encouraged to build [Towns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") within the influence zone of the corresponding **Nodes**, and protect it with creatures from the **Node**'s Realm. This will make the Town much harder to conquer.

## Controlling Nodes

The primary purpose of all **Nodes** is to provide [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power**. In order to do so, a **Node** must first be "tapped" by [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") [Melding](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") a spirit into it. Once the **Node** is claimed, it will glow with magical [Sparkles](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a5/Sparkles.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140121231841) sparkles. The color of these sparkles matches the color of the Wizard that is currently drawing power from the **Node**. Several tiles immediately adjacent to the **Node** will also glow, these indicate the **Node**'s *area of influence*.

A **Node** may change
ownership any number of times during a campaign. It can lose its owner
if said owner is ever defeated. A rival Wizard can also attempt to send
their own spirit to try and meld with the **Node**, grabbing ownership of it.

### Melding with a Node

As noted already, it is necessary to send either a [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Magic Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit")** or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")** to [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") with a **Node** in order to tap its magical energies. Both of these spirits are [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"). [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Magic Spirits](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit")** are available to any Wizard, and are always part of all Wizards' starting repertoire in the latest versions of the game. [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirits](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")**, on the other hand, belong to the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm")
(which means they are subject to availability for research if not
chosen at the start of the game) – but, in turn, provide a unique
benefit.

To [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") Meld with a **Node**, the spirit attempting this must be on the same tile as the **Node**, on the same [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane"). This is why it is normally necessary to clear out the defenders first, but this also means that any opposing army guarding a **Node** must similarly be defeated before the **Node** can be melded with. Once a spirit is in place (and has [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") remaining), the "Meld" command will become available while the spirit is selected among the active units.

Melding will always destroy the spirit itself (but will not
affect the rest of the army if multiple units are selected). If the
process is successful, the **Node** becomes tapped, and will begin to glow with magical [Sparkles](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a5/Sparkles.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140121231841) sparkles of a color matching the banner of the empire that sent the spirit. Similarly, any tiles within the **Node**'s zone of influence will also sparkle with the same color.

Melding is not always successful. This is where [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirits](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")** have an important advantage: while a **Node** is melded with by a [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")**,
any further attempts to meld with it will only have a 25% chance of
success. An unsuccessful melding attempt causes no change in the **Node**'s ownership – the spirit that attempted it is simply destroyed. The **Surveyor (F1)** can be used on a tapped **Node** to discern the type of spirit melded into it.

Because it is necessary to clear the **Node**'s
tile of enemy units before melding with it, most Wizards (including the
AI) will endeavour to place a strong army stack on top of the **Nodes** they own. Any enemy who wishes to acquire this **Node** will need to defeat the guarding stack first. Naturally, [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") from that particular **Node**'s Realm are at an advantage here, as they will always benefit from the **Node**'s aura (see above) when fighting on its tile.

### Node Power Output

The [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** provided by a **Node** depends on three main factors: the size (in tiles) of its area of influence, the *Magic Intensity* setting chosen at the start of the campaign, and the [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort") of the Wizard in control of the **Node**. Other than these, "Conjunction" type random [Events](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event "Event") and the effect of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Warp Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Node "Warp Node")** spell can also temporarily alter the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** gained from a **Node**.

#### Area of Influence

:   The size of the influence zone is determined randomly at the start of the game for every **Node**, and remains constant throughout each campaign. This can range between 5–10 tiles for **Nodes** on [Arcanus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcanus "Arcanus"), and 10–20 tiles for **Nodes** on [Myrror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Myrror "Myrror"); and will be indicated by the amount of [Sparkles](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a5/Sparkles.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140121231841) sparkling squares once the **Node** has been melded with. Because Myrran **Nodes** cover twice as much area on average, their [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power**
    output is also generally double that of their Arcanian counterparts.
    This is also why they have much stronger initial defenders.

:   The influence zone of **Nodes** always froms a field of adjacent tiles, starting from the tile that the **Node**
    itself occupies. To generate this field, the game first determines the
    amount of tiles it will contain, and then randomly select that many
    adjacent squares in the following order: the first tile is always the **Node**'s
    tile, the next 8 are semi-random squares (with diagonals being somewhat
    less frequent) adjacent to the first one, while any tiles above these
    are chosen from the ring one tile further out from these. A **Node** with an area of influence spanning 9 tiles will thus always form a square of all tiles adjacent to the **Node** in every direction. It also follows that no influence zone can extend more than two tiles away from its parent **Node**.

:   Influence zones may not overlap. In fact, when checking for this, the game even ignores [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane") boundaries, meaning that if a tile is already part of an Arcanian **Node**'s area of influence (which are always generated first), the corresponding square on Myrror is invalid for Myrran **Nodes**, and including it will cause the offending **Node** to be recreated from the beginning (i.e. choosing a new random location).

#### Magic Intensity

:   For each tile that is sparkling, the **Node**'s owner receives a certain amount of [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** for as long as they retain control of the **Node**. With "Weak" *Magic Intensity*, this is [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **0.5**; on the "Normal" setting, it's [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **1**; while in campaigns with "Powerful" magic, **Nodes** yield [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **1.5** per tile of influence. In the [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")
    and later patches, these Settings are renamed to the arguably more
    descriptive "×0.5", "×1", and "×1.5", which correspond to both the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** generated per tile, and the multiplier in the [Encounter Zone](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") budget formula (see above). There are also two higher Settings introduced in v1.40: "×2", and "×2.5".

:   For the low and the high settings (and "×2.5" in Insecticide), the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** generated by each **Node**
    is individually rounded down to the nearest whole number, before any
    other factors are considered (including the summing up of their [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** output). For example, on the "Weak" *Magic Intensity* setting, controlling two **Nodes** with 7 influence tiles each will yield a total of [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **6** ([Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **3** from each **Node**), rather than [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **7** (14 times [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **0.5**).

#### Retorts

There are several [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort") available that will boost the amount of [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** acquired from tapped **Nodes**. First and foremost, the [Node Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node_Mastery "Node Mastery") Retort will double a wizard's [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** income from any and all **Nodes** he/she controls, regardless of their types. Furthermore, the [Nature Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Mastery "Nature Mastery"), [Sorcery Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Mastery "Sorcery Mastery") and [Chaos Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Mastery "Chaos Mastery") Retorts will double the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** income from the corresponding types of **Nodes**. For example, a wizard with Chaos Mastery will get twice as much [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** from any [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")**.

The effect of these Retorts is *cumulative*. Therefore, a Wizard possessing both Nature Mastery and Node Mastery will get *4 times* as much [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** from each [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")**! This can amount to a *massive* boost of [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power**, and should typically encourage such a Wizard to gain control of as many of these **Nodes** as they can, as early in the game as they can.

On the other hand, the effect of Retorts applies only *after* any rounding is done for *Magic Intensity*
calculations, meaning that they will affect the rounded value rather
than the initial one. For instance, the combined effect of [Node Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node_Mastery "Node Mastery") and [Sorcery Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Mastery "Sorcery Mastery") will only yield [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **12** ([Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **3** × 2 × 2) from a 7-tile [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** in a "Weak" magic campaign, and not the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **14** that it would if rounding only happened at the end.

:   Naturally, Retorts acquired as "Special" [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure") for defeating Encounters will apply their effect automatically to the output of any **Nodes**
    already controlled. Better yet, Retorts awarded as Treasure also ignore
    any pre-requisites, which makes it possible to gain any of these
    Masteries during the game regardless of the amount and colors of a
    Wizard's [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").

#### Conjunctions

:   Conjunctions are the most common random [Events](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event "Event")
    in the game, simply because there are 6 different types of them (thats
    one third of the total possible Event types). Of these, 4 will have an
    effect on the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** gained from **Nodes**, while the conjunctions of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** (called "[Good Moon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event#Good_Moon "Event")" and "[Bad Moon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event#Bad_Moon "Event")" respectively), will ignore them completely.

:   Events occur randomly if they are enabled in the game's
    Settings. Events with a duration (this includes all "Conjunction" type
    Events) last for a minimum of 5 turns, after which they have a (5% + 5%
    per turn after the 5th) chance of ending naturally. There is no way to
    "force" an Event to end, although the game can be reloaded after the 5th
    turn to reroll the ending chance.

:   Each individual **Node** Realm has a corresponding "Conjunction", these are called the "[Conjunction of Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event#Conjunction_of_Chaos "Event")", the "[Conjunction of Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event#Conjunction_of_Nature "Event")", and the "[Conjunction of Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event#Conjunction_of_Sorcery "Event")". Each one *doubles* the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** gained from the associated **Nodes**, and *halves* the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** gained from the other two types.

:   This effect is cumulative with any bonus gained from [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), and is applied *before* those. Unfortuntely, the halving effect may also involve a rounding down, which will be reflected in the final [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** output. In fact, this rounding is also separate from the one that occurs during *Magic Intensity* calculations (and happens after it). Using the above "Weak" magic 7-tile [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery_Node "Sorcery Node")** example, the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** gained from this **Node** (with both Retorts) during a "Conjunction of Nature" would be only [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **4**.

:   This result is reached using the following calculations: **7** (tile count) × [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **0.5** (*Magic Intensity*) = [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **3.5**, which is rounded down to [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **3**; followed by [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **3** / 2 (for the "Conjunction") = [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **1.5**, rounded down *again* to [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **1**; and finally [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **1** × **2** × **2** = [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **4** is the effect of the two Retorts. Similarly, a Myrran **Node** with a zone of influence spanning 15 tiles on the "Powerful" *Magic Intensity* setting will yield a base [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **22**, and this is the amount that will be doubled by the appropriate Conjunction (or Retorts), instead of the unrounded [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **22.5** that would result from the initial [15 × [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **1.5**] formula.

:   Finally, the last type of "Conjunction" Event is the "[Mana Short](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Event#Mana_Short "Event")", which will completely nullify any [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** income for its duration. Naturally, this includes [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** produced by **Nodes**, which will only return to normal once the Event has ended.

### Warping Nodes

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Warp Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Node "Warp Node")** is an [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") that may be cast on the overland map to target any **Node** that is already tapped by an enemy Wizard. For a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **75**, it curses the **Node** to *draw* [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** from its controller rather than producing it for them. The spell has no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"), it is sustained by the [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** drawn from the **Node**.

Once [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Warp Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Node "Warp Node")** is cast, the targeted **Node**'s tile will begin to shimmer, indicating the warping effect. Immediately, that **Node**'s [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power** output is shut down completely, and it will now *reduce* its controller's Power base by [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **-5** instead.

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Warp Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Node "Warp Node")** can only be removed by casting [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Disenchant Area](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disenchant_Area "Disenchant Area")** (or [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Disenchant True](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disenchant_True "Disenchant True")**) on the affected **Node** tile. While the basic spell belongs to the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**Arcane** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), and is thus available to any Wizard; it is somewhat expensive to cast, and neither spell guarantees the removal of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Warp Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Node "Warp Node")** effect – they require a successful dispelling roll (see their article for more information).

While a **Node** is warped, it cannot be [Melded](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") with by any spirit sent by any Wizard. Its current controller is "stuck" with it, unless they can successfully disenchant it.

## Known Bugs

### Melding with Occupied Nodes

It is possible to [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") with a **Node**
without vanquishing its initial defenders first. In fact, there are at
least two different ways to accomplish this, although the second one
requires access to a specific spell.

#### "Blind Movement" Method

This method has no prerequisites (i.e. is available to any player).
First, position your spirit on a tile adjacent to the occupied **Node** with at least [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**0.5** remaining. Then change view to the other Plane, and, using keyboard movement, direct the unit onto the tile containing the **Node**. This can be done with any unit or stack of units that does not possess [Movement Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Type#Plane_Shift "Movement Type") **Planar Travel**,
consumes all remaining movement points, and reveals terrain as if the
unit had moved into the coordinates on the alternate Plane that match
the **Node** tile's.

#### Plane Shift Method

The [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Plane Shift](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane_Shift "Plane Shift")** spell may be cast on a spirit that is positioned at the same coordinates as a **Node** on the opposite [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane"). The game will not complain about this, and will relocate the spirit to the **Node**'s tile, which will immediately enable the "*Meld*" command as long as the spirit has any movement left (or on the next turn otherwise).

Both bugs have only been tested in v1.31 at this time. Additional testing may be necessary for other versions.
