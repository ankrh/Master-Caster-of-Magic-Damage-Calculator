**Resistance**
is one of the basic unit attributes. It describes the unit's ability to
withstand malicious magical manipulation, poison, disease, and other
ill effects. It is denoted in the *unit statistics window* as a row of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **golden cross** icons.

**Resistance** is tested against using 10-sided dice, in what are called **Resistance Checks**, **Resistance Rolls**,
or sometimes "saving throws". If the die comes up equal to, or lower,
than the attribute, then the roll is successful. Otherwise, it is a
failure. Thus, each cross can be said to symbolize a 10% chance to avoid
an ill effect. However, many such effects temporarily lower their
target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score, to make it easier for them to hurt the target.

**Resistance** increases gradually with [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Levels** for units that can acquire them. Several spells can also increase or decrease a unit's **Resistance** score. There are a few units in the game that already possess extremely high **Resistance** upon creation, while others may be exceptionally vulnerable to enemy magic until such bonuses can be acquired for them. [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can also improve **Resistance** by a significant amount. Furthermore, certain items can actually help to reduce a *target's* **Resistance** through the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Spell Save** enchantment, although this typically only works with spells that target a single unit.

## Concept

Although there are many magical spells and effects in *Master of Magic*
that are meant to deal direct damage to the target, much like a sword
strike would; other spells attack the target in indirect or complex
ways. In particular, this includes attacks on a target's mind, soul, or
connection to the primary [Planes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane"). Though they cause intangible damage to the target, these magical effects might severely impair or even kill it nonetheless.

In order to avoid being affected by such magic, the target may
attempt to "resist" the effect. Often, this is a matter of applying
one's force of will to negate the malicious effect - essentially not
giving in to it and "wishing" it away. In other cases, such as poison
for example, it is instead the target's physical durability that works
to counter the effect, simply as a product of its stamina or hardiness.

The concept of using **Resistance**
checks against these effects most likely originates from the system of
"saving throws" used in Dungeons & Dragons, although the separation
of success from failure works the opposite way here. *Master of Magic*
also uses 10-sided dice, rather than a d20, and the amount of different
types of saves is consolidated into a single statistic. Granted, one of
the most common uses of saving throws in D&D is against direct
damage magical effects, which this game handles as physical damage
instead. 10-sided dice also have the advantage of success probabilities
being easily expressed as percentages, which are easier to understand.

## Resistance Rolls

Certain spells and magical effects force their targets to make one or more [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance Rolls**,
the results of which will determine whether they suffer an ill effect,
part of an ill effect, or no harm at all. These are also often called
"Resistance Checks", and sometimes "saving throws", or simply "saves".

A **Resistance Roll** involves rolling a random number between 1 and 10 - a figurative 10-sided die. This number is then compared to the target's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**.
If it is equal to, or lower than the score, then the check is
considered "successful". This means that the ill effect has failed
against this target. Conversely, if the rolled number is *higher than* the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
score, then the save is considered "failed", and the target will suffer
the intended effect. In some particular cases, the game has to consider
the compound result of several subsequent rolls to determine an overall
effect on a target unit.

With this system, it is easy to see how **Resistance** can be referred to as a percentage chance to avoid an ill effect:

|  |
| --- |
| * A unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **0 Resistance** cannot possibly roll a number that is *lower than, or equal to* its **Resistance** score. It can be said to have a **0%** chance of successfully resisting. In other words, it will *always* suffer the ill effect that targets it. * A unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **1** will succeed only if it rolls 1 (out of 10), meaning a **10%** chance. * A unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **2** will succeed only if it rolls 1 or 2 (out of 10), giving it a **20%** chance.   [...]   * A unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **5** will succeed on a roll of 1-5, and fail on a roll of 6-10. It has an equal chance of succeeding or failing, thus **50%**.   [...]   * A unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **9** will only fail if it rolls exactly 10. On 1-9, it will succeed - a **90%** chance. This is the highest **Resistance** score that still has a chance of failure (10%). * A unit with [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** cannot possibly roll a number that is *higher than* its **Resistance** score. It can be said to have a **100%** chance of successfully resisting. In other words, it will *never* suffer the ill effect that targets it. |

Furthermore, there are units in the game with a **Resistance** score higher than [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**,
whether innately or through external buffs. A unit like that still has a
100% chance of succeeding its roll. The extra points above [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**, however, serve as a buffer: the enemy would need to reduce the unit's **Resistance** to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **9** or less if they wish to have any chance of affecting it at all. Therefore, the higher a unit's score above [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**, the safer it is against enemy magic that might try to reduce its **Resistance** score somehow.

### Ad-Hoc Resistance Modifiers During a Roll

The majority of spells that force the target to make a **Resistance Roll** will simultaneously alter its **Resistance**
score by a certain amount, in order to make it easier to affect that
target. These modifiers only remain in effect while the unit completes
the necessary roll(s), and are then removed. That is, they are strictly
"ad-hoc", unlike the more permanent modifiers applied by [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") and other effects described later in this article.

Some of these can be quite severe penalties. For example, a [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Word](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Word "Holy Word")** spell targeting an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit will reduce its **Resistance** score by [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-7**. Thus, if the target has [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **7** or less to begin with, it will have no chance of resisting the spell at all. The [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Banish](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Banish "Banish")** spells, in particular, even have adjustable [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** penalties that can be increased by paying more [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** for their casting. They can both go as far as [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-8**, which is one of the highest possible ad-hoc penalties.

When attempting to target particularly resistant units, only
effects that apply a serious penalty will have any chance of affecting
them at all. For example, a target with a **Resistance** score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **15** cannot possibly fail any **Resistance Roll**, unless targeted by effects that inflict [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-6** or greater. All other resistable spells and abilities will fail against this target.

### Spell Save Penalties

[Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") have an additional way to boost the ad-hoc penalties applied by their spells: by using [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") containing the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Spell Save** attribute. When a Hero is wearing such an item, their targets' **Resistance**
penalties are increased by the appropriate amount against certain
spells cast by the Hero. This enchantment is cumulative with whatever
penalty the spell normally inflicts, allowing the Hero to cast spells
that are incredibly hard to resist, and can thus potentially affect even
the most resilient targets.

Naturally, to be able to utilize [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Spell Save**, the Hero holding the item must have the [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster")
ability, and needs to be able to cast one of the relevant spells.
Heroes without either of these properties have no use for this
particular bonus. Not every spell benefits from [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Spell Save** either. Below is the full list of those that *do*, categorized by magical [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"):

although this greatly increases the item's value (and thus, the cost to
purchase or create it). All of these are cumulative with each other
however, yielding a maximum possible combination of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-12** for pure caster Heroes, who can equip two Accessories in lieu of an armor piece.

### Per-Figure Resistance

Many effects that test a unit's **Resistance** will actually force it to make one roll per standing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in that unit. This is typically the case for [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**-based [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") attacks initiated by spells and [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"). Here, if a unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures** remaining, it will have to make 4 separate **Resistance Rolls** simultaneously. Depending on the effect being tested, the implications of one or more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** failing their rolls may differ. Most often, a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** that fails its roll will be killed off, but other effects are possible as well.

An effect that forces each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** to roll separately typically has a higher chance of inflicting at least *some* harm on the unit (provided its **Resistance**
score is not too high to begin with), than a spell that forces only a
single roll on the entire unit. However, with a single roll, the effect
is often more likely to harm the entire unit - since it only needs one
failure to receive the full effect.

#### Example

:   The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Petrify](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Petrify "Petrify")** spell will force each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in a targeted unit to separately resist or be turned to stone. [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Vertigo](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vertigo "Vertigo")**,
    on the other hand, requires its target to make a single roll, or suffer
    some combat penalties. Neither of these spells inflict any ad-hoc **Resistance** penalties. For this example, take a target unit with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6 figures** and a **Resistance** score of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **5**.

:   In this case, the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Vertigo](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vertigo "Vertigo")** spell has a 50% chance of either affecting the entire unit, or failing to affect it altogether. [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Petrify](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Petrify "Petrify")**, on the other hand, has a 50% chance of affecting each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. Statistically, the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Petrify](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Petrify "Petrify")** spell has a much higher chance of affecting at least one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit (about 98.5%), but a terrible chance of turning *all of them* to stone (about 1.5%).

### Unusual Resistance Rolls

There are three cases where a resistable effect works slightly differently than usual. The first of these is [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage"). When an attack with this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") strikes a target, it must make a series of **Resistance Rolls** in rapid succession, instead of just one roll. The amount of rolls required is equal to the strength of the attacker's [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") ability. Each failed roll results in the target suffering exactly [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1** point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

An even more unusual roll occurs when a target is struck with [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage"). In this case, it is not enough to know whether the target has succeeded or failed in its **Resistance Roll**; if the roll fails, it is also important *how badly*. The game takes the difference between the rolled number and the unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score, and uses that to determine how much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
it suffered, as well as how much of a benefit the attacker receives as a
result. If the roll succeeds, however, then the exact value does not
matter.

Finally, it is important to mention the role of **Resistance** in determining the effect of a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Disintegrate](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegrate "Disintegrate")** spell. When this spell is cast at a target, the game will simply inspect that target's overall **Resistance** score. If it is below [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**, the target is destroyed. If it is equal to or higher than [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**, the spell has no effect whatsoever. In other words, if the spell *can* succeed, it automatically does so, while otherwise it will naturally fail. No chance is involved - only the target's effective **Resistance** score in and of itself matters at all. However, conditional modifiers still can apply, including those of any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-Spell Save** [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), which can make this spell even more powerful than it already is.

## Resistible Unit Attacks

Some units in the game possess certain [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") which allow them to make [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") against their opponent. The majority of these attacks deliver some kind of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"). Rather than allowing the target to perform [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense Rolls**, like conventional attacks do, these force it to make one or more **Resistance Checks**, as described above. The amount of rolls will vary depending on either the number of *attacking* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, or the remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the *target* unit. Furthermore, in the case of [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage"), this also depends on the strength of the [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") ability.

Ad-hoc **Resistance** penalties for [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"), if any, are determined by the strength of the Unit Ability that made the attack possible. For example, a unit with [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") **-3** would deliver [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") with an ad-hoc [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-3** penalty. However, many Special [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") can be outright countered by certain immunities. If the target possesses the correct one, then it does not need to make any **Resistance Rolls** whatsoever - it simply shrugs off the Special Damage without suffering any harmful effect.

This also includes [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"), which are bestowed to [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") holding certain [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item").
Each ability is listed along with the factors influencing the number of
rolls, the ad-hoc penalties, any immunities that would counter it, and
the effects should the target fail to resist.

## Resistance Modifiers

The **Resistance** score of
a unit can be permanently or semi-permanently modified through a
variety of means, some naturally occurring, others magical in nature.
First and foremost, as [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") gain [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Levels**, their **Resistance**
score will gradually increase. Heroes can become particularly resistant
this way, since they have many levels to gain. These modifiers are also
always universal, meaning that they will apply equally against all
spells and attacks brought around by the enemy.

Furthermore, several long-term spells, such as [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), can be cast on friendly units to increase their **Resistance**. This can be very useful when fighting against [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who use many resistable assets. However, some of these grant only a *conditional* benefit. That is, they will work against certain attacks, but not against others (see below). Finally, there are a couple of [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") and [Hero Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Abilities "Hero Abilities") whose purpose is to improve **Resistance**. These are, again, nearly all universal.

It is also possible to *reduce* an enemy target's **Resistance**
score by artificial means, although there are only a handful of spells
that can accomplish this feat. One of these in particular, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Warp Creature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Creature "Warp Creature")**, is a [Unit Curse](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"), and is itself resistable. If it succeeds however, and applies its **Resistance**
reduction effect (which is unfortunately only one out of three randomly
chosen possibilities), it effectively zeroes out the target's attribute
score.

### Experience and Resistance

With each [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level** gained by a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), the unit's **Resistance** score goes up by [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1**.
Although this does not include the first level, the increase is
permanent, and cannot be undone by any means. Normal Units can usually
go up to [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite**, which is the 4th [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level"). Thus, they can gain an overall [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+3** above their initial score - a good, but not too impressive bonus, given the low **Resistance** score they often start with. However, this can be extended by [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who possess the [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort") or an active [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade")** [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment"), both of which enable Normal Units to gain an extra level above [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite**, called [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Ultra-Elite**. In fact, if a Wizard has both of these assets, their units can even reach the 6th, [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Champion** level, which is not attainable otherwise, and grants a further [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1**, among other benefits.

Heroes, on the other hand, can all reach a 9th Experience Level, [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Demi-God**, which provides a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+8** bonus to their initial score. This is quite sufficient to surpass [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10** and render Heroes hard, if not impossible, to affect with evil magic. For them, Warlord and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade")**
do not enable any further levels, although they do raise the Heroes'
initial level by one each, an effect that they also grant to Normal
Units. It may be worth noting though, that freshly hired Heroes do not
get the extra level(s) on the turn that they are recruited, but only on
the next one.

The [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Heroism](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heroism "Heroism")** spell can also instantly increase a unit's [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level** to [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **4**, if it has not already reached that level naturally. This provides the unit [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+3**
above its initial score, but lasts only until the unit reaches that
level on its own (or until the spell is removed by other means). In
addition, a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") that possesses the right assets can produce units that start out at a higher level. The [Fighters' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fighters%27_Guild "Fighters' Guild") and [War College](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/War_College "War College") [Town Buildings](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Building "Town Building"), and the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Altar of Battle](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Altar_of_Battle "Altar of Battle")** [Town Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Enchantment "Town Enchantment") provide an additional +1, +2, or +3 levels respectively, with the spell thus allowing the recruitment of [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite** units.

### Node Auras

Magical [Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node "Node") emanate an aura strongly aligned with the [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") that the Node is connected to. These empower any [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") associated with this Realm, should they engage in battle within the aura. Among other benefits, this increases their **Resistance** score by a universal [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+2** against all hostile effects. The size of these auras is the same as the Node's *zone of influence*. These are the [Sparkles](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a5/Sparkles.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140121231841) sparkling tiles that appear around the Node once it has been [Meld](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld") [Melded](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Meld "Meld")
with. Unfortunately, there is no straightforward way to scout these
beforehand, but the auras do apply regardless of who or what occupies
the Node. It may also be worth noting that [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** units do benefit from [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** auras, should they find themselves doing battle in one.
