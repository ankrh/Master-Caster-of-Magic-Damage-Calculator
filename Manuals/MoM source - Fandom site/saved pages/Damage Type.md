A **Damage Type** is the effect (or effects) caused by an attack. Two attacks delivering the same **Damage Type** at the same target will have the same sort of effect on that target (though possibly at different magnitudes).

Any attack made by one unit on another unit will deliver at least one **Damage Type**. Many spells that affect enemy units also execute an attack on their designated target(s), and thus also deliver one or more **Damage Types**.

**Damage Types** are split into three main sub-categories: [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") and [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"). The Physical Damage category includes all **Damage Types** that attempt to inflict [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** on the target in a linear fashion (one [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** after the other), and involve the common process of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls. Area Damage types are similar to Physical Damage, but are applied to every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** separately when targeted at a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure unit**. The Special Damage category includes all other **Damage Types**, i.e. those that behave differently from Physical Damage and Area Damage.

Some [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") Types work as *tandem components* to other types of damage, and are largely responsible for the capability of some attacks to deliver more than one **Damage Type**. Attacks dealing these **Damage Types** are naturally more dangerous than others.

Finally, most **Damage Types**
can trigger one or more of the target's immunities (if it possesses
any), making the target harder (or impossible) to harm with that
particular type of damage.

## Concept

The *Master of Magic Wiki* attempts to deconstruct and analyze
the rules of the game in a manner that is easier to understand, and with
as few exceptions as possible. Therefore, the "Attack and Damage"
system was devised, which categorizes each attack in the game based on
how it is delivered (known as the "[Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type")") and by the effect it causes to the target (known as the "**Damage Type**").

Thus, any attack in the game, regardless of its source or the
method in which it is executed, can be said to deliver one or more **Types of Damage**. Depending on the delivered types, the attack can cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** to the target, kill [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** outright, or inflict some other harm or effect.

For the most part, [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") deliver at least one particular **Damage Type** by default. For example, [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") will always deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (a [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") Type).

In addition, various [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") and [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can add one or more *tandem components* to an attack on top of its default **Damage Type**. For example, the [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") ability adds [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") to each of a unit's attacks, on top of whatever **Damage Types**
those attacks deliver by default. Such complex attacks can have all
sorts of compound effects on their targets, and are really what makes
the combat system so interesting - and so difficult to understand.

## Damage Types by Category

**Damage Types** come in three distinct flavors, and this Wiki splits them into three corresponding categories. They are known as [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") types, [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") types, and [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage")
types. Physical Damage and Area Damage are two very-well-defined
groups, with members of each group being similar to each other in terms
of how they work. Members of the Special Damage group, however, are
defined by belonging in neither of the other two groups.

### Physical Damage

[Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") is a collective name that refers to a group of similar **Damage Types**.

All types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") work by trying to inflict actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** on the target. To calculate the exact amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, the attacker must make a series of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls, determining the accuracy of the attack. The target then makes a series of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, to determine how well the target managed to evade or block this incoming damage. Finally, the game applies [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target unit based on the comparison between all of these rolls.

There are 3 general types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"):

* [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage")
* [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage")
* [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage")

The specific type delivered by any attack depends on the [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type"). Therefore, [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") deliver [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"), and spells and some [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") abilities deliver [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage").

[Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") and [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage")
are then further split into "sub-types", depending on what additional
kinds of immunities they trigger, if the target possesses any. [Ranged Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage#Ranged_Magical_Damage "Ranged Damage") forms a crossover between the two types, and thus belongs to (and is described in) both.

### Area Damage

The [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") group consists of only two **Damage Types**. In essence, these are variations of [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") Types ([Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") and [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage") respectively), with the difference being in their method of application.

While [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") is always applied to a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, working through multiple figures one at a time, [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") is instead applied simultaneously to all [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**. Just like [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") however, it is meant to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target. In fact, when it is directed at a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure unit**, [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") behaves exactly like [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage").

For [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** targets, it strikes each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** within the target unit separately from the others. The process involves making separate [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against each figure, and then allowing each figure to make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls of its own. Once the "hits" and "blocks" are tallied and calculated for each figure, the resulting [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is applied to the unit as a whole (starting with the "lead" figure, and killing off figures one by one).

The two types of [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") are called "[Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage")" and "[Blizzard Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Blizzard_Damage "Blizzard Damage")". The only difference between them is the type of immunity they trigger ([Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") or [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") respectively). Both types will also trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), if any, which makes the target completely immune to both of them.

### Special Damage

[Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") is a catch-all term that refers to all **Damage Types** that do not fit into either the [Physical](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") or [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") category. Therefore, all types of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") will behave differently from [Physical](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") and [Area Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Area_Damage "Area Damage") in one or more ways.

One type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"), called [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage"), works somewhat similarly to [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") in that it will inflict actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** on the target. However it is different enough that it cannot be lumped together with any of the Physical Damage types.

The majority of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") types will actually force the target to make [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls instead of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense**
rolls, as a means of determining how much harm (if any) is caused to
that target. Several of these Special Damage types actually do not cause
[Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** in the usual sense, instead *killing off* one or more of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in the target unit outright!

Finally, a couple of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") types do no harm on their own, but instead make the target easier to hit with another **Damage Type**. These are known as "tandem damage components", usually delivered in the same attack alongside [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). The tandem component is responsible for messing with the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**
score, thereby making it harder for the target to defend itself when
subsequently struck by the Physical Damage component. In fact, [Doom Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Damage "Doom Damage") can also be thought of as a "tandem component" to what would otherwise usually be simple [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage").
