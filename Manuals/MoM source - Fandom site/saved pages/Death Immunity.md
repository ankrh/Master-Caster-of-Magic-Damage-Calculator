**Death Immunity** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*.

A unit with **Death Immunity** receives two benefits: For one, it cannot be directly targeted by most combat spell belonging to the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**. Secondly, the unit is completely immune to any [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage"), [Fear Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Damage "Fear Damage") and [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage").

**Death Immunity** is one of the more common immunities in the game. It is possessed by 15 different units, including (among others) all [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** realm as well as the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"). It is not possible to add **Death Immunity** to a unit that does not already have it, except by turning that unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.

## Description

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Magic**
is a particularly dangerous type of magic specializing in harming and
destroying living creatures. The principles behind this school of magic
involve manipulating the life force of an opponent, sucking it out or
subverting it, and potentially even causing dead corpses to rise up and
serve the spellcaster himself.

The **Death Immunity**
simply signifies a creature that is not truly alive - and thus cannot be
harmed by attacks directed at its life-force. This applies primarily to
creatures that are already dead, or creatures that are no more than
constructs without a life of their own.

## Effect

**Death Immunity** is a passive ability that protects a unit specifically from various harms associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**.

### Immunity to Death Combat Spells

During combat, a unit with **Death Immunity** cannot be targeted by the following spells from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** realm:

* [Possession](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Possession "Possession")
* [Weakness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weakness "Weakness")
* [Black Sleep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sleep "Black Sleep")

Any attempt to target these spells at a unit with **Death Immunity** will result in an error message stating that the target is invalid. You must select a different target or cancel the spell.

Furthermore, the following non-targeted spells will also have no effect on a **Death-Immune** unit:

* [Wrack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wrack "Wrack")
* [Death Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Spell "Death Spell")

The game will not object to you casting these spells, even if there are only **Death-Immune** units on the battlefield. However, units with **Death Immunity** will suffer no ill effects from them at all.

Finally, if a unit with **Death Immunity** is killed during battle, it cannot be brought back with the [Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead") spell. The game will not even give you the option to target them with this spell.

#### Exceptions

:   Note that several offensive [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death** spells *can* target and/or affect a unit with **Death Immunity**:

    * [Black Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Prayer "Black Prayer")
    * [Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")
    * [Word of Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Word_of_Death "Word of Death")
    * [Terror](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Terror "Terror")

:   Furthermore, the overland spells [Death Wish](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Wish "Death Wish") and [Black Wind](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Wind "Black Wind") will affect **Death-Immune** units as normal, completely disregarding the **Death Immunity** property. The reason for all of this is that **Death Immunity**
    does not raise the defense or resistance of the unit against Death
    Realm spells, it merely prevents spells from targeting or having an
    effect if and only if that specific spell checks for **Death Immunity** prior to targeting or effect.

### Immunity to Death-Related Attacks

A unit with **Death Immunity** is also completely unharmed by any [type of Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**.

The following types of damage will have no effect on the **Death-Immune** unit:

* [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage").
* [Fear Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Damage "Fear Damage").
* [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage").

The **Death-Immune** unit automatically succeeds any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll required to avert damage/death from these attacks, and thus will never be harmed by them.

## Acquiring Death Immunity

There is only one method in which to add **Death Immunity** to a unit: by turning that unit into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.

It is not a coincidence that most of the units with default **Death Immunity** are [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death**
creatures: these creatures are not alive, and thus have no life-force
to manipulate. Similarly, units that die and are brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units are also **Immune to Death**.

Units can be turned into [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") in several different ways:

* The [Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels") spell will turn a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by [Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain") damage is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle mostly by an enemy unit with [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the enemy.
* A unit killed in battle and then targeted by [Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead") is brought back as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), serving the caster of this spell.

Regardless of which method is used, the unit comes back with most of its original abilities intact - but now possesses the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property which, among other effects, also gives the unit **Death Immunity**.

Note that the icon for **Death Immunity** is not added to the unit's details panel. However, the presence of the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") property icon is always sufficient to indicate that **Death Immunity** is indeed in effect.
