**Long Range** is one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") available in *Master of Magic*. A unit with this ability will never suffer more than a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10% To Hit** penalty caused by distance to its target, when using a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

Only two units in the game feature this ability. Both are fairly common [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), and both use a [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attack**. It is not possible to add this ability to any other unit in the game, by any method.

## Description

Accuracy over a distance is a problem that all physical projectiles
inherently suffer from. Even today, with weapons capable of firing
projectiles at mind-boggling speeds, aerodynamic stability and various
other effects will eventually cause the projectile to veer from its
initial course. The effective range of a weapon is determined by the
most distant point where its projectiles commonly begin to display
severe instability.

In *Master of Magic*, the **Long Range**
ability represents a weapon with a decidedly-longer effective range
than any common ranged weapon. Of course, given that magical ranged
attacks suffer no penalties at any distance makes this somewhat less
impressive, but **Long Range** weapons still hold a strong advantage over ranged attacks with regular projectiles.

## Effect

As per the normal rules of combat, [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks** and [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks** suffer a certain [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalty based on the distance between the shooter and the target. Normally, the penalty is calculated as follows:

```
Penalty = -10% * (Distance / 3), rounded down
```

Therefore, if the enemy unit is 1-2 tiles away from the shooter, then
there is no distance penalty for the shot. If the target is 3-5 tiles
away from the shooter, the shooter gets [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%**. If the target is 6-8 tiles away from the shooter, the shooter gets [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20%**, and so forth. The game manual states that the maximum penalty is [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20%**, but the Strategy Guide states that there is no limit. Currently there is no conclusive info on this issue.

With reduced [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**, the [Ranged Attacker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") will (on average) inflict less of its potential damage output on the target. Given that the target can normally also make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls to block additional damage, a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") unit attacking at a great distance can end up causing no damage to its target, even with repeated attempts.

The **Long Range** ability's only function is to limit the above formula so that the resulting penalty is *never* greater than [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%**. Therefore, the same [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%**
penalty is applied whether the target is 3 tiles away or 15 tiles away.
A target at 1-2 tiles from the shooter still applies no penalty, as
normal.

This allows [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
to cause a little more damage even when used at a significant distance.
Thus, such attacks are useful immediately at the start of a battle
(when the two armies are still far from each other), where normal [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Missile Attacks** don't fare so well.

## Units with Default Long Range

Two types of units possess the **Long Range** ability. Both are [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"):

[Catapult](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult") [Catapult](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult")
[Warship](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warship "Warship") [Warship](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warship "Warship")

The [Catapult](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult") is available to almost all [Races](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Race "Race"). The [Warship](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warship "Warship"), being much stronger, is available only to a few races. Note that the [Warship](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warship "Warship")'s **Long Range** ability is one of the reasons why this unit is overpowered: it has virtually unlimited [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Attacks**,
and can move at a fairly fast rate, allowing the ship to maintain long
distance from its target while pelting it with boulders.

## Acquiring Long Range

There is currently no known way to add **Long Range** to a unit that does not have it by default, whether by spells or through [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item").
