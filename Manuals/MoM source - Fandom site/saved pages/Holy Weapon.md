**Holy Weapon** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**, it may be cast on a friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") on the overland map to empower its weapons with divine energy. As a result, the unit receives a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** bonus whenever it makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-**, [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile-**, or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attack**, allowing it to inflict more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** on average with these attacks. In addition, the unit can now bypass the effects of [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") if it could not do so already. The effect can be maintained for an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1** per turn, but will automatically dissipate if this can not be paid by the caster.

Alternatively, a cheaper version of the same spell may be cast for [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10**
during combat, which should temporarily bestow the same bonus on a unit
for the duration of the battle, with no permanent effects or Upkeep
Cost. Unfortunately, this feature is not working at all in the latest
official version of the game, and the spell will fail to confer any of
its benefits when cast in battle.

## Effects

**Holy Weapon** imbues the
weapons of the target with divine energy. This allows the unit to more
often strike true with its attacks, increasing the average amount of
damage it can deliver onto its enemies. Furthermore, these weapons are
now considered magical, in case they previously were not.

### Affected Attacks

**Holy Weapon**, as its name implies, can only enhance attacks that are considered to be made with weapons. These are [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee-** and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks**, and [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks**. The spell does not affect [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath Attacks**, [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Spell-** or [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") of any kind. Despite the logical assumption, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** are also not enhanced by **Holy Weapon** in the latest official version of the game, although they are in the unofficial patches, starting with [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide").

### To Hit Bonus

The main effect of **Holy Weapon** is a bonus of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** to the unit's chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** with the affected attacks. In other words, each die of their [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") now has an extra [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%** to score a successful hit, which increases the average amount of "raw" (i.e. before mitigation) [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
that these attacks will deliver. This benefit is cumulative with all
other bonuses of this type, although it may be worth noting that the
final chance can never be more than 100%. This means that [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** modifiers can never increase a unit's *maximum* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** potential - they improve its average output instead.

#### Example

:   A unit of [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Recruit** [Nomad Swordsmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nomad_Swordsmen "Nomad Swordsmen") contains [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6 Figures**, each of them possessing a [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength") of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** with a basic chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%**. This means that whenever they perform a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"),
    they execute six separate attacks (one for each Swordsmen), using 3
    dice (corresponding to their strength), each of which has a 30% chance
    (the base hit rate) of inflicting a point of "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** (or score a successful hit, in other words). Thus, their average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output will be [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6** × [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** × [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **5.4**.

:   Out of these three factors, **Holy Weapon** increases the last one, the unit's chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**. It does not improve the maximum [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
    that the unit can cause - these are defined by the other two
    characteristics. However, considering the amount of random rolls here,
    achieving that result with only a [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30%** is actually extremely unlikely, and this is what this enchantment helps with. With [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10%**, the new average becomes [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6** × [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** × [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **40%** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **7.2**. This is [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1.8** higher than the original - an increase of exactly 10% of the maximum [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6** × [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **3** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **18**.

:   Naturally, this also means that the higher the target's maximum [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** potential, the more it will benefit from this spell. Although in terms of relative [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, the difference may seem less pronounced as the unit's original chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** increases, the absolute gain from **Holy Weapon** actually remains the same all the way up to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **100%**.

### Magical Attack

Units enchanted with **Holy Weapon** are no longer considered to wield "normal" weapons. That is, when fighting against opponents with [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity"), the ability can no longer trigger to increase the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** of the target. This can further improve the average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output of the enchanted unit when engaging such opponents - sometimes drastically. Naturally, this aspect of **Holy Weapon** will have no effect on units that can already bypass this immunity, such as [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), or units created in a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") with an [Alchemists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild").

### Holy Arms

It is possible to enchant *all* of the [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard")'s eligible units with **Holy Weapon** at once using the [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Arms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Arms "Holy Arms")** [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment"). Although it has a fairly high base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **900**, its per-turn [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") is only [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10**, breaking even with **Holy Weapon** in this respect at a mere 10 affected units. When cast, it automatically removes all active individual **Holy Weapon**
enchantments from the player's units, replacing them with its own
effect. This is superior in that it may not be dispelled from the units
themselves, but it does create a single point of failure instead -
removing the Global Enchantment itself will also disperse the **Holy Weapon** effects from every affected unit.

## Usage

**Holy Weapon** may be cast both overland and in combat, for the basic [Casting Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50** and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **10** respectively. If the casting is successful, the game will prompt for a friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")
to be selected as the spell's target. On the overland map, this
typically requires clicking on the army stack that contains the unit
which, in turn, brings up a list view of all units in that stack,
allowing for the target to be picked individually. Only units not
already under the effect of **Holy Weapon** may be chosen. Once the spell is cast, a glowing [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[white](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** outline appears around the unit (unless it is overridden by a stronger enchantment); and **Holy Weapon**, along with its icon, will be displayed in the *unit statistics window* when inspecting this unit.

The overland version of **Holy Weapon** has an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1**
that must be paid at the beginning of every turn to keep the spell in
effect. This is automatically deducted by the game from the owner's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool as long as the enchantment remains in existence. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
in the pool to pay for the spell, then it will dissipate, ceasing its
effect immediately. It may also be cancelled manually by left-clicking
its name in the *unit statistics window* of its target unit. However, this action can only be performed outside of combat, during the caster's own turn.

The combat version of **Holy Weapon**
does not carry over to the overland map: it will disappear
automatically as soon as the battle is over. As such, it has no Upkeep
Cost; but at the same time may not be manually removed by its caster
either (as enchantments can not be cancelled during a battle).

It's worth noting that in the official version of the game, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** units still count as [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") *on the overland map*. This means that **Holy Weapon**
may be freely targeted at such a unit overland, even though the game
would not allow this during combat. The enchantment will then work as
normal if the target enters any battle. This is considered to be a bug
however, and is no longer the case in the unofficial patches, which will
instead also return an error when trying to target the unit overland.

## Acquisition

As a [Common Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Holy Weapon** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**1** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With none, the spell can naturally not be learned during the campaign.
This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Holy Weapon** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 30% (with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**1** book), which gradually increases with the amount of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**7** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **80**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**8** or more.

**Holy Weapon** may also be selected as a starting spell by any Wizard with at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**2** books. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

## Strategy

Despite sounding like a pretty basic spell, **Holy Weapon** is in fact quite dangerous - especially when cast on high-tier [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** bonuses can be exceptionally potent. They can help weaker units take on enemies with high [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") scores, and increase the overall combat usefulness of [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") units alike. In addition, the enchanted unit's ability to bypass [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") can be very helpful against certain types of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures, such as [Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves"), that are much harder to injure when their immunity triggers than when it doesn't.

## Known Bugs

When cast during battle, **Holy Weapon** has no effect at all. In addition, it is sometimes reported to be dispelled twice by [Dispel Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Magic "Dispel Magic")
and similar spells. In fact, this is the only way to be certain that
it, as well as its effect, have been removed from the target. Otherwise,
its icon remains up in the *unit statistics window*, with no way
to tell whether it counts as the combat- or overland-cast version. Both
of these issues are caused by the same bug: the game mistakenly sets the
combat-cast flag for the enchantment if the overland one is present,
instead of checking that flag for applying the spell's effect. Finally,
when it is cast overland and does work as intended, removing the spell
during combat will fail to negate the unit's ability to bypass [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity"). Although the former bug (both aspects) is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50"), the latter remains uncorrected as of yet.
