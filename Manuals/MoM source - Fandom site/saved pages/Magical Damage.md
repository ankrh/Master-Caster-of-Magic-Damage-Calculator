**Magical Damage** is a name used on this wiki and elsewhere to refer collectively to all types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") that will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). This includes damage from many harmful spells, all [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, and even [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack").

As with all other types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), **Magical Damage** attempts to inflict actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** on its target, and requires the attacker and target to perform multiple [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, respectively, in order to determine the final [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** amount.

**Magical Damage** is only a collective name that's used for simplicity's sake. The only common property of all [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") grouped under this name is that they will all trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), if present. This can have various effects, depending on the **Magical Damage**'s method of delivery.

Furthermore, two "sub-types" of **Magical Damage** exist, called [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") and [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage"). These are identical to **Magical Damage**, except they will also trigger additional immunities the target might possess ([Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") and [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), respectively). They are otherwise functionally similar to **Magical Damage**, and will trigger [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") if present as well.

## Effect

**Magical Damage** is essentially a type of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), whose only distinction is that it will always trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") in one way or another. Therefore, the basic effect of **Magical Damage** is the same as any other kind of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"): it is meant to cause actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points** to the target, and involves both [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls to determine how much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is actually inflicted.

The ***maximum*** amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to the target is equal to the strength of the attack itself. Since there are many (very different) sources of **Magical Damage**, this calculation may vary wildly, and is explained in its own section [below](#Attack_Strength).

### Rolling To Hit

With all types of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), the attacker is required to make [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**
rolls, which determine how many hits are registered against the target.
The number of rolls is equal to the strength of the attack itself (see [below](#Attack_Strength)). Each roll results in a value between 1 and 100, which is then compared to the attacker's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score (usually [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30**, before modifiers are taken into account). Each successful roll registers one hit against the target.

Note that whenever **Magical Damage** is inflicted by a spell, the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") value is always assumed to be exactly [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **30**. There is no known modifier that will apply to spells.

### Rolling To Block

The target of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") is then allowed to make [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, which will help it block or dodge some of these registered hits. The number of rolls is equal to the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score, and again each roll results in a number between 1 and 100, which is compared with the defender's [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** score (usually [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30**, for nearly all units). Each successful roll blocks one of those registered hits.

### Applying Damage

Finally, any registered hits that were not blocked are immediately translated into actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** which is applied to the target's current [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Health**. During this process, the target unit may lose one or more of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, and may end up being completely destroyed as a result.

Since [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") requires both [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, the actual amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** delivered to the target is almost always lower than the calculated maximum. It is even possible that no actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** will result, whether the attacker failed all his/her [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls or the defender managed to succeed in a sufficient number of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls.

There are also complex rules regarding [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** making or suffering [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). The attack is stronger the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** there are in the attacking unit, and the defender unit has more chance to survive if it contains more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as well.

For a complete explanation of how [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") works, including all the rolls it is required to make, see the article on [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage").

### Attack Strength

The total amount of **Magical Damage** delivered by any attack strength of that attack. **Magical Damage**
can result from extremely different sources - essentially both units
and spells - and therefore can be calculated from very different
factors.

In either case, the calculated Attack Strength indicates the absolute *maximum* amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** that can be caused to the target. Again, due to the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** and [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** rolls made in the process of calculating inflicted damage (see above), the actual results are usually *lower* than this maximum.

#### Ranged Magical Attacks

:   When a unit makes a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack**, it delivers **Magical Damage**. The strength of the attack is based on the unit's [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") score, which is denoted by a row of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Energy Bolts** in the unit's details panel. For each [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Bolt**, the unit delivers 1 point of **Magical Damage**. Thus, a unit with [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **3 Bolts** deliver 3 points of **Magical Damage** each time it makes a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

:   This becomes somewhat more complex with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units**, since each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** makes a separate [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") upon the target. For example, a unit with [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **3 Bows** and [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **4 Figures** makes 4 separate [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), each delivering 3 points of [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"). This is not the same as a single attack delivering 12 points of [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") (see [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") for a thorough explanation of why this is so).

#### Special Attacks

:   Some [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), including [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), deliver **Magical Damage**
    as well. The strength of the attack is then determined by the unit's
    respective ability score, as shown in the unit's ability panel.

:   Note that as with [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks** above, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** perform one [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**. This means that the more figures are present, the more **Magical Damage** is inflicted on the whole. Again, see [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") for a more thorough explanation of this effect.

#### Spells

:   For spells and spell effects that deliver **Magical Damage**, there is no simple indicator like that. To determine the amount of **Magical Damage**
    a spell delivers, it is often necessary to read the spell's description
    - whether in-game or on this wiki. Unfortunately, the in-game
    information is sometimes lacking; on the other hand this wiki usually
    (if not always) has definitively-accurate information.

:   On the *Master of Magic Wiki*, for simplicity and style, the strength of a spell will often be marked in the same way as a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack"), using a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Bolt icon** followed by a number. That number is the amount of **Magical Damage** delivered by the spell. This is convenient because there are very few differences in how [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") and direct-damage spells are handled by the game, at least in terms of how they hurt their targets.

:   Note also that some spells may deliver variable amounts of **Magical Damage**, depending on how much extra [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
    is invested into the spell at casting time. Again, this is usually
    explained in the documentation for that spell, whether in-game or on
    this wiki.

## Immunities

Regardless of the source of **Magical Damage**, all **Magical Damage** has one thing in common: it will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), if present.

When the source of **Magical Damage** is a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack**, triggering a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") means that the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is temporarily increased to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for the duration of the damage-dealing process (see [above](#Effect)). It will make 50 [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense** rolls, and can thus potentially block up to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **50 points of Damage** from the attack - quite a massive amount. Only the strongest [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks** have any chance of getting through this much armor.

With spells and spell-effects inflicting **Magical Damage**, the triggering of the target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") causes the damage to be *completely blocked*,
regardless of anything! The spell causes no damage and has no harmful
effect on the immune unit, even if the spell is extremely powerful.

It is currently unknown whether [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") trigger the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50 Defense** effect or are completely blocked, but current testing indicates that it is the latter; a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") will cause no damage or harm to a [Magic-Immune](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") unit.

## Magical Damage Sub-Types

There are two [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") in the game which this wiki refers to as sub-types of **Magical Damage**. They are called [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") and [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage").

These two [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") are functionally identical to **Magical Damage**, in that they will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity")
as per the rules dictated above. However, each of these damage types
will also trigger an additional immunity if the target possesses it,
making them essentially *less useful* than "plain" **Magical Damage**, at least in some circumstances.

[Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"), the more common of the two, will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") or its [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"), if *either* is present. If only [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") is present, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** temporarily for purposes of blocking this damage.

Similarly, [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage"), a rare damage type known to be delivered by only two specific spells, will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") or its [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), if *either* is present. If only [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") is present, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** temporarily for purposes of blocking this damage.

In both cases, [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage") and [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage") will trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") in the same way as all **Magical Damage** types do, as explained [in the previous chapter](#Immunities). If [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") is present it will override any [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") or [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), so refer to the previous chapter to see what happens based on the method of delivery.

## Magical Weapons

Due to the classification of [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") on the *Master of Magic Wiki*, some confusion may arise when talking about "Magical Weapons": despite their name, they *do not* deliver **Magical Damage**.

Magical Weapons are [Melee](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") weapons that have been "upgraded", in one of the following ways:

* For [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), this is done through either the [Alchemists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild") or [Alchemy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemy_(Retort) "Alchemy (Retort)") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), and causes the unit's Melee Attack strength to be marked with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Glowing Swords**.
* For [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), [Magical Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can be created or acquired, and then equipped in special slots (though they do not cause the Hero's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** indicator to change).
* Finally, several spells (like [Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")) are said to bestow a similar effect on their target: making the target's weapons "Upgraded".

In all three cases the weapon upgrade may have various benefits, but one important effect is that the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") will no longer trigger a target's [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity").
Coupled with glowing icons and the use of the term "Magical Attack" in
the source material, it is easy to assume that these weapons cease to
deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"), and instead now deliver **Magical Damage**.

However, on the *Master of Magic Wiki* a clear distinction is drawn between **Magical Damage** and [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). As established above, **Magical Damage** is defined by the fact that it will always trigger a target's [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). "Magical Weapons" simply do not do this. Therefore, those weapons described above are still said to deliver [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") - just with slightly different behavior and/or extra bonuses.

Note of course that any unit with a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** will *always* deliver **Magical Damage** with this attack - regardless of whether it has been upgraded in any way. All [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks** deliver **Magical Damage**, always.
