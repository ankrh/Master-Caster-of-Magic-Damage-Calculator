A **Ranged Attack**
is the term used to any attack made by a unit over a distance, using
bows, throwing boulders, or shooting magical bolts. Many units may
perform such an attack - almost half of all units.

The primary advantage of a **Ranged Attack** over a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
is that it may be performed at a distance - which gives some protection
to the attacker. Due to the distance between the two units, **Ranged Attacks** do not provoke retaliation ([Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")) from the target, so only the attacker gets to deal any damage! On the other hand, some **Ranged Attacks** become less effective the further away the target is - and very few are as strong overall as the strongest of Melee Attacks.

Furthermore, there is always a limit to the number of times a unit can make a **Ranged Attack** during each battle. Once a unit runs out of **Ranged Attacks**,
it must resort to other means in order to hurt the enemy - and
sometimes has to rely on much-weaker Melee Attacks to defend itself!

A **Ranged Attack** deals [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"), which is often identical to [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). The attacker makes a series of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls to determine how much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** is caused to the target. In most cases, the target then gets to make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls to try and block some or all of this damage. **Ranged Attacks** do not allow the use of most special attacks like [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") or [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") - but special properties can sometimes be added directly to the [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") the attack inflicts, and [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") may also be delivered through them.

Initiating a **Ranged Attack** costs an attacker 10 [Movement Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Point "Movement Point"), or as many as it has left (whichever is lower). Therefore, for virtually all units, only one **Ranged Attack** can be performed each turn.

## Concept

During a battle between two armies, units may have different ways of
inflicting damage upon their enemies. One of the safest ways to hurt an
enemy unit is by performing **Ranged Attacks** against it.

In the **Ranged Attack**, the attacking unit (hereby: the "**Assailant**") fires one or more projectiles at the defending unit (hereby: the "**Target**").
The number and nature of these projectiles - be they simple arrows,
large boulders, or bolts of lightning - greatly influences the amount of
damage done to the **Target**.

**Ranged Attacks** may be performed at any distance - regardless of how far away the **Target** is. **Ranged Attacks** may even be performed when the assailant and the target are immediately adjacent to each other. However, with [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Missile Attacks** and [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulder Attacks**, the further the **Target**, the less [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") it will suffer on average.

The **Target** does not get to respond to a **Ranged Attack** in any way. It will suffer whatever [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") it cannot block, and has no opportunity to retaliate with a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), as often happens during [Melee Combat](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). Thus, the **Assailant** remains completely safe while performing this attack.

Only a portion of the units in the game may perform **Ranged Attacks** at all. Furthermore, each unit capable of making **Ranged Attacks**
has a certain limit on the number of times it may perform such an
attack during each battle. For most units, this is called the unit's
"Ammunition", and decreases by 1 every time the unit makes such an
attack. Other units, particularly [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), will expend [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** from their own Mana Pool when using such attacks; thus the number of **Ranged Attacks** they can make depends on their available Mana, and whether or not they spend any of it to cast spells during combat.

If a unit's Ammunition or [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** runs out, the unit may not perform any additional **Ranged Attacks**. It must resort to [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
or spell-casting to inflict any further damage on its foes. Ammunition
and Mana are fully reset at the start of the next battle.

## Ordering a Ranged Attack

Initiating a **Ranged Attack** is simple: select a valid **Attacker**, and then click on a valid **Target**.

### Select Attacker

You must select an **Attacker** that complies with the following requirements:

* The **Attacker** must be under your control.
* The **Attacker** must have a **Ranged Attack** of any type.
* The **Attacker** must have sufficient **Ammunition** or [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to perform another **Ranged Attack**.
* The **Attacker** must have at least 0.5 [Movement Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Point "Movement Point") remaining.

### Targeting

Once a valid **Attacker** is selected, place your cursor on the desired **Target**.

If the cursor changes into the image of an arrow, a **Ranged Attack** is possible against the selected **Target**. Click the mouse to initiate the attack. Conversely, a cursor shaped like a large red "X" indicates that this **Target** may not be attacked - probably because the selected **Attacker** has run out of ammunition! In such a case, the attack is not possible - you would need to maneuver in for a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") instead, or start moving the unit away.

**Note:**  If the selected **Attacker** and **Target**
are situated in adjacent tiles, the cursor will change to the image of
two crossed swords - the same as the cursor indicating that a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") is possible. However, if the selected **Attacker** complied with all the requirements above, a **Ranged Attack** will be performed instead. This is probably an unintentional oversight by the developers, though it is often beneficial.

### Visibility

In order for any **Ranged Attack** to occur, the **Attacker** must be able to see the **Target**. There are exactly 2 cases where visibility can be impaired:

1. If the **Target** is [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility").
2. If the **Target** is behind a [Wall of Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Darkness "Wall of Darkness") [Wall of Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Darkness "Wall of Darkness").

If visibility is impaired, the game will reject the selected **Target**, and no **Ranged Attack** occurs.

Note that there ways to circumvent both of the above restrictions, primarily through the use of magic. Refer to the articles on [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") and [Wall of Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_of_Darkness "Wall of Darkness") for more details.

### Ground vs. Air

Unlike [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), a **Ranged Attack** may be performed by any unit against any enemy **Target**, regardless of their respective abilities. The only prerequisite is that the enemy **Target** must be visible to the **Attacker** (see above).

## Dealing Ranged Damage

:   *Main article: [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage")*

The main goal in most **Ranged Attacks** is to deliver [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage")
- damage from physical projectiles such as arrows and massive thrown
boulders, or damage from forceful magical bolts hurled at the enemy.

Unlike a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), which often involves a complex exchange of damage between the attacker and its target, a **Ranged Attack** consists simply of the **Attacker** attempting to deliver as much [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") as it can to the **Target** - and the **Target** attempting to block as much of it as possible. The **Attacker** will try to kill off as many of the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the **Target** unit as it can, or otherwise injure the **Target** as much as possible.

To process the damage dealt by the **Assailant** to the **Target**, the game runs through the following steps:

1. The game calculates the **Assailant'**s [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Attack Strength**. It then makes an [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **Attack Roll** for each [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Bow**. Each successful roll means a single registered "hit" by the **Assailant** on the **Target**.
2. The game calculates the **Target'**s [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. It then makes a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense Roll** for each [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Shield** of the lead [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**. Each successful roll reduces the number of registered hits to the **Target** by 1.
3. Each hit that made it through the **Target'**s defenses inflicts [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **1 Point of Damage**.
4. If enough damage has been caused to the **Target** unit, one of its [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** is killed. If this occurs, and the **Target** still has any live figures, the new lead [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** may then make additional [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls to try to block additional damage.
5. Step 4 is repeated until all hits on the **Target** have either been processed into [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** or blocked by the **Target'**s [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls. If the **Target** unit runs out of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** , it is completely destroyed.
6. If the **Assailant** possesses any [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") that can be affixed to their **Ranged Attack**, these will be resolved simultaneously with the above process.

(Substitute [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulders** or [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical Bolts** for [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Bows** as appropriate - the process is nearly identical in all cases).

## Ranged Attack Types

You might notice that different **Ranged Attack**
units have different icons representing their attack strength in the
unit's details window. This indicates the type of ranged attack such
units perform, and may influence how the game handles damage caused by
such an attack.

The majority of **Ranged Attack** units perform a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** using bows and arrows.

Several large creatures and war machines use [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks** instead.

Finally, many magical units and creatures will fire [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magic Attacks** at their enemies.

### Ranged Missile Attack

:   *Main article: [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack")*

The majority of [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") who possess a **Ranged Attack**, including [Bowmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bowmen "Bowmen"), [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers")
and other similar units, make their attack using hand-held physical
weaponry: they send a multitude of small physical projectiles (e.g.
arrows) at high speed towards their target.

The details panel for a unit possessing a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** indicates the presence of such an attack, as well as its strength, with a row of [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Bows**. Note an exception regarding [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers"), where [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Rocks** are used to represent this, even though it is still a **Ranged Missile Attack**.

[Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks** generally deliver only [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") to their targets. This is the basic kind of damage, carrying no special properties by default.

It is very important however, that [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks** are subject to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties based on the distance to the **Target**. The further away the **Target**, the less chance there is for each [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Arrow** to hit its target - thus reducing the average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done to the **Target** (more on this below).

Units with [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") will have a Defense rating of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** against this specific type of **Ranged Attack**. Few, if any, [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks** are strong enough to puncture through, making this a very practical immunity to such attacks.

### Ranged Boulder Attack

:   *Main article: [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack")*

Large [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") such as [Stone Giants](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Giant "Stone Giant"), and advanced war machinery like the [Catapult](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult "Catapult"),
will hurl massive boulders at their enemies. The impact force of such
attacks is often enough to get through armor easily, though they are
more useful against singular, powerful targets.

The details panel for a unit possessing a [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attack** indicates the presence of such an attack, as well as its strength, with a row of [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulders**. Note an exception regarding [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers"), where [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Rocks** are actually used to represent a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** (see above).

[Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks** generally deliver only [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") to their targets. This is the basic kind of damage, carrying no special properties by default.

It is very important however, that [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks** are subject to [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties based on the distance to the **Target**. The further away the **Target**, the less chance there is for each [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulder** to hit its target - thus reducing the average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** done to the **Target** (more on this below).

### Ranged Magical Attack

:   *Main article: [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack")*

Units capable of casting spells are often also capable of a
long-ranged attack using magical missiles. The visual effect
accompanying this attack may differ from unit to unit, but for the most
part they all work the same.

The details panel for a unit possessing a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** indicates the presence of such an attack, as well as its strength, with a row of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Flame Balls**.

The primary advantage of a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** (compared to other **Ranged Attacks**) is that it suffers no [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties based on distance to the target. This attack is equally effective against any **Target** on the battlefield regardless of how far away it is.

All [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks** deliver [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") to their targets. This is important primarily because **Target** units possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") will have a Defense rating of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** against this specific type of **Ranged Attack**. This gives practical immunity to the **Target** from [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, but not total immunity. **Ranged Magical Attacks** possessed by [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") can become strong enough to defeat even this obstacle.

## Limitations on Number of Ranged Attacks

Unlike [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), **Ranged Attacks** only have a limited number of uses during a single battle. The number of times a unit can use its **Ranged Attacks** per battle is *extremely* important. Furthermore, unlike Melee Attacks, most units can only make a single **Ranged Attack** per turn.

### Ammunition

Any unit that can perform **Ranged Attacks** at all is limited in the number of such attacks it can perform during each battle.

For most units, this limit is called "**Ammunition**". It is indicated by a special ability icon in the unit's abilities list, which appears in the following format:

| In the game | On this Wiki |
| --- | --- |
| [Ability Quiver](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a5/Ability_Quiver.png/revision/latest?cb=20120103133144) Arrows/Rocks/Spells x8 | [Ability Quiver](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a5/Ability_Quiver.png/revision/latest?cb=20120103133144) **Ranged Attack** x8 |

The number at the end of the label indicates how many **Ranged Attacks** remain. Each **Ranged Attack** uses up one **Ammunition** point.

A unit that runs out of **Ammunition** may no longer perform any **Ranged Attacks** against any **Target** until the end of the current battle. Of course, it may still make [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), and might still be able to cast spells.

**Ammunition** is reset to its full value at the start of the
next battle. Note that this "full value" is different for each unit. For
example, a [Stone Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Giant "Stone Giant") may make only 2 [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulder Attacks** per battle, while a unit of [High Men Magicians](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Magicians "High Men Magicians") can make 4 [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magic Attacks** per battle.

#### Mana as Ammunition

:   Several units (especially [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")) who possess a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** do not use **Ammunition** at all when performing this attack. Instead, they spend [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** directly out of their own private Mana pool (not their controlling wizard's).

:   Each [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attack** performed by such a unit takes [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **3** out of its pool. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** remaining in that unit's pool, it may not make any further **Ranged Attacks** for the remainder of the battle.

:   Spellcasting also draws [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** from the unit's private Mana pool, so such units need to decide whether they prefer to cast spells or make **Ranged Attacks** - or any combination of these two options - to most efficiently use their Mana to help win the battle.

:   The unit's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana Pool** is completely replenished at the start of the next battle.

### Movement Costs

Any **Ranged Attack** initiated by a unit costs that unit 10 [Movement Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Point "Movement Point"),
or as many as it has left (whichever is lower). Since virtually all
units have fewer than 10 Movement Points per turn to begin with, a **Ranged Attack** will therefore eat up *all* of the unit's remaining [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance").

As a result, a unit may only make one **Ranged Attack** per turn. Also, this means that a unit may not perform any other action *after* it makes its **Ranged Attack**, until the start of its next combat turn. However, it may perform any action *before* the **Ranged Attack**, and then make the attack - so long as it has *any* number of Movement Points left (even 0.5!).

Units with 0 Movement Points remaining may not make a **Ranged Attack**.

Apart from over-geared [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), only the [Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste") [Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste") spell can break these rules, as it will allow some units to get a [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") higher than 10 points per turn. Such a unit may be able to make a **Ranged Attack** and then have sufficient remaining Movement Points to move - or to attack again! In addition, [Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste") [Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste") also causes the enchanted unit to fire two [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack")/[Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") projectiles on a single Attack command (but not [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack")).

## Environmental Effects

The usefulness of a **Ranged Attack** can change greatly from situation to situation, regardless of the properties of the **Attacker** and the **Target**. Environmental variables, particularly distance between the **Attacker** and **Target**, can strongly influence it - usually in a negative way.

### Range Penalties

:   *Main article: [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit")*

As explained in the [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") article, the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** output of a **Ranged Attack** is subject to random variations based on the **Attacker'**s [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** score. The use of the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** value for [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") calculations is overall similar to how it is used during [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack").

One exception however is that the **Ranged Attack** unit's [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** value can be heavily influenced by the distance between itself and its **Target**. This occurs for both [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** and [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks**, but not [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**. The greater the distance, the heavier [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** penalties are applied for the attack. However, this can never lower a unit's chance to hit below [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **10%**.

While this doesn't affect the maximum potential damage that can be inflicted by the **Attacker**, it does lower the statistical average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** it will cause with each attack.

| Range | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** Penalty | with [Long Range](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Long_Range "Long Range") |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 Tile (adjacent)\* | No Penalty | No Penalty |
| 2 Tiles | No Penalty | No Penalty |
| 3,4 or 5 Tiles | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** Penalty | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** Penalty |
| 6,7 or 8 Tiles | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-20%** Penalty | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** Penalty |
| Etcetera | Final to hit is at least [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **10%** | [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **-10%** Penalty |

*\* "Adjacent" means the attacker and the target are right next to each other (including diagonals). This is [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") range, but as explained above, **Ranged Attacks** may also be performed at this distance.*

### Firing into City Walls

During battle in a town protected by [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls"), **Ranged Attack**
units with any ammunition remaining may attack any of the enemy units,
regardless of their position inside the walls. This is unlike [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), which can only be performed through a gate or breach in the wall.

**Ranged Attacks**
against enemy targets that are immediately adjacent to a standing wall
section are handled differently than attacks against those that are not.
Either way though, the walls grant a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** bonus to all units inside! Even walls destroyed by [Wall Crusher](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_Crusher "Wall Crusher") [Wall Crusher](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wall_Crusher "Wall Crusher") units or special spells will still give some bonus to units hiding behind them.

| Target's Position | Target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** bonus |
| --- | --- |
| Inside the walls but not immediately adjacent to a wall section | [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1** in [Version 1.31](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Master_of_Magic_Wiki "Master of Magic Wiki") [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** in [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") |
| Adjacent to a standing wall section | [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** |
| Adjacent to a destroyed wall section | [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1** |

## Special Attacks and Damage Types

:   *Main article: [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")*
:   *See also: [Special Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type#Special_Damage "Damage Type")*

Most [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), such as [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") or [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), may not be delivered when the unit makes a **Ranged Attack** against any target. [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), on the other hand (especially those conferred by innate [Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability")), will usually apply.

It is quite possible for a unit's **Ranged Attack** to deliver multiple types of damage simultaneously. A good example is the [Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord"), whose **Ranged Attack** delivers [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") as well as a [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") Touch Attack (which, in turn, delivers [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage")). As a result, the process of calculating the amount of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** caused to the target is quite different.

There are many units delivering two or even more additional Damage Types with their **Ranged Attacks**. This is always a result of either an innate [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability"), a [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), or an [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). Therefore, to learn whether a unit's **Ranged Attack** has any special properties, it's usually best to refer to its list of abilities (in the unit's details panel).

## Known Bugs

A ranged unit may use its [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") instead of its **Ranged Attack** against adjacent enemies. This happens when:

* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Strength** is at least two times as big as [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") **Ranged Strength**
* enemy is immune to the **Ranged Attack** (i.e. [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") vs. [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity"), or [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") vs. [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"))
* enemy is [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") (and the ranged unit has no [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"))

The latter 2 cases are problematic, if a ranged [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Ground** unit tries to attack a [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit: The ranged unit won't do any damage in contrast to the flying unit!

Imagine the following situation: A human player knows the [Flight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flight "Flight") spell and has the hero [Shin Bo the Ninja](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shin_Bo_the_Ninja "Shin Bo the Ninja") in his service. In this case, the player is able to defeat every **non-**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**death** [Encounter](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter") zone with strong ranged units inside (e.g. [Colossus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Colossus "Colossus"), [Storm Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Storm_Giant "Storm Giant")). He just has to move the [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying**
Shin Bo towards the enemy and continuously hit the Space button. The
ranged units will eventually kill themselves without harming Shin Bo.
