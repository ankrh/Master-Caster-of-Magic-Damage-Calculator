On the *Master of Magic Wiki*, an **Attack Type**
describes the method by which any specific attack is delivered. The
delivery method of an attack determines primarily when/where it can be
used, and may also determine which immunities (if the target possesses
any) will be triggered by the attack.

The most common **Attack Type** is the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), which most units can perform, and requires the attacker to be directly adjacent to its target. When one unit makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against any target, that target often executes a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") in retaliation.

Many units can make a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
(of one of three possible sub-types), which may be performed at any
distance (though longer distances sometimes weaken the attack).

Finally, a great many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities"), [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") and [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") can allow a unit to make one or more [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"). These are executed only when the unit makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or is subjected to an enemy [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") come in several different "flavors", and can behave radically differently from one another.

Spells may also occasionally be said to make an attack on their target(s). This is normally referred to as a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

The **Attack Type** only describes the delivery method of an attack. The effect of an attack on its target is called the [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), and is discussed in a separate article.

## Concept

The *Master of Magic Wiki* attempts to deconstruct and analyze
the rules of the game in a manner that is easier to understand, and with
as few exceptions as possible. Therefore, the "Attack and Damage"
system was devised, which categorizes each attack in the game based on
how it is delivered (known as the "**Attack Type**") and by the effect it causes to the target (known as the "[Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type")").

Any attack in the game, regardless of what effect it has on the target, can be said to have a certain "**Attack Type**".
This determines when the attack can be delivered, limit which targets
it can pick, and even determine which immunities it will trigger, if the
target possesses any. Different **Attack Types** can behave very differently from one another, making it hard to generalize whenever speaking about them.

For the most part, all units have the ability to execute a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") on most enemy targets, and the ability to execute a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") whenever any enemy makes a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")
upon them. Aside from a few special cases, mostly involving the use of
ill magic, all units should be able to execute both of these.

Additional types of attacks are available only to certain types of units, or as a result of certain [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities")
or positive magic affecting a unit. A unit with more types of attacks
available has more flexibility; some units rely on being able to switch
between [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") as the situation requires. Other units may be able to execute a variety of [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") alongside their [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), thus making them significantly dangerous in close combat.

## Main Attack Types

**Attack Types** can be
broken into three primary groups: Melee Attacks, Ranged Attacks and
Special Attacks. These are radically different from one another, though
the specific **Attack Types** within each group are very similar to each other.

### Melee Attacks

:   *Main article: [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")*

The [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") is the most common of all **Attack Types**. By default, each and every unit in the game can execute a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"). Only ill magic can prevent a unit from making [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), and such cases are rare.

[Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") involve hand-to-hand combat, so the attacker must first move adjacently to its target before being allowed to initiate a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack").

Once the attacker is assaulting the target, the target usually also gets to retaliate, using what is called a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")
are nearly identical for most purposes, so the attacker and the
defender are essentially both at risk, pitting themselves against one
another to see who comes out on top.

The primary purpose of any [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") is to deliver [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") at the opponent. Potentially, this will kill off one or more of the enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** (ideally, all of them). Some [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") can deliver additional [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), but this requires the presence of unique [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") and/or [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"). Without them, only [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") is delivered.

The close proximity between the two units during a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")/[Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") also allows both the attacker and defender to execute any [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") they may have (see below). This increases the potential harm delivered by either unit as appropriate.

Since [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") involve hand-to-hand combat, [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** and [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** units normally cannot initiate a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against any enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** unit. [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units, on the other hand, can attack any opponent. [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") are allowed against any incoming [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), but there are some magical effects that will prevent a unit from [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

### Ranged Attacks

:   *Main article: [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")*

A [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") is an attack made by one unit at another, using either ranged weapons, launched boulders, or magical energy bolts. All [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") can be performed at any distance on the battlefield, though most [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") are significantly more effective when used at short range than at long range.

The primary advantage of a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") is that the enemy may not immediately retaliate. Thus the [Ranged Attacker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") remains safe while executing this attack (unlike [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), which provoke a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") and thus put the attacker itself at risk).

[Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") are further separated into three "sub-types": [Ranged Missile Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack"), [Ranged Boulder Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack"), and [Ranged Magical Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack").
These differ from one another in several ways, though on the whole they
function the same (as described above). The primary difference is in
whether or not the attack suffers penalties when used at a great
distance, and which immunities it triggers if the target possesses any.

All [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") deliver [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"), whose purpose is to injure and kill enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. Certain [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") and other magical effects may add more [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") to this attack, making it more deadly in one way or another.

Just under half of all units possess a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
of one specific sub-type. There is normally a limit to the number of
times this attack type can be used in each battle - usually determined
by the amount of ammunition the unit is carrying, or the amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** it has left. Once these run out, the unit must resort to its [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") and/or [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), as available.

[Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack")
can generally be executed at any target on the battlefield, though
again distance usually plays a large part in determining whether a
target should be attacked at a given time. [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") require the target to be visible to the attacker, so some effects (particularly [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility")) can render a target immune to [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

Note that spells which attack their target (often called "direct-damage" spells on this wiki) are said to deliver a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). This makes them simpler to discuss.

### Special Attacks

:   *Main article: [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")*

Roughly one quarter of all units in the game possess additional attacks called "[Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")", which they get through their [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities"), through [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), and/or through [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item").

These [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") will never be performed on their own. Instead, they will automatically be executed when the unit performs a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

[Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") usually deliver exotic [types of damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"), with a wide variety of possible effects on the target. This usually makes [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") extremely deadly.

There are either four or five types of [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"), depending on whether or not the original game manual is to be taken at face value. They include [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), [Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack"), and optionally [Fear Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fear_Attack "Fear Attack").
The difference between them is in the sequence in which they are
executed while the attacker and its target are locked in Melee combat.

[Breath Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") and [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") occur first, and can only be executed by the attacker. [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") are performed next. Finally, the attacker and defender exchange [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") at the same time as they exchange [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") with each other. The sooner a [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") occurs, the more tactical advantage it can confer to its user.

Finally, when a [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** or [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** unit possesses a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") or [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), that unit may execute [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against enemy [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units.
