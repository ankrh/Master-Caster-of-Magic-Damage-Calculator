A **Touch Attack** is one of the many [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") Types featured in *Master of Magic*. **Touch Attacks** are executed *in addition to* a unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**, [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack")[Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack")[Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged**, and possibly [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown**, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath**, or [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"). One **Touch Attack** will be delivered by each and every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the unit whenever they perform their other attacks.

**Touch Attacks** are only available from three sources:

* units possessing one of the many [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") that bestow such an attack (e.g. [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch"), [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal"))
* [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") with [Magical Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") enchanted to confer some type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") (this will only apply to attack types associated with the weapon).
* units enchanted with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Immolation](#Immolation)** or possessing the [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](#Immolation) ability (a hybrid [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") that is *triggered* as a **Touch Attack** but otherwise does not conform to the same mechanics).

Each of these abilities provides the unit with a **Touch Attack** that delivers one specific kind of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"). The type of damage delivered by the attack determines its effect, and it can be quite different from one **Touch Attack** to the next.

The strength of a **Touch Attack**
is indicated in a unit's details panel as the value of its relevant
touch ability. For example, a unit's details panel may list "[Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") **-4**", "[Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") **15**", and so forth. This value can have different meanings depending on the type of damage delivered by the attack. For example, "[Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") **-4**" inflicts a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-4 Resistance** penalty on the target. To understand how these values work, it is best to read the article explaining the relevant ability.

The strength of a unit's **Touch Attack**
will likely stay the same throughout the unit's existence, and normally
cannot be modified by any spells or effects. Again, specific types of
touch-related abilities may behave differently from others, so please
read the relevant ability articles for more information.

## Concept

In *Master of Magic*, the vast majority of units rely on, or are at least capable of, a physical attack against any target. This is called a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), where one unit assaults the other using any physical weaponry - whether actual weapons, claws, teeth, and so forth.

In addition, many of these weapons will work not just by the
force of physical impact with the target, but through additional magical
effects that can cause various types of harm. The very contact between
the weapon and its target delivers this effect, so it occurs only during
the heat of close combat, when blows are being exchanged by the unit
and its target.

**Touch Attacks**, as they are called, are therefore more of a special property of an ordinary attack, rather than being a "true" [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack"). In all cases, the effect of a **Touch Attack** is delivered at the same time as another [type of damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") is being dealt - with both striking the target simultaneously. Perhaps the clearest example of this would be [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch"), where poison is assumed to be injected into the target at the same time as it is hit by the attacker's weapons.

Of course, since the **Touch Attacker**
must usually get into physical contact with the enemy in order to
deliver the effect, it is also often exposed to the target's
retaliation. A [Giant Spider](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Giant_Spiders "Giant Spiders")
attempting to bite an armed man would have to expose itself to that
man's weapon, at the same time as it is going for that venomous bite.
Thus, **Touch Attacks** are seen as tactically inferior to [Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), [Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), or [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), which occur before physical contact is achieved, and may kill or weaken the target, reducing the risk of retaliation. **Touch Attacks**, however, are far more common than any of those - and units possessing a **Touch Attack** are still very dangerous opponents, assuming a tactical advantage against them cannot be secured.

## Effect

The effect of a **Touch Attack** depends entirely on the type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") that it delivers. All **Touch Attacks** deliver one damage type, and the exact type is determined by the [Unit Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Ability "Unit Ability") that gave the unit this kind of attack.

Unlike a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), **Touch Attacks** are delivered on a per-figure basis, that is, each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit will execute it exactly once, affecting only a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** of the target unit (although resulting actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** can still roll over to multiple enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**). This also means that [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** will deliver their **Touch Attacks** as many times as they have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. In return, however, their **Touch Attack** grows weaker in comparison if they start losing [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**.

Each of these [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") behaves differently than the others, although they all share the common concept of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** being used to protect against them.

:   *Main article: [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage")*

[Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") is by far the most commonly delivered by **Touch Attacks**, forcing the target to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** or suffer [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage Points**.

[Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") is also fairly common, can heal the attacker while hurting the target, and can raise the target as an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead").

[Stoning Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Damage "Stoning Damage") and [Dispel Evil Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil_Damage "Dispel Evil Damage") are less common. They force the target to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resist** or die - often while it is suffering from severe penalties to [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **Resistance**.

[Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") and [Disintegration Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disintegration_Damage "Disintegration Damage") only appear on **Touch Attacks** as a result of [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"), and are thus only available to [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

### Melee Touch Attacks

When associated with [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), **Touch Attacks** are executed at or near the very end of the Melee Attack sequence - simultaneously with the delivery of [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). This means that the effects of a **Touch Attack** on its target (particularly killing off enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**)
will not have any impact on the current combat round. It will not
diminish the strength of the opponent's retaliation, and thus does not
reduce the risk to the **Touch Attacker**, like other [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") often do.

**Touch Attacks** may be executed by either or both of the participants during a Melee Attack. Normally, all Melee Damage and **Touch Attacks** are delivered simultaneously: The game first calculates the effect of these, and only then (once the calculations are done for *both* combatants) does it apply the damage to each unit and kills off [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** as appropriate.

### Special Touch Attacks

Apart from [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") [First Strike](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/First_Strike "First Strike") and [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation"), there are only three ways for a **Touch Attack** to resolve before [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is dealt to the unit with the attack. These are actually all attached to *other* [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") types:

* enchanting Non-Fantastic Units possessing the [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") ability with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** to get an additional [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") ([Manticores](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Manticores "Manticores"), [Nightblades](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nightblades "Nightblades"), [Wyvern Riders](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wyvern_Riders "Wyvern Riders"), or [Tumu](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tumu "Tumu")). In this case, however unlikely, the [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") **will** be executed along with the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, and can significantly increase its effectivenes, especially in the case of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** (such as [Nightblades](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nightblades "Nightblades") with their [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **6**).
* equipping [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") with **Touch Attack** [Item Powers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") on [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") who have innate [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") ([Bahgtru](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bahgtru "Bahgtru"), [Gunther](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gunther "Gunther"), and [Shalla](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shalla "Shalla")). In these situations, the **Touch Attack** also gets delivered by these Heroes' [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**. Although still only affecting a single enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, an [Axe](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe") containing multiple **Touch Attack** powers will deliver all of those simultaneously, and may be quite capable of destroying multiple enemy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** before they get a chance to retaliate.
* the [Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn") delivers its [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") every time it makes its [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") (but only once per Gaze Attack phase, not once for each of its 3 such attacks).

### Ranged Touch Attacks

Some **Touch Attack** abilities can also be [enchanted](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting") onto [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") [Ranged Weapons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). This will result in their effects being applied simultaneously with dealing [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"), but in turn *will not work in Melee* combat.

On the other hand, [Demon Lords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord") and [Ravashack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ravashack "Ravashack") can execute their innate [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") ability *on both* their [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks** by default.

## Immunities

Nearly all **Touch Attacks** performed against a target possessing [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") are completely ineffective. The only exception is [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch"), against which no spells confer direct protection.

On the other hand, since all of these attacks (including [Poison](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage")) can be [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **resisted**, having a high [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** score can render units completely impervious to all but the strongest **Touch Attacks**. In particular, the [Charmed](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charmed "Charmed") [Charmed](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charmed "Charmed") [Hero Ability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero_Ability "Hero Ability") will make [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") possessing it practically immune to all **Touch Attacks**. A more comprehensive list of Resistance Modifiers can be found in the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Resistance_Modifiers "Resistance") article.

There are also [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") conferring various immunities, that are effective against **Touch Attacks**, as noted in the table above. Spells that confer increased [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** against certain Realms of magic are also represented by their icons. Among these, of particular importance are [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")** ([Elemental Armor](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_Armor "Elemental Armor")) and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")** ([Righteousness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Righteousness "Righteousness")), as they grant high enough [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** bonuses to generally make the target immune to their affected Realms' **Touch Attacks**.

## Disabling Touch Attacks

It needs to be mentioned that as a result of the duality of **Touch Attacks** (that is, them being dependent on a *delivering* [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type")), it may be possible to completely *disable* a unit's **Touch Attack**. This can be achieved if it is possible to reduce the magnitude of the *delivering* attack's strength to **0** or lower. When this happens, the *delivering* attack is never executed, which results in the **Touch Attack** itself never being executed either.

## Comparison with Gaze Attacks

:   *See also: [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")*

**Gaze Attacks** have as many differences from **Touch Attacks**
as they have similarities to them. They are included here as reference
mainly because of them being the only other way for units to deliver
some of the [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") types generally associated with **Touch Attacks**.

**Gaze Attacks** have their own, special placement in the [Melee Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Attack_Sequence "Melee Attack"), and are the only other [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") usable while [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") and [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") also use the same [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") as [Death (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_(Item_Power) "Death (Item Power)") [Death Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_(Item_Power) "Death (Item Power)") and [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") respectively.

The main difference between the two attack types lies in the way they are resolved when dealt or suffered by [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**. **Touch Attacks** deliver their effect once for every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** with the ability, and affect the target once for each of these. In contrast, **Gaze Attacks** are only executed once (regardless of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** with the ability), but in turn are applied separately to *every* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the target unit. In other words, the amount of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls made against the attack will be the amount of attacking [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** for a **Touch Attack**, and the amount of target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** for a **Gaze Attack**.

#### Example 1

:   Take a [Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk") for example. This creature possesses the [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") **-1** ability. It is also a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure Unit**. Lets say it is fighting a unit of [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Recruit** [High Elf Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Elf_Spearmen "High Elf Spearmen") ([Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8** figures with Resistance [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **6**).

:   Whenever the Basilisk attacks or counterattacks, it will execute its [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze"). Since this is a **Gaze Attack**,
    it will get executed once for each Melee Attack or Couter Attack that
    the Basilisk makes. However, it will apply separately to each and *every* [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the Spearmen unit, forcing it to make 8 individual [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls (since High Elf Spearmen have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8** figures). With the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-1** modifier, each Spearman has a 50% chance to survive, and thus the Basilisk will kill, on average, **4** Spearmen with its Gaze Attack.

:   So, what if the Basilisk had the [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") **-1** ability instead? As a **Touch Attack**, this would be executed once for each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** with the ability. With the Basilisk being a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figure Unit**,
    this would mean once for each Melee or Counter Attack, just like above.
    However, this time, the ability would only apply once. Thus, only one [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll would be required of the Spearmen, with the Basilisk being able to kill at most **1** of them (with a 50% chance) with its Special Attack.

#### Example 2

:   [Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices") are the only creatures possessing a [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") [Stoning Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Touch "Stoning Touch") ability. Their unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4** figures, and their **Touch Attack** carries a **-3** [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **Resistance** penalty. Lets say they are trying to take out a [Behemoth](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Behemoth "Behemoth") (a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") figure with Resistance [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**).

:   When they attack the Behemoth, the Cockatrices will execute four individual **Touch Attacks** on it, since they have [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4** figures. With [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-3**,
    the Behemoth has 70% chance to survive each Touch, with a total of (0.7
    \* 0.7 \* 0.7 \* 0.7 \* 100% = ) 24.01% chance to survive all four. That
    is, about 3 out of 4 times (75.99%), the Behemoth will turn to stone.

:   If the Cockatrices had [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") [Stoning Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stoning_Gaze "Stoning Gaze") **-3**
    instead, the Behemoth's chances of survival would be the other way
    around. In this case, the Cockatrices would only execute one Gaze
    Attack. Because the Behemoth is a single [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") figure, it would only have to make one successful [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** check. Even with a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance#Spell_Save_Penalties "Resistance") **-3** penalty, it would still have a 70% chance of *not* turning to stone.

As a general rule of thumb, this makes **Touch Attacks** more useful for [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**, whereas this does not matter for **Gaze Attacks**. At the same time, **Touch Attacks** are much more deadly against units with low [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** counts, while Gaze Attacks excel versus high [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** count units.

## Immolation

:   *See also: [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation")*
:   *See also: [Immolation (Spell)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)")*

**Immolation** is a type of hybrid [Special Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack")
that combines mechanics in such a way that makes it disctinctively
different from all other types of Special Attacks. It is unique in its
category, and can be conferred onto any unit using the [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)")**. The [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Bat](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Bat "Doom Bat")** also possesses an innate [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") **Immolation** ability.

With respect to its triggering, **Immolation** behaves exactly like a **Touch Attack**.
Throughout this wiki, it is often referred to as such for this reason.
It is triggered each and every time the unit possessing it executes any
other attack type. It also "stacks" with other **Touch Attacks**, just like they do with each other (i.e. if a unit has more than one **Touch Attack** they can all be used simultaneously). **Immolation** can be affixed to both [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee**, [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack")[Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack")[Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged**, and [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack")[Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath")[Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack")**Special Attacks**; and it can be delivered multiple times during the [Melee Sequence](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack#Melee_Attack_Sequence "Melee Attack").

With respect to how it affects its target, **Immolation** behaves like a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack").
Rather than being delivered once for every figure with the ability, it
is only triggered once, and instead affects each target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** individually. In other words, when performed, it is unleashed on every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** of the opposing unit separately, just like a Gaze Attack, independent of the amount of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures** in the initiating unit. This naturally makes it more effective against units with high [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** counts.

Finally, with respect to its actual effect, **Immolation** delivers conventional [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"). On this wiki, it may also be described as delivering [Immolation Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_Damage "Immolation Damage"), but this is equivalent to dealing Fire Damage to every [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** of the target unit separately. Much like [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath** and [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** attacks, this involves both a series of [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** Rolls and a series of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** Rolls. The strength of the **Immolation** ability is a static 4 (regerdless of whether it is innate or enchanted), thus it will always make 4 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against each target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**.

Unlike other **Touch** or Gaze Attacks, **Immolation** is not [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistable**, but is instead defended against as regular [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage"). Each target [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** gets to make as many rolls [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** their respective damage as they have [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score. Units with a Defense of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **4** or higher thus stand a good chance of shrugging off the majority of the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, and will likely only take a few stray hits. On the other hand, units with [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **3** or lower, especially [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** (such as early game [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit")) can be decimated by this ability.

#### Example

:   Let's extend the above example with the [Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk") and the [High Elf Spearmen](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Elf_Spearmen "High Elf Spearmen"). Lets say the Basilisk is also enchanted with **[Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation_(Spell) "Immolation (Spell)")**.

:   When the Basilisk executes its Gaze Attack, it will also, at the same time, hit the Spearmen with **Immolation**. Since the two attacks occur simultaneously, the Spearmen will be considered as having [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8** figures for both abilities, regardless of how many they are likely to lose to each attack individually. Thus, the **Immolation** attack will make 4 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against all 8 Spearmen [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**. For the sake of simplicity, lets assume that each Spearman's Defense score of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2** reduces the amount of rolls by 2.

:   This leaves 2 [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** rolls against each of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8** figures, or a total of 16 rolls. This will do, on average, (16 \* 0.3 = ) [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **4.8**. With the Spearmen having [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** each, **Immolation** will likely kill 4 or 5 of their [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**.
    Coupled with the 4 average kills of the Gaze Attack (see above), this
    will generally result in there being no Spearman left standing by the
    time the Basilisk would close in to exchange Melee Damage.
