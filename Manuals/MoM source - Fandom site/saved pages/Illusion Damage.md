**Illusion Damage** (sometimes called **Illusory Damage** or **Illusionary Damage**) is a type of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") that can be delivered by various units *in tandem with* their attacks. An attack delivering **Illusion Damage** lowers the target's Defense to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** for the purposes of blocking that attack. Thus, any attack containing an **Illusion Damage** component will inflict more harm on its target, on average.

**Illusion Damage** can be difficult to understand as a separate [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") since it inflicts *no actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** on the target*. An attack delivering **Illusion Damage** must deliver it in tandem with other damage types, commonly just [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") or [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage"), as these will be the components that actually hurt the target. **Illusion Damage** only makes it easier to inflict that damage, by reducing the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score temporarily. Once the attack is over, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score returns to normal.

**Illusion Damage** is found as a component in the attacks of two [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") and one [Champion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Champion "Champion"), thanks to their innate [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") ability. Furthermore, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may be equipped with "[Phantasmal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantasmal_(Item_Power) "Phantasmal (Item Power)")" weapons; These will add an **Illusion Damage** component to one of the Hero's attacks.

## Effect

**Illusion Damage** is responsible for negating the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score temporarily, making it an irrelevant factor in damage calculations. **Illusion Damage** delivers no actual damage on its own, relying on other components of the attack to do so. The target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** is restored once the attack is concluded.

### No Defense

Whenever an attack containing an **Illusion Damage** component strikes any target on the battlefield, the target temporarily loses its ability to defend against this attack.

In technical terms, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is reduced to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** during the resolution of the **Illusion Damage** attack, and is thus extremely vulnerable to any other [Damage Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") that the same attack also delivers.

:   For example, lets take a target unit with [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **7 Defense**. Normally, this would allow it to make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **7 Defense Rolls** against any incoming [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), potentially blocking a fair amount of the incoming [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage"). However if the same attack includes an **Illusion Damage** component, the target cannot make any [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **Defense rolls** against it, since its Defense is [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** for the duration of resolving the attack. Thus, the target will suffer much more of the incoming [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") (on average) from this enhanced attack.

The same principle would apply when using **Illusionary** [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), which deliver [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") and **Illusion Damage** together. The target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is reduced to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** by the **Illusion Damage**, allowing the [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") (or [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage")) to get through unimpeded.

The target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") is reduced to *exactly* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**, regardless of how high it was before struck by **Illusion Damage**. Therefore, the higher the target's original [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score, the more effective the **Illusion Damage** is against that target. Dropping a unit's Defense from [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**
is a significant penalty, and can potentially make a tough opponent
easily defeatable. On the other hand, reducing a unit's Defense from [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **2** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** is not as impressive - although if the attacker is also quite weak, every little bit helps.

**The reduction in the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense")**Defense **applies only for the duration of the attack.** Therefore, as explained below, an attack *must* deliver **Illusion Damage** in tandem with at least some amount of [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") in order to exploit the target's reduced [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**. An attack that delivers *only* **Illusion Damage** would be useless: it lowers the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score, and then does nothing to exploit it before finally removing the penalty.

### No Actual Damage

**Illusion Damage** is
distinct from most other damage types in that it delivers no actual
damage on its own. It is responsible solely for reducing the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rating - whereupon a [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") component needs to actually *hurt the target*. Therefore, all attacks delivering **Illusion Damage** must deliver it in tandem with [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") (which causes [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **physical harm** to the target) in order to exploit the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** penalty!

Thus, it should not be surprising that **Illusion Damage** is always added to a unit's *existing* attacks. Most commonly, it is added directly to the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"): Such an attack would deliver the **Illusion Damage** first, and then inflict [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") on the target to exploit its reduced Defense.

**Illusion Damage** is added in the same way to a unit's [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") (if it possesses one), which would then deliver **Illusion Damage** followed by [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage"). While **Illusion Damage** technically also works with [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown** and [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Breath Attacks**, in practice, it is only possible to achieve for [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**.

The "secondary" [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") delivered by the attack has to be [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") - any damage type which forces the target to make [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls against it. Otherwise, the Defense reduction would not really help. For example, a hypothetical attack delivering **Illusion Damage** along with [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") does not benefit, since [Death Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Damage "Death Damage") forces the target to make [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** rolls - not [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls. Since it doesn't exploit the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** penalty from **Illusion Damage**, the attack is not overall any more effective.

## Immunities

Naturally, **Illusion Damage** has no effect on units whose [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score is already [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0** - such as [Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors"), [Phantom Beasts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Beast "Phantom Beast"), or any unit affected by the [Berserk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Berserk "Berserk") spell. These units already cannot make any [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls, so **Illusion Damage** is redundant against them.

Furthermore, if a target possesses [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"), it will negate the **Illusion Damage** component of any incoming attack. When such an attack is made against an Illusions-Immune target, the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** score will not be reduced. The attack can still hurt that target, but has to get past its armor first as normal.

It's also important to note that the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** penalty from **Illusion Damage** is applied *after* the target's other immunities are triggered (see the [section](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense#Order_of_Operations_for_Resolving_Defense_and_Immunities "Defense")
in the Defense article for a detailed list). Thus, practically all
immunities that would normally apply to an attack (including [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity")) will be superseded by the **Illusion Damage** component unless the target also possesses [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Immunity to Illusions](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"). The only [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") bonus that is not overridden by **Illusion Damage** is that granted by [City Walls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City_Walls "City Walls") (whether intact or damaged).

### Example

:   [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks** are a prime example for this. These attacks normally hurt their target by inflicting [Ranged Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Damage "Ranged Damage") on it. However, when a Ranged Missile Attack is used against a target with [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity"), the immunity raises the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** - and will thus completely block all but the strongest [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attacks**.

:   Now, if an **Illusion Damage** component was added to the same [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack**, it would also reduce the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**. The question would then be: which effect is applied first? The answer is: the immunity, every time. Thus, [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") is of little help against a marksman shooting Illusionary projectiles.

## Sources of Illusion Damage

**Illusion Damage** is fairly rare, and found mainly as a component in the attacks of specific units. In this case, its source is the [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") ability, which adds **Illusion Damage** to each of a unit's attacks. In addition, [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") may equip themselves with [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") possessing the "[Phantasmal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantasmal_(Item_Power) "Phantasmal (Item Power)")" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"), which adds **Illusion Damage** to one of the Hero's attacks.

### Units with Illusion

:   *Main article: [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion")*

The [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") ability adds an **Illusion Damage** component to the unit's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"); and [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), if any.

The benefit of **Illusion Damage**
depends greatly on the unit's existing attacks types and their
strength, with stronger attackers generally benefiting more from **Illusion Damage** - though the weakest Illusionary units are still quite powerful thanks to it.

Refer to the article on [Illusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion") for a full list of all units possessing this ability.

### Phantasmal Weapons

:   *Main article: [Phantasmal (Item Power)](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantasmal_(Item_Power) "Phantasmal (Item Power)")*

Another source of Illusion Damage comes from weapons imbued with the "Phantasmal" [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power"). This enchantment can be placed only on [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")' weapon slot equipment ([Swords](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sword "Sword"), [Maces](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mace "Mace"), [Axes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Axe "Axe"), [Bows](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bow "Bow"), [Wands](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wand "Wand"), and [Staves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Staff "Staff")).

When the enchanted item is equipped on a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), it adds an **Illusion Damage** component to the attack associated with the item. A Sword or Mace will add an Illusion Damage component to the Hero's [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"). An Axe will, in addition, convey the effect to [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** (for [Bahgtru](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bahgtru "Bahgtru"), [Gunther](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gunther "Gunther"), and [Shalla](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shalla "Shalla")). A Bow, Wand, or Staff will only apply the component to the Hero's [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack").

During the custom [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting") process, the "Phantasmal" bonus is only available to [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") possessing at least [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**6 Sorcery** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). Weapons containing this bonus seldom appear as rewards for defeating [Encounters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter "Encounter") due to the spellbook restrictions on [these particular items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantasmal_(Item_Power)#Acquisition "Phantasmal (Item Power)").

Note that, as explained above, "[Phantasmal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantasmal_(Item_Power) "Phantasmal (Item Power)")" does not change the actual strength of the attack, nor the [types of damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") it delivers. The only difference is that the attack will now also deliver **Illusion Damage** to nullify the target's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score as described above.

The [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")'s attacks will only deliver **Illusion Damage** while the Hero is holding the enchanted item. If the item is removed or lost, the Hero's attacks no longer deliver **Illusion Damage**.

### Psionic Blast Spell

The [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Sorcery** spell [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Psionic Blast](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Psionic_Blast "Psionic Blast")** can be seen as a [Magical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Damage "Magical Damage") attack with an **Illusion Damage** component. It will ignore the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** of units that have neither [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") or [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"). Units with [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") will make their defense rolls as usual, while units with [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") are completely immune to the spell.
