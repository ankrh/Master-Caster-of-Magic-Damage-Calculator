**Chaos Channels** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Magic** realm. It may only be cast on a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") on the overland map, for the base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**. **Chaos Channels** imbues the target unit with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** energy, turning it into a [Fantastic Creature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. The unit also receives one permanent bonus, selected at random. It can be a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+6** bonus, a [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") of [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**2**, or a new [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **2** ability. A unit affected by **Chaos Channels** may not be targeted by the spell again.

**Chaos Channels** is a very unusual [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), as it behaves more like an [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell"): it has no [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"),
and may not be dispelled after being successfully cast. Its effects,
however, last as long as the targeted unit remains in the game.

## Effects

**Chaos Channels** mutates a friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") into a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**, and gives the unit a bonus to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense"), *or* the ability to [Fly](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying"), *or* the ability to [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath").

### Fantastic Creature of Chaos

When a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") is successfully affected by **Chaos Channels**, it immediately becomes a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") belonging to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos Realm**. Strangely, the game itself treats this unit as a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") some of the time, while treating it as a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") the rest of the time.

#### Fantastic Unit Properties

The **Chaos Channeled** unit is now permanently associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** realm. As a result, it will benefit from all spells and effects that only apply to [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** creatures, such as [Chaos Surge](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Surge "Chaos Surge") and [Star Fires](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Star_Fires "Star Fires"). It will also benefit from the aura projected by [Chaos Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node"), which makes it stronger when fighting in the vicinity of such nodes.

As a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), this unit may not be targeted by the **Chaos Channels** spell a second time - the game will refuse this, claiming that Fantastic Units are not valid targets. The [Lycanthropy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lycanthropy "Lycanthropy") spell will also refuse to target this unit, though the [Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels") spell *does work* as normal.

During combat, the unit is considered a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") for all intents and purposes. It cannot be targeted by spells that only target [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), but *can* be targeted by spells that only target [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit").

Furthermore, this unit will not gain any [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") from participating in a victorious battle (1.31 only), 1.40 and above Chaos channel units will gain combat experience.

#### Normal Unit Properties

The unit retains some of its [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") properties after the transformation.

Chaos-channeled units are still effective at quelling unrest (1 rebel per 2 garrisoned troops) while inside a city.

It is also still possible to target this unit with some Normal-Unit-Only spells, such as [Flame Blade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flame_Blade "Flame Blade") or [Holy Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Weapon "Holy Weapon")
- but only if these spells are cast on the unit while on the overland
map. The spells will behave normally, and will carry over into combat as
normal. [Holy Arms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Arms "Holy Arms") spell also affects the chaos channeled units as normal.

The chaos-channeled unit also retains its current [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level"),
as well as any bonuses bestowed by that level. Although it no longer
gains any further Experience from combat (see above), the unit will
still get [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **1 Experience Point** per overland turn, and *will*
go up in Experience levels as normal, gaining bonuses as appropriate.
The unit's icon on the overland map will not indicate its level as it
would for other [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

[Armsmaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armsmaster "Armsmaster") heroes may continue to train chaos-channeled units.

For their own part, it is a bad idea in practice to enchant heroes with **Chaos Channels**. Heroes rely heavily on combat experience to achieve the higher levels, and being limited to [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **1** per turn effectively halts their advancement.

### Additional Bonuses

When **Chaos Channels** is
cast on a unit, it will randomly receive one of three possible permanent
bonuses that improve its abilities in one way or another.

#### Option #1: Demon-Skin Armor

If this option is selected, the unit receives a permanent [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") bonus. The size of this bonus, however, is a point of contention.

Both the game's manual and in-game tooltips explain that Demon-Skin Armor should give the unit a Defense bonus of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+2**. However, in actuality the unit receives [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+6** (respectively [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3** in the [unofficial 1.40 Insecticide patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")).

#### Option #2: Fiery Breath

If this option is selected, the unit receives an innate ability of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") at strength [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **2**.

Whenever this unit performs a deliberate [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") against any enemy, it will first deliver a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") with a strength of [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **2** per each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **live figure** in the unit. The resulting [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** from this attack is applied *before* [Melee Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Damage "Melee Damage") is calculated, so any enemy figures killed by the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") will not get to deal any Melee Damage during their [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

As a [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), this ability also allows the unit to attack [Flying](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying") creatures, even if it does not possess the Flying ability itself.

This attack delivers [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"), so targets with [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") and/or [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") will suffer no damage from it.
*(technically, their [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score is raised to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** for purposes of blocking the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath"), meaning that they are extremely unlikely to suffer any damage).*

[Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") only works when the unit attacks an enemy, and not when [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack").

**Note:**  If the unit already has a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"), or [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack"), **Chaos Channels** will never pick this option.

Also Note: If the unit has the thrown ability and fire breath is
chosen, fire breath will replace the thrown ability. Since chaos
channels is permanent, there is no way to undo this and so the unit will
lose thrown permanently.

#### Option #3: Demon Wings

If this option is selected, the unit's [Movement Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Type "Movement Type") and [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") are changed to [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**2**.

The unit may now move over any overland tile (i.e. both Land and [Ocean](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ocean "Ocean") tiles). It spends exactly 1 Movement Point per tile moved, regardless of the terrain. The unit's overland Sight Range is increased to 2 tiles, if it is not already that high.

During combat, the unit may also move freely on the battlefield at the cost of 1 Movement Point per tile. This unit is *usually* treated just like any other [Flying](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying") unit - but due to a bug this may not always be true, with potentially mind-boggling side-effects. See also "**Known Bugs**", below.

**Note:**  If the unit already has the *innate* ability to [Fly](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying") at any speed, **Chaos Channels** will never pick this option. **Chaos Channels** will also never pick this option for a [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Sailing** unit. However, if the unit is getting a [Flying](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying") ability from a spell (such as "[Flight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flight "Flight")"), this option is still valid - and would render the other spell redundant.

### Random Choice Mechanism

When casting **Chaos Channels** on a unit, its chances of getting any of the *valid* bonuses are equal.

Therefore, if all three bonuses are valid for the unit, each has a
33% chance of being selected at random by the game. If only two options
are valid, each has a 50% chance of being selected.

If the unit has both [Flying](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying") ability and [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") of its own, **Chaos Channels** will bestow the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense")
bonus on that unit with 100% certainty, as it is the only remaining
option. Note that there are no situations where this bonus is invalid,
so this is the "default" option.

## Usage

**Chaos Channels** may only be cast on friendly [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). It cannot target [Fantastic Creatures](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Creature "Fantastic Creature"). The spell also cannot target any unit that has already been mutated by a **Chaos Channels** spell, so no unit may ever gain more than one of the benefits listed above during its lifetime.

If the desired target is in an overland army stack, click on that
stack to get a list of units inside it, and then select your desired
unit.

**Chaos Channels** may only be cast on the overland map, and has a base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **50**. Although normally considered a [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), and adding an icon to the unit's ability list that resembles a [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") icon in every respect, **Chaos Channels** does not behave like an enchantment. The mutation of the unit is entirely permanent, and is not held in place with magic.

This means that **Chaos Channels** may not be dispelled, may not be canceled by its caster, and does not require any [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"). The unit will remain "mutated" until it is destroyed.

As mentioned above, **Chaos Channels**
adds one of three different icons to the unit's abilities list,
indicating which bonus it received from the mutation. Though the icon
resembles a [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") icon (purple background), clicking on this icon does not allow canceling the **Chaos Channels**
effect. Note also that the unit does not receive any other sort of
visual indicator that it is affected, neither on the unit icon nor the
tactical display.

## Acquisition

As an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** spell, **Chaos Channels** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least one [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is almost never guaranteed.

Customized Wizards possessing [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**11**
Spellbooks at creation time may choose this spell as one of their
default spells before starting the game, in which case the spell will
already be researched and available for casting immediately on the first
turn.

Wizards who possess at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**8** Spellbooks, or Wizards with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**11** Spellbooks who did not select **Chaos Channels** as a guaranteed spell, will be able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") this spell at some point during their campaign. Wizards with fewer than [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**8** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to Research it. The chance for this spell to appear increases with the number of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or obtains during gameplay.

**Chaos Channels** has a base [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **400**.

With at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**1** Spellbook, the **Chaos Channels** spell *may be* acquired as a reward for winning encounters in creature [Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lair "Lair"), [Towers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower"), et cetera, or when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival wizard who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

Casting **Chaos Channels** on a unit has its advantages and its drawbacks, leading to very different strategies concerning its use.

On the one hand, this is a permanent benefit which you can give to any [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), which can potentially make them significantly more useful. Units can always benefit from extra defense, and a permanent [Flying](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying")
ability for a normally non-flying unit is a terrific bonus. The
mutation also works great when the casting wizard possesses spells that
increase the effectiveness of all [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** creatures (such as [Chaos Surge](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Surge "Chaos Surge")),
and so forth. The best part of course is that once the spell is cast,
it requires no upkeep - the affected unit is permanently better.

On the other hand, drawbacks include the permanent transformation of the unit into a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"),
and the fact that this spell cannot affect such units. Therefore,
wizards relying greatly on summoned creatures may not have many chances
to apply this spell. Furthermore, since several good [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**Chaos** [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") will only affect [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), **Chaos Channels** reduces the number of valid targets for such spells.

Furthermore, it is not possible to know beforehand which mutation
the target unit will receive. Therefore, you can end up with units
having the "wrong" kind of mutation (i.e. one that isn't as beneficial
as the one you'd hoped for), requiring repetitive production of [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") (or summoning of [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")) and casting the spell on them until the desired result occurs.

But in the case of the low-end, multi-figure units that dominate a
campaign's early stages, any outcome will drastically improve the
unit's performance. The "Demon Wings" are easy enough to appreciate,
but note that the two alternatives apply to the unit on a *per-figure* basis, such that an [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8-Fig** unit will issue eight separate [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fiery Breath 2** attacks if it receives the "Fiery Breath," or that each figure in line will wield the full [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") bonus of the "Demon-Skin Armor."

## Removing Chaos Channels

Normally, it is not possible to remove **Chaos Channels** from a unit after it has been cast on that unit. However, there is one exception to this: [Resurrecting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resurrection "Resurrection") a dead [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

When a [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") falls in battle, the game actually stores that Hero's data in memory, just as he was, *minus the Enchantments* affecting that Hero - including **Chaos Channels**.

When the [Resurrection](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resurrection "Resurrection") spell is cast, the Hero will return to life with most of his previous stats intact. Since **Chaos Channels** was removed from the Hero upon death, the resurrected Hero is "free" of the **Chaos Channels**
spell. So on one hand, the spell must be re-cast on this Hero to regain
its bonuses. However, this still allows you to "take back" a **Chaos Channels** spell that produced a less-desirable effect on that Hero. Most often, players will want their Heroes to get the [Flying](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying") ability, so if **Chaos Channels**
did not grow a pair of Demon Wings on the hero, you could get him
killed, resurrect him, and re-cast the spell (hoping it'll work this
time).

Granted, this may require a large amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** and time...

## Known Bugs

As of *Master of Magic v1.31*, the "Demon-Skin" and "Demon-Wings" bonuses bestowed by **Chaos Channels** are associated with two bizarre bugs. Both of these bugs can be very advantageous to **Chaos Channels**' caster, if they occur.

On the other hand, there is one apparently unintentional drawback to the "Fire Breath" bonus, if cast on a unit possessing a [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack").

### Extra Defense Bug

When **Chaos Channels** gives the unit "Demon-Skin Armor", it is *supposed* to bestow a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") bonus of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+2** according to the ingame tool-tip textes.

However when examining the enchanted unit's details panel by
right-clicking the unit on the overland map, it would seem that the unit
actually receives [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+3**.

More weirdly, examining the unit's details during combat shows an *even greater* bonus, which can easily reach [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+6**!
This may occur during any combat, regardless of what spells (if any)
affect the units or the battlefield, how many units there are, etcetera.

The unit actually receives this gigantic bonus. It confers a great advantage to the unit - especially [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure units** - well beyond the intended amount. Note that the [unofficial 1.40 Insecticide patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") fixes the bug that the defense bonus is applied twice in combat.

### Flying Ability Bug

The bug concerning "Demon Wings" is even more severe - a true
game-breaker than can potentially allow the player to win the game very
easily. It essentially makes the enchanted unit invincible.

Normally, [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**Walking** and [Water Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Water_Movement "Water Movement")**Swimming** units may not attack [Air Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Movement "Air Movement")**Flying** units. This is one of the essential rules of combat, and one of the reasons why [Flying](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flying "Flying") is such a useful quality. This normally applies also to units that receive the Flying ability thanks to **Chaos Channels**' "Demon Wings" bonus: they cannot be attacked by non-flying creatures.

However, the game becomes confused whenever a battle occurs in
which the Flying Demon-Winged unit is accompanied by other non-flying
units. In this case, enemy non-Flying creatures *can and will* attack the Demon-Winged unit!

This may sound as a serious disadvantage, but in fact the
opposite is true - because when enemy non-fliers attack the Demon-Winged
unit, they inflict *no damage on it whatsoever*. On the other hand, the Demon-Winged unit *does* inflict damage with its [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")!

The result is that this unit can simply fly forward of the line,
positioning itself closer to the enemies who will flock towards it and
attempt to kill it - but instead be slowly (or quickly!) killed
themselves while causing no damage to the flier. Note that this only
occurs when the Demon-Winged unit is [Counter Attacking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"); it will suffer damage as normal if it attempts to attack the non-flying enemy units.

From a technical perspective, this is caused by what was likely a
last minute addition to the game : After selecting a target to
attack, if multiple units of identical type are present, the AI will
change their target to the closest and/or most wounded one. However this
change of targets ignores all rules and assumes the units of the same
type are identical, which isn't the case when they are affected by a
spell individually. Later, the game correctly recognizes the attacker
has no attack type capable of attacking a flying unit, thus the attack
does no damage, but still triggers retaliation. A similar result can be
observed when a unit tries to shoot arrows at a Flying unit with Missile
Immunity from melee range : the attack will automatically get
switched to melee due to the presence of immunity, but due to flight, it
counts as invalid and deals no damage.

The 1.50 patch fixes this bug by removing the AI's ability to
override the carefully chosen valid target after the fact for reasons
the default target selection process already considered anyway.

Note that Flying vs. Flying combat is handled normally, so the
Demon-Winged unit is only invincible when the enemy possesses no other
flying units (at least, none that can kill it).

There may be further complications resulting from this effect. If you know of any, please add them here for record-keeping.

### Fire Breath Bug

If **Chaos Channels** is cast on a unit that has a [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**, and picks the "Fire Breath" bonus to give to this unit, the unit will gain [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath** but will lose its Thrown Attack!

This can be a problem, because generally a Thrown Attack is
significantly better than a Fire Breath attack for several reasons. The
first reason is that some creatures have [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity"),
allowing them to shrug off damage from Fire Breath, while no unit is
immune to a Thrown Attack. The second reason is that many spells will
increase the strength of a Thrown Attack, while very few will increase
the strength of a Fire Breath attack.

Therefore it is often better to either avoid casting **Chaos Channels** on units possessing a [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), or simply dismiss those that get [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") as a result of the mutation. Naturally, some players simply ignore this bug, if it doesn't affect their strategy too badly.

### No Experience in Combat Bug

**Chaos Channeled** units do not gain experience in battle. They still gain [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience** for each passed overland turn, especially when they are accompanied by an [Armsmaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armsmaster "Armsmaster") hero.

### Normal Unit Spells Bug

**Chaos Channeled** units can be a valid target for normal unit spells in battle, but not on overland map. The [unofficial 1.40 Insecticide patch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide") will forbid casting normal unit spells on **Chaos Channeled** unit, but will not remove already existing normal unit spells from them.
