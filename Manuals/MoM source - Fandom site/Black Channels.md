**Black Channels** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **100** it may be cast on a friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), or [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") on the overland map to boost all of its combat attributes, including a rare increase to its maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**. If the unit was not Undead before, it will now permanently become one. That is, this property will remain even if **Black Channels** is removed from the unit.

## Effects

**Black Channels** turns a regular, living [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") into a vile [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
creature, but also bestows several powerful bonuses on that unit. This
transformation is dramatic in itself, carrying both added bonuses *and penalties*, and is entirely permanent. However, **Black Channels** can also be used to simply empower a unit that is already Undead, although it may not be cast on native [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit").

### Undeath

:   *Main article: [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")*

Before **Black Channels**' temporary bonuses are considered, it is important to explain its permanent effect – turning a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") into an [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
unit. This is an attribute that is neither an ability nor an
enchantment. It could best be described as a curse, but it's not exactly
that either – it can not be removed by any means, short of the complete
destruction of the unit. Naturally, the transformation only occurs when
**Black Channels** is cast for the first time. Afterwards, any subsequent castings can only re-apply the temporary effects.

[Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") require no sustenance, and are unquestioningly loyal. Normal Units and Heroes who become Undead no longer have an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") in either [Food](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Food "Food")**Food** or [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **Gold**. While persistent magic spells cast on them still need to be maintained, including **Black Channels**, the unit itself requires no [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** Upkeep either. If **Black Channels** is removed or dispelled, the unit will have no Upkeep Cost at all!

Undead [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heroes "Heroes") and [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Units "Normal Units") retain all of the abilities and numeric attributes they had in life, including any benefits they have already gained from [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**. However, they stop accumulating any more [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience Points**, and can thus gain no more [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Levels**. They are now considered to be [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"),
and will no longer be affected by spells and abilities that only work
on Normal Units or Heroes, either harmful, or beneficial. On the other
hand, effects that apply to- or against [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures will all treat them as such. Certain magics of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Realm are also extra effective against these units.

[Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") also gain some of the signature immunities of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**
units, although it should be noted that this feature is bugged in the
official game, and most of these will only actually apply while **Black Channels**
is still in effect. As such, they are listed among the spell's other
effects instead. The one that does get added without the enchantment is [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity"), which renders Undead units impervious to many spells and [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Realm.

Being [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") does have one significant drawback. It disables [natural healing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points#Natural_Healing "Damage Points"), and nullifies the effect of most healing spells as well. Undead can only restore lost [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** in one of two ways: by dealing [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage"), or by possessing the [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") ability. Heroes have an easier time here, as both options are available to them by finding and equipping the right [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), and spellcasters can also utilize [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** if their controller knows this spell. Normal Units however, can only gain access to the latter, and even that requires the [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") and expensive [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** spell [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration_(Spell) "Regeneration (Spell)")**. Unless, of course, they are [Trolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Trolls "Trolls"), in which case they likely already have this ability.

There are also two inconsistencies in the latest official version
of the game that are worth noting here. The first one is that while [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** may not be cast on Undead, **Black Channels** *can* be used on a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** unit. In this case, the unit will keep its Realm association, and is still considered to be a [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** creature, although *all* other effects of also being Undead do apply. In addition, the [Armsmaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armsmaster "Armsmaster") [Armsmaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armsmaster "Armsmaster") Hero ability continues to grant [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**
to Undead Normal Units, even though they are prevented from gaining any
from all other sources – this is one of the penalties for their
transformation.

### Melee Strength Bonus

While **Black Channels** is affecting a unit, the strength that unit's [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") is increased by [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+2**, although only if it had such an attack to begin with. This grants the unit two extra dice to use on its [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") in melee – including [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") – improving both its maximum "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** potential as well as its average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output. Because this bonus applies on a per-[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** basis, it naturally benefits [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units** more than [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure** ones.

### Ranged Attack Strength Bonus

While **Black Channels** is affecting a unit, that unit's Ranged [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") is improved by **+1**. It gets one extra die on its [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll"), increasing both its maximum and average "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output. That is, if the unit has any kind of [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") in the first place. This effect is not limited to long-range ([Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Missile**, [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Magical**, or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Boulder**) attacks, however; it also applies to short-range abilities – [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath Attacks**, and even the [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") component of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack") are also increased. Similarly to the [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength") bonus, this one is also more beneficial for [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**, as a single attack action allows each of their individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** to perform a separate attack.

### Defense Bonus

**Black Channels** grants its target a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") bonus of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1** as long as it remains in effect. This improves both the maximum- and average [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") reduction of the unit by allowing it to use one more die on its [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll"). This can be particularly useful against [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** opponents, as it will apply against each individual [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s attack.

### Resistance Bonus

While **Black Channels** is affecting a unit, that unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") score is increased by [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1**. This makes it easier for the target to avoid the effects of [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") and [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") attacks.

### Hit Points Bonus

**Black Channels** is one of the very few effects in the game that can increase a unit's maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points"), also known as its *[Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure***
value. When cast it will, in practice, also "heal" the unit by this
amount, since this improvement is applied without any adjustment to the [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") recorded for the unit. That is, its current health is also increased by the same amount as its maximum: [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** for each of its active [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**. Of course, this again makes **Black Channels** more beneficial the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** its target has.

Having more [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** means that each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit can take more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**
before being destroyed, thereby increasing the unit's survivability.
However, it should be noted that in the latest official version of the
game, there is a peculiar bug associated with the removal of **Black Channels** because of this benefit. This is explained in more detail [below](#Known_Bugs).

### Death Creature Immunities

While under the effect of **Black Channels**, the enchanted unit gains the four signature immunities of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures. Although the game documentation claims that these are granted by the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") attribute, this is not actually the case in the latest official version, in which that only bestows [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity"). As long as **Black Channels** remains active however, its target does gain the benefits of [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity"), [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"), and [Poison Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Immunity "Poison Immunity") [Poison Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Immunity "Poison Immunity") as well.

The first of these renders the unit nearly impervious to [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage"), a [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type") dealt by the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Ice Bolt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Bolt "Ice Bolt")** and [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Ice Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Storm "Ice Storm")** spells. The unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") is considered to be [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **50** against them which, in practice, means that it is extremely unlikely to ever take any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** from either. The second immunity not only lets the **Black Channeled** unit see through [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility"), but also prevents it from being targeted by [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") from the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), and lets it use its full [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** value against any [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") directed at it. Finally, the unit will never take any [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage") either, as it will automatically pass all [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") rolls it ever has to make against it.

It may be worth noting that starting with the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50"), [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") Undead do correctly gain these immunities as well, and will thus retain them even if **Black Channels** is removed.

## Usage

**Black Channels** may be cast only on the overland map, for the basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **100**. If the casting is successful, the game will prompt for a friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), or [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
unit to be selected as the spell's target. On the overland map, this
typically requires clicking on the army stack that contains the unit
which, in turn, brings up a list view of all units in that stack,
allowing for the target to be picked individually. Only units not
already under the effect of **Black Channels** may be chosen. Once the spell is cast, a glowing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[purple](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** outline appears around the unit (unless it is overridden by a stronger enchantment); and **Black Channels**, along with its icon, will be displayed in the *unit statistics window* when inspecting this unit.

**Black Channels** has an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1**
that must be paid at the beginning of every turn to keep the spell in
effect. This is automatically deducted by the game from the owner's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool as long as the enchantment remains in existence. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
in the pool to pay for the spell, then it will dissipate, ceasing its
effect immediately. It may also be cancelled manually by left-clicking
its name in the *unit statistics window* of its target unit. However, this action can only be performed outside of combat, during the caster's own turn.

It's worth noting that in the official version of the game, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** units still count as [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") *on the overland map*. This means that **Black Channels**
may be freely targeted at such a unit overland, even though the game
would not allow this during combat. The enchantment will then work as
normal if the target enters any battle. This is considered to be a bug
however, and is no longer the case in the unofficial patches, which will
instead also return an error when trying to target the unit overland.

## Acquisition

As an [Uncommon Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Black Channels** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**1** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With none, the spell can naturally not be learned during the campaign.
This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Black Channels** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 10% (with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**1** book), which gradually increases with the amount of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**8** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **500**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**8** or more.

**Black Channels** may also be selected as a starting spell by players who allocate all of of their picks into [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** books when creating their Wizard. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

## Strategy

**Black Channels** offers an interesting trade-off, and should not just be cast randomly. Units turning [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead")
receive several negative properties that they didn't have previously,
such as the inability to heal, susceptibility to some spells, and
inability to take [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") [Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"). Thus, casting **Black Channels**
on a unit that would otherwise be powerful could turn it into a
liability. On the other hand, the bonuses bestowed both by Undeath and
by **Black Channels** itself may be worth this. The unit becomes stronger *and* more durable, and gains all of those fantastic immunities that can make it very useful against [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")** and even [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** armies and opponents.

One approach would therefore be to make a large army of low- or mid-tier Normal Units and use **Black Channels**
on all of them. After all, these are cheap, and so it wouldn't be too
much of a shame to lose them to an anti-Undead force – and they get
stronger bonuses in the meanwhile. Unfortunately, **Black Channels**' relatively high [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") may make this strategy less useful than it sounds. Having access to an [Armsmaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armsmaster "Armsmaster") [Armsmaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armsmaster "Armsmaster") [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") in this case can be extremely beneficial, as they can keep training these troops despite their inability to gain [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience** otherwise.

Alternatively, **Black Channels** can be used to turn high-tier units into Undead, and boost their power even further. This is particularly useful for [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") that also have access to the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration_(Spell) "Regeneration (Spell)")** spell – the only method to heal Undead Normal Units. With this enchantment, the **Black Channeled** unit will no longer be lost when its [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** run out. Undead Heroes may be able to heal themselves even without [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") too, as the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Vampiric](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vampiric_(Item_Power) "Vampiric (Item Power)")** [Item Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Power "Item Power") and the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain")** spell both deal [Life Stealing Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage") and, if they are lucky, they could also find or purchase a [Magical Item](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item") with the [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration_(Item_Power) "Regeneration (Item Power)")** power.

It's also worth remembering that **Black Channels** can be cast on any [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") unit, even if they were originally [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"),
despite the fact that it can't otherwise turn them into one. This
presents a natural combination with units and spells that can create
Undead from slain enemies, and can empower such "acquisitions" further.
Should **Black Channels** be dispelled, it may also be re-cast to restore its bonuses, although this unfortunately can not be done during combat.

## Known Bugs

The game has a significant design oversight when it comes to maximum [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** granted by spell effects. When such an effect begins or ends, it simply adjusts the unit's [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**, without ever modifying its recorded [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points").
While this may be considered appropriate when applying the effect,
meaning that it also "heals" the target that it affects, doing the same
upon its removal can result in effectively *damaging* the unit. If it has taken enough [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** to not survive without those extra [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, then it should be destroyed if this was indeed the intended behaviour.

However, this never happens, the computer does not even check whether the unit has sustained more [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** than it has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** without the spell. Thus, even if it had, it is still allowed to continue its existence as a "phantom" unit with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **0 figures**.
It remains visible on the overland map, and can even be commanded
during combat despite being completely invisible (there are no [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** in the unit, so nothing is drawn on-screen). If such a unit ever receives *any* [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**, it will be destroyed immediately. It also does not heal naturally – but then again in this case the unit is already [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), so this doesn't matter.

Even if the unit has enough [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points** left to survive, removing **Black Channels** will likely yield an unexpected result if it has suffered virtually any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. This is because instead of removing one [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Point** from all remaining [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, the game simply recalculates the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** count from the unit's base statistics, which typically means that it will have less [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**
than it had right before the spell was removed. This is a very messy
way of doing things, and the examples below should illustrate why.

#### Example #1

:   Lets take a unit of [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers"). This unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8 figures**, with each Slinger having [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1**, for a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**. Under the effect of **Black Channels**, each Slinger has [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2 Hit Points** – double the amount they originally had. The unit's total number of Hit Points is [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **16**, so we can say that **Black Channels** increased the unit's Hit Points by [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** – this will be important later.

:   Now lets assume this unit is damaged: [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **3 figures** are lost. The unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **5 figures** remaining, each with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2**. The unit survives the battle, but after it's done, we remove **Black Channels** from it. Here's what happens:

:   1. The unit's total Hit Points count is [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** × [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **5** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10**.
    2. We reduce this by the same amount that the unit received from **Black Channels**, which was [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**. Therefore, the unit now has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **10** − [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2 Hit Points**.
    3. Finally, we recalculate how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** the unit would now have, given that it only has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** left. Without **Black Channels**, each Slinger has only [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1**, so the unit now has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **2 figures** with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** each.

:   Due to this recalculation, we started with a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **5-figure** unit with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** each, but have ended up with a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **2-figure** unit having [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** each. This is because the game does not actually track [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hit Points**, only [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** taken. Explaining it this way, the [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **3 figures** the unit has lost in the battle amount to [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **3** × [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** = [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **6** points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**. Removing **Black Channels** does not modify this value, thus we end up with a unit that has [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **6** missing out of its now maximum of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8** × [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **1** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**, resulting in only [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **2 figures** remaining.

#### Example #2

:   Now lets assume that the same unit suffered [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4** casualties during combat while **Black Channels** was still in effect. So, the unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures** left, with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** each, for a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** in the entire unit. This is what happens now, when the spell is removed:

:   1. The unit's total Hit Points count is [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **2** × [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**.
    2. We reduce this by the same amount that the unit received from **Black Channels**, which was [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8**. Therefore, the unit now has a total of [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** − [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **8** = [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **0 Hit Points**! We can already see the problem right there.
    3. Finally we recalculate how many [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** the unit would now have. Obviously, with [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **0 Hit Points**, the unit has [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **0 figures** left.

:   This confuses the heck out of the game, because as far as it is
    concerned the unit should still be alive. Logically it should be – it
    had [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4 figures**
    just a second ago. The program simply doesn't bother to check whether
    the unit should still be allowed to exist. This results in a phantom
    unit which, as explained above, continues to exist until anything so
    much as sneezes at it – at which point it'll be destroyed.

### So what does this mean?

A phantom unit is inherently unstable; the game was not designed to handle the existence of units with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **0 figures**,
and may end up crashing for all sorts of subsidiary reasons (especially
divide by 0). This is more likely to happen if the unit goes into
combat and ends up being targeted by enemy spells.

Since the unit does not get natural healing, it will likely be
stuck in this condition permanently – or until destroyed. Fortunately,
being [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"), it also has no [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost")
(except any enchantments still affecting it, but these can be
cancelled). If there is no way of healing the unit in an artificial
manner, it may be better to *disband it* immediately to avoid
crashes, though the unit may also just be sent off as a scout or to
harass enemy armies. It has no other use.

Of course, the best way to handle this is to never cancel **Black Channels** while a unit is severely injured, but sometimes this is not up to the player. If at all possible, **Black Channels**
should be cast again on the unit, as it will immediately fix any of the
problems caused by its previous removal. Another good way to recreate
this unit is to cast [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration_(Spell) "Regeneration (Spell)")** on it and then send the unit into battle (not alone!). Once in combat, the unit *does* heal each turn, and will also be completely restored to full health and with [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** if the battle is ultimately won – even if the unit itself is destroyed while fighting.
