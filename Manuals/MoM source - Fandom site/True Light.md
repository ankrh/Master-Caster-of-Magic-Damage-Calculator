**True Light** is a [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **20** it may be cast during battles to both enhance [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Realm and hinder those of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**. This manifests as bonuses of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1**, [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1**, and a **+1** to all non-spell [Attack Strengths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** units; and respectively penalties of the same magnitude for [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** and [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") ones. The effect lasts until the end of the battle, or until dispelled by the opponent.

## Effect

**True Light** bathes the battlefield in divine energy. This increases the combat prowess of all [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** creatures, while simultaneously reducing that of all [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures.

### Life Creature Bonuses

While **True Light** is in effect over the battlefield, *each and every* [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") will receive the bonuses outlined below. The spell doesn't care about the unit's allegiance though, so it will affect enemy [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** creatures as well as friendly ones.

#### Attack Strength Bonus

:   **True Light** gives a universal [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") bonus of **+1** that applies to nearly all [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks that the affected units make, with the exceptions being those that deal [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **spell damage**. This is extremely straightforward for [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** creatures though, as they can only ever make [Melee Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), which essentially transforms this effect to a simple [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+1** in practice. The only other ways for a [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** units to deliver Conventional Damage are through the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation")** [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") or the [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") ability, neither of which are affected by **True Light**, as they are always considered to deal [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **spell damage**.

:   The extra point of Melee Strength allows the use of one extra die on each of the unit's [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll"), improving their "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output by an average of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0.3**, plus an additional [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0.1** for every [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** they have above the base chance. Naturally, this benefit is stronger for [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Unicorns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unicorns "Unicorns")** - the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm")'s only [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Unit** - as they can make more attacks with a single action.

#### Defense Bonus

:   **True Light** improves the [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") of the affected units by [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1**. As a result, they can use an extra die on their [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") when trying to mitigate [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage"). This yields an average reduction of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **+0.3**, or [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **+0.4** if [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[High Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Prayer "High Prayer")** are also in effect. Although [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Unicorns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unicorns "Unicorns")** can be said to receive more of a benefit from this as [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**, this is only true if, after a first Defense Roll, an attack still inflicts at least as much [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** as they have [Hit Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hit_Points "Hit Points") **Hits** per [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** - the condition for triggering another roll.

#### Resistance Bonus

:   **True Light** also improves the [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") of the affected units by [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1**. This makes them harder to affect with [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") and most forms of [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage"). Any unit that reaches [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **10**
    thanks to this bonus is now immune to all ill effects that don't impose
    a Resistance penalty, as they can no longer fail their checks against
    them.

### Death Creature Penalties

While **True Light** is in effect over the battlefield, *each and every* [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), including all [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units, will receive the penalties outlined below.

#### Attack Strength Penalty

:   **True Light** reduces the [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") of nearly all [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks made by the affected units, with the exceptions being any attacks that deal [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **spell damage**. Unlike [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** units however, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures are capable of possessing nearly every type of attack there is, owing largely to the fact that all [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") are irrevocably associated with this [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), even if they belonged to a different one in life.

:   In general, this decreases the amount of dice they can use on their [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") by one, resulting in an average "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output loss of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0.3** plus [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0.1** per every [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **+10% To Hit** that the unit has above the base chance. Naturally, [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**
    are struck harder by this, as they can normally perform multiple
    attacks with a single unit action. The same is true for units with [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown-** or [Breath Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Breath_Attack "Breath Attack") **Breath Attacks**. However, a reduction in Attack Strength can also have further implications. Namely, if it is reduced to zero as a result.

:   When the strength of a certain conventional [Attack Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") goes below 1, a unit is no longer able to perform that attack any more. In the case of Melee Strength, it of course can't [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")
    either. More importantly though, in the official game, disabling an
    attack this way also disables any "added effect" attacks that normally
    accompany it. This affects [Touch Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Touch_Attack "Touch Attack") and [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"). For the former, this is straightforward, even if not exactly intuitive. For example, if the Melee Strength of a unit with [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") [Poison Touch](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Touch "Poison Touch") is reduced to zero via magic, then its poison attack will no longer trigger from [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or Counter Attacks, even though the poison's strength is unaffected. The effect is simply not executed at all.

:   With Gaze Attacks, the biggest problem is that their exact
    method of operation is not documented in either the game's manual or its
    *Official Strategy Guide*. In fact, Gaze Attacks work more or less the same way as Touch Attacks, apart from the way they determine how many [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**
    checks need to be made by the target. They are also "added effects"
    that are triggered by Conventional Damage attacks. Except this attack is
    hidden from the player entirely, and its Attack Strength is not listed
    in the *unit statistics window*. As such, it is important to know that all Gaze Attack units have a [Short-Range Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Gaze_Attacks "Attack Strength") of [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **1**. Apart from the [Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn") that is, which has a [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **4** thanks to its [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze").

:   This matters because even small negative effects, like that of **True Light**, can easily reduce this to [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **0**, thereby disabling a Gaze Attack entirely! A prime example of this is the [Night Stalker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Night_Stalker "Night Stalker"), a native [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** unit with a powerful [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze") that, when subjected to **True Light**, will turn from a very dangerous opponent into an almost harmless one.

:   It's also worth noting that starting with the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50"),
    the zero Attack Strength mechanic is changed such that "added effects"
    are no longer disabled by it. This means both that Gaze Attacks can not
    be negated with universal Attack Strength penalties, and that when
    forced to attack or Counter Attack, zero Melee Strength units will still
    execute any Touch Attacks they may have.

#### Defense Penalty

:   **True Light** inflicts a [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") penalty of [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **-1** to the affected units. As a result, they will have 1 less die to use on their [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") when trying to mitigate [Conventional Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conventional_Damage "Conventional Damage") attacks, decreasing their [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** reduction by an average of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **0.3**. This makes these units more vulnerable to weaker attacks and, in the case of [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**, increases the likelyhood of them losing more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** to a stronger attack.

#### Resistance Penalty

:   **True Light** also inflicts a [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") penalty of [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-1** to the affected units. This makes them slightly easier to affect with [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"), and more prone to suffer [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage").

### True Light vs. Darkness

**True Light** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** are designed to be identical and opposite. **True Light** gives bonuses to [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** creatures and penalizes those of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**, while [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** exerts the exact same effect the other way around. The two spells are *not*
mutually exclusive on the battlefield. Therefore, it is possible to
have both spells affecting the battle simultaneously. Because they are
equal and opposite effects, they essentially cancel each other out.

### Heavenly Light

[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Heavenly Light](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heavenly_Light "Heavenly Light")** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Town Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Enchantment "Town Enchantment") that causes **True Light** to automatically be active in every battle that takes place at the enchanted [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town"). Instead of being displayed in the defender's *enchantment window*
however, it is shown in the combat information window ("Info" button)
as "Holy Light". These effects are one and the same though, to the point
that the defender can't cast **True Light** if "Holy Light" is already active.

## Usage

**True Light** may only be cast during combat, for a basic [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **20**. If the casting is successful, the spell's icon will appear in one of the *enchantment windows* at the bottom of the screen, underneath the name of the [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") whose side has cast the spell (left or right depending on whether it is cast by the defending or attacking force). **True Light**
will then start exerting its effect immediately, and remains active
until the end of the battle, or until it is dispelled. Since it does not
carry over to the overland map, the spell has no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

## Acquisition

As a [Common Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") of the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **True Light** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**1** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With none, the spell can naturally not be learned during the campaign.
This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **True Light** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 30% (with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**1** book), which gradually increases with the amount of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**7** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **190**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**8** or more.

**True Light** may also be selected as a starting spell by any Wizard with at least [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**2** books. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

## Strategy

In theory, **True Light** is a great and quick way to empower an army made up primarily of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** units. However, that's a fairly rare scenario in practice, not only because there are only 5 types of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**
units in the game (one of whom is actually unique), but they are also
generally better suited to support an army rather than form the bulk of
it. As such, this effect, by itself, is often not worth the [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") or, to be more precise, spending that [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** on other spells may be much more effective.

On the other hand, **True Light** is an excellent spell to weaken an enemy army made up primarily of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** and/or [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units. Of course, if they are controlled by another [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard"), then there's a high chance that they will respond with casting [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** - but then it's also likely that they would have done that anyway, and **True Light** is not a bad way of countering it. Where this spell really shines though are [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") featuring defenders from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm").
Here, it will almost always be worth its Cost if there are multiple
enemy units. Unless, of course, it can't be afforded, for example in an
early game where [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Prayer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Prayer "Prayer")** is also available.
