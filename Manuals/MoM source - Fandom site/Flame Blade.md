**Flame Blade** is an [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Unit Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **125**, it may be cast on a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") on the overland map to ignite its weapons with a magical flame. The unit receives an [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") bonus of **+2** to its [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack"), [Ranged Missile-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack"), and [Thrown Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack"), provided that it possesses such attacks. The unit also gains the ability to bypass the effects of [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") if it couldn't before.

Alternatively, a cheaper version of the same spell may be cast for [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25**
during combat, which will temporarily bestow the same bonus on a unit
for the duration of a single battle – with no permanent effects or [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost").

## Effects

**Flame Blade** causes the weapons of a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") to ignite with a magical flame that makes attacks with them more powerful.

### Magical Attack

[Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") that don't have at least [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **magical weapons** typically do reduced [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** against opponents with [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity"), as the ability raises its bearer's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **10** against such units if it would be lower otherwise. **Flame Blade** negates that effect, preventing Weapon Immunity from triggering against any attacks made by the enchanted unit.

### Melee Strength Bonus

If the unit has a non-zero [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength"), it is increased by **2**. This grants each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** in the unit 2 extra dice on their [Attack Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Roll "Attack Roll") when performing [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), improving the unit's potential- and average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage** output in hand-to-hand combat. Naturally, the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** the enchanted unit has, the more beneficial this effect becomes. Similarly, the higher the unit's chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**, the more it stands to gain from **Flame Blade**, since each extra die is more likely to be successful in causing a point of "raw" [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

### Ranged Missile Strength Bonus

If the unit has [Ranged Missile Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack"), their strength is increased by **2**. Like the melee bonus, this also applies on a per-[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** basis, and will be more pronounced on units with high [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure** counts and/or chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**. However, **Flame Blade** does not affect [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical-** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks** in any way.

### Thrown Attack Strength Bonus

If the unit possesses the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability, its [Short-Range Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Short-Range_Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") is increased by **2**. Like the previous two modifiers, this one is also more beneficial to units with multiple [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures** and/or a high chance [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit**.

### Not Fire Damage

Despite its name and implied effect, **Flame Blade** does not cause the unit's attack to deliver [Fire Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Damage "Fire Damage"). This is important because there are host of different conditional [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") modifiers that trigger against Fire Damage – with [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") [Fire Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Immunity "Fire Immunity") being merely the most obvious one –, and these would all provide protection against the enchanted unit if **Flame Blade** did transform all or part of its attacks to this [Damage Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Type "Damage Type"). For the same reason however, the spell doesn't allow bypassing [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") [Missile Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Missile_Immunity "Missile Immunity") either, although the target will likely have a slightly higher chance of overcoming it thanks to its increased [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **damage**.

## Usage

**Flame Blade** may be cast both overland and in combat, for the basic [Casting Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **125** and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **25** respectively. If the casting is successful, the game will prompt for a friendly [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")
to be selected as the spell's target. On the overland map, this
typically requires clicking on the army stack that contains the unit
which, in turn, brings up a list view of all units in that stack,
allowing for the target to be picked individually. Only units not
already under the effect of **Flame Blade** may be chosen. Once the spell is cast, a glowing [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[red](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** outline appears around the unit (unless it is overridden by a stronger enchantment); and **Flame Blade**, along with its icon, will be displayed in the *unit statistics window* when inspecting this unit.

The overland version of **Flame Blade** has an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **2**
that must be paid at the beginning of every turn to keep the spell in
effect. This is automatically deducted by the game from the owner's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** pool as long as the enchantment remains in existence. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
in the pool to pay for the spell, then it will dissipate, ceasing its
effect immediately. It may also be cancelled manually by left-clicking
its name in the *unit statistics window* of its target unit. However, this action can only be performed outside of combat, during the caster's own turn.

The combat version of **Flame Blade**
does not carry over to the overland map: it will disappear
automatically as soon as the battle is over. As such, it has no Upkeep
Cost; but at the same time may not be manually removed by its caster
either (as enchantments can not be cancelled during a battle).

It's worth noting that in the official version of the game, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** units still count as [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") *on the overland map*. This means that **Flame Blade**
may be freely targeted at such a unit overland, even though the game
would not allow this during combat. The enchantment will then work as
normal if the target enters any battle. This is considered to be a bug
however, and is no longer the case in the unofficial patches, which will
instead also return an error when trying to target the unit overland.

## Acquisition

As an [Uncommon Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), **Flame Blade** may become available to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who acquires at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**1** [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").
With none, the spell can naturally not be learned during the campaign.
This is the minimum requirement for it to be traded for, appear in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure"), or be found in the spoils of victory when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it.

The base chance for **Flame Blade** to be [researchable](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research "Spell Research") (at some point) in the campaign is roughly 10% (with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**1** book), which gradually increases with the amount of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Spellbooks possessed or found during gameplay. With [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**8** or more, the spell is certain to show up sooner or later, unless acquired from another source. It has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Research#Research_Cost "Spell Research") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **450**, although its research is quicker for Wizards possessing the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master"), and/or [Chaos Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Mastery "Chaos Mastery") [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"); or a bookshelf containing [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**8** or more.

**Flame Blade** may also be selected as a starting spell by players who allocate all of of their picks into [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** books when creating their Wizard. In this case, it will be available for casting as soon as the game begins.

## Strategy

**Flame Blade** confers a significant bonus to [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and [Ranged Missile Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") – and especially to units that either have both of these [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type"), or possess the [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") [Thrown](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown "Thrown") ability, since they can effectively gain double the benefit. While the spell does help [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single-Figure Units**, its full potential will only manifest on [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** ones – and the more [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figures**, the stronger the effect.

Because of this, **Flame Blade** can be particularly useful early in the game, as it can make low-tier [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") considerably more powerful than they would be otherwise. The same is true of the spell's effect of bypassing [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity"), as later on, [Alchemists' Guild](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild "Alchemists' Guild") usually become common enough that this is no longer a problem – although some [Races](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Races "Races") are unable to construct this [Town Building](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Building "Town Building"), which means that for them, **Flame Blade** can remain relevant throughout their campaign.
